## Getting started

1. If 1 apple costs £2, how much do 2 apples cost?
2. In direct proportion, if one quantity doubles, what happens to the other?
3. Using y = 2x, find y when x = 4.
4. In y = 3x, what is the constant of proportionality?
5. If 2 workers take 6 hours in an inverse relationship, would more workers take more or less time?
6. Using y = 12/x, find y when x = 3.

8. Which equation is inverse proportion: y = 8x or y = 8/x?

11. y is directly proportional to x. When x = 2, y = 10. Find y when x = 5.
12. y is inversely proportional to x. When x = 4, x × y = 20. Find y when x = 5.

## Reasoning (AO2)

24. Mia says that in direct proportion, when x doubles, y also doubles. Is she correct? Explain.

25. **Always, sometimes, or never?** In inverse proportion, when x doubles, y halves.

26. **True or false?** y = kx + 2 represents direct proportion. Explain.

27. Priya says that if y is inversely proportional to x, then x × y is always the same value. Is she correct? Explain.

28. Explain the difference between direct and inverse proportion, using one example of each.

29. Sam says "the more workers on a job, the less time it takes" is an example of direct proportion. Is Sam correct? What type of proportion is it really?

30. Leo has a table: x = 1, y = 12; x = 2, y = 6; x = 3, y = 4. Is this direct or inverse proportion? Explain how you know.

## Problem (AO3)
