## Getting started

1. A square has side 2 cm. Find its area.
2. A cube has side 2 cm. Find its volume.
3. Double 3 cm. What is the new length?
4. A square's side doubles from 1 cm to 2 cm. Its area changes from 1 cm² to how many cm²?
5. A cube's side doubles from 1 cm to 2 cm. Its volume changes from 1 cm³ to how many cm³?
6. Complete: an area scale factor uses the length scale factor squared, written k___.
7. Complete: a volume scale factor uses the length scale factor cubed, written k___.
8. Two squares have side lengths 1 cm and 2 cm. Write their areas as a ratio.
9. A square has side 3 cm. Find its area.
10. A cube has side 3 cm. Find its volume.
11. Two similar shapes have length scale factor 3. What is the area scale factor?
12. Two similar shapes have length scale factor 2. What is the volume scale factor?

## Reasoning (AO2)

24. Mia says that if you double the side of a square, the area also just doubles. Is she correct? Use an example to check.

25. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Enlarging a 2D shape by a length scale factor k multiplies its area by k².

26. **True or false?** Enlarging a 3D solid by a length scale factor k multiplies its volume by k³. Explain.

27. Priya says two similar cubes with a side ratio of 1 : 2 must have the same ratio for surface area and for volume. Is she correct? Explain.

28. Explain, using a square, why the area scale factor equals (length scale factor)².

29. Sam says if the area of a shape is multiplied by 9, the length scale factor must be 9. Is Sam correct? Find the actual length scale factor.

30. Leo says the volume scale factor for a solid enlarged by scale factor 1/2 is 1/2. Is Leo correct? Find the correct volume scale factor.
