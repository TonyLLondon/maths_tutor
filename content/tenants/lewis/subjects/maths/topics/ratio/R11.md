## Getting started

1. A car travels 10 miles in 1 hour. Find its speed.
2. A runner covers 20 metres in 4 seconds. Find the speed.
3. Complete: speed = distance ÷ ___.
4. At 3 miles per hour for 2 hours, how far do you travel?
5. A 2 kg bag costs £4. Find the price per kg.
6. Complete: density = mass ÷ ___.
7. A 6 kg object fills 2 m³. Find its density.
8. A 12-mile journey at 4 mph takes how many hours?
9. A car travels 30 miles in 2 hours. Find its speed in miles per hour.
10. A 5 kg object has volume 2 m³. Find its density in kg/m³.
11. At 60 mph for 0.5 hours, how far do you travel?
12. Complete: pressure = force ÷ ___.

## Reasoning (AO2)

24. Mia says speed = distance ÷ time always gives units like "miles per hour" or "m/s." Is she correct? Explain what these units tell you.

25. **Always, sometimes, or never?** If you know an object's density and mass, you can find its volume.

26. **True or false?** Converting different pack sizes to "price per kg" (or per litre) is a fair way to compare their value. Explain.

27. Priya travels 60 miles in 1.5 hours and says her speed was 40 mph. Is she correct?

28. Explain why speed, density and price per unit are all called "compound units."

29. Sam has 2 litres of a liquid weighing 2.5 kg and says its density is 1.25 g/cm³. Check Sam's answer (2.5 kg = 2500 g, 2 litres = 2000 cm³).

30. Leo says a car that goes faster always uses more fuel per mile. Is this necessarily true? Explain.
