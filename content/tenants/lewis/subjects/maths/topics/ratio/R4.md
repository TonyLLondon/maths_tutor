## Getting started

1. Write “2 red for every 3 blue” as a ratio red : blue.
2. How many numbers are in the ratio 4 : 5?
3. Simplify 2 : 4.
4. Simplify 3 : 6.
5. Complete: 1 : 2 = 2 : ___.
6. Complete: 2 : 3 = 4 : ___.
7. Are 1 : 2 and 2 : 4 equivalent?

9. Simplify the ratio 6 : 9.
10. Write the ratio 10 : 5 in simplest form.
11. Complete the equivalent ratio: 3 : 5 = 9 : ___.
12. Are the ratios 2 : 3 and 4 : 6 equivalent?

## Reasoning (AO2)

24. Mia simplifies 8 : 12 to 2 : 3. Is she correct? Show the working.

25. **Always, sometimes, or never?** A ratio can be simplified if both numbers share a common factor greater than 1.

26. **True or false?** The ratio 4 : 6 is the same as the ratio 6 : 4. Explain your answer.

27. Priya writes a ratio as "1 : 2.5" and says this isn't a "proper" ratio because it has a decimal. Is she right? How could you rewrite it using whole numbers?

28. Explain why the ratio 10 : 15 and the ratio 2 : 3 describe the same relationship.

29. Sam has a bag with red and blue marbles in the ratio 3 : 4. He says there must be exactly 3 red marbles. Is Sam correct? Explain.

30. A ratio is given as a : b = 4 : 7. Explain what happens to the ratio if you swap it to b : a.
