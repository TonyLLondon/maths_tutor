## Getting started

1. A shape is copied at the same size. What is its scale factor?
2. A 3 cm line is enlarged by scale factor 2. How long is the new line?
3. A 10 cm line is reduced by scale factor 1/2. How long is the new line?
4. Does a scale factor of 3 enlarge or reduce a shape?
5. Does a scale factor of 1/4 enlarge or reduce a shape?
6. A map uses 1 cm for 2 km. What real distance does 1 cm represent?
7. A map uses 1 cm for 2 km. What real distance does 3 cm represent?
8. Complete: at scale 1 : 10, 1 cm on a model represents ___ cm in real life.
9. A 4 cm line is enlarged by scale factor 3. How long is the new line?
10. A map uses 1 cm for 5 km. What real distance does 2 cm represent?
11. Does a scale factor of 1/2 enlarge or reduce a shape?
12. At scale 1 : 100, 2 cm on a drawing represents ___ cm in real life.

## Reasoning (AO2)

26. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Multiplying a shape's dimensions by a scale factor between 0 and 1 makes the shape larger.

27. Two maps of the same area use scales 1 : 10 000 and 1 : 25 000. Which map would show more detail? Explain using the scale numbers.
