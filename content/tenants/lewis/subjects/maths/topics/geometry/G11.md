## Getting started

1. Point A is at (2, 1). What is its x-coordinate?
2. Point A is at (2, 1). What is its y-coordinate?
3. Start at (0, 0) and move 3 right. What point do you reach?
4. Start at (0, 0) and move 2 up. What point do you reach?
5. Which point is farther right: (1, 2) or (4, 2)?
6. Points (1, 1) and (1, 4) lie on the same vertical line. How many squares apart are they?
7. Points (2, 3) and (6, 3) lie on the same horizontal line. How many squares apart are they?
8. What point is halfway between (0, 0) and (4, 0)?

## Fluency (AO1)

9. Point A is at (2, 3) and point B is at (2, 7). Find the distance AB.
10. Point C is at (1, 5) and point D is at (6, 5). Find the distance CD.
11. Find the midpoint of A(2, 3) and B(2, 7).
12. Find the midpoint of (0, 0) and (8, 6).
13. Point (3, 4) is reflected in the x-axis. Find its image.
14. Point (3, 4) is reflected in the y-axis. Find its image.
15. A square has three vertices at (0, 0), (4, 0), and (4, 4). Find the fourth vertex.
16. Complete: the midpoint of (x₁, y₁) and (x₂, y₂) is ( (x₁+x₂)/2 , __________ ).
17. A point moves from (1, 2) to (5, 2). How far did it move, and in which direction?
18. Points (0, 3) and (0, −5) both lie on which axis?
19. Points (4, 0) and (−6, 0) both lie on which axis?
20. **True or false?** The midpoint of a line segment always lies exactly halfway between its two endpoints.


## More fluency

21. Find the midpoint of (2, 4) and (6, 8).

22. Find the distance between (0, 0) and (3, 4).

## Reasoning (AO2)

23. A rectangle has three vertices at (1, 1), (5, 1), and (5, 4). Find the fourth vertex.

24. Explain how to find the midpoint of two points, using the example (2, 8) and (6, 2).

25. A line segment has endpoints (1, 1) and (7, 9). Find its length, using the right-angled triangle formed by the horizontal distance (6), the vertical distance (8), and Pythagoras' theorem.

26. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Reflecting a point in the x-axis changes its y-coordinate but keeps its x-coordinate the same.

27. A parallelogram has vertices A(0, 0), B(4, 0), C(6, 3), in order round the shape. Find vertex D.

28. Sam says the midpoint of (2, 2) and (8, 8) is (5, 5). Is Sam correct? Check and explain.

29. Point P(4, 6) is the midpoint of A(2, 2) and B. Find the coordinates of B.

30. Two points are (3, −2) and (3, 6). Find the distance between them and explain your method.

## Problem (AO3)

31. Archer plots three corners of a rectangle on a map grid: (2, 1), (2, 5), (7, 5). Find the fourth corner.

32. A treasure map marks two points: a well at (1, 2) and an oak tree at (9, 8). The treasure is buried at the midpoint between them. Find its coordinates.

33. A straight path in a park runs from (0, 0) to (6, 8) on a grid (units in metres). Find the length of the path using Pythagoras' theorem.

34. A square garden has one corner at (2, 2) and the opposite corner at (8, 8). Find the coordinates of the centre of the garden (the midpoint of that diagonal).

35. A kite shape has vertices A(0, 4), B(3, 0), C(0, −4), D(−3, 0). Find the length of diagonal AC and the length of diagonal BD.

36. A logo's original point is (5, −3). To make a symmetric design, the logo is reflected in the x-axis. Find the reflected point.

## Stretch

37. A line segment has endpoints (−2, 1) and (4, 9). Find its midpoint, then find its length using Pythagoras' theorem.

38. Explain how you would find a fourth vertex of a parallelogram if you know the other three vertices, using the idea that opposite sides are equal and parallel.

39. A point (a, b) is reflected in the x-axis and then in the y-axis. Find its final coordinates in terms of a and b, and explain why this combined effect is the same as rotating the point 180° about the origin.

40. Find the midpoint of the line segment joining $(2, 4)$ and $(8, 10)$.
