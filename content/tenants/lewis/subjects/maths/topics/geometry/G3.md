## Getting started

1. What do we call the turn at a corner?
2. How many right angles does a square have?
3. How many degrees are in a right angle?
4. Which is smaller: an acute angle or a right angle?
5. Which is larger: an obtuse angle or a right angle?
6. True or false? A straight line makes a half turn.
7. Two equal angles make one right angle. How many degrees is each angle?
8. A right angle is split into 30° and another angle. Find the other angle.

## Fluency (AO1)

9. Angles on a straight line add up to __________°.
10. Angles around a point add up to __________°.
11. Vertically opposite angles (made where two lines cross) are always __________ (equal/different).
12. Two angles on a straight line are 70° and x. Find x.
13. Three angles around a point are 100°, 150°, and y. Find y.
14. The angles inside a triangle add up to __________°.
15. A triangle has angles 50° and 60°. Find the third angle.
16. The angles inside a quadrilateral add up to __________°.
17. When a line crosses two parallel lines, matching angles in the same corner position are called __________ angles, and they are equal.
18. Alternate angles (the "Z" pattern) between two parallel lines are always __________.
19. Co-interior angles (the "C" pattern) between two parallel lines add up to __________°.
20. A straight line is split into three angles: 35°, 85°, and z. Find z.


## More fluency

21. Angles on a straight line add to ___ degrees.

22. Vertically opposite angles are ___ (equal/unequal).

## Reasoning (AO2)

23. Two parallel lines are cut by a third line (a transversal). One angle is 65°. Find its corresponding angle, and explain how you know.

24. Explain, describing a diagram with two parallel lines and a transversal, why alternate angles are equal.

25. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Vertically opposite angles are equal.

26. A quadrilateral has angles 90°, 90°, 100°, and w. Find w and explain your method.

27. Sam says, "Co-interior angles are always equal." Is Sam correct? Explain, using an example.

28. The exterior angle of a triangle equals the sum of the two interior angles at the other two vertices. A triangle has interior angles 50° and 70°. Find the exterior angle at the third vertex.

29. Three angles meet at a point. Two of them are in the ratio 2 : 3, and the third angle is 100°. Find the two angles in the ratio.

30. Leo says a quadrilateral can never have three right angles and one obtuse angle. Is Leo correct? Explain your answer.

## Problem (AO3)

31. A ladder leans against a vertical wall, making a 65° angle with the ground. Assuming the wall and ground meet at a right angle, what angle does the ladder make with the wall?

32. Two roads are parallel, and a crossing road creates one angle of 118° where it meets the first road. Give the sizes of the two *different* angles found at that crossing (there are only two distinct sizes among the four angles).

33. A roof truss is an isosceles triangle with a base angle of 72° on both sides. Find the angle at the top.

34. A gate hinge crosses two bars, forming vertically opposite angles of (3x + 10)° and (2x + 40)°. Find x, then find the angle.

35. A kite-shaped flag has two equal angles of 100° each. Its other two angles are in the ratio 3 : 1. Find both of the other angles.

36. A bridge support triangle has an exterior angle of 130° at one vertex. The two interior angles at the other vertices are equal to each other. Find each of those two angles.
