## Getting started

1. A shape slides 3 squares right. Has it turned?
2. Which word describes a mirror move: flip, slide, or grow?
3. Which word describes moving a shape without turning it: slide or flip?
4. A shape makes a quarter turn. Has its size changed?
5. True or false? A mirror image has the same size as the original.
6. A 2 cm square is made twice as large. What is its new side length?
7. A shape is copied in a new place without changing its size or direction. Did it slide?
8. Which move can make a shape bigger: enlargement or reflection?

## Fluency (AO1)

9. Name the transformation that slides a shape without turning or flipping it.
10. Name the transformation that turns a shape around a fixed point.
11. Name the transformation that flips a shape over a mirror line.
12. Name the transformation that makes a shape bigger or smaller from a centre of enlargement.
13. Which three transformations (translation, rotation, reflection, enlargement) always produce a **congruent** image?
14. **True or false?** Enlargement always produces a similar (not congruent) shape, unless the scale factor is 1.
15. A shape is translated using the column vector (3, −2). Explain what this translation does.
16. A shape is turned 90° clockwise about the origin. Is this a rotation, reflection, or translation?
17. A shape is flipped over the line y = x. What is this line called?
18. A shape is enlarged by scale factor 2. Does the image become bigger or smaller than the original?
19. A shape is enlarged by scale factor ½. Does the image become bigger or smaller than the original?
20. Complete: for a **negative** scale factor, the image appears on the __________ side of the centre of enlargement, turned upside down.


## More fluency

21. After a translation, is a shape congruent to the original?

22. After an enlargement scale factor 2, is the image similar to the original?

## Reasoning (AO2)

23. A triangle is translated by vector (4, 1) and then reflected in the x-axis. Describe, step by step, what happens to the shape.

24. Explain why translations, rotations, and reflections always produce congruent shapes, but enlargement (with scale factor not equal to 1) does not.

25. A shape is enlarged from a centre of enlargement by scale factor 3. A side on the original shape is 2 cm. Find the length of the matching side on the image.

26. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Reflecting a shape twice in the same mirror line returns it to its original position.

27. A shape has area 12 cm². It is enlarged by scale factor 2. Find the area of the enlarged image.

28. Sam says, "If I rotate a shape by 360°, it ends up in a different position." Is Sam correct? Explain.

29. A shape is enlarged by scale factor ⅓. Its original side is 9 cm. Find the matching side on the image.

30. Explain the difference between the *centre of rotation* and a *mirror line* — what role does each play in its transformation?

## Problem (AO3)

31. A logo is moved 5 cm right and 3 cm up to make a repeated pattern. Write this movement as a column vector.

32. A model car is an enlargement of a real car with scale factor 1/20. The real car is 4 m long. Find the length of the model, in centimetres.

33. A photograph is enlarged by scale factor 2.5 for a poster. The photo is 10 cm wide. Find the width of the poster.

34. A triangle with area 8 cm² is enlarged by scale factor 4. Find the area of the enlarged triangle.

35. A shape is reflected in the y-axis, then translated by vector (2, 0). Point A(3, 4) is on the original shape. Find its coordinates after both transformations.

36. A shape is rotated 90° clockwise about the origin, using the rule (x, y) → (y, −x). Point (2, 5) is on the shape. Find its image.

## Stretch

40. A triangle is translated and then reflected. Is the final image congruent to the original? Explain briefly.
