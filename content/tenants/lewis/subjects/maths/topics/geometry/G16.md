## Getting started

1. A rectangle is 4 squares long and 2 squares wide. How many unit squares cover it?
2. A rectangle has 3 rows of 5 unit squares. Find its area in square units.
3. A triangle is exactly half of a 6-square rectangle. What is its area?
4. A parallelogram can be rearranged to make a rectangle. Does its area change?
5. A box is 3 cubes long, 2 cubes wide, and 1 cube high. How many unit cubes fill it?
6. A cube has side length 2 cm. How many 1 cm cubes fill it?
7. Which unit measures area: cm² or cm³?
8. Which unit measures volume: cm² or cm³?

## Fluency (AO1)

9. Area of a triangle = 1/2 × base × height. Find the area of a triangle with base 8 cm and height 5 cm.
10. Area of a parallelogram = base × height. Find the area of a parallelogram with base 6 cm and height 4 cm.
11. A triangle has base 10 cm and height 6 cm. Find its area.
12. A parallelogram has base 9 cm and height 5 cm. Find its area.
13. Area of a trapezium = 1/2 × (a + b) × height, where a and b are the parallel sides. A trapezium has parallel sides 4 cm and 8 cm, and height 3 cm. Find its area.
14. How many unit cubes (1 cm cubes) fit into one layer of a cuboid that is 4 cubes long and 3 cubes wide?
15. The same cuboid is 2 cubes high. Find its volume in cm³.
16. Volume of a cuboid = length × width × height. Find the volume of a cuboid 5 cm × 2 cm × 2 cm.
17. Volume of a prism = area of cross-section × length. A triangular prism has a cross-sectional area of 12 cm² and length 10 cm. Find its volume.
18. Volume of a cylinder = π × radius² × height. Find the volume of a cylinder with radius 3 cm and height 10 cm (use π ≈ 3.14, round to the nearest whole number).
19. A cube has edge length 4 cm. Find its volume.
20. A triangle has base 12 cm and area 30 cm². Find its height.

## Reasoning (AO2)

21. Explain why the area formula for a parallelogram (base × height) works, thinking about cutting a triangle off one end and moving it to the other to make a rectangle.

22. A trapezium has parallel sides a and b, and height h. Using the idea of splitting it into a rectangle and a triangle (or two triangles), explain why its area formula is 1/2 × (a + b) × h.

23. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Doubling the base of a triangle doubles its area, if the height stays the same.

24. A parallelogram and a rectangle have the same base and the same height. Compare their areas.

25. Sam says, "A prism's volume is always length × width × height, just like a cuboid." Is Sam correct? Explain using a triangular prism.

26. Two cuboids both have volume 12 cm³. One is 6 × 2 × 1 cm. Give different whole-number dimensions for another cuboid with the same volume.

27. A cylinder and a cuboid have the same height. Explain what determines which one has the larger volume.

28. Leo says, "Doubling both the base and height of a triangle doubles its area." Is Leo correct? Explain using an example.
