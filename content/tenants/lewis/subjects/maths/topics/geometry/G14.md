## Getting started

1. A square has side length 2 cm. Find its perimeter.
2. A rectangle is 3 cm long and 2 cm wide. Find its perimeter.
3. How many 1 cm squares cover a rectangle that is 3 squares long and 2 squares wide?
4. Which measures the distance around a shape: area or perimeter?
5. Which measures the space inside a flat shape: area or perimeter?
6. A square has side length 4 cm. Find its perimeter.
7. A rectangle has 4 rows of 2 unit squares. Find its area.
8. What unit is suitable for the area of a small rectangle: cm or cm²?

## Fluency (AO1)

9. A rectangle is 8 cm long and 5 cm wide. Work out its **perimeter**.
10. The same rectangle (8 cm by 5 cm). Work out its **area**.
11. Write the formula for the area of a rectangle using **length** and **width**.
12. A square has side length 6 m. Find its perimeter and its area.
13. Change 200 cm to metres. Change 3 m² to cm² (remember: 1 m = 100 cm).
14. Use the table. An L-shape is made from two rectangles that share one side. Find the **total area** of the L-shape.
15. Write the formula for the perimeter of a rectangle using **length** and **width**.
16. A square has area 49 cm². What is the length of each side?
17. A rectangle has length 12 cm and area 60 cm². Find its width.
18. Find the perimeter of a regular pentagon with each side 7 cm.
19. A rectangular field is 40 m by 25 m. Find its perimeter in metres.
20. Change 5 m to cm. Change 15 000 cm² to m².

## Reasoning (AO2)

21. Two rectangles each have area 24 cm². Rectangle A is 6 cm by 4 cm. Rectangle B is 12 cm by 2 cm. Do they have the same **perimeter**? Show working for both perimeters.

22. **Always, sometimes, or never?** If you double the length and width of a rectangle, you double its area. Test with the rectangle 3 cm by 2 cm.

23. Sam finds the perimeter of a 7 cm by 3 cm rectangle by calculating $7 \times 3 = 21$ cm. Explain Sam’s mistake and give the correct perimeter.

24. Two shapes have the same perimeter of 20 cm. Shape A is a square. Shape B is a rectangle 8 cm by 2 cm. Do they have the same area? Show your working.

25. Priya says a shape with a bigger perimeter always has a bigger area. Give an example to show she is wrong.

26. A rectangle’s length is doubled but its width stays the same. What happens to its perimeter? What happens to its area? Explain using an example.

27. Leo says that all rectangles with area 36 cm² have the same perimeter. Test this with two different rectangles of area 36 cm².

28. **True or false?** A square is the rectangle with the smallest perimeter for a given area. Test with a 6 cm × 6 cm square compared with a 9 cm × 4 cm rectangle (both area 36 cm²).

## Problem (AO3)

29. A classroom wall is covered with rectangular posters. Each poster is 50 cm wide and 70 cm tall. How much **wall area** do three posters cover altogether? Give your answer in cm².

30. A garden path is an L-shape. The whole shape fits in the outer rectangle in the table, but the grass corner shown is not path. Work out the **area of the path** in m².

31. A school football pitch is 90 m long and 45 m wide. A groundsperson needs to walk around the pitch twice to mark the lines. How far do they walk in total?

32. Carpet costs £12 per m². How much would it cost to carpet a rectangular room that is 4 m by 3.5 m?

33. A picture frame and its outer size are shown in the table. Find the **area of just the wooden border**.

34. A rectangular allotment, 12 m by 8 m, is divided into two equal rectangular plots by a fence down the middle, parallel to the shorter (8 m) side. Find the area of one plot and the length of the new dividing fence.
