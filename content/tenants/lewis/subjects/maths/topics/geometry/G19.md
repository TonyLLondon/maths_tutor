## Getting started

1. Two pictures have the same shape, but one is bigger. Are their shapes similar?
2. A 2 cm line is doubled in length. What is its new length?
3. A 3 cm side is made three times as long. Find the new length.
4. A square with side 2 cm is enlarged so every side doubles. Find the new side length.
5. True or false? Enlarging a shape changes its angles.
6. A 10 cm side is shrunk to half its length. Find the new length.
7. One triangle has angles 40°, 60°, and 80°. A similar triangle has the same angles. What is its smallest angle?
8. A side grows from 3 cm to 6 cm. What number was it multiplied by?

## Fluency (AO1)

9. Two shapes are similar. What must be true about their angles?
10. Two shapes are similar. What must be true about their corresponding sides?
11. Triangle A has a side of 4 cm. The matching side on similar triangle B is 12 cm. Find the scale factor from A to B.
12. Triangle A has another side of 5 cm. Using the scale factor from Q11, find the matching side on triangle B.
13. Two similar rectangles have widths 3 cm and 9 cm. Find the scale factor.
14. Using the scale factor from Q13, if the smaller rectangle has length 5 cm, find the length of the larger rectangle.
15. Two similar triangles have corresponding sides 6 cm and 15 cm. Find the scale factor.
16. A photo measuring 10 cm by 15 cm is enlarged so that the 10 cm side becomes 25 cm. Find the scale factor.
17. Using the scale factor from Q16, find the new length of the 15 cm side.
18. Two similar shapes have a scale factor of 4 from the smaller to the larger. The larger shape has a side of 20 cm. Find the matching side on the smaller shape.
19. Are two squares always similar to each other, regardless of their size? Explain briefly.
20. Are two rectangles always similar to each other, regardless of their dimensions? Explain briefly.


## More fluency

22. Two similar triangles have sides 4 cm and 10 cm on matching sides. Find the scale factor (small to large).

## Reasoning (AO2)

24. **True or false?** All circles are similar to each other.

25. Ravi has two triangles: Triangle A has sides 3, 4, 5 cm. Triangle B has sides 6, 8, 11 cm. Check whether the triangles are similar by comparing the ratios of corresponding sides.

26. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Two similar shapes are congruent.

27. Priya says that if one shape's sides are all 3 cm longer than another shape's, the shapes must be similar. Explain why this is not necessarily true, using an example.

28. Explain why all equilateral triangles are similar to each other, but not all isosceles triangles are similar to each other.

29. Sloan finds the scale factor between two similar shapes by subtracting one side from the other, instead of dividing. Explain her mistake, using an example with sides 4 cm and 12 cm.

30. Two similar triangles have sides in the ratio 2:5. Leo says the scale factor must be 2. Explain why this is incomplete, and give the correct scale factor.

## Problem (AO3)

31. A ladder leaning against a wall forms a triangle similar to a smaller triangle formed by a shorter support further up the wall. The full ladder triangle has a base of 3 m and height of 4 m. The smaller similar triangle has a base of 1.5 m. Find its height.

32. Two similar flagpoles cast shadows at the same time of day. The taller pole is 6 m tall and casts a 4 m shadow. The shorter pole casts a 2.4 m shadow. Find the height of the shorter pole (using similar triangles).

33. A model bridge is built at a scale where a real support beam of 18 m is represented by a 30 cm model beam. Find the scale factor from the real bridge to the model, then find the model length of a real beam that is 12 m long.

34. Two similar triangles have perimeters 24 cm and 36 cm. One side of the smaller triangle is 8 cm. Find the scale factor, and the length of the matching side on the larger triangle.

35. A tree casts a shadow of 12 m. At the same time, a 1.5 m tall person standing nearby casts a shadow of 2 m. Using similar triangles, find the height of the tree.

36. Two similar kites have diagonals in the ratio 3:7. The smaller kite has a diagonal of 15 cm. Find the length of the matching diagonal on the larger kite.

## Stretch

37. Two similar triangles share a vertex and have parallel bases. Use the table. Find the lengths of the other two sides of the larger triangle.

38. A rectangular photo of width 8 cm and height 12 cm is to be enlarged to fit a frame of width 20 cm, keeping it similar (not stretched). Find the height needed for the enlarged photo to remain similar to the original.

39. Two similar triangles have scale factor $k$ from the smaller to the larger. If one side of the smaller triangle is $x$ cm and the matching side of the larger triangle is $(x + 10)$ cm, and $k = 3$, find the value of $x$.

40. A surveyor uses similar triangles to find the height of a building: a 2 m pole casts a 3 m shadow, and at the same time the building casts a 45 m shadow. Find the height of the building, showing your method using similar triangles.


## Mixed practice

42. A 1.8 m tall person casts a 2.4 m shadow. A flagpole casts 8 m shadow. Find the flagpole height.
