## Getting started

1. How many right angles does a right-angled triangle have?
2. What is the longest side of a right-angled triangle called?
3. Which number squared equals 9?
4. Which number squared equals 16?
5. Calculate 3².
6. Calculate 4².
7. Add 3² + 4².
8. Which positive number squared equals 25?

## Fluency (AO1)

9. A right-angled triangle has shorter sides (legs) 3 cm and 4 cm. Use Pythagoras' theorem to find the hypotenuse.
10. A right-angled triangle has a hypotenuse of 10 cm and one leg of 6 cm. Find the other leg.
11. A right-angled triangle has legs 5 cm and 12 cm. Find the hypotenuse.
12. Write down the three trigonometric ratios: $\sin\theta$, $\cos\theta$, $\tan\theta$, each in terms of the opposite, adjacent, and hypotenuse sides.
13. In a right-angled triangle, the hypotenuse is 8 cm and an angle is 30°. Find the length of the side opposite this angle using $\sin\theta = \dfrac{\text{opposite}}{\text{hypotenuse}}$ (to 1 dp).
14. In a right-angled triangle, the adjacent side is 6 cm and the angle is 40°. Find the hypotenuse using $\cos\theta = \dfrac{\text{adjacent}}{\text{hypotenuse}}$ (to 1 dp).
15. In a right-angled triangle, the opposite side is 5 cm and the adjacent side is 7 cm. Find the angle using $\tan\theta = \dfrac{\text{opposite}}{\text{adjacent}}$ (to 1 dp).
16. A right-angled triangle has legs 8 cm and 15 cm. Find the hypotenuse.
17. A ladder (hypotenuse) is 13 m long and its base is 5 m from a wall. Find how high up the wall it reaches, using Pythagoras.
18. In a right-angled triangle, the hypotenuse is 12 cm and the angle is 25°. Find the opposite side (to 1 dp).
19. In a right-angled triangle, the opposite side is 9 cm and the hypotenuse is 15 cm. Find the angle using $\sin\theta = \dfrac{\text{opposite}}{\text{hypotenuse}}$ (to 1 dp).
20. A right-angled triangle has a hypotenuse of 25 cm and one leg of 7 cm. Find the other leg.

## Reasoning (AO2)

23. Mia says Pythagoras' theorem can be used on any triangle. Explain why she is wrong.

24. **True or false?** In Pythagoras' theorem, $c$ must always represent the longest side (the hypotenuse).

25. Ravi calculates the hypotenuse of a triangle with legs 6 cm and 8 cm as $6 + 8 = 14$ cm. Explain his mistake and find the correct hypotenuse.

26. **Always, sometimes, or never?** In a right-angled triangle, the hypotenuse is the side opposite the right angle.

27. Priya uses $\tan\theta$ to find a missing hypotenuse. Explain why this is the wrong ratio to use, and describe which ratio she should use instead if she has the opposite and hypotenuse sides.

28. Explain, using the letters SOH, CAH, TOA, how to decide which trigonometric ratio to use for a given problem.

29. Sloan says that as an angle in a right-angled triangle gets bigger (closer to 90°), the tangent of the angle also gets bigger without limit. Is this a reasonable idea? Explain briefly.

30. Leo finds a missing leg using $a^2 = c^2 - b^2$ but forgets to take the square root at the end. Explain his mistake, using the example $c = 10$, $b = 6$.

## Problem (AO3)

31. A rectangular field is 24 m long and 7 m wide. Find the length of the diagonal path across the field, using Pythagoras.

32. A tree casts a shadow, and the angle of elevation from the end of the shadow to the top of the tree is 35°. The shadow is 20 m long. Find the height of the tree using $\tan\theta = \dfrac{\text{opposite}}{\text{adjacent}}$ (to 1 dp).

33. A ship sails 30 km east, then 40 km north. Find the direct distance back to the starting point, using Pythagoras.

34. A ramp rises 1.5 m over a horizontal distance of 8 m. Find the angle of the ramp with the horizontal, using $\tan\theta = \dfrac{\text{opposite}}{\text{adjacent}}$ (to 1 dp).

35. A kite string is 50 m long and the kite is flying at a height of 30 m directly above a point on the ground. Find the horizontal distance from the person holding the string to that point, using Pythagoras.

36. A ladder 6 m long leans against a wall, making an angle of 65° with the ground. Find how high up the wall the ladder reaches, using $\sin\theta = \dfrac{\text{opposite}}{\text{hypotenuse}}$ (to 1 dp).

## Stretch

37. A right-angled triangle has a hypotenuse of 17 cm and one leg of 8 cm. Find the other leg, then find the smallest angle in the triangle (to 1 dp).

38. Two ships leave a port at the same time. Ship A sails due north at 20 km/h. Ship B sails due east at 15 km/h. Find the distance between the ships after 2 hours.

39. A flagpole is 12 m tall. A wire runs from the top of the flagpole to a point on the ground 9 m from the base. Find the length of the wire, and the angle the wire makes with the ground (to 1 dp).

40. A right-angled triangle has legs $x$ and $x + 7$, and a hypotenuse of 17 cm. Form and solve an equation using Pythagoras' theorem to find the value of $x$.
