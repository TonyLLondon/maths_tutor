## Getting started

1. How many sides does every triangle have?
2. How many sides does every quadrilateral have?
3. Name the triangle with three equal sides.
4. Name the quadrilateral with four equal sides and four right angles.
5. How many right angles does a rectangle have?
6. True or false? A square is also a rectangle.
7. Which has more sides: a triangle or a rectangle?
8. A triangle has two equal sides. What type of triangle is it?

## Fluency (AO1)

9. Name the quadrilateral: opposite sides parallel and equal, diagonals bisect each other, no right angles needed.
10. A rhombus has four equal sides. Do its diagonals cross at right angles?
11. **True or false?** A rectangle's diagonals are always equal in length.
12. How many lines of symmetry does a square have?
13. How many lines of symmetry does a (non-square) rectangle have?
14. How many lines of symmetry does an isosceles triangle have?
15. How many lines of symmetry does an equilateral triangle have?
16. How many pairs of parallel sides does a trapezium have?
17. How many pairs of equal *adjacent* sides does a kite have?
18. Do the diagonals of a parallelogram bisect each other (cut each other exactly in half)?
19. Do the diagonals of a kite cross at right angles?
20. Name a triangle with a right angle and two equal sides.


## More fluency

## Reasoning (AO2)

23. Explain why every square is a rhombus, but not every rhombus is a square.

24. Use the diagram. A parallelogram has one angle marked. Find its other three angles.

25. **Always, sometimes, or never?** The diagonals of a rectangle are perpendicular (cross at right angles).

26. Maya says, "A kite always has a pair of parallel sides." Is Maya correct? Explain your answer.

27. Use the diagram. An isosceles triangle has one base angle marked. Find its other two angles.

28. Sam says, "An isosceles trapezium has two pairs of equal sides." Is Sam correct? Explain, describing which sides are equal.

29. **True or false?** A rhombus is a special type of parallelogram.

30. Leo draws a quadrilateral whose diagonals bisect each other but are *not* equal in length and do *not* cross at right angles. What is the best name for Leo's shape?

## Problem (AO3)

31. Use the diagram. A parallelogram-shaped field has one angle marked. Find the sizes of the other three angles.

32. Use the diagram. An isosceles triangle has its apex (top) angle marked. Find each of its two base angles.

33. A rhombus has diagonals crossing at right angles at their midpoints. One half-diagonal is 6 cm and the other is 8 cm. Is this rhombus also a square? Explain using what you know about a square's diagonals.

34. Archer says, "A shape with two pairs of parallel sides is called a trapezium." Correct Archer and explain the actual definition of a trapezium.

35. A trapezium has parallel sides of 10 cm and 6 cm. It is a **right trapezium**, meaning one of its non-parallel sides is perpendicular to the parallel sides. What size must at least one of its angles be?

36. A quadrilateral has all four sides equal in length but is not a square. Name the shape, and describe what is true about its opposite angles.
