## Reasoning (AO2)

100. Sloan says $(-3)^2 = -9$ because the base is negative. Explain the mistake and give the correct value.
101. **Always, sometimes, or never?** The cube root of a negative number is negative.
102. Archer says $\sqrt{81} + \sqrt{81} = \sqrt{162}$. Is he correct? Explain.
103. **Always, sometimes, or never?** If $a^2 > b^2$ then $a > b$ for whole numbers.
104. Compare $5^2$ and $2^5$. Which is bigger? Show your working.
105. **Always, sometimes, or never?** $1^n = 1$ for any whole number $n$.
106. Sloan thinks $0^0 = 0$. Explain why this is not used in school maths, and state the value of $0^1$.
107. **Always, sometimes, or never?** $(-2)^3$ and $(-2)^4$ have the same sign.
108. Explain why $\sqrt{25} = 5$ but we do not write $\sqrt{25} = -5$ when using the $\sqrt{}$ symbol.
109. **Always, sometimes, or never?** The volume of a cube is always a cube number.
110. Archer says $3^2 \times 3^3 = 3^6$. Use correct power rules to show the right single power.
111. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Squaring an odd number gives an odd result.
112. Sloan compares $\sqrt{16}$ and $\sqrt{25}$ by working out $\sqrt{16 + 25}$. Explain why this method is wrong.
113. **Always, sometimes, or never?** $(-1)^n$ is negative when $n$ is odd.
114. Explain in one sentence what $2^4$ means without using a calculator.
115. **Always, sometimes, or never?** $\sqrt[3]{n}$ is always less than $n$ for whole numbers $n > 1$.
116. Archer claims $10^3 = 10 \times 3$. What is the correct value and what does the power 3 mean?
117. **Always, sometimes, or never?** A number and its square root can be equal.
118. Sloan says $4^2 = 2^4$. Is she correct? Work out both to check.
119. **Always, sometimes, or never?** $(a^2)^2 = a^4$.
120. Explain why $\sqrt{49} \times \sqrt{4} = \sqrt{196}$ without using decimals.
121. **Always, sometimes, or never?** $2^3$ equals $3^2$.
122. Archer writes $(-5)^2 = -25$. Identify the error and write the correct answer.
123. **Always, sometimes, or never?** The square of a whole number ending in 5 ends in 25.
124. Sloan says $\sqrt[3]{8} \times \sqrt[3]{8} = \sqrt[3]{16}$. Is this true? Test with numbers.
125. **Always, sometimes, or never?** $0^n = 0$ for every positive whole number $n$.
126. Compare $(-3)^2$ and $-3^2$. Are they equal? Show working.
127. **Always, sometimes, or never?** If $n^2 = 36$ then $n = 6$.
128. Explain why a square number cannot have units digit 2, 3, 7 or 8.
129. **Always, sometimes, or never?** $a^3 \times a^2 = a^5$ when $a$ is a positive whole number.
