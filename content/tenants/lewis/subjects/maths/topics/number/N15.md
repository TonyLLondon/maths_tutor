## Reasoning (AO2)

100. Archer writes $23.5 \leq L < 24.5$ for a length of 24 cm to the nearest cm, then says the table could be exactly 24.5 cm long. What is wrong?
101. **Always, sometimes, or never?** A number rounded to the nearest 10 is closer to its recorded value than to the next multiple of 10.
102. Sloan claims the error interval for 100 correct to the nearest 100 is $100 \leq n < 200$. Explain the mistake and give the correct interval.
103. Compare the error intervals for 8.0 cm to 1 d.p. and 8 cm to the nearest cm. Which allows a longer actual length, and why?
104. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Doubling a measurement's recorded value doubles the width of its error interval.
105. Is 2.445 included in the error interval for 2.45 to 2 d.p.? Explain.
106. **Always, sometimes, or never?** If $a$ and $b$ are both measured to the nearest cm, the greatest possible value of $a + b$ is the sum of the upper bounds.
107. Kim rounds 4.45 to 1 decimal place and gets 4.4; Lee gets 4.5. Who followed the usual GCSE "round 5 up" rule?
108. **Always, sometimes, or never?** The error interval for rounding to the nearest 1000 is always 1000 wide.
109. Explain in one sentence why the upper bound uses $<$ not $\leq$.
110. **Always, sometimes, or never?** A value on the lower bound still rounds to the recorded number.
111. A number rounds to 70 to the nearest 10 and to 68 to the nearest 2. Could the actual number be 69? Explain using bounds.
112. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Rounding twice to the nearest 10 gives the same result as rounding once.
113. Archer says 0.849 rounded to 2 d.p. is 0.84 because "4 is less than 5". Is he correct?
114. **Always, sometimes, or never?** For nearest-unit rounding, every integer lies in exactly one error interval.
115. Which is wider: the interval for 100 to nearest 10 or for 100 to nearest 100? Justify.
116. **Always, sometimes, or never?** If two numbers round to the same value to the nearest 10, their error intervals are the same.
117. Sloan gives the interval $9.5 \leq t < 10.5$ for 10 minutes to the nearest minute but writes the width as 2 minutes. Correct her.
118. **Always, sometimes, or never?** A measurement correct to 1 d.p. is always more precise than one correct to the nearest 10.
119. Explain why 4499 rounds to 5000 to the nearest 1000 even though it is less than 4500.
120. **Always, sometimes, or never?** The smallest possible value of a rounded number is always halfway below the recorded value.
121. A rectangle's width is 5 cm to nearest cm and length 8 cm to nearest cm. Can the perimeter exceed 27 cm? Explain with bounds.
122. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Zero has an error interval of width 1 when rounded to the nearest whole number.
123. Is the recorded value always the centre of the error interval? Answer yes or no and explain briefly.
124. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Adding 0.5 to the lower bound gives the recorded value for nearest-integer rounding.
125. Archer compares intervals for 1.5 to nearest 0.5 with 2 to nearest 1. Which recorded value represents a wider range of true values?
126. **Always, sometimes, or never?** If $x$ is in an error interval, rounding $x$ to the stated accuracy gives the recorded value.
127. Explain why writing 2.650 to 2 d.p. before rounding can change whether you get 2.65 or 2.66.
128. **Always, sometimes, or never?** The error interval for 0 rounded to the nearest 10 is $-5 \leq n < 5$.
129. Sloan says "correct to 2 d.p." means the number has exactly two digits after the decimal point. Is she correct?
