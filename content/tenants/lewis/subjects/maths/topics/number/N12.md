## Reasoning (AO2)

100. Layla says "20% off £50 means you pay £10." What mistake has she made?

101. **Always, sometimes, or never?** 25% of a number is the same as finding **1/4** of it.

102. Is finding **2/5** of a positive number always less than finding **3/5** of the same number? Explain briefly.

103. Explain in one sentence why 10% of 80 equals 80 ÷ 10.

104. **Always, sometimes, or never?** 0% of any number is 0.

105. Kim says increasing 100 by 50% and then decreasing by 50% returns to 100. Is Kim correct? Use the numbers to decide.

106. Which is larger: 20% of 100 or 25% of 80? Show both calculations.

107. **Always, sometimes, or never?** 100% of a number equals the number itself.

108. A label says "save **1/5**". What percentage discount is that?

109. Sloan compares 15% of 200 with 20% of 150. Which is larger, or are they equal?

110. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Doubling a number is the same as increasing it by 100%.

111. Archer thinks "**3/4** off" means you pay **3/4** of the price. What fraction of the price should you actually pay?

112. **Always, sometimes, or never?** If a positive price is reduced by more than 50%, the sale price is less than half the original.

113. Explain why 5% of 200 equals 10% of 100.

114. **Always, sometimes, or never?** 50% off followed by another 50% off is the same as 100% off.

115. Is 0.25 the same as 25%? Answer yes or no and explain briefly.

116. **Always, sometimes, or never?** For a positive number, 10% of it is greater than 5% of it.

117. A student writes 30% = **3/10** and 40% = **2/5**. Are both conversions correct?

118. **Always, sometimes, or never?** 120% of a positive number is greater than the original number.

119. Explain one reason why finding **1/3** of a number can be done by dividing by 3.

120. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Reducing a positive number by 100% gives zero.

121. Which is correct for 15% of 60: $0.15 × 60$ or $60 ÷ 100 × 15$? What answer do they give?

122. **Always, sometimes, or never?** **1/2** of a number plus **1/2** of the same number equals the whole number.

123. A £30 item has 15% off and the reduction is £4.50. Is the label correct?

124. **Always, sometimes, or never?** 75% is the same as **3/4**.

125. Why might you find 10% first when working out 5% of an amount?

126. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Finding **2/3** of an amount is the same as multiplying the amount by **2/3**.

127. A sale says "pay **7/10** of the price". What percentage discount is that?

128. **Always, sometimes, or never?** 200% of 5 equals 10.

129. Priya says 1% of 200 is 20. Is she correct? Explain.
