## Reasoning (AO2)

100. Archer adds $\dfrac{1}{2} + \dfrac{1}{3}$ and gets $\dfrac{2}{5}$. Explain the mistake and find the correct answer.
101. **Always, sometimes, or never?** The circumference of a circle is $2\pi r$.
102. Sloan says $\dfrac{1}{2} \times \dfrac{1}{3} = \dfrac{2}{6}$. Is her working correct? Explain.
103. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Leaving an answer as $5\pi$ cm is exact.
104. Explain why $\dfrac{3}{4} \div \dfrac{1}{2}$ equals $\dfrac{3}{2}$.
105. **Always, sometimes, or never?** A diameter is always twice the radius.
106. Archer works out the area of a radius-5 cm circle as $5\pi$ cm². Explain the error and give the correct exact area.
107. **Always, sometimes, or never?** $\dfrac{a}{b} + \dfrac{c}{d} = \dfrac{a+c}{b+d}$.
108. Sloan says $\dfrac{2}{3} \times \dfrac{3}{4} = \dfrac{6}{12}$, so the simplest answer is $\dfrac{6}{12}$. What should she do next?
109. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Multiplying two proper fractions gives a result smaller than both fractions.
110. Explain in one sentence why $\pi d$ and $2\pi r$ give the same circumference.
111. **Always, sometimes, or never?** $\dfrac{1}{2} \div \dfrac{1}{4} = \dfrac{1}{8}$.
112. Archer says a semicircle's curved length is $\pi r$. Is he correct when the radius is $r$? Explain.
113. **Always, sometimes, or never?** $\dfrac{3}{5} - \dfrac{1}{5} = \dfrac{2}{0}$.
114. Explain why finding a common denominator helps when adding $\dfrac{1}{4} + \dfrac{1}{6}$.
115. **Always, sometimes, or never?** The area of a circle is $\pi r^2$, not $2\pi r$.
116. Sloan converts $2\dfrac{1}{2}$ to $\dfrac{5}{2}$ before multiplying. Is this a good strategy? Explain.
117. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Dividing by a fraction is the same as multiplying by its reciprocal.
118. Archer gives the exact area of a radius-10 cm circle as 314 cm². Explain why $100\pi$ cm² is better.
119. **Always, sometimes, or never?** $\dfrac{2}{3} + \dfrac{2}{3} = \dfrac{4}{6}$.
120. Explain why $\dfrac{4}{5} \div \dfrac{2}{5} = 2$.
121. **Always, sometimes, or never?** A circle with radius 0 has area $0\pi$.
122. Sloan says $\dfrac{1}{8} + \dfrac{1}{8} = \dfrac{2}{16}$. Is the sum correct in simplest form?
123. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Mixed numbers must be turned into improper fractions before multiplying.
124. Explain the difference between exact answers with $\pi$ and decimal approximations on a calculator.
125. **Always, sometimes, or never?** $\dfrac{3}{4} \times \dfrac{8}{3} = 2$.
126. Archer finds $\dfrac{1}{2}$ of 30 and gets 15. Explain how this links to $\dfrac{1}{2} \times 30$.
127. **Always, sometimes, or never?** If circumference is $10\pi$, the radius is 5 cm.
128. Explain why $\dfrac{7}{12} + \dfrac{1}{4}$ needs a common denominator of 12.
129. **Always, sometimes, or never?** $\dfrac{5}{6} \div \dfrac{5}{6} = 1$.
