## Reasoning (AO2)

100. **Always, sometimes, or never?** The product of two prime numbers is odd.
101. Priya says all multiples of 9 are also multiples of 3. Is she correct? Explain.
102. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Two numbers with HCF 1 are both prime.
103. Explain why 1 is not counted as a prime number.
104. **Always, sometimes, or never?** If a number ends in 0, it has at least three factors.
105. Archer claims the LCM of two numbers is always greater than both numbers. Is he correct? Give an example.
106. **Always, sometimes, or never?** The HCF of two different prime numbers is 1.
107. Kim says 49 is prime because 7 is prime. Explain why Kim is wrong.
108. **Always, sometimes, or never?** A square number has an odd number of factors.
109. Sloan thinks the only way to check if 87 is prime is to divide by every number from 2 to 86. What quicker method could she use?
110. **Always, sometimes, or never?** The LCM of 2 and any even number is that even number.
111. Explain why 2 is the only even prime number.
112. **Always, sometimes, or never?** If $n$ is a factor of 12, then 12 is a multiple of $n$.
113. Jess says HCF(8, 12) = 4 and HCF(8, 16) = 8, so the HCF always equals one of the numbers. Is Jess correct? Explain.
114. **Always, sometimes, or never?** A number greater than 1 that is a perfect square cannot be prime.
115. Which is greater: the HCF or the LCM of 6 and 8? Explain.
116. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Multiplying two numbers always gives a common multiple of both.
117. Mi says 15 and 22 share no common factors except 1, so they are both prime. Explain the mistake.
118. **Always, sometimes, or never?** The sum of two even numbers is a multiple of 4.
119. Explain how you know 143 is not prime without listing all its factors.
120. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Every composite number has an even number of factors.
121. Archer says if HCF($a$, $b$) = $b$, then $b$ must divide $a$. Is he correct? Explain.
122. **Always, sometimes, or never?** The LCM of two odd numbers is odd.
123. Two consecutive whole numbers always have HCF 1. Is this true? Explain with an example.
124. **Always, sometimes, or never?** A prime number times a prime number has exactly four factors.
125. Sloan says 0 is a multiple of every number. Is she correct? Explain.
126. **Always, sometimes, or never?** If $a$ is a factor of $b$ and $b$ is a factor of $c$, then $a$ is a factor of $c$.
127. Explain why 2 appears in the prime factorisation of every even number greater than 2.
128. **Always, sometimes, or never?** HCF × LCM = product of the two numbers.
129. Priya says 100 has more factors than 99 because 100 is bigger. Is Priya correct? Explain.
