## Reasoning (AO2)

100. Priya writes 0.0008 as $8 \times 10^4$. Explain her mistake and give the correct standard form.
101. **Always, sometimes, or never?** In standard form, the first number can equal 10.
102. Archer says $3.2 \times 10^3$ and $32 \times 10^2$ are both written correctly in standard form. Is he right? Explain.
103. Explain in one sentence why $10^{-2}$ means “divide by 100”.
104. Sloan says $6 \times 10^{-5}$ is negative because of the minus sign. Is she correct? Explain.
105. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Multiplying two numbers in standard form with positive powers always gives a product larger than both starting numbers.
106. Which is further from zero: $2 \times 10^{-4}$ or $2 \times 10^{-3}$? Explain briefly.
107. Kim writes 5600 as $56 \times 10^2$. Why is this not standard form? Give the correct version.
108. **Always, sometimes, or never?** If $a \times 10^n$ is in standard form and $n < 0$, then the ordinary number is less than 1.
109. Explain why comparing $4 \times 10^6$ and $39 \times 10^5$ using only “4 is less than 39” can mislead you.
110. **Always, sometimes, or never?** $1 \times 10^n$ is the same as $10^n$.
111. Without a calculator, is $2.5 \times 10^4$ bigger than 24,000? Show how you know.
112. Explain in one sentence why standard form puts exactly one non-zero digit before the decimal point.
113. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Dividing by $10^k$ is the same as multiplying by $10^{-k}$.
114. Jamie writes $0.003 = 3 \times 10^3$. What went wrong?
115. Two road races are $5 \times 10^7$ m and $4.9 \times 10^7$ m long. Which is longer, and how do the first numbers tell you?
116. **Always, sometimes, or never?** A negative power in standard form makes the whole value negative.
117. Explain why $7.8 \times 10^0 = 7.8$.
118. **Always, sometimes, or never?** $(2 \times 10^3) \times (3 \times 10^2)$ equals $6 \times 10^5$ before you adjust the first number if needed.
119. A student orders $10^{-3}$, $10^{-2}$, and $10^{-5}$ from largest to smallest as $10^{-5}$, $10^{-3}$, $10^{-2}$. Describe the error.
120. Explain why $450 = 4.5 \times 10^2$, not $45 \times 10^1$.
121. **Always, sometimes, or never?** The ordinary form of $5 \times 10^{-4}$ has four zeros after the decimal point before the first non-zero digit.
122. Which ordinary number has more digits before the decimal point: $3.2 \times 10^6$ or $3.2 \times 10^4$? Explain.
123. **Always, sometimes, or never?** If $n > m$, then $10^n > 10^m$.
124. Explain in one sentence what a positive index tells you when you write an ordinary large number.
125. **Always, sometimes, or never?** $6.0 \times 10^3$ and $6 \times 10^3$ represent the same value.
126. Dad says $10^{-6}$ means “six times smaller than 1”. Archer says it means divide 1 by $10^6$. Who uses the index correctly?
127. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Writing 0.5 as $5 \times 10^{-1}$ is correct standard form.
128. Why is knowing that $100 = 10^2$ useful when you write 5000 as $5 \times 10^3$?
129. **Always, sometimes, or never?** $(1 \times 10^2) \times (1 \times 10^3) = 1 \times 10^5$.
