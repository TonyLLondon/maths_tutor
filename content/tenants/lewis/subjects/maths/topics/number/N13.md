## Reasoning (AO2)

100. Archer says 1 mile is about 1.6 km, so 5 miles is exactly 8 km. Is he correct? Explain.
101. **Always, sometimes, or never?** When you measure the same length in a larger unit, the number gets smaller.
102. Sloan converts 2 hours 30 minutes to 230 minutes. Explain her mistake and give the correct number of minutes.
103. Which is faster: 10 m/s or 36 km/h? Convert to check.
104. **Always, sometimes, or never?** For pure water, 1 cm³ has a mass of about 1 g.
105. Explain in one sentence why speed is written with two units, such as km/h.
106. Layla says 500 ml is half a litre. Is she correct? Explain briefly.
107. **Always, sometimes, or never?** To change kg to g you multiply by 1000.
108. Compare 72 km/h and 20 m/s by converting one speed to match the other.
109. A block has mass 50 g and volume 80 cm³. Will it float on water (density of water ≈ 1 g/cm³)? Explain using density.
110. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Minutes can only measure time, not distance.
111. When might tonnes be a sensible unit for mass? Give one example.
112. Kim says 0.5 km equals 500 cm. Explain what is wrong with her conversion.
113. **Always, sometimes, or never?** mph and km/h both measure speed.
114. Is 3 m/s faster than 10 km/h? Show how you know.
115. Archer says density has no units because it is mass divided by volume. Is he correct? Explain.
116. **Always, sometimes, or never?** 1000 mg = 1 g.
117. Why might a scientist prefer m/s instead of mph?
118. Runner A covers 500 m in 40 s; Runner B covers 800 m in 70 s. Who has the greater average speed?
119. **Always, sometimes, or never?** 1 hour = 3600 seconds.
120. In everyday talk, people say "weight" when they mean mass on scales. What unit do kitchen scales usually show?
121. **Always, sometimes, or never?** In maths questions, capacity in litres and volume in litres mean the same amount of liquid.
122. To change metres to kilometres, do you always divide by 1000? Answer yes or no and explain.
123. One juice carton holds 250 ml. How many litres of juice are in 4 identical cartons?
124. **Always, sometimes, or never?** A heavier object always has a higher density than a lighter one.
125. Sloan runs 6 km in 0.5 hours. Archer says her speed is 3 km/h. Is Archer correct? Explain.
126. Which unit is better for the thickness of a sheet of paper: mm or m?
127. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Travelling at 60 km/h for 1 hour means you go 60 km.
128. A speed is given in km/h but the time is in minutes. What must you do before using distance ÷ time?
129. **Always, sometimes, or never?** 2.5 litres of water has a volume of 2500 cm³.
