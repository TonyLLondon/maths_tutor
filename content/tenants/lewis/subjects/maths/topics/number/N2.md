## Reasoning (AO2)

100. Explain in one sentence why 340 × 100 is not the same as 340 + 100.
101. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Dividing by 10 is the same as multiplying by 0.1 (for positive numbers).
102. Priya writes 5 × 10³ as 500. Explain the mistake and give the correct value.
103. **Always, sometimes, or never?** $A × B = B × A$ when $A$ and $B$ are whole numbers.
104. Sloan says 450 ÷ 10 = 45.0, so the answer must still be 450 because of the zero. What went wrong?
105. Without a calculator, show that 48 × 25 equals 1200 using a splitting or doubling method.
106. **Always, sometimes, or never?** The result of 12 ÷ 0 is zero.
107. Explain why 0.5 × 10 is 5 but 0.5 × 100 is 50, using place value.
108. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Multiplying a positive number by a number less than 1 makes the answer smaller.
109. Archer calculates 1000 − 999.9 and gets 0.01 instead of 0.1. Describe the place-value error.
110. **Always, sometimes, or never?** If you add the same amount to both sides of a balance, it stays balanced.
111. Why is (−3) × 4 negative but (−3) × (−4) positive? Answer in one sentence.
112. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Zero divided by any non-zero number is zero.
113. A pupil writes 6.02 × 10 = 0.602. Describe the mistake and state the correct answer.
114. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Doubling a number and then halving it gives the number you started with.
115. Explain in one sentence why dividing a whole number by 100 makes it one hundred times smaller.
116. **Always, sometimes, or never?** $(−a) + (−b)$ is the same as $−(a + b)$.
117. Kim finds 15 × 99 by calculating $15 × 100 − 15$. Is this correct? Show the result.
118. **Always, sometimes, or never?** The digit 0 in a number always has value 0.
119. Explain why 2.40 × 10 is 24, not 2.400.
120. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Subtracting 10 three times is the same as subtracting 30.
121. Place-value columns go units, tens, hundreds, thousands. Name the next two columns further to the left.
122. **Always, sometimes, or never?** $a ÷ 1 = a$ for any number $a$.
123. Without calculating fully, decide whether 99 × 41 is nearer to 4000 or 400. Explain briefly.
124. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Adding two odd numbers gives an even number.
125. Explain the difference between $−5^2$ and $(−5)^2$.
126. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Multiplying by 10 and then dividing by 10 returns the original number.
127. Why does 7030 − 6997 equal 33, not 1033? Mention borrowing or place value.
128. **Always, sometimes, or never?** If $x × 5 = 0$ then $x$ must be 0.
129. A number multiplied by 100 gives 47.6. What was the original number?
