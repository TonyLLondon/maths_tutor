## Getting started

1. Share 8 strawberries equally between 2 children. How many does each child get?
2. What fraction of 6 counters is 3 counters?
3. A bag has 2 red and 2 blue balls. What fraction are red?
4. There are 12 cakes. One quarter are chocolate. How many are chocolate?
5. Share 15 stickers in the ratio 1:2. How many stickers are in the smaller share?
6. A box has 3 cats and 1 dog toy. What fraction of the toys are cat toys?
7. There are 10 apples. 2 are green. What fraction are green?
8. Mia gives half of 14 pencils away. How many pencils does she keep?
9. Share 10 sweets equally between 2 children. How many does each child get?
10. What fraction of 8 is 4?
11. A tin has 3 red and 3 blue counters. What fraction of the counters are red?
12. Half of 20 is ___.

## More fluency

## Reasoning (AO2)

27. Archer says a ratio of 1:3 means the first part is $\dfrac{1}{3}$ of the total. Is he correct? Explain the mistake and give the correct fraction.

28. **Always, sometimes, or never?** In a ratio $a:b$, the first part is $\dfrac{a}{a+b}$ of the total.

29. Sloan says a ratio of 2:2 means each part is a different fraction of the whole. Is she correct? Explain.

30. **Always, sometimes, or never?** If a ratio is 1:1, both fractions of the whole are equal.

31. Compare the ratio 3:5 written as fractions of the whole with the ratio 5:3. Explain why the fractions swap around.

## Problem (AO3)

32. A charity raises £500 split between two projects in the ratio 3:2. What fraction of the money goes to the larger project, and how much money is that?

33. In a school of 400 pupils, the ratio of those who walk to school to those who don't is 3:5. How many pupils walk to school?

36. A fruit drink is made from orange juice and water in the ratio 2:7. What fraction of a 900 ml bottle is orange juice, and how many ml is that?

## Reasoning (AO2)

100. Sloan says in ratio 3:2 the larger share is **3/2** of the total. Explain why this is wrong and give the correct fraction for the first part.
101. **Always, sometimes, or never?** If you swap the two parts of a ratio, each part's fraction of the whole stays the same.
102. Archer thinks ratio 4:4 means the first part is **1/4** of the total. Is he correct? Explain.
103. **Always, sometimes, or never?** In a ratio with three parts, the largest share is always more than **1/3** of the whole.
104. Compare ratios 2:5 and 5:2. What fraction of the whole is the first part in each case?
105. Priya says **1/3** always means a ratio of 1:3. Give a counterexample showing **1/3** can come from a different ratio.
106. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Adding 1 to both parts of a ratio keeps the fractions of the whole the same.
107. Explain in one sentence why the first part of ratio 5:1 is **5/6** of the total, not **5/1**.
108. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Two ratios that simplify to the same form give the same fractions of the whole.
109. Kim writes ratio 1:2 and says the second part is **1/2** of the total. What fraction should Kim use instead?
110. **Always, sometimes, or never?** If the first part of a ratio is half the second part, the first fraction of the whole is **1/2**.
111. Sloan compares 3:7 and 4:7 and says the second fractions must be equal because the second part is 7 each time. Explain the mistake.
112. **Always, sometimes, or never?** In ratio $a:b$, if $a = 2b$ then the first part is **2/3** of the whole.
113. Archer says a ratio of 1:9 means the first part is a tiny fraction of the total. Work out the exact fraction to check.
114. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Writing a ratio backwards changes which fraction is larger.
115. Explain why ratio 6:8 and 3:4 give the same fractions of the whole.
116. **Always, sometimes, or never?** If one part of a ratio is zero, both fractions of the whole are zero.
117. A ratio is written 2:3:5. Do the three fractions of the whole add to 1? Explain in one sentence.
118. **Always, sometimes, or never?** The fraction for the first part in $a:b$ is less than **1/2** whenever $a < b$.
119. Mi says ratio 10:2 simplifies to 5:1 so the first part is **5/6** of the total in both cases. Is Mi correct?
120. **Always, sometimes, or never?** If two ratios have the same total number of parts, the fractions of the whole are the same only when the ratios are identical.
121. Compare the fraction of the whole for the second part in ratios 1:4 and 4:1.
122. **Always, sometimes, or never?** In a two-part ratio, the smaller part is always less than **1/2** of the whole.
123. Explain in one sentence what mistake is made if you treat 2:3 as “2 is **2/3** of the total”.
124. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Multiplying only the first part of a ratio doubles its fraction of the whole.
125. Archer claims ratio 5:10 gives the same fractions as 1:2. Is he correct? Show the fractions for each.
126. **Always, sometimes, or never?** A ratio where all parts are equal gives each part the same fraction of the whole.
127. For ratio 4:5, is the first part closer to **1/2** or **1/3** of the total? Give the exact fraction.
128. **Always, sometimes, or never?** The sum of the two fractions of the whole for a two-part ratio is always 1.
129. Sloan says if $\dfrac{a}{a+b} = \dfrac{1}{2}$ then $a = b$. Is Sloan correct? Explain.
