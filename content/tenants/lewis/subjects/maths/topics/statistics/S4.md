## Getting started

1. Which number appears most often in: 2, 2, 3?
2. Put these numbers in order from smallest to largest: 5, 1, 3.
3. What is the middle number in: 2, 4, 6?
4. What is the highest number in: 3, 8, 5?
5. What is the lowest number in: 7, 2, 9?
6. Find the range of: 2, 5.
7. Find the total of: 2, 3, 5.
8. Share 12 equally between 3 children. How many does each child get?

## Reasoning (AO2)

21. Two classes have the same **mean** score on a test. Class A's scores are all close to the mean; Class B's scores are spread out. Which class would you expect to have a **higher** highest score? Explain in one or two sentences.

22. **Always, sometimes, or never?** The mean of a list of whole numbers is always a whole number. Give an example if it is *sometimes* true.

23. Mia says the mode is always the number in the middle of an ordered list. Is Mia correct? Explain the difference between the mode and the median.

24. **True or false?** A list of numbers can have more than one mode. Give an example.

25. Archer says that the range tells you the average of a list of numbers. Is Archer correct? Explain what the range actually measures.

26. A list of 5 numbers has no repeated values. Sloan says this list cannot have a mode. Is Sloan correct? Explain.

27. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Adding one very large number to a list increases the mean by more than it increases the median. Explain your answer using an example.

## Problem (AO3)

29. Sloan's netball scores over 6 matches are: 12, 15, 9, 12, 18, 12. Find the mean, median, mode, and range of her scores.

30. Four friends' heights (cm) are: 132, 140, 135, 129. What is their mean height? If a fifth friend who is 144 cm tall joins, what is the new mean height?

31. Archer's weekly pocket money over 5 weeks is £4, £4, £6, £4, £7. Find the mode and the mean.

32. A school recorded the midday temperature (°C) for a week: 18, 21, 19, 21, 17, 20, 21. Find the mode, median, and range of the temperatures.

33. Archer and Sloan compare their spelling test scores over 4 weeks. Archer: 6, 7, 8, 7. Sloan: 5, 9, 6, 8. Both have a mean of 7. Who is more consistent? Use the range to explain your answer.

34. Six pupils' shoe sizes are: 3, 4, 4, 5, 4, 6. What is the mode, and what does it tell you about the most common shoe size in this group?

## More reasoning (AO2)

65. **Always, sometimes, or never?** The mode of a data set is always greater than the median. Explain with an example if it is *sometimes* or *never* true.
66. Priya says you need at least three numbers to find a range. Is Priya correct? Explain.
67. **True or false?** If every value in a list increases by 5, the mean increases by 5.
68. **Always, sometimes, or never?** The mean of a list lies strictly between the smallest and largest values (not equal to either). Explain.
69. Lex says a list with an even count of values has no median. Is Lex correct? Explain.
70. **True or false?** Removing the largest value from a list always lowers the mean.
71. **Always, sometimes, or never?** The range is zero only when every number in the list is the same.
72. Dana says adding one very large outlier changes the median as much as the mean. Is Dana correct? Explain.
73. **True or false?** A list where every value appears exactly once can have no mode.
74. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Adding a new value equal to the current mean leaves the mean unchanged.
75. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Adding a value strictly between the current minimum and maximum changes the range.
76. **True or false?** For 2, 4, 6, and 8, the mean equals the median.
77. Give one reason the **median** might describe typical house prices on a street better than the **mean**.
78. **Always, sometimes, or never?** The mode must be one of the values that appear in the list.
79. Finn says the mean of whole numbers is always a whole number. Is Finn correct? Explain.
80. **True or false?** If you double every number in a list, the range doubles.
81. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Two different lists can have the same mean but different ranges.
82. Is it possible for the mode to be less than the smallest value in the list? Explain.
83. **True or false?** For an odd count of sorted values, the median is always one of the values in the list.
84. **Always, sometimes, or never?** If the mean of a list increases, the median must increase too.
85. Rory says the range tells you how far a typical value is from the mean. Is Rory correct? Explain.
86. **True or false?** For 1, 100, and 100, the median is closer to 100 than the mean is.
87. **Always, sometimes, or never?** A bimodal list contains exactly two different numbers in total.
88. Two cyclists both have a mean speed of 15 km/h over ten days, but different ranges. What does comparing the **ranges** tell you?
89. **True or false?** The median of 5, 5, 5, 5, and 100 is 5.
90. **Always, sometimes, or never?** The mean is always at least as large as the mode.
