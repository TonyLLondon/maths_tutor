## Getting started

1. Five children choose their favourite colour: red, blue, red, green, red. How many choose red?
2. Look at the tally chart. How many children chose cats?
3. Use the table. Which fruit has the greater frequency?
4. Use the bar chart. How many children chose dogs?
5. Use the pictogram. How many children are shown?
6. Use the survey table. How many children were surveyed altogether?
7. Use the frequency table. Which colour is least popular?
8. Use the bar chart. How many more votes did football receive than tennis?

## Reasoning (AO2)

22. A pictogram uses a symbol worth 4 pupils, but one row has half a symbol. Sloan says this must mean 2 pupils. Is Sloan correct? Explain.
