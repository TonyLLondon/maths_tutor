## Getting started

1. Five children choose their favourite colour: red, blue, red, green, red. How many choose red?
2. A tally chart shows 3 marks for cats. How many children chose cats?
3. A table shows 4 apples and 2 bananas. Which fruit has the greater frequency?
4. A bar chart has a bar reaching 6 for dogs. How many children chose dogs?
5. A pictogram uses one star for one child. How many children are shown by 4 stars?
6. A survey records: walk 5, car 3. How many children were surveyed altogether?
7. A frequency table shows red 2, blue 4, green 1. Which colour is least popular?
8. A bar chart shows 7 votes for football and 5 for tennis. How many more votes did football receive?

## Reasoning (AO2)

22. A pictogram uses a symbol worth 4 pupils, but one row has half a symbol. Sloan says this must mean 2 pupils. Is Sloan correct? Explain.

23. **Always, sometimes, or never?** A pie chart and a bar chart showing the same data will have the same shape. Explain your answer.

24. Archer says a tally chart and a frequency table show exactly the same information, just in a different way. Is Archer correct? Explain.
