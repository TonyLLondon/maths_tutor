26. Two graphs, $y = x^2$ and $y = -x^2$, are both quadratics. Describe how their shapes compare.

27. **True or false?** As $x$ gets very large in $y = x^3$, $y$ grows much faster than it does for $y = x^2$.

## Problem (AO3)

31. A scientist plots data and notices the points curve upward slowly at first, then very steeply, matching a cubic pattern. Which equation type would best model this: linear, quadratic, or cubic?

32. A shop finds that as the price of an item, $x$, increases, the number sold, $y$, decreases according to $y = \dfrac{200}{x}$. What is $y$ when $x = 10$? What happens to $y$ as $x$ gets very large?

33. Explain why a reciprocal graph like $y = \dfrac{10}{x}$ can never touch the $x$-axis or the $y$-axis.

34. A ball's height over time follows a quadratic pattern (rises, then falls). Explain why a linear equation would not correctly model the ball's full flight.

35. A company's costs follow $y = x^3$ for very large orders, $x$. Explain, using the shape of the graph, why costs increase much faster for large $x$ than for small $x$.

36. Given the equations $y = 4x - 1$, $y = x^2 + 2$, and $y = x^3 - 5$, identify which one has a graph that is always increasing without ever turning back on itself in the same way a straight line does.

39. A student says all cubic graphs cross the $x$-axis exactly once. Test this idea by considering $y = x^3 - x$, finding where it equals 0 for $x = -1, 0, 1$.

40. Explain how you could tell, just from an equation, whether its graph belongs to the linear, quadratic, cubic, or reciprocal family, without plotting any points.
