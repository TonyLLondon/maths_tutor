## More reasoning

91. Jess solves $4x + 2 = 18$ and writes $x = 5$. Find Jess's error and the correct $x$.

92. **Always, sometimes, or never?** An equation of the form $ax + b = c$ has exactly one solution when $a \neq 0$.

93. Aisha says $2x + 4 = 2(x + 2)$ has no solution because $x$ cancels. Is she right? Explain.

94. Two pupils solve $5x - 3 = 2x + 6$. One subtracts $2x$ first; one adds 3 first. Will they get the same $x$? Show briefly.

95. **True or false?** If $3x = 12$ and $x + 4 = 8$, then any value of $x$ that satisfies one must satisfy the other.

96. Ben expands $3(x + 4) = 12$ as $3x + 4 = 12$. What should he have written instead?

97. Compare solving $x + 5 = 9$ and $5x + 5 = 9$. Why is only one of them a single-step equation in $x$?

98. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Subtracting the same expression from both sides of an equation changes the solution.

99. Cara writes $2x = x + 7$ then $x = 7$ without showing $x$ on both sides. Is her final answer correct? Show proper steps.

100. Dev says the equation $4x + 1 = 4x + 5$ is impossible. Explain why there is no solution.

101. **True or false?** The equation $2(x + 3) = 2x + 6$ is true for every value of $x$.

102. Erin checks $x = 3$ in $2x + 1 = 7$ by substituting into $2x + 1$ only. Explain a better check.

103. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Dividing both sides of an equation by $x$ is a safe step when solving.

104. Finn solves $\dfrac{x}{2} = 4$ by multiplying only the left side by 2. What should Finn do instead?

105. **True or false?** If you multiply every term in $x + 2 = 5$ by 3, the new equation has the same solution.

106. Grace thinks $x = 0$ cannot be a solution because $x$ means “a number”. Give an equation where $x = 0$ **is** the solution.

107. Two equations: $2x + 1 = 9$ and $x + 0.5 = 2.25$. Do they share a solution? Show working.

108. **Always, sometimes, or never?** An equation with $x$ on both sides must be solved by collecting $x$ terms.

109. Hugo writes $5 - x = 2$ as $x = 5 - 2$. Is his rearrangement valid? Find $x$.

110. **True or false?** Adding 0 to one side of an equation can change the solution.

## Problem practice

111. A phone plan costs £8 per month plus a £25 setup fee. The first bill totals £57. Write and solve an equation for the number of months, $m$, billed.

112. A rope of length $L$ cm is cut into 5 equal pieces, each 14 cm long. Form and solve an equation for $L$.

113. The sum of two consecutive even numbers is 46. Write and solve an equation to find the smaller number.

115. A number is tripled, then 11 is subtracted. The result is 34. Find the number.

116. A rectangle has width $w$ cm and length $(2w + 1)$ cm. The perimeter is 32 cm. Find $w$.

117. Archer cycles at 12 km/h for 1.5 hours. Write and solve an equation to find the distance $d$ km he covers ($d = 12 \times 1.5$).

118. Sloan's piggy bank has £3 more than twice Archer's. Together they have £24. If Archer's amount is £$a$, write and solve for $a$.

119. A train ticket costs £14 plus £0.50 per stop. A journey of $s$ stops costs £22. Find $s$.

120. Three siblings share £45 so the oldest gets £5 more than the middle, and the middle gets £5 more than the youngest. If the youngest gets £$y$, write and solve for $y$.

121. A hot-drink machine takes 40p coins only. After inserting $n$ coins the display shows £3.20. Write and solve an equation for $n$.

122. A garden border uses $b$ bushes in a row; each bush needs 60 cm spacing and 30 cm end gaps, totalling 450 cm. Write and solve $60b + 60 = 450$ (30 cm gap at each end).

123. The difference between a number and 8 is 15. Find the number (consider both interpretations and pick the one that fits “difference” as positive distance).

124. A school trip bus holds 48 seats. There are already 31 students on the bus. Write and solve an equation for how many more students $s$ can board if the bus must stay under capacity by leaving at least 5 seats empty.

125. A recipe for 4 people uses 600 ml milk. Write and solve an equation for the milk $m$ ml needed for 7 people at the same rate.

126. Two angles on a straight line are $(3x + 10)°$ and $(2x + 20)°$. Find $x$.

127. A shop reduces a £40 jacket by £$x$ so the sale price is £28. Write and solve for $x$.

128. A water tank holds 500 litres. After leaking $L$ litres per hour for 3 hours, 425 litres remain. Find $L$.

129. A charity walk is 12 km. Nina walks $k$ km, rests, then walks another 5 km to finish. Write and solve if she walked the same $k$ twice before the rest (total 12).

130. A parking ticket shows 30 minutes left after $t$ hours of paid time were used. Each hour paid adds 60 minutes. If £2 was inserted (2 hours paid), write $120 - 60t = 30$ and solve for $t$.

131. A number machine adds 4 then doubles. The output is 26. Find the input.

132. An L-shaped path has horizontal section $x$ m, vertical 4 m, horizontal 3 m, and vertical 2 m, with total length 18 m. Write and solve an equation for $x$.

133. A cinema sells 120 tickets for £840. Adult tickets cost £8 and child tickets £5. If $a$ adult tickets sold, write $8a + 5(120 - a) = 840$ and solve for $a$.

134. A tank is filled by pipe A at 6 litres/min and emptied by pipe B at 2 litres/min. Both run for $t$ minutes starting empty; 80 litres remain in a 200-litre tank. Write $6t - 2t = 200 - 80$ and solve.

135. The mean of four test scores is 72. Three scores are 68, 70, and 75. Find the fourth score $s$ using $\dfrac{68 + 70 + 75 + s}{4} = 72$.

136. A coach travels at 50 mph for $t$ hours then 40 mph for 2 hours, total distance 290 miles. Write $50t + 80 = 290$ and solve.

137. A piece of wire 48 cm is bent into a rectangle with length 2 cm more than width $w$. Perimeter equation $2w + 2(w + 2) = 48$ — solve for $w$.

138. A shopkeeper buys $n$ notebooks at 60p and sells them at 90p, making £4.50 profit total. Write $(0.9 - 0.6)n = 4.5$ and solve.

139. Archer thinks of a number, doubles it, subtracts 9, and gets the same as adding 3 to the original. Write $2x - 9 = x + 3$ and solve.

140. A temperature falls from $T°C$ by 12° to reach −3°. Write and solve $T - 12 = -3$.
