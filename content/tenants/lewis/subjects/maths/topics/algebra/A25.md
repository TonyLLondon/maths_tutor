## Reasoning (AO2)

23. Sam finds the nth term of 2, 5, 8, 11, … and writes $3n$. Explain Sam's mistake and find the correct rule.

24. **Always, sometimes, or never?** The nth term of a linear sequence gives a positive value.

25. Priya says the nth term $2n + 1$ will always give an odd number, whatever whole number $n$ is. Is she correct? Explain.

26. Two sequences have nth terms $3n + 2$ and $3n - 1$. Explain why they have the same common difference but different starting values.

27. **True or false?** If the nth term rule contains "$-n$", like $10 - 2n$, the sequence is decreasing.

28. Leo finds the nth term of 7, 10, 13, 16, … and writes $3n + 3$. Check Leo's rule against the first two terms of the sequence — is he correct?

29. Compare the nth term rules $5n$ and $5n + 2$. Which sequence's terms are bigger for the same value of $n$, and by how much?

30. Mo says 50 is a term in the sequence with nth term $3n + 2$. Is Mo correct? Show your working.

## Problem (AO3)

31. A pattern of matchstick squares uses $3n + 1$ matches for pattern number $n$. How many matches are needed for pattern 12?

32. A theatre charges a £5 booking fee plus £8 per ticket. Write the nth term rule for the total cost of $n$ tickets, then find the cost of 6 tickets.

33. Archer saves money according to the rule $4n + 10$ pounds after $n$ weeks. How much has he saved after 8 weeks?

34. A sequence of tile patterns has nth term $2n + 3$. Which pattern number has exactly 25 tiles?

35. A café stacks chairs so that the nth stack has $5n - 3$ chairs. How many chairs are in the 6th stack, and what is the term-to-term rule for this sequence?

36. Is 100 a term in the sequence with nth term $6n - 2$? Show your working to justify your answer.

## Stretch

40. Design your own linear sequence whose nth term rule includes a negative coefficient of $n$, such as $-2n$ or $10 - 3n$. Write the rule and give its first four terms.
