## Getting started

1. Which is greater: 5 or 3?
2. Which is smaller: 2 or 6?
3. Complete with $<$ or $>$: $2$ □ $4$.
4. Complete with $<$ or $>$: $7$ □ $3$.
5. Is $3 < 5$ true or false?
6. Is $6 < 4$ true or false?
7. Which whole numbers can fill the box: □ $< 3$?
8. Which whole numbers can fill the box: $2 <$ □ $< 5$?

## Reasoning (AO2)

23. Sam solves $-2x > 8$ and writes $x > -4$. What did Sam forget to do? Find the correct solution.

24. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Multiplying both sides of an inequality by a negative number reverses the inequality sign.

25. Priya says the solution to $x + 3 < 10$ is the same set of numbers as the solution to $x < 7$. Is she correct? Explain.

26. Two inequalities, $2x > 10$ and $x > 5$, look different. Explain why they have exactly the same solution.

27. **True or false?** The inequality $x \ge 4$ includes the number 4 itself. Give a reason for your answer.

28. Leo solves $-3x < 12$ and writes $x < -4$. Explain Leo's mistake, then solve the inequality correctly.

29. Compare the solutions of $x > 3$ and $x \ge 3$. What is the key difference between them?

30. Mo says every inequality has infinitely many solutions. Is Mo correct? Explain using an example.

## Problem (AO3)

31. Archer has £20 and wants to buy pens costing £3 each. Write an inequality for the number of pens, $p$, he can afford, and solve it.

32. A lift has a weight limit of 500 kg. Four people, each of mass $m$ kg, must not exceed this limit together. Write and solve an inequality for $m$.

33. Sloan needs more than 24 points to win a prize. She already has 15 points and earns 3 points per game. Write and solve an inequality for the number of games, $g$, she needs to play.

34. A rectangle has width $x$ cm and length $(x + 4)$ cm. Its perimeter must be less than 40 cm. Write and solve an inequality for $x$.

35. To ride a rollercoaster you must be at least 120 cm tall. Write this as an inequality using $h$ for height, and state whether a rider exactly 120 cm tall is allowed.

36. A car park charges £2 plus £1.50 per hour. Archer has £11 to spend. Write and solve an inequality for the number of hours, $t$, he can park.
