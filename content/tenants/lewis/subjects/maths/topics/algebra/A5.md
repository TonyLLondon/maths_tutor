## Getting started

1. A bag has 2 red counters and $b$ blue counters. Write the total.
2. There are $n$ birds, then 1 more arrives. Write the new total.
3. A box holds 2 rows of $x$ toys. Write the total number of toys.
4. Use $C = a + b$. Find $C$ when $a = 2$ and $b = 3$.
5. Use $P = 2 + w$. Find $P$ when $w = 4$.

## Reasoning (AO2)

27. **True or false?** If $y = 2x + 5$, then subtracting 5 from both sides and dividing by 2 gives $x$ in terms of $y$.

## More reasoning (AO2)

93. **True or false?** Rearranging $y = x + 5$ to $x = y - 5$ is correct. Explain one step.

94. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Dividing both sides by the same non-zero number keeps an equation balanced.

95. Mo rearranges $P = 2l + 2w$ to $w = P - 2l$. Explain the error.

96. **True or false?** Making $x$ the subject means writing $x = \ldots$ alone on one side.

97. **Always, sometimes, or never?** $y = 3x$ and $x = \dfrac{y}{3}$ say the same relationship.

98. Priya divides only the first term when rearranging $2x + 3 = y$. Explain why that fails.

99. **True or false?** To undo $+ 5$ you subtract 5 from both sides.

100. **Always, sometimes, or never?** $F = ma$ can be rearranged to $a = \dfrac{F}{m}$ when $m \neq 0$.

101. **True or false?** Swapping sides of $x = 5$ gives $5 = x$ with the same meaning.

102. **Always, sometimes, or never?** A formula with three letters can be rearranged for any one subject.

103. Leo rearranges $y = \dfrac{x}{2}$ to $x = 2y$ by multiplying by 2. Is that valid?

104. **True or false?** $C = 5 + 2n$ rearranges to $n = \dfrac{C - 5}{2}$.

105. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Adding the same amount to both sides is an inverse of subtracting.

106. **True or false?** $y = -x + 4$ rearranges to $x = 4 - y$.

107. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Multiplying by $\dfrac{1}{2}$ is the same as dividing by 2.

108. Nina forgets to divide **every** term in $2x + 4 = y$. Show the correct rearrangement for $x$.

109. **True or false?** $A = \pi r^2$ can be rearranged to $r = \sqrt{\dfrac{A}{\pi}}$.

110. **Always, sometimes, or never?** $y = x^2$ can be rearranged to $x = \sqrt{y}$ without thinking about $\pm$.

111. **True or false?** $3x = y$ implies $x = \dfrac{y}{3}$.

112. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Rearranging is the same skill as solving equations.

113. **True or false?** $P = 4s$ rearranges to $s = \dfrac{P}{4}$.

114. **Always, sometimes, or never?** $y = \dfrac{x + 1}{2}$ rearranges to $x = 2y - 1$.

115. **True or false?** You may subtract $x$ from both sides to collect $x$ terms.

116. **Always, sometimes, or never?** $v = u + at$ can be rearranged to $t = \dfrac{v - u}{a}$ when $a \neq 0$.

117. **True or false?** $y = 5$ already has $y$ as the subject.

118. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Dividing by $x$ is allowed when rearranging if $x \neq 0$.
