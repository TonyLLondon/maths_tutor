## Getting started

1. Continue the sequence: 1, 2, 3, __.
2. Continue the sequence: 2, 3, 4, __.
3. Continue the sequence: 2, 4, 6, __.
4. Continue the sequence: 1, 3, 5, __.
5. Continue the sequence: 5, 4, 3, __.
6. What is the rule for 1, 2, 3, 4?
7. What is the rule for 2, 4, 6, 8?
8. Fill the gap: 3, 5, __, 9.

## Reasoning (AO2)

21. Sam says the sequence 2, 4, 8, 16, … adds 2 each time. What is wrong with Sam’s rule? Write the correct term-to-term rule.

22. Two sequences start at 100 and go down. Sequence A subtracts 7 each time; Sequence B subtracts 10. After 5 more terms (not counting the start), which sequence has the smaller value? **Show your working.**

23. **Always, sometimes, or never?** A sequence that adds the same number each time will eventually reach 1000, if it starts below 1000 and keeps going. Explain your answer.

24. Priya says a sequence that doubles each time will always be bigger than one that adds 10 each time, no matter how many terms you compare. Test this with “start at 1, double” versus “start at 1, add 10” for the first 5 terms. Is Priya right so far?

25. Sequence A is 5, 10, 15, 20, … Sequence B is 100, 95, 90, 85, … Check the first four terms of each — is there ever a term where both sequences give the same value?

26. **True or false?** If a sequence’s term-to-term rule is “add a negative number”, the sequence is decreasing. Give an example.

27. Two sequences both start at 10. Sequence A’s rule is “× 2”. Sequence B’s rule is “+ 15”. Which sequence has the bigger 4th term? **Show your working.**

28. Mo says that to find any term in a sequence, you always need to list every term before it. Give an example of a sequence where you could find, say, the 10th term directly using a position-to-term rule, without listing terms 1 to 9.

## Problem (AO3)

29. In a board game, your score after each turn is 5, 12, 19, 26, … (same add-on rule each turn). What score will you have after turn 8?

30. A gym membership costs £20 to join plus £15 per month. Write the total cost as a sequence for months 1, 2, 3, and 4. What is the total cost after 6 months?

31. A plant is 4 cm tall when first measured and grows 3 cm every week. Write the sequence of heights for weeks 0 to 4. How tall will it be after 10 weeks?

32. Archer saves £5 in week 1 and increases his saving by £2 each week after that. How much does he save in week 6? What is his total savings after 6 weeks?

33. A stack of chairs has 1 chair in the front row shown, and each row behind has 3 more chairs than the row in front: 1, 4, 7, 10, … How many chairs are in the 6th row?

34. A car park charges according to the sequence £2, £4, £6, £8, … for each extra hour parked. If the pattern continues, how much would 7 hours cost?
