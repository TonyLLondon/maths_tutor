## Getting started

2. Which is the bigger sample: 5 children or 50 children?
3. A coin is flipped once. Is one flip a large sample or a small sample?
4. A spinner is spun 100 times. Is this sample larger or smaller than 10 spins?
5. To learn which fruit a class likes, is asking 1 child or 20 children more useful?
6. True or false: doing more trials usually gives us more information.
7. A dice is rolled 3 times. Would rolling it 30 more times give a bigger sample?
8. Which result would you trust more: 4 coin flips or 400 coin flips?
9. Which sample is larger: 15 trials or 150 trials?
10. True or false: doing more trials usually gives a better estimate.
11. A dice is rolled 50 times instead of 5 times. Which relative frequency is usually more reliable?
12. To test a spinner fairly, is 200 spins or 20 spins better?

15. A survey of 10 pupils finds 6 prefer football. Estimate the probability a pupil prefers football, and state the sample size.
16. A survey of 500 pupils finds 300 prefer football. Estimate the probability, and compare the sample size to question 11.
17. Which survey (10 pupils or 500 pupils) is likely to give the more accurate estimate of the true proportion who prefer football? Explain.
18. A quality control test checks 20 items out of 10,000 produced. Is this a large enough sample to be confident about the defect rate? Explain.
19. A pollster asks 50 people who they will vote for. Suggest one way to make the estimate more reliable.
20. As the number of trials in an experiment increases, what generally happens to the relative frequency compared with the theoretical probability?
21. A spinner is tested with 20 spins, then again with 2000 spins. Which result would you trust more as an estimate of the true probability, and why?
22. A dice is rolled once and lands on 4. Explain why you cannot estimate P(4) from a single roll.
23. Explain why flipping a coin 4 times and getting 4 heads does not prove the coin is biased.
24. A shop tests 5 batteries out of a delivery of 1000 and finds all 5 work. Is this a reliable way to estimate the proportion of working batteries in the whole delivery? Explain.

## Reasoning (AO2)

25. Archer says, "A bigger sample always gives the exact true probability." Is Archer correct? Explain using the idea of an estimate versus an exact value.

26. Two students test the same biased coin (true P(heads) = 0.6). One flips it 20 times, the other 2000 times. Whose relative frequency is likely to be closer to 0.6? Explain.

27. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Increasing the sample size makes an estimate less accurate. Explain.

28. Sloan tests a spinner 8 times and gets "win" 6 times, estimating P(win) = 0.75. Explain why this estimate might be misleading.

29. A national survey asks only 2 people about their favourite football team and claims this represents the whole country. Explain what is wrong with this method.

30. **True or false?** A sample of 100 is always big enough to trust an estimate. Explain, thinking about what "big enough" might depend on.

31. Explain why doubling the sample size does not automatically double the accuracy of an estimate, but generally does make the estimate more reliable.

## Problem (AO3)

32. A school wants to estimate the proportion of pupils who walk to school. Explain which sample would give a more reliable estimate: asking 15 pupils in one class, or asking 300 pupils across the whole school, and why.

33. A dice is rolled 12 times, giving four 6s (relative frequency 1/3). Explain why this result does not necessarily mean the dice is biased, and describe what you would do to test it further.

34. A company tests a new online game and asks 4000 players if they enjoyed it; 3200 say yes. Estimate the probability a player enjoys the game, and explain why this is likely to be a reliable estimate.

35. Two market researchers estimate the proportion of people who prefer a new snack: Researcher A surveys 30 people and gets 60%; Researcher B surveys 3000 people and gets 52%. Which estimate is more likely to be close to the true proportion, and why?

36. A machine is tested on 50 items from a batch of 50,000, finding 2 faulty. Estimate the number of faulty items in the whole batch, and explain one limitation of this estimate.

37. Explain why opinion polls before an election usually survey thousands of people rather than just a few dozen.

38. The table shows spinner test results. Estimate P(red) as a simplified fraction, and explain why this is likely to be a good estimate of the true probability.

## Stretch
