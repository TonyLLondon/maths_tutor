## Getting started

1. One coin can show heads or tails. How many outcomes are in its possibility space?
2. Two coins are flipped. Complete the list: HH, HT, TH, ___.
3. A red-or-blue spinner is spun once. List its possibility space.
4. A coin is flipped and a spinner numbered 1 and 2 is spun. How many combined outcomes are there?
5. List the outcomes for a coin flip and a spinner numbered 1 and 2.
6. Two coins are flipped. Which outcome means both coins show heads?
7. Two coins are flipped. Which two outcomes show exactly one head?
8. A 2-by-3 possibility-space grid has how many cells?
9. A fair dice is rolled once. How many outcomes are in its possibility space?
10. Two fair coins are flipped. How many outcomes are in the possibility space?
11. A spinner numbered 1 and 2 is spun with a coin flip. How many combined outcomes are there?
12. Complete the list for two coin flips: TT, TH, HT, ___.

## Fluency (AO1)

13. Describe a sample space diagram for rolling two fair dice, showing all possible totals. How many outcomes are there in total?
14. Using a two-dice sample space diagram, how many outcomes give a total of 8?
15. Using the same diagram, what is the probability of rolling a total of 12?
16. A coin is flipped and a fair 4-sided dice (numbered 1–4) is rolled. Describe a possibility space, and state how many outcomes there are.
17. Using the coin-and-dice possibility space, what is P(heads and an even number)?
18. Two fair coins are flipped. List the possibility space (HH, HT, TH, TT).
19. Using the two-coin possibility space, what is P(exactly one head)?
20. A spinner with sections 1, 2, 3 is spun twice. How many outcomes are in the possibility space?
21. Using the spinner-spun-twice possibility space, what is P(both spins show the same number)?
22. Two fair dice are rolled. What is the probability the two dice show the same number?
23. Two fair dice are rolled. What is the probability that the total is an even number?
24. A fair dice is rolled and a fair coin is flipped. What is the probability of getting a 6 and a tail?

## Reasoning (AO2)

25. Archer draws a possibility space for two dice but only lists 21 outcomes instead of 36. Explain what mistake he has likely made.

26. Explain why a possibility space diagram (grid) is a useful way to find probabilities for two combined events.

27. **True or false?** In a possibility space for two fair dice, all 36 outcomes are equally likely. Explain.

28. Sloan says the probability of a total of 7 with two dice is the same as the probability of a total of 2. Is Sloan correct? Use the possibility space to explain.

29. Explain why there are more ways to make a total of 7 than a total of 12 when rolling two dice, using the grid.

30. Priya says a possibility space for flipping 3 coins would have 6 outcomes, since there are 3 coins and 2 sides. Is Priya correct? Explain the correct total.

31. Explain the difference between listing a possibility space as a grid (rows/columns) and as a plain list, and why the grid method is often clearer for two combined events.

## Problem (AO3)

32. Two fair dice are rolled and their scores multiplied. Using a possibility space (a multiplication grid), find the probability that the product is greater than 20.

33. A spinner with sections 1–5 is spun, and a fair coin is flipped. Draw a possibility space and find P(an odd number and heads).

34. Two fair four-sided dice (numbered 1–4) are rolled and their scores added. Draw the possibility space of totals and find the probability the total is at least 6.

35. A game uses two spinners: one with sections A, B, C and the other with sections 1, 2. Draw the possibility space and find the probability of getting (B, 2).

36. Two dice are rolled and the difference between the two scores (larger minus smaller) is recorded. Using a possibility space, find the probability the difference is 0.

37. A fair coin is flipped three times. Using a systematic list or tree, find the possibility space and the probability of getting exactly two heads.

38. Two fair six-sided dice are rolled. Find the probability that at least one dice shows a 6, using the possibility space.

## Stretch

42. Design your own possibility space problem involving two different spinners of your choice. State the probability of one specific combined outcome, and show your working.
