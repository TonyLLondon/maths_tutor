## Getting started

1. A coin is flipped 10 times and shows heads 5 times. How many heads were recorded?
2. The table shows spinner results. Write the result as “2 out of ___”.
3. A dice is rolled 6 times and shows a 4 once. What fraction of the rolls showed 4?
4. A bag is tested 10 times and a blue counter is picked 7 times. What fraction of the picks were blue?
5. A footballer takes 5 shots and scores 4. Write the scoring result as a fraction.
6. A coin shows tails 3 times in 6 flips. What fraction of the flips were tails?
7. An event happens 0 times in 8 tries. What is its relative frequency?
8. An event happens every time in 9 tries. What is its relative frequency?
9. A coin shows heads 6 times in 12 flips. What is the relative frequency of heads?
10. A red counter is picked 3 times in 15 tries. What is the relative frequency of red?
11. An event happens 5 times in 25 trials. What is its relative frequency?
12. An event happens 0 times in 10 trials. What is its relative frequency?

## Reasoning (AO2)

25. Archer flips a coin 10 times and gets heads 7 times, giving a relative frequency of 0.7. Explain why this is not a good estimate for P(heads).

26. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Relative frequency equals the true theoretical probability exactly. Explain.

27. Sloan says, "The more trials I do, the more reliable my relative frequency estimate becomes." Is Sloan correct? Explain.

28. A dice is rolled 6 times and lands on 3 exactly once. Priya says this proves P(3) = 1/6 for this dice. Is Priya correct? Explain.

29. Two students test the same coin: one flips it 20 times, the other 500 times. Whose relative frequency is likely to give a better estimate of the true probability? Explain.

30. **True or false?** A relative frequency can be greater than 1. Explain.

31. A dice is rolled many times, giving a relative frequency of rolling a 6 close to 0.3. Mia says a fair dice would give a similar result. Is Mia correct? Explain using the theoretical probability of a fair dice.

## Problem (AO3)

32. A quality inspector tests 250 bulbs and finds 10 faulty. Estimate how many faulty bulbs there would be in a batch of 4000, using the relative frequency.

33. Over a season, a striker scores in 18 out of 30 matches. Estimate the probability the striker scores in a randomly chosen match, and use it to predict the number of scoring matches in the next 10 games.

34. The table shows a spinner’s sections and test results. Estimate P(win), and compare it to a fair spinner with 4 equal sections. Is this spinner fair?

35. The table shows results from 300 dice rolls. Does this dice appear to be fair? Explain using the relative frequencies.

36. A driving instructor finds 27 out of 45 pupils pass their test first time. Estimate the probability a new pupil passes first time, giving your answer as a percentage.

37. A charity has run a raffle for several years; historically 1 in 40 tickets wins a prize. If 2000 tickets are sold this year, estimate the number of winning tickets.

38. A gardener plants 60 seeds and 51 germinate. Estimate the probability that a seed germinates, and use it to predict how many would germinate out of 500 seeds.
