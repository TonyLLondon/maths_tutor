## Getting started

1. A fair coin is flipped 10 times. Would you expect about 5 heads or about 10 heads?
2. A fair coin is flipped 6 times. About how many tails would you expect?
3. A spinner has 2 equal parts, red and blue. It is spun 8 times. About how many reds would you expect?
4. A bag has equal numbers of red and blue counters. You pick and replace a counter 10 times. About how many blue counters would you expect?
5. A fair dice is rolled 6 times. About how many times would you expect to roll a 1?
6. A spinner has 4 equal colours. It is spun 8 times. About how many times would you expect one chosen colour?
7. If an event is impossible, how many times would you expect it in 20 tries?
8. If an event is certain, how many times would you expect it in 12 tries?
9. A fair coin is flipped 40 times. About how many heads would you expect?
10. P(win) = 1/4 on a spinner. It is spun 20 times. How many wins would you expect?
11. A bag has 1 red and 3 blue counters. Pick and replace 20 times. About how many red picks?
12. A fair dice is rolled 30 times. About how many sixes would you expect?

## Reasoning (AO2)

25. Archer rolls a fair dice 30 times and gets a 6 only twice, when he expected 5. Explain why this doesn't mean the dice is unfair.

26. Sloan says, "If I expect 10 heads from 20 flips, I will always get exactly 10." Is Sloan correct? Explain.

27. **Always, sometimes, or never?** An expected outcome is a whole number. Give an example to support your answer.

28. A spinner is expected to land on red 15 times out of 60 spins. Priya says this means P(red) = 1/4. Is she correct? Explain.

29. Mia flips a coin 10 times and gets 8 heads. She says the coin must be biased. Explain why a small number of trials may not prove this.

30. **True or false?** The more times you repeat an experiment, the closer the actual results tend to get, proportionally, to the expected outcomes. Explain.

31. A fair dice is rolled 600 times and lands on 6 exactly 90 times. Is this close to what you would expect from a fair dice? Use the theoretical probability to explain your answer.
