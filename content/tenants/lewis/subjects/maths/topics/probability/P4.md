## Getting started

1. A coin lands on either heads or tails. Can it land on both in one flip?
2. A dice roll is either odd or even. Do these two groups cover every possible roll?
3. If it will certainly rain or not rain, and the chance of rain is 1, what is the chance of no rain?
4. A bag contains only red and blue counters. If picking red is impossible, what colour must you pick?
5. On one dice roll, can you roll both a 2 and a 5?
6. Which two outcomes cover every possible result of one coin flip?
7. A spinner can land only on red, blue, or green. Are those three outcomes exhaustive?
8. Complete the sentence: the probability of an event plus the probability of it not happening equals ___.
9. P(rain) = 0.3. Find P(not rain).
10. On one fair dice roll, P(even) + P(odd) = ___.
11. P(A) = 0.7. Find P(not A).
12. Two outcomes are mutually exclusive. Can both happen on one trial?

## Fluency (AO1)

13. Events A and B are mutually exclusive with P(A) = 0.3 and P(B) = 0.4. What is P(A or B)?
14. A bag has only red, blue, and green counters. P(red) = 0.2 and P(blue) = 0.5. What is P(green)?
15. P(rain tomorrow) = 0.35. What is P(no rain tomorrow)?
16. A spinner has sections red, blue, and yellow. If P(red) = 1/4 and P(blue) = 1/3, what is P(yellow)?
17. Are rolling an even number and rolling an odd number on a dice mutually exclusive? Explain your answer in one sentence.
18. List all the mutually exclusive outcomes when flipping a coin once.
19. P(A) = 0.6. What is P(not A)?
20. Are "rolling a 6" and "rolling an even number" on a fair dice mutually exclusive? Explain your answer.
21. Three mutually exclusive events X, Y, Z are exhaustive, with P(X) = 0.2 and P(Y) = 0.5. Find P(Z).
22. P(pass exam) = 7/10. What is P(fail exam)?
23. A bag has only red and blue counters. P(red) = 3/8. What is P(blue)?
24. Are "drawing a King" and "drawing a Heart" from a pack of cards mutually exclusive? Explain your answer.

## Reasoning (AO2)

25. Archer says two events can never be both mutually exclusive and exhaustive at the same time. Is Archer correct? Give an example to support your answer.

26. **True or false?** If two events are mutually exclusive, then P(A and B) = 0. Explain.

27. Sloan says "rolling a number greater than 3" and "rolling a number less than 5" on a dice are mutually exclusive and exhaustive. Is Sloan correct? Explain, listing the outcomes in each event.

28. A weather forecaster gives P(sunny) = 0.4, P(cloudy) = 0.35, and P(rainy) = 0.3. Priya notices something wrong. What is it, and why must it be a mistake?

29. Explain the difference between mutually exclusive events and exhaustive events, using an example of each.

30. Mia rolls a dice and says "getting an even number" and "getting a multiple of 3" cannot both happen, so they must be mutually exclusive. Is Mia correct? Explain, thinking about the number 6.

31. **Always, sometimes, or never?** For any two mutually exclusive events A and B, P(A) + P(B) ≤ 1. Explain your answer.

## Problem (AO3)

32. A box of chocolates contains only milk, dark, and white chocolates. P(milk) = 0.45 and P(dark) = 0.3. What is P(white)? If there are 60 chocolates, how many are white?

33. In a class, each pupil studies exactly one of French, Spanish, or German. P(French) = 2/5 and P(Spanish) = 3/10. What fraction of the class studies German?

34. A game show contestant can win Gold, Silver, Bronze, or nothing (exhaustive and mutually exclusive). P(Gold) = 0.05, P(Silver) = 0.15, P(Bronze) = 0.2. What is P(nothing)?

35. A survey asks 120 people their favourite season (exactly one choice from spring, summer, autumn, winter): 30 chose spring, 42 chose summer, and 18 chose autumn. How many chose winter, and what is the probability of choosing winter?

36. A spinner has red, blue, green, and yellow sections (mutually exclusive and exhaustive). P(red) = P(blue) = 0.2 and P(green) = 0.3. Find P(yellow), then find the probability of landing on red or yellow.

37. A test has grades A, B, C, D, or Fail (exhaustive, mutually exclusive). P(A) = 0.1, P(B) = 0.25, P(C) = 0.35, P(D) = 0.2. Find P(Fail), and the probability of getting a C or better.

38. A raffle ticket can win a big prize, a small prize, or nothing (only one can happen per ticket). P(big) = 1/50 and P(small) = 9/50. What is P(none)? Out of 500 tickets, how many would you expect to win nothing?

## Stretch

39. Explain why P(A) + P(not A) always equals 1, using a real example, and describe how this connects to mutually exclusive and exhaustive events.

40. A bag contains only red, blue, and green marbles. You are told P(red or blue) = 0.7 and P(blue) = 0.25. Using the fact that red and blue are mutually exclusive, find P(red) and then P(green).

41. Design a spinner with four mutually exclusive and exhaustive outcomes where none of the probabilities are equal. State each probability and check that they add to 1.

42. Explain why "raining" and "sunny" are not always mutually exclusive in real life (think about partly cloudy days), even though in a simplified maths problem we might treat them as if they were.
