## What lives here

Geometry is all about **shapes, space, and how things fit together**. You learn the names of shapes, how to measure angles and lengths, and how to draw neat diagrams. You also explore how shapes move on a grid, how circles work, and how 3D objects look from different sides.

When you get good at geometry, you can read maps, build models, spot patterns in tiles and buildings, and solve puzzles where a picture makes the maths clearer.

## You might already know

- Shapes like triangles, squares, and circles, and how many sides they have.
- That a corner turn can be small (acute) or big (obtuse), and that a square corner is a [right angle](../glossary/right-angle.md).
- How to use a ruler, and maybe compasses, to draw lines and circles.
- That parallel train tracks never meet, and that a mirror flips a picture left–right.
- How to read simple points on a grid, like in a game or on [coordinates](../glossary/coordinates.md).
