Number is the part of maths about counting, comparing, calculating, and measuring. You use these skills every day: shopping, cooking, telling the time, and sharing things fairly.

## You might already know

- Which of two whole numbers is bigger or smaller.
- Adding, subtracting, multiplying, and dividing small numbers.
- Simple [fractions](../glossary/fraction.md) like halves and quarters.
- That money and measures use different units (pence, metres, grams).

## Topics in this area

- **Ordering numbers** — put integers, [decimals](../glossary/decimal.md), and fractions in order.
- **Four operations and place value** — add, subtract, multiply, divide, and understand digits in columns.
- **Order of operations and inverse operations** — use [BIDMAS](../glossary/bidmas.md) and undo a calculation to check it.
- **Factors, multiples and primes** — [factors](../glossary/factor.md), [multiples](../glossary/multiple.md), [prime](../glossary/prime.md) numbers, [HCF](../glossary/hcf.md), and [LCM](../glossary/lcm.md).
- **Systematic listing** — list choices without missing any or repeating.
- **Powers and roots** — squares, cubes, and square roots.
- **Roots and indices** — laws of indices (powers).
- **Exact calculation with fractions and π** — fraction arithmetic and exact circle answers.
- **Standard form** — very big and very small numbers in [standard form](../glossary/standard-form.md).
- **Fractions and decimals** — switch between fractions, decimals, and percentages.
- **Fractions in ratio problems** — fractions of a whole when things are shared in a [ratio](../glossary/ratio.md).
- **Fractions and percentages as operators** — find a fraction or [percentage](../glossary/percentage.md) of an amount.
- **Standard units and compound measures** — convert units and work with speed, density, and similar measures.
- **Estimate and check** — sensible approximations and checking with inverses.
- **Rounding and error intervals** — [rounding](../glossary/rounding.md) and what a measured value might really be.
- **Limits of accuracy** — upper and lower bounds when numbers are rounded.
