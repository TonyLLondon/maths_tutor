## What lives here

This area is about comparing amounts and how they change together. You will work with [ratios](../../glossary/ratio.md), [fractions](../../glossary/fraction.md), and [percentages](../../glossary/percentage.md). You will also meet speed, density, and other “per unit” ideas, plus [proportion](../../glossary/proportion.md) on graphs and in real situations like maps and recipes.

Numbers still matter, but the focus is on **relationships**: “twice as much”, “shared fairly in parts”, or “goes up by 10% each year”. These skills help with cooking, shopping, travel, sport stats, and science measurements.

## You might already know

- How to simplify a [fraction](../../glossary/fraction.md) like $\frac{2}{4}$ to $\frac{1}{2}$.
- That 50% means half, and 100% means the whole amount.
- How to multiply and divide whole numbers and decimals.
- That 1 km = 1000 m and other basic unit swaps.
- How to read simple tables and draw straight lines on a graph.
