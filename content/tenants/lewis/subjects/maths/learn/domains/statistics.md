## What lives here

Statistics is about **collecting information**, **organising it**, and **using it to answer questions**. You might count how many pupils walk to school, record test scores, or plot how height and shoe size are linked. The guides here help you read charts, find averages, compare groups fairly, and spot patterns in data.

## You might already know

- Counting how many people chose each option in a vote.
- Drawing or reading a simple bar chart.
- Finding which number appears most often in a list.
- Saying whether something went up, down, or stayed steady over time.

## Topics in this area

- **Collecting and showing data** — tallies, tables, bar charts, and pictograms (`S1`).
- **Tables and charts** — pie charts, two-way tables, line graphs, and grouped tables (`S2`).
- **Averages and spread** — mean, median, mode, and range (`S4`).
- **Comparing groups** — samples, typical values, and how spread out data is (`S5`).
- **Scatter graphs** — correlation, lines of best fit, and outliers (`S6`).

## How to use these guides

Pick the topic that matches what you are practising. Read the guide once to get the big picture, then try questions in the app. If you get stuck on a step, open **Help** on that question for a shorter reminder — you can come back to these pages anytime for a fuller explanation.
