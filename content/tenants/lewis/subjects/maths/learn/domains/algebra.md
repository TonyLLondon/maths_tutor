Algebra is the part of maths where you use letters to stand for numbers you do not know yet — or numbers that can change. It helps you find missing values, describe patterns, draw graphs, and solve real problems like working out prices, distances, and scores.

## You might already know

- Finding a missing number in a sum like □ + 3 = 8.
- That a letter like $x$ can mean “some number”.
- Plotting points on a grid and reading [coordinates](../glossary/coordinates.md).
- Following [BIDMAS](../glossary/bidmas.md) when you calculate with numbers.

## Topics in this area

- **Algebraic notation** — missing numbers, letters, and function machines.
- **Substitution** — replace a letter with a number and calculate.
- **Expressions, equations and identities** — tell expressions, [equations](../glossary/equation.md), and always-true statements apart.
- **Simplify and expand** — collect like terms and multiply brackets.
- **Formulae and rearranging** — use a [formula](../glossary/formula.md) and make one letter the subject.
- **Equations vs identities** — when a statement is true for one value or for every value.
- **Functions as inputs and outputs** — rules that turn an input into an output.
- **Coordinates in four quadrants** — negative and positive $x$ and $y$ on a grid.
- **Straight-line graphs** — lines like $y = 2x + 1$.
- **Gradient and intercept** — steepness and where a line crosses the $y$-axis.
- **Quadratic graphs** — U-shaped curves from $x^2$.
- **Graph families** — linear, quadratic, cubic, and reciprocal shapes.
- **Real-context graphs** — distance–time and other stories on a graph.
- **Linear equations** — solve for $x$ step by step.
- **Quadratic equations** — solve when $x$ is squared.
- **Simultaneous equations** — two equations, two unknowns.
- **Forming equations from context** — turn a word problem into an equation.
- **Linear inequalities** — answers that are a range of numbers, not just one.
- **Sequences** — lists that follow a rule.
- **Special sequences** — square numbers, Fibonacci, and more.
- **nth term of linear sequences** — a rule for the $n$th number in a list.
