### In plain English

The **LCM** (lowest common multiple) is the **smallest** number that is in the times tables of both numbers. It helps when you need a common denominator or when events repeat on a cycle.

### Example

Multiples of $4$: $4, 8, 12, 16, \ldots$ Multiples of $6$: $6, 12, 18, \ldots$ The smallest number in both lists is $12$, so LCM$(4, 6) = 12$.

### Watch out

- LCM is usually **larger** than the original numbers; HCF is usually **smaller**.
- For two numbers, LCM $\times$ HCF $=$ product of the numbers (a handy check).
