### In plain English

A **line graph** joins points with straight segments to show how something **changes**, often over **time**. The slope between points shows whether values go up, down, or stay steady.

### Example

You measure plant height each week: week $0$ is $2$ cm, week $1$ is $5$ cm, week $2$ is $7$ cm. Plot points and join them to see growth over time.

### Watch out

- The horizontal axis often shows **time**, but read the labels — not always.
- A steep line means a **fast** change; a flat line means little change.
