### In plain English

A **bearing** tells you which **direction** to go, measured **clockwise from north**. Bearings are written as three digits, like $045°$ or $270°$, and north is always $000°$.

### Example

You stand at a campsite. A hut is straight east of you. East is one quarter turn clockwise from north, so the bearing of the hut from the campsite is $090°$.

### Watch out

- Always measure **from north**, turning **clockwise**.
- Write three figures when you can: $005°$ not $5°$ on a bearing diagram.
