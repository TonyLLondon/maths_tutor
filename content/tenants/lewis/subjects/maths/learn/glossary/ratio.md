### In plain English

A **ratio** compares two or more amounts using **colon** notation, like $2 : 3$. It tells you how many parts of one thing match parts of another, not necessarily the total count.

### Example

Red and blue paint are mixed in ratio $2 : 3$. That means for every $2$ parts red you use $3$ parts blue — $4$ and $6$, or $10$ and $15$, work the same way.

### Watch out

- Order matters: $2 : 3$ is not the same as $3 : 2$.
- Ratios do not have units unless the question mixes units — keep parts consistent.
