### In plain English

The **gradient** of a straight line tells you how **steep** it is. It is how much the line goes **up** for each step **across**. A bigger gradient means a steeper uphill line.

### Example

From one point to another you go up $6$ while going across $3$. Gradient $= \frac{6}{3} = 2$ — for every $1$ across, you go up $2$.

### Watch out

- Going **downhill** gives a **negative** gradient.
- “Rise over run” means **vertical change ÷ horizontal change**.
