### In plain English

A **decimal** is a way to write numbers using a **decimal point**. The digits after the point show parts of a whole — tenths, hundredths, and so on.

### Example

$2.5$ means $2$ whole ones and $5$ tenths. That is the same as $2\frac{1}{2}$.

### Watch out

- $0.5$ is bigger than $0.05$ — the **place** of each digit matters.
- When lining up for addition, keep the **decimal points** in a column.
