### In plain English

**Probability** measures how **likely** something is, from **impossible** ($0$) to **certain** ($1$). You can write it as a fraction, decimal, or percentage of the time it would happen if you repeated the experiment many times.

### Example

On a fair die, probability of rolling an even number ($2, 4,$ or $6$) is $\frac{3}{6} = \frac{1}{2}$ — three favourable outcomes out of six equally likely ones.

### Watch out

- Probabilities must be between **$0$ and $1$** (or $0\%$ and $100\%$).
- “Or” and “and” need different rules — read whether events overlap or are independent.
