### In plain English

A **bar chart** uses **bars** to show how much of something you have in each category. Taller bars mean bigger counts or amounts. The bars do not touch if each category is separate.

### Example

You ask ten friends their favourite fruit. Three choose apple, five banana, two cherry. You draw three bars with heights $3$, $5$, and $2$ and label each bar with the fruit name.

### Watch out

- Read the **scale** on the axis carefully — sometimes each square is worth $2$ or $10$, not $1$.
- Bar charts show **categories**; **line graphs** are often better for changes over time.
