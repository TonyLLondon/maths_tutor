### In plain English

The **radius** of a circle is the distance from the **centre** to any point on the edge. All radii of the same circle are the same length.

### Example

A circular table has radius $40$ cm. That is how far you reach from the middle to the edge. The diameter across the table is $80$ cm — twice the radius.

### Watch out

- Radius is **half** the diameter: $r = d \div 2$.
- Do not confuse radius with **circumference** (distance around the outside).
