### In plain English

A **factor** is a whole number that divides another number **exactly** with no remainder. Factors are the numbers you multiply together to make the original number.

### Example

The factors of $12$ are $1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12$ because $12 = 1 \times 12 = 2 \times 6 = 3 \times 4$.

### Watch out

- $1$ and the number itself are always factors.
- Do not confuse **factor** with **multiple** — multiples are bigger “times table” numbers.
