### In plain English

A **fraction** shows **part of a whole**. The bottom number (denominator) tells how many equal parts the whole is split into; the top number (numerator) tells how many of those parts you have.

### Example

If a chocolate bar is cut into $4$ equal pieces and you eat $3$, you ate $\frac{3}{4}$ of the bar.

### Watch out

- $\frac{1}{2}$ and $\frac{2}{4}$ are the **same amount** — you can simplify fractions.
- The denominator cannot be **zero** — you cannot split a whole into zero parts.
