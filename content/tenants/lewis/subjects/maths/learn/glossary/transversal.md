### In plain English

A **transversal** is a line that **crosses** two or more other lines. When it cuts two parallel lines, it creates matching angle pairs you can use to find missing angles.

### Example

Two horizontal parallel lines are crossed by one sloping line. That sloping line is the transversal. It forms alternate, corresponding, and co-interior angles with the parallels.

### Watch out

- The transversal is the **crossing** line, not the parallel lines themselves.
- Angle facts (alternate, corresponding, and so on) need **parallel** lines unless the question marks them equal for you.
