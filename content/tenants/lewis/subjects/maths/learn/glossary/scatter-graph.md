### In plain English

A **scatter graph** plots **pairs** of measurements as dots to see if two things are linked — for example height and shoe size. You do not join the dots with lines like a line graph.

### Example

You record each pupil’s hours of sleep and test score as a point $(x, y)$. Lots of dots sloping upward might suggest more sleep goes with higher scores — but it does not prove cause.

### Watch out

- A **line of best fit** is a smooth trend line, not connecting every point in order.
- Correlation is **not** the same as saying one thing **causes** the other.
