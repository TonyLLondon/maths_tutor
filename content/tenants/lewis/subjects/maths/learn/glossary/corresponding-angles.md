### In plain English

**Corresponding angles** are in matching corners when one line crosses two **parallel lines** — same side of the crossing line and same position (both above or both below each parallel). When the lines are parallel, corresponding angles are **equal**.

### Example

A crossing line makes a $65°$ angle above the lower parallel line on the left. The matching angle above the upper parallel line on the left is also $65°$.

### Watch out

- They must be in **corresponding** positions — trace an “F” shape to check.
- If the lines are not parallel, you cannot assume the angles are equal.
