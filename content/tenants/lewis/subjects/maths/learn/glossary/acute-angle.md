### In plain English

An **acute angle** is an angle that is **smaller than a right angle**. Right angles are exactly $90°$, so acute angles are **less than $90°$**. They often look sharp or “pointy”.

### Example

If you open a book just a little, the angle between the covers might be about $45°$. That is an acute angle because $45° < 90°$.

### Watch out

- Do not mix up **acute** (small) with **obtuse** (bigger than $90°$ but less than $180°$).
- A very tiny angle, like $5°$, is still acute.
