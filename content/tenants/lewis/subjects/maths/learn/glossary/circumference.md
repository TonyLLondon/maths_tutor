### In plain English

The **circumference** is the **distance all the way around** a circle — like the length of the rim of a wheel. It is a length, so you measure it in cm, m, and so on.

### Example

A circular pond has diameter $10$ m. A useful rule is circumference $\approx \pi \times$ diameter, so the rim is about $3.14 \times 10 = 31.4$ m around (you may use $\pi$ on your calculator).

### Watch out

- Circumference is **perimeter** for a circle, not **area** inside the circle.
- Do not mix up **radius** (centre to edge) with **diameter** (edge to edge through the centre).
