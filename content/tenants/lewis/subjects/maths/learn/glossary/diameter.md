### In plain English

The **diameter** of a circle is the distance **across** it through the **centre**, from one edge to the opposite edge. Every diameter is twice as long as the **radius**.

### Example

A pizza is $30$ cm across through the middle. The diameter is $30$ cm, so the radius from the centre to the crust is $15$ cm.

### Watch out

- The diameter must pass through the **centre** — a chord that misses the centre is not a diameter.
- Do not confuse diameter with **circumference** (distance around the outside).
