### In plain English

The **HCF** (highest common factor) is the **biggest** whole number that divides two or more numbers exactly. It is useful for simplifying fractions or sharing things into equal piles.

### Example

Factors of $12$: $1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12$. Factors of $18$: $1, 2, 3, 6, 9, 18$. The largest number in both lists is $6$, so HCF$(12, 18) = 6$.

### Watch out

- HCF is about **factors** (usually smaller); **LCM** is about **multiples** (usually bigger).
- If the only common factor is $1$, the numbers are called **coprime** — HCF is $1$.
