### In plain English

**BIDMAS** is a reminder of the **order** to work out mixed calculations: **B**rackets first, then **I**ndices (powers), then **D**ivision and **M**ultiplication (left to right), then **A**ddition and **S**ubtraction (left to right). Some people say BODMAS instead — the idea is the same.

### Example

Work out $3 + 4 \times 2$. Multiplication comes before addition, so $4 \times 2 = 8$, then $3 + 8 = 11$.

### Watch out

- Brackets change the order: $(3 + 4) \times 2 = 14$, not $11$.
- After brackets and powers, **÷ and ×** have the same priority — work **left to right**.
