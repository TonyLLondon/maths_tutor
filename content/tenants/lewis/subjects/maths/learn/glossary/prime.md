### In plain English

A **prime** number is a whole number greater than $1$ that has **exactly two** factors: $1$ and itself. You cannot split it into smaller whole-number factors except those two.

### Example

$7$ is prime because only $1 \times 7$ works. $6$ is not prime because $2 \times 3 = 6$ as well.

### Watch out

- $1$ is **not** prime — it only has one factor.
- $2$ is the **only even** prime number.
