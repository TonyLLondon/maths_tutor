### In plain English

An **expression** is a mix of numbers, letters, and operations (like $+$, $-$, $\times$) **without** an equals sign. You can simplify it or substitute values, but it is not yet a full “this equals that” statement.

### Example

$3a + 5$ is an expression. If $a = 4$, its value is $3 \times 4 + 5 = 17$.

### Watch out

- $2x + 1$ is an expression; $2x + 1 = 9$ is an **equation**.
- Like terms (same letter part) can be **collected** — $x + 2x = 3x$.
