### In plain English

The **range** is the **spread** of a data set: take the **largest** value minus the **smallest**. A big range means the numbers are spread out; a small range means they are close together.

### Example

Daily temperatures $12°, 14°, 19°, 11°$. Largest $19°$, smallest $11°$, range $= 19 - 11 = 8°$.

### Watch out

- Range uses only **two** numbers (max and min), not every value.
- **Range** in statistics is not the same as **domain** or “range of a graph” in algebra — read the topic.
