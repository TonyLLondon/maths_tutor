### In plain English

**Standard form** writes very big or very small numbers as $a \times 10^n$, where $a$ is at least $1$ and less than $10$, and $n$ is a whole number. It keeps long strings of zeros manageable.

### Example

$4{,}500{,}000 = 4.5 \times 10^6$. The $6$ tells you how far the decimal point moved.

### Watch out

- The first number must be **$1 \le a < 10$** — not $45 \times 10^5$ for the same value.
- Negative powers of ten count for **small** numbers, like $3.2 \times 10^{-4}$.
