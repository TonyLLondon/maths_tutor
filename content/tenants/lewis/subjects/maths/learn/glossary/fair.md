### In plain English

In probability, **fair** means everyone or everything has an **equal chance**. A fair coin is equally likely to land heads or tails. An unfair spinner might favour one colour.

### Example

You roll a fair six-sided die. Each face from $1$ to $6$ has the same chance, so the probability of rolling a $4$ is $\frac{1}{6}$.

### Watch out

- “Fair” is about **equal chances**, not about winning often.
- Real objects can be slightly unfair — maths questions usually say **fair** unless they tell you otherwise.
