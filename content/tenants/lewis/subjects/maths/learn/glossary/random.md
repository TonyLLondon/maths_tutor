### In plain English

**Random** means there is no pattern you can use to predict the next result — each fair outcome has its set chance, but you cannot know exactly what will happen on one try.

### Example

If you mix a deck well and pick a card without looking, the choice is random (each card equally likely). You might get any card even if you got hearts last time.

### Watch out

- Random does **not** mean “equally likely” for every situation — read if the experiment is **fair**.
- A **random sample** should represent a group without bias.
