### In plain English

**Co-interior angles** (also called **allied angles**) lie **between** two parallel lines on the **same side** of a crossing line. When the lines are parallel, co-interior angles **add up to $180°$**.

### Example

Two parallel lines are cut by a sloping line. One co-interior angle is $110°$. The other on the same side between the parallels must be $180° - 110° = 70°$.

### Watch out

- They sit on the **same side** of the crossing line — not opposite like alternate angles.
- They **add to $180°$**, they are not equal (unless both are $90°$).
