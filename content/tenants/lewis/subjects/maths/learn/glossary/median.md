### In plain English

The **median** is the **middle value** when you put numbers in order. If there are two middle values, take their mean. The median is less affected by extreme high or low scores than the mean.

### Example

Ordered data: $3, 5, 7, 9, 20$. The median is $7$ — the middle number. The big $20$ does not change that middle position.

### Watch out

- **Sort** the data first — the median is not the middle number in the original messy list.
- With an **even** count, average the two central values.
