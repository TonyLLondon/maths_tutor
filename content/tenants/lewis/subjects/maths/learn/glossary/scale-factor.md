### In plain English

A **scale factor** tells you how many times **bigger or smaller** a shape becomes after enlargement. Scale factor $3$ means every length is multiplied by $3$.

### Example

A photo is enlarged with scale factor $2$. A side that was $4$ cm becomes $4 \times 2 = 8$ cm. Area scales by $2 \times 2 = 4$ times.

### Watch out

- **Lengths** multiply by the scale factor; **areas** multiply by scale factor **squared**.
- A scale factor between $0$ and $1$ makes the shape **smaller**.
