### In plain English

**Proportion** links two amounts so they grow or shrink **together**. If two things are in proportion, multiplying one side of the relationship multiplies the other the same way.

### Example

If $3$ pencils cost $60$p, then $6$ pencils (double) cost $120$p (double) — the cost is in proportion to the number bought.

### Watch out

- Proportion is about **consistent scaling**, not just any two numbers added together.
- **Direct proportion** means both increase together; **inverse** means one goes up as the other goes down.
