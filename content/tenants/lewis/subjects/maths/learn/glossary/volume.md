### In plain English

**Volume** is the amount of **three-dimensional space** inside a solid — how much it can hold. It is measured in cubic units like cm³ or m³.

### Example

A box $2$ cm by $3$ cm by $4$ cm has volume $2 \times 3 \times 4 = 24$ cm³. That is like stacking $24$ little $1$ cm cubes inside.

### Watch out

- Volume is **not** surface area (area of the outside skin).
- Changing length units affects volume by a **cube** — doubling each edge makes volume $8$ times bigger.
