### In plain English

**Coordinates** are a pair of numbers that tell you **where a point is** on a grid. You write them as $(x, y)$: first how far across, then how far up (or down if negative).

### Example

The point $(3, 2)$ means go $3$ units right from the origin, then $2$ units up. The point $(3, -1)$ is still $3$ right but $1$ down.

### Watch out

- Order matters: $(2, 5)$ is not the same place as $(5, 2)$.
- On many school grids, **$x$ is across** and **$y$ is up**.
