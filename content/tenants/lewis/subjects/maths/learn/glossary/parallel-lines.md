### In plain English

**Parallel lines** stay the **same distance apart** and never meet, even if you extend them. Think of the two rails on a straight train track.

### Example

In a rectangle, opposite sides are parallel. The top and bottom edges go in the same direction and never cross.

### Watch out

- Parallel lines must lie in the **same flat plane** — on paper, they look like two identical slopes.
- A crossing line through parallels gives special angle pairs (alternate, corresponding, co-interior).
