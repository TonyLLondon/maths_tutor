### In plain English

Two events are **independent** if one **does not change** the chance of the other. Flipping a fair coin and rolling a fair die are independent — the coin result does not affect the die.

### Example

You flip a coin and roll a die. The probability of heads **and** a $5$ is $\frac{1}{2} \times \frac{1}{6} = \frac{1}{12}$ because the two results do not affect each other.

### Watch out

- Picking two cards **without putting the first back** is usually **not** independent.
- “And” for independent events often means **multiply** the probabilities.
