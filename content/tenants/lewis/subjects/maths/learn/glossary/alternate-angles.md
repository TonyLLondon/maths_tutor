### In plain English

**Alternate angles** are equal angles that sit on **opposite sides** of a line that cuts through two **parallel lines**. They are in a “Z” shape if you trace around them.

### Example

Two parallel horizontal lines are crossed by a sloping line. The angle above the bottom line on the left matches the angle below the top line on the right. Those two are alternate angles, so they have the same size.

### Watch out

- Alternate angles are only guaranteed to be equal when the two lines are **parallel**.
- Do not confuse them with **corresponding angles**, which sit on the same side of the crossing line.
