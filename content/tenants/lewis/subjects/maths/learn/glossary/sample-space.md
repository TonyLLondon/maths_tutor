### In plain English

The **sample space** is the **list of all possible outcomes** in one go. When every outcome is equally likely, probability is “number of good outcomes ÷ total in the sample space”.

### Example

Flip two coins. Outcomes are HH, HT, TH, TT — four equally likely results. That set of four is the sample space.

### Watch out

- Make sure outcomes are **complete** and **do not overlap**.
- For dice and coins, “equally likely” only applies if they are **fair**.
