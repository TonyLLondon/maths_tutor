### In plain English

**Rounding** means replacing a number with a nearby **simpler** value, usually to a set place (nearest ten, one decimal place, and so on). You look at the next digit to decide whether to round up or down.

### Example

Round $3476$ to the nearest hundred: look at the tens digit ($7$). It is $5$ or more, so round up — $3500$.

### Watch out

- If the digit after your cut-off is **$5$ or more**, round **up**; otherwise round **down**.
- Say **what** you rounded to — “about $3500$ to the nearest hundred”.
