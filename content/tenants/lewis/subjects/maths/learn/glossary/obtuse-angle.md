### In plain English

An **obtuse angle** is **larger than a right angle** ($90°$) but **smaller than a straight line** ($180°$). It looks wide or “opened up” more than a square corner.

### Example

The hands of a clock at about ten past two make an obtuse angle — roughly $120°$, which is between $90°$ and $180°$.

### Watch out

- Angles on a straight line add to $180°$ — an obtuse angle is less than that.
- Do not call $90°$ obtuse; that size is a **right angle**.
