### What this is about

A **linear [equation](../glossary/equation.md)** has the unknown to power 1 only — no $x^2$. Solving means finding the value of $x$ that makes both sides equal.

Think of an equation as a **balanced scale**. Whatever you do to one side, you must do to the other to keep balance. To undo “+ 5”, subtract 5 from both sides. To undo “× 3”, divide both sides by 3.

Harder equations need several steps: expand brackets, collect $x$ terms on one side and numbers on the other, then divide. The same rules work for word problems once you have written the equation.

Linear equations model “fixed amount plus so much per item”, ages, perimeters, and any situation with a steady rate.

### Words to know

- **Linear equation** — unknown to power 1, e.g. $3x - 4 = 11$.
- **Solution** — the value of the unknown that makes the equation true.
- **Balance method** — do the same operation to both sides.
- **Collect like terms** — gather all $x$ terms and all numbers separately.
- **Check** — substitute your answer back into the original equation.

### Worked example 1

Solve $4x + 7 = 31$.

Subtract 7: $4x = 24$. Divide by 4: $x = 6$.

Check: $4(6) + 7 = 31$ ✓

### Worked example 2

Solve $5(x - 2) = 3x + 6$.

Expand: $5x - 10 = 3x + 6$. Subtract $3x$: $2x - 10 = 6$. Add 10: $2x = 16$. $x = 8$.

Check: left $5(6) = 30$, right $3(8)+6 = 30$ ✓

### Tips for practice

- Expand brackets **before** moving terms across the equals sign.
- When $x$ appears on both sides, move **all** $x$ terms to one side.
- Do not divide only one term — divide the **whole** side after isolating $x$.
- Always check with substitution — sign errors show up quickly.
