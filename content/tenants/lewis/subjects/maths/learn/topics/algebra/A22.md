### What this is about

An **inequality** tells you a range of values that work, not just one answer. Instead of $x = 5$, you might see $x > 5$ (“$x$ is greater than 5”) or $x \le 3$ (“$x$ is less than or equal to 3”). The symbols $<$, $>$, $\le$, and $\ge$ compare two sides.

**Linear inequalities** look like linear equations but keep the inequality sign. You solve them almost the same way: add, subtract, multiply, or divide both sides to isolate $x$. **Watch out:** if you multiply or divide by a **negative** number, you must **flip** the inequality sign — e.g. $-2x > 8$ becomes $x < -4$.

Answers are often shown on a **number line** (open circle for strict $<$ or $>$, closed dot for $\le$ or $\ge$). Inequalities model “at least this many”, “under a speed limit”, or “scores that pass a test”.

### Words to know

- **Inequality** — a comparison with $<$, $>$, $\le$, or $\ge$ instead of $=$.
- **Strict inequality** — $<$ or $>$; the boundary value itself may not count.
- **Integer solutions** — whole-number values that fit; list them carefully when asked.
- **Number line** — a picture of values along a line; shading shows all solutions.
- **Flip the sign** — required when multiplying or dividing both sides by a negative.

### Worked example 1

Solve $3x - 4 < 11$.

Add 4: $3x < 15$. Divide by 3: $x < 5$.

**Answer:** any value of $x$ less than 5 (not including 5 if the question asks for strict $<$).

### Worked example 2

Solve $-2x + 6 \ge 14$.

Subtract 6: $-2x \ge 8$. Divide by $-2$ and **flip** the sign: $x \le -4$.

Check with $x = -4$: $-2(-4) + 6 = 14$ ✓ (boundary allowed because of $\ge$).

### Tips for practice

- Treat the inequality like an equation until you multiply or divide by a negative — then flip.
- When listing **integers**, read whether the boundary is included ($\le$, $\ge$) or not ($<$, $>$).
- Test a value in your head: pick an $x$ inside your answer and see if the original inequality is true.
- Do not mix up “greater than” with the wide end of the symbol — the open mouth points to the **larger** side.
