### What this is about

Real problems rarely arrive as “solve $3x + 5 = 20$”. You first **translate** the story into algebra. Pick a letter for the unknown — “let $x$ be the number of tickets” — then build an [expression](../glossary/expression.md) or [equation](../glossary/equation.md) from the words.

“Five more than a number” becomes $x + 5$. “Three times a number, then add 2” becomes $3x + 2$. “Perimeter of a rectangle” might be $2w + 2(w + 4)$ if the length is 4 cm more than the width $w$. When the story says **equals** or **is**, you write an equation with $=$.

Getting the algebra right is half the battle. Once the equation is written, you use the same solving steps as in linear equations. Practising formation makes word problems feel less mysterious — you are just naming a quantity and writing what the story tells you.

### Words to know

- **Unknown** — what you do not know yet; often called $x$, $n$, or $w$.
- **Expression** — algebra without an equals sign, e.g. $2m + 3$.
- **Equation** — two expressions linked by $=$, e.g. $2m + 3 = 17$.
- **Perimeter** — distance around a shape; add all side lengths.
- **Fixed fee plus rate** — a starting cost plus so much per item or mile.

### Worked example 1

Mia has some stickers. After buying 6 more she has 19. Write and solve an equation for how many she had at the start.

Let $s$ be stickers at the start. Story: $s + 6 = 19$.

Subtract 6: $s = 13$.

**Answer:** Mia had 13 stickers.

### Worked example 2

A phone plan costs £8 per month plus a one-off £25 setup fee. The first bill total is £57. Write and solve an equation for the number of months, $m$.

Total = setup + monthly charge: $25 + 8m = 57$.

Subtract 25: $8m = 32$. Divide by 8: $m = 4$.

**Answer:** 4 months on the bill (including the setup month as written).

### Tips for practice

- Read the question twice; underline **equals**, **total**, **each**, **more than**, **less than**.
- Name your letter in a short note (“$x$ = age now”) so you do not forget mid-solve.
- For geometry, draw a quick sketch and label sides before writing algebra.
- Check the answer in the story — does a negative length or half a person make sense?
