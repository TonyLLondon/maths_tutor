### What this is about

A **[formula](../glossary/formula.md)** is a rule written with letters — a ready-made [equation](../glossary/equation.md) linking quantities. The perimeter of a rectangle might be $P = 2l + 2w$. If you know length and width, you **substitute** and calculate $P$.

**Rearranging** (changing the subject) means **isolating one letter** on one side so you can find it when the others are known. If $v = u + at$ and you need $t$, you undo the steps around $t$ until $t$ stands alone.

Formulae appear everywhere: speed, area, temperature conversion, and costs. Rearranging is the algebra version of “working backwards” through a function machine — same inverse operations, but with letters.

### Words to know

- **[Formula](../glossary/formula.md)** — a rule linking variables, often with $=$.
- **Subject of the formula** — the letter alone on one side (e.g. $t$ in $t = …$).
- **Rearrange** — rewrite so a different letter is the subject.
- **[Substitute](../glossary/substitute.md)** — plug known numbers into a formula.
- **Inverse operation** — undo addition, multiplication, etc., in reverse order.

### Worked example 1

The cost of hiring a bike is $C = 8 + 3h$, where $h$ is hours. Find $C$ for $h = 5$.

$C = 8 + 3(5) = 8 + 15 = 23$.

**Answer:** £23 (or 23 in whatever units the problem uses).

### Worked example 2

Make $w$ the subject of $P = 2l + 2w$.

Subtract $2l$ from both sides: $P - 2l = 2w$.

Divide both sides by 2: $w = \dfrac{P - 2l}{2}$.

**Answer:** $w = \dfrac{P - 2l}{2}$.

### Tips for practice

- When rearranging, do the same operation to **both sides** — keep the formula balanced.
- Undo “last applied” operations first when unwinding nested steps.
- Treat $2w$ as “$2 × w$” — divide by 2, not “move the 2” without thinking.
- Always say what the subject is before you start rearranging.
