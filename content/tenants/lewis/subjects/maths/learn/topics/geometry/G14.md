### What this is about

Maths about shapes needs the **right units** and the **right words**. Length uses centimetres and metres. [Area](../../glossary/area.md) measures space **inside** a flat shape and uses square units like cm². [Perimeter](../../glossary/perimeter.md) measures the **distance around** the edge and uses ordinary length units like cm.

You change between units when a question mixes them: 1 m = 100 cm, and 1 m² = 10 000 cm² (because both length and width get multiplied by 100).

This topic also practises [area](../../glossary/area.md) and [perimeter](../../glossary/perimeter.md) of rectangles and squares, and simple compound shapes made from two rectangles. Getting units wrong is one of the most common mistakes—always write cm, m, cm², or m² in your answer.

Real life uses these ideas for fencing a garden, buying carpet, or working out how much paint covers a wall.

### Words to know

- **[Area](../../glossary/area.md)** — space inside a 2D shape; units like cm² or m².
- **[Perimeter](../../glossary/perimeter.md)** — total distance around a shape; units like cm or m.
- **Length and width** — two sides of a rectangle (sometimes called breadth).
- **[Formula](../../glossary/formula.md)** — a rule written with letters, e.g. area = length × width.
- **Square unit** — a 1 cm × 1 cm tile; area counts how many tiles fit.
- **Compound shape** — made from two or more simple shapes joined together.
- **Conversion** — changing from one unit to another (cm to m, or cm² to m²).
- **[Rounding](../../glossary/rounding.md)** — giving a sensible approximate value when needed.

### Worked example 1

**Task:** A rectangle is 8 m long and 5 m wide. Find its perimeter and its area.

**Steps:**

1. **Perimeter:** add all four sides. Opposite sides match: $2 \times 8 + 2 \times 5 = 16 + 10 = 26$ m.
2. **Area:** multiply length × width: $8 \times 5 = 40$ m².
3. Write answers with units: **26 m** and **40 m²**.

Perimeter is one number in metres; area is in square metres—they are not interchangeable.

### Worked example 2

**Task:** An L-shape is made from a 6 cm × 4 cm rectangle and a 3 cm × 2 cm rectangle with no overlap. Find the total area.

**Steps:**

1. Area of the larger piece: $6 \times 4 = 24$ cm².
2. Area of the smaller piece: $3 \times 2 = 6$ cm².
3. Add them: $24 + 6 = 30$ cm² total.

For area of compound shapes, split, find each part, then add (or subtract a missing bite). Perimeter needs you to walk **only the outside** edge once.

### Tips for practice

- Never write cm² for perimeter or cm for area—check every final line.
- For perimeter of an L-shape, trace the outer boundary; do not add the shared internal edge twice.
- When converting m² to cm², multiply by 10 000, not by 100.
- Rectangle area is **not** length + width; it is length × width.
- If a square has area 49 cm², each side is $\sqrt{49} = 7$ cm—square units and side length are linked.
