### What this is about

A **translation** slides a shape straight across the page. It does **not** turn, flip, or stretch. Every point moves the **same** distance in the **same** direction.

You describe the move with a **column vector** $\binom{x}{y}$. The top number is horizontal: **positive** means right, **negative** means left. The bottom number is vertical: **positive** means up, **negative** means down.

On a [coordinate](../../glossary/coordinates.md) grid, translating a point adds the vector to its coordinates: $(a, b) + \binom{p}{q} = (a + p, b + q)$. Shapes keep the same size and orientation; only the position changes.

Translations appear in games (moving a character), maps (shifting a route), and tiling patterns.

### Words to know

- **Translation** — slide without rotate or reflect.
- **Column vector** — $\binom{x}{y}$ written top then bottom.
- **Image** — where the point or shape lands after the move.
- **Object** — the starting point or shape before the move.
- **[Coordinates](../../glossary/coordinates.md)** — $(x, y)$ position on a grid.
- **Horizontal component** — top number in the vector.
- **Vertical component** — bottom number in the vector.
- **Zero vector** — $\binom{0}{0}$; nothing moves.

### Worked example 1

**Task:** Point $P$ is at $(3, -1)$. It is translated by $\binom{-4}{5}$. Find the image point.

**Steps:**

1. Add to $x$: $3 + (-4) = -1$.
2. Add to $y$: $-1 + 5 = 4$.
3. Image is **$(-1, 4)$**.

Same rule works for every corner of a shape.

### Worked example 2

**Task:** Point $A$ is at $(2, 7)$ and its image is $(2, 1)$. Find the translation vector.

**Steps:**

1. Horizontal change: $2 - 2 = 0$ (no sideways move).
2. Vertical change: $1 - 7 = -6$ (6 down).
3. Vector is **$\binom{0}{-6}$**.

You can also think “what do I add to the start to reach the finish?”

### Tips for practice

- Draw a small arrow on squared paper: right/up match positive components.
- When describing in words, turn “3 left and 2 up” into $\binom{-3}{2}$.
- Every vertex of a shape uses the **same** vector in one translation.
- Do not confuse translation with reflection—the shape does not mirror.
- Check signs: “down” means negative $y$; “left” means negative $x$.
