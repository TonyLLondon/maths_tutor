### What this is about

Geometry often starts with **measuring**. You use a **ruler** (or tape) for lengths and a **protractor** for angles. Lengths need **units**—millimetres, centimetres, metres—and should be read carefully from the zero mark unless you are measuring between two marks.

Angles describe how much one line turns away from another. A [right angle](../../glossary/right-angle.md) is exactly 90°. Smaller turns are [acute angles](../../glossary/acute-angle.md); larger ones up to 180° are [obtuse angles](../../glossary/obtuse-angle.md). Bigger than 180° but less than 360° is a reflex angle.

You also meet **bearings**: angles measured **clockwise from North**, written with three digits (e.g. 045°). That is different from an angle of elevation (looking up from the horizontal).

Accurate measuring matters for maps, DIY, sports fields, and checking that corners are square.

### Words to know

- **Line segment** — the straight part between two points.
- **Protractor** — tool for measuring angles in degrees.
- **[Right angle](../../glossary/right-angle.md)** — 90°, like the corner of a book.
- **[Acute angle](../../glossary/acute-angle.md)** — less than 90°.
- **[Obtuse angle](../../glossary/obtuse-angle.md)** — between 90° and 180°.
- **Reflex angle** — between 180° and 360°.
- **[Bearing](../../glossary/bearing.md)** — direction clockwise from North, e.g. 120°.
- **Millimetre (mm)** — there are 10 mm in 1 cm.
- **Scale** — on a map, a distance on paper stands for a real distance.

### Worked example 1

**Task:** A line starts at the 2 cm mark on a ruler and ends at the 9.4 cm mark. Find its length to the nearest millimetre.

**Steps:**

1. Subtract the start from the end: $9.4 - 2 = 7.4$ cm.
2. Convert to mm: $7.4 \times 10 = 74$ mm.
3. To the nearest mm it is already **74 mm** (or **7.4 cm**).

Always subtract when you did not start at zero.

### Worked example 2

**Task:** On a map, 1 cm represents 500 m. A path measures 6.2 cm on the map. Find the real distance in km.

**Steps:**

1. Real distance in metres: $6.2 \times 500 = 3100$ m.
2. Change to km: $3100 \div 1000 = 3.1$ km.

For bearings, draw North as an upward arrow, place the angle at your position, and measure clockwise.

### Tips for practice

- Line up the protractor centre exactly on the **vertex** (corner of the angle).
- Protractors have two scales—use the one that starts from 0 on the ray you aligned with the baseline.
- Saying a length is “about 8” without cm or mm is incomplete.
- Angles in a triangle should add to 180°—if your three measurements do not, remeasure.
- Bearings use three figures: 60° is written **060°** so digits are not dropped on maps.
