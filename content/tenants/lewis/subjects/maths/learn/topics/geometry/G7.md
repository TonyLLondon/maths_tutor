## What this is about

Shapes can **move** on a grid or in a plane without changing who they are — or they can grow and shrink while keeping the same shape. The four main moves are **translation** (slide), **rotation** (turn), **reflection** (flip), and **enlargement** (scale up or down).

Translation, rotation, and reflection give an image **congruent** to the original — same size and shape. Enlargement changes size unless the [scale factor](../../glossary/scale-factor.md) is $1$; the image is **similar** (same shape, different size). A negative scale factor flips the shape across the centre as well as resizing.

Video games, maps, and logos all use these moves. Learning the words helps you describe animations and spot when two pictures are really the same tile repeated.

## Words to know

- **Translation** — slide every point the same distance and direction.
- **Rotation** — turn about a fixed **centre** through a given angle.
- **Reflection** — flip across a **mirror line**.
- **Enlargement** — stretch from a **centre of enlargement** using a [scale factor](../../glossary/scale-factor.md).
- **Image** — the shape after the transformation.
- **Object** — the original shape before the move.
- **Congruent image** — same size and shape as the object.
- **Similar image** — same shape, angles match; side lengths scale.
- **Column vector** — $\begin{pmatrix} a \\ b \end{pmatrix}$ means $a$ right/left and $b$ up/down in coordinate grids.
- **[Coordinates](../../glossary/coordinates.md)** — ordered pair $(x, y)$ locating a point.

## Worked example 1

**Task:** A point starts at $(2, 5)$ and is translated by $\begin{pmatrix} 4 \\ -3 \end{pmatrix}$. Find the image.

**Steps:**

1. Add $4$ to the $x$-coordinate: $2 + 4 = 6$.
2. Add $-3$ to the $y$-coordinate: $5 + (-3) = 2$.

**Answer:** Image point $(6, 2)$.

## Worked example 2

**Task:** A triangle is enlarged from centre $O$ with scale factor $3$. A side on the object is $2\,\text{cm}$. Find the matching side on the image.

**Steps:**

1. Enlargement multiplies every length by the scale factor.
2. $2 \times 3 = 6$.

**Answer:** $6\,\text{cm}$ on the image.

## Tips for practice

- Trace the object on paper and physically slide, turn, or flip to check your answer.
- For rotation, note **clockwise vs anticlockwise** and the centre point — they change everything.
- Scale factor $\frac{1}{2}$ makes the shape **half** as big; factor $2$ doubles lengths.
- Negative scale factor? The image is on the **opposite** side of the centre and upside down compared with a positive factor.
- Translation never rotates — if the picture turned, it was not a pure translation.
