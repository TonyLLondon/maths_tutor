## What this is about

Some geometry is about **drawing accurately**, not guessing. With a ruler and compasses you can split a line exactly in half, bisect an angle, and find paths made by special rules.

A **locus** (plural *loci*) is the set of all points that obey one rule — for example, every point $4\,\text{cm}$ from a fixed peg, or every point the same distance from two towns. Those rules often make circles, straight lines, or arcs.

This skill shows up in real design: fair meeting points, safety zones around equipment, and clean diagrams in engineering sketches. It also trains you to think “what points satisfy this condition?”

## Words to know

- **Construction** — a drawing made with ruler and compasses, step by step.
- **Compasses** — the tool that draws circles and arcs (not a protractor).
- **Midpoint** — the point exactly halfway along a segment.
- **Perpendicular bisector** — a line through the midpoint, at $90°$ to the segment.
- **Angle bisector** — a line that cuts an angle into two equal parts.
- **Locus** — all points that follow one geometric rule.
- **Radius** — distance from the centre of a circle to the edge (see [radius](../../glossary/radius.md)).
- **Arc** — part of a circle’s edge.
- **Equidistant** — the same distance from two or more places.

## Worked example 1

**Task:** A line segment is $12\,\text{cm}$ long. Find the midpoint and the distance from the midpoint to each end.

**Steps:**

1. The midpoint splits the segment into two **equal** parts.
2. Half of $12\,\text{cm}$ is $6\,\text{cm}$.
3. Each end is $6\,\text{cm}$ from the midpoint.

**Answer:** Midpoint is at $6\,\text{cm}$ from either end; distance $6\,\text{cm}$ each way.

## Worked example 2

**Task:** A dog is tied to a post with a $5\,\text{m}$ lead in a flat field. Describe the locus of places the dog can reach.

**Steps:**

1. The rule is: distance from the post is **at most** $5\,\text{m}$ (the lead can be slack or tight).
2. The **tightest** path is a circle of [radius](../../glossary/radius.md) $5\,\text{m}$ around the post.
3. Inside that circle, the dog can stand anywhere; on the circle, the lead is fully stretched.

**Answer:** A filled disc (circle and its inside) of radius $5\,\text{m}$ centred on the post.

## Tips for practice

- Keep your compasses tight so arcs do not wobble — small errors grow fast.
- For a perpendicular bisector, use two equal arcs from each end of the segment; join where they cross.
- Remember: **perpendicular bisector** = same distance from **both ends** of a segment.
- A locus question often starts with “all points such that…” — translate that into a distance or angle rule.
- Label points clearly (A, B, C) so you can follow your own diagram.
