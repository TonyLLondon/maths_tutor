## What this is about

Angles measure **how much turn** there is at a corner or between two lines. Once you know a few facts, you can find missing angles without measuring every time.

You work with angles on a straight line ($180°$), around a point ($360°$), inside triangles ($180°$ total), and inside quadrilaterals ($360°$ total). You also use facts for **[parallel lines](../../glossary/parallel-lines.md)** cut by a **[transversal](../../glossary/transversal.md)** — [corresponding](../../glossary/corresponding-angles.md), [alternate](../../glossary/alternate-angles.md), and [co-interior](../../glossary/co-interior-angles.md) angles.

These rules are the toolkit for proof-style reasoning later. They also explain why roof trusses and ladder angles behave predictably.

Start with what you can see: a straight line, a point where several rays meet, or a triangle with two angles given. Write one fact, find one new angle, then repeat until everything you need is filled in.

## Words to know

- **Degree ($°$)** — unit for measuring angles; a full turn is $360°$.
- **[Right angle](../../glossary/right-angle.md)** — $90°$; a quarter turn.
- **[Acute angle](../../glossary/acute-angle.md)** — less than $90°$.
- **[Obtuse angle](../../glossary/obtuse-angle.md)** — more than $90°$ but less than $180°$.
- **Vertically opposite angles** — equal angles made where two lines cross.
- **Straight line angles** — angles on one line add to $180°$.
- **Angles at a point** — angles around one point add to $360°$.
- **[Corresponding angles](../../glossary/corresponding-angles.md)** — same position at each intersection; equal when lines are parallel.
- **[Alternate angles](../../glossary/alternate-angles.md)** — “Z” pattern; equal when lines are parallel.
- **[Co-interior angles](../../glossary/co-interior-angles.md)** — “C” pattern; add to $180°$ when lines are parallel.

## Worked example 1

**Task:** Two angles lie on a straight line. One is $47°$. Find the other.

**Steps:**

1. Angles on a straight line sum to $180°$.
2. Subtract: $180 - 47 = 133$.

**Answer:** $133°$.

## Worked example 2

**Task:** Three angles meet at a point: $110°$, $95°$, and $a°$. Find $a$.

**Steps:**

1. Angles around a point sum to $360°$.
2. Known total: $110 + 95 = 205$.
3. $a = 360 - 205 = 155$.

**Answer:** $a = 155°$.

## Tips for practice

- Mark equal angles with the same letter or tick marks on your sketch.
- For parallel lines, trace the “F”, “Z”, and “C” shapes with your finger to spot which rule fits.
- Triangle missing angle? Subtract the two given angles from $180°$.
- Do not mix up **alternate** (equal) with **co-interior** (add to $180°$).
- If a diagram looks busy, extend one line lightly so you can see a straight line or a full turn at a point.
