## What this is about

Before you can solve harder shape problems, you need a shared language for them. This topic is about **naming 2D shapes**, counting sides and corners, and describing how lines behave.

You learn words like triangle, quadrilateral, pentagon, and hexagon. You also meet **parallel** lines (they never meet) and **perpendicular** lines (they meet at a [right angle](../../glossary/right-angle.md)). Triangles can be scalene, isosceles, or equilateral depending on their sides.

Knowing this vocabulary helps everywhere — from reading diagrams in science to spotting symmetry in art. When a question says “quadrilateral”, you know to think “four-sided shape” straight away.

## Words to know

- **Side** — a straight edge of a polygon.
- **Vertex (vertices)** — a corner where sides meet.
- **Triangle** — a three-sided polygon.
- **Quadrilateral** — any four-sided shape (square, rectangle, rhombus, and more).
- **[Parallel lines](../../glossary/parallel-lines.md)** — lines in the same direction that stay the same distance apart and never cross.
- **Perpendicular lines** — lines that meet at $90°$.
- **Scalene triangle** — no equal sides.
- **Isosceles triangle** — at least two equal sides.
- **Equilateral triangle** — three equal sides (and three equal angles).
- **Regular polygon** — all sides and all angles equal (e.g. a regular hexagon).

## Worked example 1

**Task:** A shape has four equal sides and four [right angles](../../glossary/right-angle.md). Name the shape and find each angle.

**Steps:**

1. Four sides means it is a **quadrilateral**.
2. Four equal sides and four right angles describe a **square**.
3. Every angle in a square is $90°$.

**Answer:** Square; each angle is $90°$.

## Worked example 2

**Task:** Two lines run east–west and never meet. A third line runs north–south and crosses both. Describe the east–west pair and the north–south line.

**Steps:**

1. Lines that never meet are **[parallel](../../glossary/parallel-lines.md)** — the two east–west lines are parallel.
2. North–south meets east–west at $90°$, so the north–south line is **perpendicular** to each east–west line.

**Answer:** The horizontal pair is parallel; the vertical line is perpendicular to them.

## Tips for practice

- Say the shape name out loud when you count sides — it helps memory.
- A square is a special rectangle, but **not** every rectangle is a square (sides must all match).
- “Vertices” just means corners — count them the same way you count sides on a closed shape.
- If a diagram shows little square symbols at corners, those mark right angles.
- Use a ruler when a question asks you to draw — neat lines make checking easier.
