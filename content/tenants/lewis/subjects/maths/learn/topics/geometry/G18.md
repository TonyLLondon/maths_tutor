### What this is about

A full circle is 360° all the way round. Often you only need **part** of the circle—like a slice of pizza or the curved edge of a garden path.

An **arc** is the **curved part** of the edge between two points on the circle. A **sector** is the **wedge-shaped piece** inside, bounded by two radii and an arc. A semicircle is a 180° sector; a quarter-circle is 90°.

Because a sector is a **fraction** of the full circle, its arc length and area are the same fraction of the whole [circumference](../../glossary/circumference.md) and whole [area](../../glossary/area.md). The angle at the centre (in degrees) out of 360 tells you that fraction.

This shows up in fan shapes, gear teeth, and questions that combine circles with straight edges.

### Words to know

- **Arc** — curved section of the circle’s edge.
- **Sector** — “slice” from centre out to the arc.
- **Central angle** — angle at the centre between the two radii ($\theta$ in degrees).
- **[Radius](../../glossary/radius.md)** — needed for both arc length and sector area.
- **Fraction of a circle** — e.g. 90° is $\frac{90}{360} = \frac{1}{4}$.
- **[Circumference](../../glossary/circumference.md)** — full distance round; arc is a part of this.
- **[Area](../../glossary/area.md)** — full circle $\pi r^2$; sector area is a part of this.
- **Chord** — straight line joining two points on the circle (not the same as the arc).

### Worked example 1

**Task:** A sector has radius 6 cm and angle 120°. Find the arc length and sector area. Use $\pi \approx 3.14$.

**Steps:**

1. Fraction of circle: $\frac{120}{360} = \frac{1}{3}$.
2. Full circumference: $2 \times 3.14 \times 6 = 37.68$ cm. Arc: $\frac{1}{3} \times 37.68 = 12.56$ cm.
3. Full area: $3.14 \times 6^2 = 113.04$ cm². Sector: $\frac{1}{3} \times 113.04 = 37.68$ cm².

Same fraction applies to both arc and area.

### Worked example 2

**Task:** A quarter-circle lawn has radius 8 m. Find the length of the curved edge only (the arc), and the area of the whole quarter-circle. Use $\pi \approx 3.14$.

**Steps:**

1. Quarter of circumference: $\frac{1}{4} \times 2 \times 3.14 \times 8 = 12.56$ m arc.
2. Quarter of area: $\frac{1}{4} \times 3.14 \times 8^2 = 50.24$ m².

If the question wanted the **perimeter** of the quarter shape including the two straight radii, you would add $8 + 8 + 12.56$.

### Tips for practice

- Always write the fraction as $\theta \div 360$ before multiplying.
- Arc length uses $2\pi r$; sector area uses $\pi r^2$—same fraction, different “whole”.
- Read carefully: **arc only** vs **perimeter of the sector** (arc plus two radii).
- Larger angle → larger slice → longer arc and bigger area.
- Check that the angle given is the **centre** angle, not something at the edge of a diagram.
