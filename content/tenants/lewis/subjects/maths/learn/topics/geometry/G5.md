## What this is about

Two shapes are **congruent** when one is an exact copy of the other — same size, same shape, all matching parts line up. For triangles, you do not need to check every side and angle one by one; there are **shortcut rules** that are enough to prove congruence.

The main rules are **SSS** (three sides), **SAS** (two sides and the angle between them), **ASA** (two angles and a matching side), and **RHS** for right-angled triangles (right angle, hypotenuse, and one other side). There is also **SSA**, which is **not** reliable on its own.

Congruence explains why two triangular roof brackets with the same measurements are interchangeable, and why a template shape can be traced to cut identical pieces.

## Words to know

- **Congruent** — identical in size and shape; one fits exactly on the other.
- **Corresponding parts** — matching sides and angles in the same order on two triangles.
- **SSS** — three pairs of equal sides.
- **SAS** — two sides and the **included** angle (the angle between those sides).
- **ASA** — two angles and a side that connects them (included side) or a matching side in the correct position.
- **RHS** — right angle, hypotenuse, and one other side equal (right-angled triangles only).
- **Hypotenuse** — longest side, opposite the right angle.
- **Included angle** — the angle sandwiched between two named sides.
- **Similar vs congruent** — similar can be bigger or smaller; congruent cannot.

## Worked example 1

**Task:** Triangle P has sides $5\,\text{cm}$, $7\,\text{cm}$, and $9\,\text{cm}$. Triangle Q has sides $5\,\text{cm}$, $7\,\text{cm}$, and $9\,\text{cm}$. Which rule shows they are congruent?

**Steps:**

1. All three sides of P match all three sides of Q.
2. That is the **SSS** rule.

**Answer:** SSS proves the triangles are congruent.

## Worked example 2

**Task:** Two triangles each have a $40°$ angle between sides of $6\,\text{cm}$ and $8\,\text{cm}$. Are they necessarily congruent?

**Steps:**

1. Two sides and the **included** angle match: $6\,\text{cm}$, $8\,\text{cm}$, and $40°$ between them.
2. That is **SAS**, which is a valid congruence proof.

**Answer:** Yes — SAS applies, so the triangles are congruent.

## Tips for practice

- Same angles alone (**AAA**) does **not** prove congruence — one triangle could be a scaled copy.
- For SAS, the angle must sit **between** the two given sides.
- Mark equal sides with tick marks and equal angles with arcs on your diagram.
- RHS only works when there is a [right angle](../../glossary/right-angle.md) in both triangles.
- If a question lists SSA as a option, treat it with caution — it is not a standard valid rule.
