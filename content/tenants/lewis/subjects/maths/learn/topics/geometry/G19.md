### What this is about

Two shapes can look the same but be different sizes—like a small photo and a big print of the same picture. They are **similar** when matching angles are equal and matching sides are in the **same ratio**.

The **[scale factor](../../glossary/scale-factor.md)** tells you how many times longer each side becomes when you go from the smaller shape to the larger. If the scale factor is 3, every matching side triples. Angles do **not** change when you enlarge or shrink.

You use similarity to find a missing side on a bigger or smaller copy, or to work out how much a model grew. Maps and scale drawings use the same idea.

Similar is **not** the same as “same size”. Congruent means same size and shape; similar means same shape, possibly different size.

### Words to know

- **Similar shapes** — equal angles, sides in proportion.
- **[Scale factor](../../glossary/scale-factor.md)** — multiplier for lengths (small → large).
- **Corresponding sides** — sides that match in the same position on each shape.
- **Corresponding angles** — equal angles in matching corners.
- **Enlargement** — scale factor greater than 1.
- **Reduction** — scale factor between 0 and 1 (or think “divide by something”).
- **Ratio** — comparison of two lengths, e.g. 2 : 5.
- **Congruent** — identical in size and shape (stricter than similar).

### Worked example 1

**Task:** Two similar triangles have a pair of matching sides 7 cm and 21 cm. The smaller triangle’s other matching side is 5 cm. Find that side on the larger triangle.

**Steps:**

1. Scale factor (small to large): $21 \div 7 = 3$.
2. Multiply the other side: $5 \times 3 = 15$ cm on the larger triangle.

Always match sides that belong together.

### Worked example 2

**Task:** Two similar rectangles have widths 4 cm and 10 cm. The smaller rectangle’s length is 6 cm. Find the larger length. Are all rectangles similar to each other?

**Steps:**

1. Scale factor: $10 \div 4 = 2.5$.
2. Larger length: $6 \times 2.5 = 15$ cm.
3. **Not all rectangles are similar**—a 2 × 8 rectangle and a 4 × 4 rectangle have different shape even though both are rectangles. Angles match but side ratios do not.

All **squares** are similar to each other, because every angle is 90° and sides match in ratio.

### Tips for practice

- Label corresponding sides with the same colour or mark before you divide.
- Scale factor from large to small is the **reciprocal** of small to large—read which direction the question wants.
- Similar shapes have the same angles—if one angle is 40°, the matching angle on the other is 40°.
- Do not add scale factors; multiply lengths.
- In diagrams, check which shape is the image and which is the object before you pick your factor.
