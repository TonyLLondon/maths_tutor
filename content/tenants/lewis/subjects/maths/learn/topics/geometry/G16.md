### What this is about

You already know how to find the [area](../../glossary/area.md) of a rectangle: length × width. Many other flat shapes use that idea.

A **triangle** is half of a matching rectangle with the same base and height, so area = $\frac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{height}$. The **height** must be **perpendicular** (at a right angle) to the base—not always a slanted side.

A **parallelogram** can be cut and rearranged into a rectangle with the same base and height, so its area is **base × height**. A **trapezium** has one pair of parallel sides; its area uses the average of those two parallel lengths times the height.

This topic also introduces **volume**: space inside a 3D object, measured in cm³ or m³. For prisms and cuboids you multiply areas of the end face by how long the solid is.

### Words to know

- **[Area](../../glossary/area.md)** — space inside a 2D shape (cm²).
- **Base** — the side you choose to measure from (often drawn at the bottom).
- **Height (perpendicular height)** — straight up from the base to the opposite side, at 90°.
- **Parallelogram** — opposite sides parallel, like a pushed-over rectangle.
- **Trapezium** — one pair of parallel sides (the “parallel sides” in the formula).
- **[Volume](../../glossary/volume.md)** — space inside a 3D solid (cm³).
- **Cross-section** — the face you see if you slice a prism straight through.
- **Prism** — solid with the same cross-section all along its length.
- **[Formula](../../glossary/formula.md)** — e.g. volume of cuboid = length × width × height.

### Worked example 1

**Task:** Find the area of a triangle with base 10 cm and perpendicular height 6 cm.

**Steps:**

1. Use area = $\frac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{height}$.
2. $\frac{1}{2} \times 10 \times 6 = 30$ cm².

If the height were 6 cm on a slant, you would need the perpendicular height instead.

### Worked example 2

**Task:** A trapezium has parallel sides 5 cm and 11 cm, and height 4 cm. Find its area. Then a prism has that trapezium as its cross-section and length 8 cm. Find its volume.

**Steps:**

1. Trapezium area = $\frac{1}{2} \times (5 + 11) \times 4 = \frac{1}{2} \times 16 \times 4 = 32$ cm².
2. Volume = cross-sectional area × length = $32 \times 8 = 256$ cm³.

### Tips for practice

- Draw the perpendicular height as a dashed line at 90° to the base—it is often **not** a side of the triangle.
- Doubling the base doubles the triangle’s area if the height stays the same.
- For parallelograms, use vertical height, not the slanted side length.
- Volume units are **cubic** (cm³); area units are **square** (cm²)—do not mix them.
- Check whether a question wants flat area or 3D volume before you start multiplying.
