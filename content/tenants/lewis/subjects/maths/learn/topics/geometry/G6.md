## What this is about

This topic joins the angle facts you know into **multi-step puzzles**. You might use a triangle sum, then a straight line, then [parallel-line](../../glossary/parallel-lines.md) rules in one problem. You also reason about **isosceles** triangles, where equal sides mean equal base angles, and about whether side lengths can even form a triangle.

The goal is not just to get a number — it is to **explain a path**: which fact you used first, what that unlocked next, and why the answer makes sense. That is how geometry becomes a story instead of a guess.

Engineers and architects chain these ideas when they work out loads on frames or check that a design closes properly. On paper, you are training that same careful chain.

## Words to know

- **Base angles** — in an isosceles triangle, the two angles opposite the equal sides.
- **Apex angle** — the angle between the two equal sides in an isosceles triangle.
- **Isosceles triangle** — at least two equal sides; base angles are equal.
- **Equilateral triangle** — three $60°$ angles.
- **[Transversal](../../glossary/transversal.md)** — a line crossing two or more other lines.
- **[Alternate angles](../../glossary/alternate-angles.md)** / **[co-interior angles](../../glossary/co-interior-angles.md)** — parallel-line tools from G3.
- **Vertically opposite angles** — equal pairs at a crossing.
- **Triangle inequality** — the longest side must be shorter than the other two sides added together.
- **Exterior angle** — angle formed by extending one side of a triangle.

## Worked example 1

**Task:** An isosceles triangle has two equal angles of $48°$. Find the third angle.

**Steps:**

1. Angles in any triangle sum to $180°$.
2. The two equal angles use $48 + 48 = 96°$.
3. Third angle: $180 - 96 = 84$.

**Answer:** $84°$.

## Worked example 2

**Task:** Two [parallel lines](../../glossary/parallel-lines.md) are cut by a transversal. Co-interior angles are $2x°$ and $3x°$. Find $x$ and each angle.

**Steps:**

1. Co-interior angles add to $180°$: $2x + 3x = 180$.
2. $5x = 180$, so $x = 36$.
3. Angles are $2 \times 36 = 72°$ and $3 \times 36 = 108°$.

**Answer:** $x = 36$; angles $72°$ and $108°$.

## Tips for practice

- Write a one-line reason next to each step (“alternate angles”, “isosceles base angles equal”).
- If you see $2x$ and $3x$, look for a straight line ($180$) or a point ($360$) or co-interior ($180$).
- In isosceles triangles, mark the equal sides first — then you know which angles match.
- For “can these sides make a triangle?”, compare the longest side to the sum of the other two.
- Sketch larger than you think you need; cramped diagrams hide equal angles.
