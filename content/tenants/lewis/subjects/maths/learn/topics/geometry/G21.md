### What this is about

Your calculator gives long decimals for sin, cos, and tan. For special angles—**0°, 30°, 45°, 60°, and 90°**—you can write **exact** answers using fractions and surds (square roots). These values come up again and again, so it is worth learning them by heart.

The angles 30° and 60° often appear in equilateral triangles cut in half. 45° appears when you split a square corner in two. 0° and 90° describe flat along the ground or straight up.

Exact values keep working in algebra and in tests that say “leave in surd form”. They also help you spot when a calculator answer should be something neat like $\frac{1}{2}$ or $\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$.

You still use SOH CAH TOA, but you plug in these known ratios instead of pressing sin on the calculator.

### Words to know

- **Exact value** — fraction or surd, not a rounded decimal.
- **Surd** — square root that stays under the root sign, e.g. $\sqrt{2}$.
- **sin, cos, tan** — ratios in a right-angled triangle (see the G20 guide).
- **30–60–90 triangle** — half of an equilateral triangle; sides in ratio 1 : $\sqrt{3}$ : 2.
- **45–45–90 triangle** — isosceles right triangle; legs equal, hypotenuse $\sqrt{2}$ times a leg.
- **Degree** — measure of angle; 90° is a [right angle](../../glossary/right-angle.md).
- **Quadrant** — for 0° and 90°, think of the angle along horizontal or vertical.

### Worked example 1

**Task:** Write the exact values of $\sin 30°$, $\cos 60°$, and $\tan 45°$.

**Steps:**

1. $\sin 30° = \frac{1}{2}$.
2. $\cos 60° = \frac{1}{2}$ (same number as sin 30°).
3. $\tan 45° = 1$ (both legs equal in a 45°–45°–90° triangle).

Notice how sin 30° and cos 60° match—that pattern is part of the table.

### Worked example 2

**Task:** A right-angled triangle has a 45° angle and hypotenuse 6 cm. Find the exact length of each leg without a calculator decimal.

**Steps:**

1. For 45°, legs are equal. If leg = $x$, then $x^2 + x^2 = 6^2$, so $2x^2 = 36$, $x^2 = 18$.
2. $x = \sqrt{18} = 3\sqrt{2}$ cm each leg.

Or use $\sin 45° = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} = \frac{x}{6}$ to get the same result.

### Tips for practice

- Build a small table for 0°, 30°, 45°, 60°, 90° for sin, cos, and tan—test yourself often.
- $\tan\theta = \frac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta}$ helps when you forget tan.
- At 90°, sin is 1 and cos is 0—tan is not defined there.
- If a question says “exact”, do not write 0.866—write $\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$.
- Pair angles: sin 30° = cos 60°, sin 60° = cos 30°.
