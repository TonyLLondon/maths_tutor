### What this is about

[Fractions](../glossary/fraction.md), [decimals](../glossary/decimal.md), and [percentages](../glossary/percentage.md) are different ways to write the **same amount**. $\dfrac{1}{2} = 0.5 = 50\%$.

To turn a fraction into a decimal, divide top by bottom (or use known facts: $\dfrac{1}{4} = 0.25$). To turn a decimal into a fraction, use place value: $0.35 = \dfrac{35}{100} = \dfrac{7}{20}$ in simplest form.

Percent means “out of 100”. $0.6 = \dfrac{60}{100} = 60\%$.

Grids of 100 squares help you **see** that 0.35 is 35 out of 100.

Recurring decimals (like $0.333…$ for $\dfrac{1}{3}$) may need a dot notation in books; in practice you often round or leave as a fraction. When a question asks for “simplest form”, cancel common factors — $\dfrac{12}{16} = \dfrac{3}{4}$ before you convert.

**Percentages** fit a 100-square grid: shade 35 squares and you have 35%, 0.35, and $\dfrac{35}{100}$ linked together. For harder fractions like $\dfrac{7}{8}$, division gives 0.875 — three decimal places because eighths step in thousandths.

You use these switches in shops (50% off), sports stats, and measuring.

### Words to know

- **[Fraction](../glossary/fraction.md)** — $\dfrac{\text{part}}{\text{whole}}$.
- **[Decimal](../glossary/decimal.md)** — base-ten with a decimal point.
- **[Percentage](../glossary/percentage.md)** — parts per hundred; symbol %.
- **Simplest form** — fraction with no common factor on top and bottom.
- **Equivalent** — same value, different look ($\dfrac{3}{5} = 0.6$).

### Worked example 1

Write $\dfrac{9}{20}$ as a decimal.

$9 \div 20 = 0.45$.

Or: $\dfrac{9}{20} = \dfrac{45}{100} = 0.45$.

### Worked example 2

Write 0.375 as a fraction in simplest form.

$0.375 = \dfrac{375}{1000}$. Divide top and bottom by 125: $\dfrac{3}{8}$.

Write $\dfrac{3}{4}$ as a percentage: $\dfrac{3}{4} = 0.75 = 75\%$.

Another link: $\dfrac{3}{8} = 0.375$, so as a percentage that is $37.5\%$ (move the decimal two places).

### Tips for practice

- Know the “family”: $\dfrac{1}{2}$, $\dfrac{1}{4}$, $\dfrac{1}{5}$, $\dfrac{1}{10}$ as decimals.
- When simplifying, divide top and bottom by the same number.
- 0.7 and 0.70 are equal — do not let extra zeros confuse ordering.
- Percentage → divide by 100 to get decimal; decimal → multiply by 100 for percent.
- A fraction bar means divide: $\frac{3}{4}$ means $3 \div 4$.
- Two decimal places means hundredths — good link to percentages out of 100.
- When writing a decimal from a fraction, division must finish or repeat — $\frac{1}{3}$ is not 0.3 exactly.
- Money answers often need two decimal places (pence).
