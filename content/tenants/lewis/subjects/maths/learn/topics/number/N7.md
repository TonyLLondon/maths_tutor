### What this is about

Once you know what $2^3$ means, you can **combine** powers with rules called **laws of indices**.

When you **multiply** powers with the same base, **add** the indices: $3^2 \times 3^4 = 3^{2+4} = 3^6$.

When you **divide**, **subtract** indices: $5^6 \div 5^2 = 5^{6-2} = 5^4$.

When you **raise a power to another power**, **multiply** indices: $(a^2)^3 = a^{2 \times 3} = a^6$.

Special cases: any non-zero number to the power 0 is **1** ($4^0 = 1$). A **negative** index means “one over”: $2^{-3} = \dfrac{1}{2^3} = \dfrac{1}{8}$.

These rules work with numbers and with letter bases in algebra.

Think of indices as a shorthand for repeated multiplication. If you expand $3^2 \times 3^4$ as $(3 \times 3) \times (3 \times 3 \times 3 \times 3)$, you have six factors of 3 — that is $3^6$. Dividing cancels matching factors on top and bottom, which is why you subtract indices.

When a question says “write as a single power”, you are usually applying one of these laws, not a calculator job.

### Words to know

- **Index / indices** — the power you raise to.
- **Base** — the repeated factor ($x$ in $x^5$).
- **Simplify** — write as one power or a clear fraction.
- **[Expression](../glossary/expression.md)** — often a mix of letters and powers before it becomes an [equation](../glossary/equation.md).
- **Reciprocal** — flip a fraction; linked to negative indices.

### Worked example 1

Simplify $3^2 \times 3^4$.

Add indices: $3^{2+4} = 3^6$ (which is 729 if you expand).

### Worked example 2

Simplify $y^7 \div y^3$ and work out $2^{-3}$ as a fraction.

$y^7 \div y^3 = y^{7-3} = y^4$.

$2^{-3} = \dfrac{1}{2^3} = \dfrac{1}{8}$.

Also $(b^4)^2 = b^{4 \times 2} = b^8$. And $10^0 = 1$.

### Tips for practice

- Same base required to add/subtract indices — $2^3 \times 3^2$ cannot merge to one power.
- $2^0$ is 1, not 0 — a common slip.
- Negative index: move to denominator as positive power.
- When dividing, top index minus bottom index.
- Write the base unchanged when adding indices — only the power number changes.
- Fraction answers from negative indices should be in simplest form ($\frac{1}{4}$, not $\frac{2}{8}$).
