### What this is about

Sometimes you must leave an answer **exact** — as a [fraction](../glossary/fraction.md) or in terms of **π** (pi), not a rounded decimal from a calculator.

For fractions: find a **common denominator** to add or subtract. Multiply tops and bottoms to multiply. To divide, **multiply by the reciprocal** (flip the second fraction).

For circles, π links the [diameter](../glossary/diameter.md) and [circumference](../glossary/circumference.md). If the [radius](../glossary/radius.md) is $r$ cm, circumference $= 2\pi r$ cm and area $= \pi r^2$ cm². Leaving $6\pi$ cm is exact; 18.85… cm is only an approximation.

Mixed numbers (e.g. $1\frac{1}{4}$) can be turned into improper fractions before calculating.

Subtracting fractions needs enough pieces at the bottom: $\dfrac{2}{3} - \dfrac{1}{6} = \dfrac{4}{6} - \dfrac{1}{6} = \dfrac{1}{2}$. Multiplying does not need a common denominator — multiply straight across, then simplify.

Circle questions often give radius or diameter; diameter is twice the radius. Always use the same unit (cm) in the formula.

### Words to know

- **[Fraction](../glossary/fraction.md)** — part of a whole in $\dfrac{a}{b}$ form.
- **Numerator / denominator** — top and bottom of a fraction.
- **Simplest form** — no common factor on top and bottom.
- **π (pi)** — special constant for circles; never ends or repeats as a decimal.
- **[Circumference](../glossary/circumference.md)** — distance around a circle.
- **[Formula](../glossary/formula.md)** — rule like $C = 2\pi r$.

### Worked example 1

Work out $\dfrac{1}{4} + \dfrac{1}{8}$ in simplest form.

Eighths: $\dfrac{2}{8} + \dfrac{1}{8} = \dfrac{3}{8}$.

### Worked example 2

A circle has radius 5 cm. Give its area exactly.

Area $= \pi r^2 = \pi \times 5^2 = 25\pi$ cm².

For $\dfrac{3}{4} \div \dfrac{1}{2}$: multiply by reciprocal → $\dfrac{3}{4} \times \dfrac{2}{1} = \dfrac{6}{4} = \dfrac{3}{2}$.

### Tips for practice

- Always simplify the final fraction.
- For circle answers, say “in terms of π” unless asked for a decimal.
- When dividing fractions, flip the second fraction and multiply.
- Check addition with a quick estimate (e.g. $\frac{1}{4} + \frac{1}{8}$ should be less than $\frac{1}{2}$).
- For $1\frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{4}$, convert the mixed number to $\frac{3}{2}$ or $\frac{6}{4}$ first.
- Circumference uses $2\pi r$; area uses $\pi r^2$ — do not swap them.
