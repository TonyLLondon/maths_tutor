### What this is about

A **[factor](../glossary/factor.md)** of a number divides it exactly with no remainder. Factors of 12 are 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, and 12. A **[multiple](../glossary/multiple.md)** is what you get when you multiply a number by 1, 2, 3, … — multiples of 8 start 8, 16, 24, …

A **[prime](../glossary/prime.md)** number has exactly two factors: 1 and itself. 2, 11, and 17 are prime. 1 is **not** prime (only one factor). 9 is not prime (3 × 3).

The **[HCF](../glossary/hcf.md)** (highest common factor) is the largest number that is a factor of **both** numbers. The **[LCM](../glossary/lcm.md)** (lowest common multiple) is the smallest number that is a **multiple of both**.

You use these ideas for simplifying fractions, sharing equally, and spotting patterns in numbers.

### Words to know

- **[Factor](../glossary/factor.md)** — divides exactly into a number.
- **[Multiple](../glossary/multiple.md)** — result of ×1, ×2, ×3, … of a number.
- **[Prime](../glossary/prime.md)** — exactly two factors; greater than 1.
- **Composite** — not prime; has more than two factors.
- **[HCF](../glossary/hcf.md)** — biggest shared factor.
- **[LCM](../glossary/lcm.md)** — smallest shared multiple.
- **Product of prime factors** — write a number as primes multiplied (e.g. $45 = 3 \times 3 \times 5$).

### Worked example 1

List all factors of 36.

$1 \times 36$, $2 \times 18$, $3 \times 12$, $4 \times 9$, $6 \times 6$.

**Factors:** 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 9, 12, 18, 36.

### Worked example 2

Find the HCF of 18 and 27 and the LCM of 4 and 6.

Factors of 18: 1, 2, 3, 6, 9, 18. Factors of 27: 1, 3, 9, 27. **HCF = 9**.

Multiples of 4: 4, 8, 12, … Multiples of 6: 6, 12, … **LCM = 12**.

### Tips for practice

- List factor pairs in order so you do not miss one.
- Every even number greater than 2 has 2 as a factor — so it cannot be prime.
- For LCM, the answer is never smaller than the larger of the two numbers (unless one divides the other).
- Prime-check: try dividing by 2, 3, 5, 7… up to the square root idea in your head.
- Venn diagrams with two bubbles can show shared factors for HCF and shared multiples for LCM.
- Product of prime factors: keep splitting until every piece is prime.
