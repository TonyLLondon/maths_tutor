### What this is about

When several operations appear in one line, you need a fixed order so everyone gets the same answer. In the UK this is **[BIDMAS](../glossary/bidmas.md)** (also called BODMAS): **B**rackets first, then **I**ndices (powers), then **D**ivision and **M**ultiplication (left to right), then **A**ddition and **S**ubtraction (left to right).

Brackets group work that must happen first. $(6 + 3)^2$ means “add, then square”: $9^2 = 81$. That is different from $6 + 3^2 = 6 + 9 = 15$.

**Inverse operations** undo each other. Addition undoes subtraction and the other way round. Multiplication undoes division. You use inverses to find missing numbers and to check answers.

Powers (indices) come before multiply/divide. So $5 + 2^2 \times 3 = 5 + 4 \times 3 = 5 + 12 = 17$.

### Words to know

- **[BIDMAS](../glossary/bidmas.md)** — the order: brackets, indices, divide/multiply, add/subtract.
- **Brackets** — symbols ( ) that group part of a calculation.
- **Index / power** — how many times you multiply a number by itself ($2^3 = 2 \times 2 \times 2$).
- **Inverse operation** — the opposite that reverses a step.
- **Expression** — a string of numbers and operations without an equals sign (see [expression](../glossary/expression.md) in algebra too).

### Worked example 1

Work out $18 - 3 \times 4$.

Multiply first: $3 \times 4 = 12$. Then subtract: $18 - 12 = 6$.

### Worked example 2

Insert brackets to make a true statement: $3 + 4 \times 5 = 35$.

Without brackets, $3 + 4 \times 5 = 3 + 20 = 23$. Put brackets around $3 + 4$:

$(3 + 4) \times 5 = 7 \times 5 = 35$.

Check inverses: if $7 + 6 = 13$, then $13 - 6 = 7$ confirms the addition.

### Tips for practice

- Underline the bracketed part and do it first.
- Powers next — never square before brackets unless they are inside the brackets.
- Multiply and divide: work **left to right**, do not always multiply before divide.
- When checking, use the inverse of the last operation you did.
- Write the order B-I-D-M-A-S down the margin on hard questions until it feels automatic.
- If only + and − remain, work left to right: $10 - 6 + 2 = 4 + 2 = 6$, not $10 - 8$.
