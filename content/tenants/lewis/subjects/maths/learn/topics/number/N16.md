### What this is about

When numbers in a calculation are rounded, the **answer** also has a range. **Limits of accuracy** means finding the **maximum** and **minimum** possible values of a result using **upper** and **lower bounds** of each input.

If $a = 12$ to the nearest whole number, $a$ is from 11.5 up to 12.5 (not including 12.5). Same idea for every measured value.

For **adding**, maximum → add upper bounds; minimum → add lower bounds.

For **subtracting**, maximum → upper of first minus lower of second; minimum → lower of first minus upper of second.

For **multiplying** and **dividing**, plug in the bound pairs that make the result biggest or smallest — often largest × largest for max product, but for division think carefully which fraction is biggest.

This uses **[rounding](../glossary/rounding.md)** ideas from error intervals and careful **[estimate](../glossary/estimate.md)** thinking.

### Words to know

- **Upper bound (UB)** — top of the error interval.
- **Lower bound (LB)** — bottom of the error interval.
- **Maximum possible value** — often using UBs and LBs in a set pattern.
- **Minimum possible value** — opposite combination.
- **Correct to** — rounding level of a measurement.

### Worked example 1

$a = 12$ and $b = 5$, both correct to the nearest whole number. Find max and min of $a + b$.

$a$: $11.5 \le a < 12.5$. $b$: $4.5 \le b < 5.5$.

Max $a + b$ ≈ $12.5 + 5.5 = 18$ (use values just below upper bounds if required).

Min $a + b$ ≈ $11.5 + 4.5 = 16$.

### Worked example 2

$x = 20$ cm and $y = 4$ cm, both nearest whole cm. Find max and min of $x \times y$.

$x$: $19.5 \le x < 20.5$. $y$: $3.5 \le y < 4.5$.

Max product: $20.5 \times 4.5 = 92.25$ cm² (or use just under bounds per your worksheet rule).

Min product: $19.5 \times 3.5 = 68.25$ cm².

For max $a - b$ with values above: $12.5 - 4.5 = 8$; min $a - b$: $11.5 - 5.5 = 6$.

### Tips for practice

- Write bounds for **each** letter before combining.
- Addition/subtraction: match UB with UB for max sum; for difference, UB − LB for max.
- Multiplication: max usually UB × UB if both positive; min LB × LB.
- Division: max often UB ÷ LB when all positive — test with your numbers if unsure.
