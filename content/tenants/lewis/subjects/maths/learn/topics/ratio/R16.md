## What this is about

**Growth** means a quantity increases by a [percentage](../../glossary/percentage.md) of itself — population, savings, or prices. **Decay** means it shrinks the same way — depreciation on a car or radioactive-style models in simplified form.

Instead of adding 8% then another 8% by hand each time, you use a **multiplier**: 8% increase → × 1.08; 15% decrease → × 0.85. One year of change is one multiply; several equal years mean multiplying the same factor repeatedly (without jumping to formulas beyond what the question needs).

Understanding multipliers links back to percentages and helps you read “value after 3 years at 5% per year” style problems step by step.

## Words to know

- **Multiplier** — decimal factor applied to the current amount.
- **Growth** — increase; multiplier greater than 1.
- **Decay / depreciation** — decrease; multiplier less than 1.
- **[Percentage](../../glossary/percentage.md)** — the rate of change per period.
- **Compound change** — apply the multiplier again on the **new** amount each period.

## Worked example 1

£500 grows by 6% in one year. Find the new amount.

Multiplier = 1.06.  
New amount = $500 \times 1.06 = £530$.

**Answer:** £530.

## Worked example 2

A car worth £8000 depreciates by 12% per year for **two** years (same rate each year). Find its value after 2 years.

One year: × 0.88. Two years: × $0.88 \times 0.88 = 0.7744$.  
Value = $8000 \times 0.7744 = £6195.20$.

**Answer:** £6195.20.

## Tips for practice

- Increase  $p$%  → multiplier **$1 + p/100$**; decrease  $p$%  → **$1 - p/100$**.
- Each new period uses the **latest** value, not always the very first (unless the question says otherwise).
- Do not add 10% + 10% to get 20% of the original over two years — use two multipliers.
- Interest “per year” for multiple years usually means repeat the same yearly multiply.
- Write the multiplier before pressing calculator keys — catches sign errors on decay.
