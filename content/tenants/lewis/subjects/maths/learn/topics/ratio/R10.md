## What this is about

Two quantities can be linked in two main ways. **Direct [proportion](../../glossary/proportion.md)** means when one goes up, the other goes up by the same factor — more apples, more cost. The rule looks like $y = kx$ for a fixed number $k$.

**Inverse proportion** means when one goes up, the other goes **down** so their product stays fixed — more workers sharing a fixed job, less time each. The rule looks like $y = \frac{k}{x}$ or $xy = k$.

Telling them apart from words (“per item” vs “fixed total”) helps with science, travel plans, and graph sketches.

## Words to know

- **Direct proportion** — double one → double the other; $y = kx$.
- **Inverse proportion** — double one → halve the other; $xy = k$ constant.
- **Constant of proportionality** — the $k$ in $y = kx$ or $y = k/x$.
- **Per** — often signals direct (miles **per** hour at steady speed links distance and time directly when speed is fixed).
- **[Equation](../../glossary/equation.md)** — $y = 5x$ or $y = 12/x$ models the relationship.

## Worked example 1

$y$ is directly proportional to $x$. When $x = 3$, $y = 18$. Find $y$ when $x = 7$.

$k = y \div x = 18 \div 3 = 6$, so $y = 6x$.  
When $x = 7$, $y = 42$.

**Answer:** 42.

## Worked example 2

$y$ is inversely proportional to $x$. When $x = 4$, $y = 10$. Find $y$ when $x = 5$.

$k = xy = 4 \times 10 = 40$, so $y = 40/x$.  
When $x = 5$, $y = 40 \div 5 = 8$.

**Answer:** 8.

## Tips for practice

- Direct: ratio $\frac{y}{x}$ stays the same. Inverse: product $xy$ stays the same.
- $y = \frac{5}{x}$ is inverse; $y = 5x$ is direct — watch where $x$ sits.
- More people on a **fixed** amount of work → less time each → inverse.
- Price of **n** identical items → total cost vs $n$ is direct.
- Always find $k$ from one pair of values before answering a new $x$ or $y$.
