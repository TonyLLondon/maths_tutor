## What this is about

When $y$ is **inversely proportional** to $x$, the [equation](../../glossary/equation.md) is $y = \frac{k}{x}$ for a fixed constant $k$. Equivalently, $x \times y = k$ every time. If $x$ doubles, $y$ halves so the product stays the same.

You find $k$ from one pair of values, then use it for any new $x$ or $y$. This models fixed work shared among more people, brightness spread over a larger area, or travel time when distance is fixed and speed changes.

Graphs of inverse relationships curve downward — they are not straight lines like direct proportion. In practice questions you rarely need the graph; finding $k$ and substituting is enough. Always check that the story fits inverse: one quantity grows while the other shrinks, and “together they stay at the same total product”.

## Words to know

- **Inverse proportion** — product $xy$ is constant.
- **Constant $k$** — the number in $y = k/x$.
- **Reciprocal relationship** — multiply one, divide the other.
- **[Proportion](../../glossary/proportion.md)** — the linked change between $x$ and $y$.
- **Rearrange** — from $y = k/x$, multiply both sides by $x$ to get $k = xy$.

## Worked example 1

$y$ is inversely proportional to $x$. When $x = 5$, $y = 12$. Find $y$ when $x = 8$.

$k = 5 \times 12 = 60$, so $y = 60/x$.  
$y = 60 \div 8 = 7.5$.

**Answer:** 7.5.

## Worked example 2

$y = \frac{48}{x}$. Find $x$ when $y = 6$.

$6 = 48/x$ → $6x = 48$ → $x = 8$.

**Answer:** 8.

## Tips for practice

- Start with **$k = xy$** from any given pair — quick and clear.
- Label which letter is which in word problems (time vs workers) before writing $y = k/x$.
- If $x$ is multiplied by 3, $y$ is divided by 3 (not multiplied by 3).
- Do not mix inverse with direct: $y = 3x$ is **not** inverse.
- When finding $x$, multiply both sides by $x$ to clear the fraction.
- Sketch a small table: as $x$ increases, $y$ should decrease if inverse fits.
