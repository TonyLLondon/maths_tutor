## Fluency (AO1)

1. List all the factors of 24.
2. List the first five multiples of 7.
3. Circle the prime numbers: 9, 11, 15, 17, 21.
4. Work out the highest common factor (HCF) of 12 and 18.

## Reasoning (AO2)

5. Sam says every multiple of 6 is also a multiple of 3. Is Sam correct? Explain in one or two sentences.

6. **Always, sometimes, or never?** The HCF of two numbers is bigger than both numbers. Give an example if it is *sometimes* true.

## Problem (AO3)

7. Archer and a friend share 24 stickers and 36 pencils into identical bags. Each bag has the same stickers and the same pencils, with none left over. What is the greatest number of bags they can make? How many stickers and pencils in each bag?

## Stretch

8. Find two different pairs of numbers whose HCF is 6.
