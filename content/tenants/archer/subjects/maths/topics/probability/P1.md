## Fluency (AO1)

1. A fair six-sided dice is rolled once. List all the possible outcomes.
2. Which is more likely on one roll of a fair dice: getting a **3** or getting a **number greater than 4**? Circle one and write the two probabilities as fractions.
3. This spinner is split into four equal sections labelled **Red**, **Blue**, **Green**, and **Yellow**. What is the probability of landing on **Blue**?
4. A bag has 5 red counters and 5 blue counters. You pick one counter without looking. What is the probability it is red?
5. A bag has 3 yellow counters and 7 green counters. What is the probability of picking a yellow counter?
6. A fair coin is flipped once. What is the probability of getting heads?
7. A spinner has 10 equal sections numbered 1 to 10. What is the probability of landing on an even number?
8. A box has 4 red pens, 4 blue pens, and 2 green pens. What is the probability of picking a green pen?
9. Write the probability of an **impossible** event and the probability of a **certain** event, as numbers.
10. A fair 8-sided dice is numbered 1 to 8. What is the probability of rolling a number less than 3?
11. A bag has only red counters in it. What is the probability of picking a blue counter?
12. There are 20 raffle tickets in a hat, and only 1 of them is the winning ticket. What is the probability of drawing the winning ticket?

## Reasoning (AO2)

13. Mia says, “On a fair dice, 6 is the hardest number to roll, so 6 is less likely than 2.” Is Mia correct? Explain in one or two sentences.

14. **Fair or unfair?** A spinner has eight equal sections: six say **Win** and two say **Lose**. Is this spinner fair for a “Win / Lose” game? Give a reason.

15. Sloan flips a coin and gets heads five times in a row. Sloan says tails is “due” to come up next. Is Sloan correct? Explain using the idea that each flip does not depend on the last one.

16. **True or false?** A probability can never be more than 1. Explain what a probability of 1 means.

17. A spinner has 5 equal sections: 2 red, 2 blue, and 1 green. Archer says red and blue are equally likely. Is Archer correct? Explain.

18. Priya has two bags that both contain 2 red counters. Bag A has 4 counters altogether and Bag B has 10 counters altogether. Priya says the probability of picking red is the same for both bags. Is Priya correct? Explain, using the two probabilities.

19. A weather forecast says there is a 30% chance of rain tomorrow. What does this tell you about whether it will rain? Explain in one or two sentences.

## Problem (AO3)

20. Archer and a friend each roll a fair dice once. Archer wins if the **total** of the two rolls is 7. List all the ways to get a total of 7. How many equally likely outcomes are there altogether for two dice?

21. A bag has 4 red counters, 6 blue counters, and some green counters. There are 20 counters in the bag altogether. What is the probability of picking a green counter?

22. In a class of 30 pupils, 18 are girls and 12 are boys. A pupil is picked at random to be class monitor. What is the probability that the pupil picked is a boy?

23. A fairground spinner has 12 equal sections: 3 of them win a prize. A visitor plays the game 60 times. About how many times would you expect them to win?

24. Sloan buys 5 raffle tickets out of 100 sold in total, and there is 1 prize. What is the probability that Sloan wins the prize?

25. A bag of sweets has strawberry, orange, and lime flavours in the ratio 2 : 3 : 5, with 10 sweets in total. What is the probability of picking a lime sweet?

26. A set of 25 cards is numbered 1 to 25. What is the probability of picking a card that is a multiple of 5?

## Stretch

27. Design your own **fair** spinner for a game with exactly three different colours. Draw it and write the probability of each colour.

28. A bag has red, blue, and green counters. You are told that P(red) = 1/2 and P(blue) = 1/3. What is P(green)? Explain your reasoning.

29. Two fair six-sided dice are rolled together. Without listing every outcome, explain why getting a total of 7 is more likely than getting a total of 2.

30. Make a table showing the number of ways to make each possible total (2 to 12) when rolling two fair dice. Which total is the most likely, and what is its probability?
