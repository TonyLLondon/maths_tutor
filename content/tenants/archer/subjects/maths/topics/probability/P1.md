## Fluency (AO1)

1. A fair six-sided dice is rolled once. List all the possible outcomes.
2. Which is more likely on one roll of a fair dice: getting a **3** or getting a **number greater than 4**? Circle one and write the two probabilities as fractions.
3. This spinner is split into four equal sections labelled **Red**, **Blue**, **Green**, and **Yellow**. What is the probability of landing on **Blue**?
4. A bag has 5 red counters and 5 blue counters. You pick one counter without looking. What is the probability it is red?

## Reasoning (AO2)

5. Mia says, “On a fair dice, 6 is the hardest number to roll, so 6 is less likely than 2.” Is Mia correct? Explain in one or two sentences.

6. **Fair or unfair?** A spinner has eight equal sections: six say **Win** and two say **Lose**. Is this spinner fair for a “Win / Lose” game? Give a reason.

## Problem (AO3)

7. Archer and a friend each roll a fair dice once. Archer wins if the **total** of the two rolls is 7. List all the ways to get a total of 7. How many equally likely outcomes are there altogether for two dice?

## Stretch

8. Design your own **fair** spinner for a game with exactly three different colours. Draw it and write the probability of each colour.
