## Fluency (AO1)

1. A dice is rolled 50 times and lands on 6 eight times. What is the relative frequency of rolling a 6?
2. A coin is flipped 40 times, landing on heads 22 times. What is the relative frequency of heads, as a fraction and a decimal?
3. A spinner is spun 25 times and lands on red 5 times. What is the relative frequency of red?
4. In 80 trials, an event occurs 20 times. What is the relative frequency, as a decimal?
5. A survey of 200 people finds 150 prefer tea. What is the relative frequency of preferring tea, as a percentage?
6. A dice is rolled 30 times with these results: 1 (4), 2 (6), 3 (5), 4 (3), 5 (7), 6 (5). What is the relative frequency of rolling a 5?
7. A machine produces 500 items, and 15 are faulty. What is the relative frequency of a faulty item?
8. A bag of counters is picked with replacement 60 times, and 24 are red. What is the relative frequency of red, as a fraction in its simplest form?
9. A netball player scores from 40 out of 50 free throws. What is the relative frequency of scoring?
10. A spinner is spun 100 times and lands on green 40 times. Estimate the probability of green from this data.
11. A weather station records rain on 9 out of 30 days. Estimate the probability of rain on a day, as a fraction.
12. In 120 games, a team wins 72. What is the relative frequency of winning, as a decimal?

## Reasoning (AO2)

13. Archer flips a coin 10 times and gets heads 7 times, giving a relative frequency of 0.7. Explain why this is not a good estimate for P(heads).

14. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Relative frequency equals the true theoretical probability exactly. Explain.

15. Sloan says, "The more trials I do, the more reliable my relative frequency estimate becomes." Is Sloan correct? Explain.

16. A dice is rolled 6 times and lands on 3 exactly once. Priya says this proves P(3) = 1/6 for this dice. Is Priya correct? Explain.

17. Two students test the same coin: one flips it 20 times, the other 500 times. Whose relative frequency is likely to give a better estimate of the true probability? Explain.

18. **True or false?** A relative frequency can be greater than 1. Explain.

19. A dice is rolled many times, giving a relative frequency of rolling a 6 close to 0.3. Mia says a fair dice would give a similar result. Is Mia correct? Explain using the theoretical probability of a fair dice.

## Problem (AO3)

20. A quality inspector tests 250 bulbs and finds 10 faulty. Estimate how many faulty bulbs there would be in a batch of 4000, using the relative frequency.

21. Over a season, a striker scores in 18 out of 30 matches. Estimate the probability the striker scores in a randomly chosen match, and use it to predict the number of scoring matches in the next 10 games.

22. A spinner is tested 500 times, landing on "win" 125 times. Estimate P(win), and compare it to a theoretical spinner with 4 equal sections. Is this spinner fair?

23. A dice is rolled 300 times with these results: 1 (48), 2 (52), 3 (49), 4 (51), 5 (50), 6 (50). Does this dice appear to be fair? Explain using the relative frequencies.

24. A driving instructor finds 27 out of 45 pupils pass their test first time. Estimate the probability a new pupil passes first time, giving your answer as a percentage.

25. A charity has run a raffle for several years; historically 1 in 40 tickets wins a prize. If 2000 tickets are sold this year, estimate the number of winning tickets.

26. A gardener plants 60 seeds and 51 germinate. Estimate the probability that a seed germinates, and use it to predict how many would germinate out of 500 seeds.

## Stretch

27. Explain why a very small number of trials can give a relative frequency very different from the true probability, using an example.

28. Design an experiment with a coin, dice, or spinner to test whether it is fair. Explain how many trials you would use and why, and what result would make you suspect it is biased.

29. A company claims a spinner game has a 1 in 5 chance of winning. After 1000 games, 150 wins were recorded. Does this support or contradict the company's claim? Explain using relative frequency.

30. Explain the difference between theoretical probability and experimental (relative frequency) probability, giving one real-life example of each.
