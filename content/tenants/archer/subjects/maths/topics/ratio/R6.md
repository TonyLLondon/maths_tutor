## Getting started

1. There are 2 cats and 3 dogs. Write the ratio cats : dogs.
2. There are 2 red counters out of 5 counters. Write the red fraction of the total.
3. Complete: twice as many means multiply by ___.
4. A number changes from 4 to 8. What multiplier was used?
5. Write “3 times as many” as the ratio original : new.
6. In the ratio 1 : 4, the second amount is how many times the first?
7. Write the ratio 1 : 2 as a fraction comparing the first amount with the second.
8. There are 3 apples and 2 oranges. What fraction of all the fruit are apples?
9. There are 4 cats and 1 dog. Write the ratio cats : dogs.
10. A quantity changes from 5 to 15. What multiplier was used?
11. Write “half as many” as the ratio new amount : original amount.
12. There are 2 apples and 6 oranges. What fraction of all the fruit are apples?

## Fluency (AO1)

13. There are 4 boys and 6 girls in a class. Write the ratio boys : girls, and also write the number of boys as a fraction of the total.
14. A price increased from £20 to £25. Write this change as a multiplier of the original price.
15. Write "3 times as many" as a ratio in the form 1 : n.
16. A number is multiplied by 5. Write this relationship as a ratio original : new.
17. In a fruit basket, apples : oranges = 3 : 4. Write the number of apples as a fraction of the total fruit.
18. A recipe uses flour and butter in the ratio 5 : 2. Write the amount of butter as a fraction of the amount of flour.
19. Convert "the new value is 0.75 times the old value" into a whole-number ratio old : new.
20. Two lengths are 12 cm and 18 cm. Write the multiplier that converts 12 cm to 18 cm.
21. Two numbers are in the ratio 5 : 8. Write the second number as a fraction of the first.
22. A class has 10 pupils wearing glasses out of 25 pupils in total. Write this as a ratio glasses : no glasses.
23. A quantity doubles, then doubles again. Write the overall change as a single ratio original : final.

## Reasoning (AO2)

24. Mia says a ratio and a fraction can never describe the same relationship. Is she correct? Use an example to check.

25. **Always, sometimes, or never?** If two quantities are in the ratio a : b, then the first is (a/b) times the second.

26. **True or false?** "Increased by a factor of 3" means the ratio new : old is 3 : 1. Explain, and contrast this with "increased by 300%".

27. Priya says a part-to-whole fraction (like 2/5) can always be turned directly into a part-to-part ratio without extra working. Is she correct? Explain with an example.

28. "A is 3/4 of B" (a fraction) and "A : B = 3 : 4" (a ratio) — do these describe the same relationship or a different one? Explain.

29. Sam says that if a price is "increased in the ratio 5 : 4" (new : old), this means the new price is 5/4 times the old price. Is Sam correct?

30. Leo has £30 and Mo has £45. Give **both** a ratio and a fraction/multiplier to describe how Leo's money compares with Mo's.

## Problem (AO3)

31. In a school orchestra of 24 pupils, the ratio of string players to wind players is 5 : 3. What fraction of the orchestra plays strings?

32. A journey time is multiplied by 1.4 because of traffic. Write this as a whole-number ratio original time : new time.

33. A recipe for 6 people uses 300 g of rice. Sloan wants to make it for 9 people. What multiplier should she use, and how much rice is needed?

34. On a maths test, Archer scored 3/5 of the total marks. Write his marks as a ratio to the marks he lost, in simplest form.

35. A company's profit grew from £40,000 to £60,000. Write this growth as a ratio (old : new) and as a percentage increase.

36. Two bus routes cover the same distance with journey times in the ratio 4 : 5 (Bus A : Bus B). If Bus A takes 24 minutes, how long does Bus B take, and what fraction longer is that?

37. In a survey, 3/8 of people preferred tea over coffee. Write the ratio tea : coffee preference in simplest form.

## Stretch

38. A quantity is increased in the ratio 7 : 4 (new : old). Express this as a percentage increase.

39. Two numbers have the relationship "the second is 5/3 of the first." Write this as a whole-number ratio, then find the second number if the first is 21.

40. A car depreciates to 3/5 of its original value after one year. Write the ratio original : new value, and find the new value if the original was £15,000.

41. Explain why "increase by a ratio of 5 : 4" (new : old) is not the same as "increase by 5/4" added on top of the original. Which one actually means "multiply by 5/4"?

42. Design your own real-life multiplicative comparison (prices, distances, or populations), and express the relationship **both** as a ratio and as a fraction/multiplier.
