## Fluency (AO1)

1. A square has side 3 cm. Find its area. Now double the side to 6 cm and find the new area. What is the ratio of the two areas?
2. A cube has side 2 cm. Find its volume. Now double the side to 4 cm and find the new volume. What is the ratio of the two volumes?
3. Two squares have sides in the ratio 1 : 3. What is the ratio of their areas?
4. Two cubes have sides in the ratio 1 : 2. What is the ratio of their volumes?
5. A rectangle 4 cm by 5 cm is enlarged by scale factor 3. Find the new area and the ratio of the two areas.
6. Two similar shapes have a length scale factor of 5. What is the area scale factor?
7. Two similar solids have a length scale factor of 2. What is the volume scale factor?
8. A triangle has area 12 cm². It is enlarged by a length scale factor of 2. Find the new area.
9. A cuboid has volume 30 cm³. It is enlarged by a length scale factor of 3. Find the new volume.
10. Two similar rectangles have areas in the ratio 4 : 9. What is the ratio of their side lengths?
11. Two similar cubes have volumes in the ratio 8 : 27. Find the ratio of their side lengths.

## Reasoning (AO2)

12. Mia says that if you double the side of a square, the area also just doubles. Is she correct? Use an example to check.

13. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Enlarging a 2D shape by a length scale factor k multiplies its area by k².

14. **True or false?** Enlarging a 3D solid by a length scale factor k multiplies its volume by k³. Explain.

15. Priya says two similar cubes with a side ratio of 1 : 2 must have the same ratio for surface area and for volume. Is she correct? Explain.

16. Explain, using a square, why the area scale factor equals (length scale factor)².

17. Sam says if the area of a shape is multiplied by 9, the length scale factor must be 9. Is Sam correct? Find the actual length scale factor.

18. Leo says the volume scale factor for a solid enlarged by scale factor 1/2 is 1/2. Is Leo correct? Find the correct volume scale factor.

## Problem (AO3)

19. A model of a statue is built at a length scale of 1 : 10 compared to the real statue. If the model has volume 5 cm³, find the volume of the real statue.

20. Two similar photographs have a length scale factor of 2.5. The smaller photo has area 40 cm². Find the area of the larger photo.

21. Two similar tanks have a length scale factor of 3. The smaller tank holds 4 litres. Find the capacity of the larger tank.

22. A garden pond is a scale model of a real lake, with length scale factor 1 : 200. The model pond has a surface area of 0.5 m². Find the real lake's surface area.

23. Two similar cakes have volumes 64 cm³ and 216 cm³. Find the ratio of their side lengths.

24. A scale drawing has an area scale factor of 1 : 16 compared to a real wall. Find the length scale factor.

25. Two similar boxes have a volume ratio of 1 : 1000. Find the ratio of their side lengths.

## Stretch

26. Two similar solids have surface areas in the ratio 9 : 16. Find the ratio of their volumes.

27. A model boat is built at length scale 1 : 25. The real boat needs 500 litres of paint to cover its surface, and paint needed is proportional to surface area. Find how much paint the model needs, in millilitres.

28. Two similar cylinders have radii in the ratio 2 : 5. Find the ratio of (a) their curved surface areas, and (b) their volumes.

29. Explain why doubling all the dimensions of a 3D object increases its volume by a factor of 8, not 2. Use a cuboid example with actual numbers.

30. Design your own length : area : volume scale-factor problem with two similar shapes, and solve it.
