## Fluency (AO1)

1. Write the ratio of 4 red counters to 6 blue counters in its simplest form.
2. Simplify the ratio 15 : 20.
3. Simplify the ratio 24 : 36 : 60.
4. Write the ratio 2.5 : 5 using whole numbers, in its simplest form.
5. Write the ratio 1/2 : 1/3 using whole numbers.
6. Fill in the blank: 3 : 4 = 9 : ___
7. Fill in the blank: 5 : 2 = ___ : 8
8. Write 12 : 18 in the form 1 : n.
9. Write 3 : 15 in the form 1 : n.
10. A ratio is written 2 : 3 : 5. What fraction of the total does the middle part represent?
11. Write "for every 3 cats there are 5 dogs" using ratio notation.

## Reasoning (AO2)

12. Mia simplifies 8 : 12 to 2 : 3. Is she correct? Show the working.

13. **Always, sometimes, or never?** A ratio can be simplified if both numbers share a common factor greater than 1.

14. **True or false?** The ratio 4 : 6 is the same as the ratio 6 : 4. Explain your answer.

15. Priya writes a ratio as "1 : 2.5" and says this isn't a "proper" ratio because it has a decimal. Is she right? How could you rewrite it using whole numbers?

16. Explain why the ratio 10 : 15 and the ratio 2 : 3 describe the same relationship.

17. Sam has a bag with red and blue marbles in the ratio 3 : 4. He says there must be exactly 3 red marbles. Is Sam correct? Explain.

18. A ratio is given as a : b = 4 : 7. Explain what happens to the ratio if you swap it to b : a.

## Problem (AO3)

19. A fruit drink mixes orange and mango juice in the ratio 5 : 3. Write the ratio in the form 1 : n.

20. A paint mix uses blue and white paint in the ratio 2 : 9. Can this be simplified? Write how many parts of white are needed for every 1 part of blue.

21. A smoothie uses banana, milk and yoghurt in the ratio 40 : 100 : 60. Simplify this ratio fully.

22. On a school trip, the ratio of adults to pupils must be 1 : 8. Write this ratio as pupils : adults.

23. A cleaning fluid is mixed using a ratio of 6 : 15. Simplify the ratio, then write the amount of the second ingredient needed for every 2 parts of the first.

24. A cake recipe uses flour, sugar and butter in the ratio 8 : 4 : 2. Simplify this ratio fully.

25. Two numbers are in the ratio 7 : 3, and their difference is 20. Use the ratio to find the two numbers.

## Stretch

26. If a : b = 2 : 3 and b : c = 4 : 5, find a : b : c by combining the ratios.

27. Two lengths are in the ratio 5 : 8. If both lengths are increased by 4 cm, the new ratio becomes 3 : 4. Find the two original lengths.

28. A ratio 3 : x is equivalent to 12 : 20. Find x.

29. Explain, using an example, why you cannot generally find a : b : c just by simplifying a : b and b : c separately without matching the shared term.

30. Write your own three-term ratio problem based on a recipe with three ingredients, then simplify your ratio fully.
