## Getting started

1. Find 10% of £100.
2. Increase £100 by 10%.
3. Decrease £100 by 10%.
4. Write the multiplier for a 10% increase.
5. Write the multiplier for a 10% decrease.
6. A population of 20 doubles. Find the new population.
7. £50 earns 10% interest once. How much interest is earned?
8. A value is multiplied by 1.2. Is this growth or decay?
9. Find 5% of £200.
10. Write the multiplier for a 5% increase.
11. £80 increases by 10%. Find the new amount.
12. Write the multiplier for a 20% decrease.

## Fluency (AO1)

13. A savings account pays 5% interest per year. £200 is invested. Find the amount after 1 year.
14. A car worth £10,000 depreciates by 10% in its first year. Find its value after 1 year.
15. A population of 500 grows by 20% in a year. Find the new population.
16. Find the multiplier for a 15% increase.
17. Find the multiplier for a 20% decrease.
18. £300 grows by 8%. Find the new amount.

## More fluency

19. A colony of bacteria doubles every hour. If it starts at 50, find the number after 1 hour.
20. Find the multiplier for a 4% increase.
21. £500 is invested at 3% simple interest for 1 year. Find the interest earned.
22. A price of £80 increases by 25%. Find the new price.
23. A value of £1000 decreases by 12%. Find the new value.

## Reasoning (AO2)

24. Mia says applying "growth of 10%" twice in a row is the same as applying "growth of 20%" once. Is she correct? Check using £100.

25. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Repeated percentage growth increases a value by more than the sum of the individual percentages, after two or more steps.

26. **True or false?** A multiplier of 1.05 represents a 5% increase. Explain.

27. Priya says a multiplier of 0.9 represents a 90% decrease. Is she correct? Explain what 0.9 actually represents.

28. Explain the difference between simple growth (adding the same amount each time) and compound growth (multiplying by the same factor each time).

## Mixed practice

29. Sam says a value that decays by 50% each year will reach £0 after 2 years. Is Sam correct? Explain using a starting value of £100.

30. Leo says decay (decreasing) can be modelled using a multiplier greater than 1. Is Leo correct?

## Problem (AO3)

31. A car worth £18,000 depreciates by 15% each year. Find its value after 1 year, and after 2 years.

32. A population of bacteria starts at 200 and grows by 50% every hour. Find the population after 2 hours.

33. £2,000 is invested at 4% compound interest per year. Find the value after 2 years.

34. A house valued at £250,000 increases in value by 6% per year. Find its value after 2 years.

35. A shop reduces a £400 item by 10% in a sale, then by a further 10% in a clearance sale. Find the final price.

36. A tree's height grows by 8% each year. If it starts at 150 cm, find its height after 2 years.

37. A radioactive substance decays by 30% each hour. Starting at 800 g, find the mass remaining after 2 hours.

## Stretch

38. £5,000 is invested at 3% compound interest per year for 3 years. Find the final amount, to the nearest penny.

39. A car depreciates by 20% each year. After how many whole years will its value first drop below half of its original value? Show your working year by year.

40. Two savings accounts both start with £1,000. Account A grows by 5% each year (compound). Account B grows by £50 each year (simple). After 3 years, which account has more money?

41. Explain why compound growth eventually grows faster than simple (linear) growth, even if the compound rate seems small. Use the idea of "growth on top of previous growth."

42. Design your own growth or decay problem using a repeated percentage change over 2 or 3 steps, and solve it.
