## Getting started

1. Complete: 1 : 2 = 2 : ___.
2. Complete: 2 : 3 = 4 : ___.
3. Are 1 : 2 and 2 : 4 equal ratios?
4. Are 1 : 2 and 2 : 3 equal ratios?
5. Multiply both parts of 3 : 4 by 2. Write the new ratio.
6. Simplify 4 : 8.
7. A recipe for 2 people uses 4 eggs. How many eggs are needed for 4 people?
8. Complete the equivalent ratio: 5 : 1 = 15 : ___.
9. Complete: 4 : 6 = 2 : ___.
10. Simplify the ratio 15 : 5.
11. Multiply both parts of 2 : 5 by 3. Write the new ratio.
12. A recipe for 3 people uses 6 eggs. How many eggs are needed for 6 people?

## Fluency (AO1)

13. Are the ratios 2 : 3 and 4 : 6 equal? Explain.
14. Solve: 3 : 5 = 12 : x. Find x.
15. Solve: x : 4 = 15 : 20. Find x.
16. A recipe for 4 people needs 200 g of rice. How much rice is needed for 8 people, keeping the same proportion?
17. Check whether 5 : 8 and 15 : 24 are equivalent ratios.
18. Solve: 7 : 2 = 21 : x. Find x.
19. Solve: 6 : 9 = 2 : x. Find x.
20. A photo has a width : height ratio of 4 : 3. If the width is 20 cm, find the height using the equivalent ratio.
21. Solve: y : 12 = 3 : 4. Find y.
22. Are the ratios 9 : 6 and 3 : 2 equal? Show why.
23. Solve: 5 : x = 10 : 14. Find x.

## Reasoning (AO2)

24. Mia says 3 : 4 and 6 : 9 are equivalent ratios because you can multiply 3 by 2 to get 6. Is she correct? Check by comparing both parts.

25. **Always, sometimes, or never?** If a : b = c : d, then a × d = b × c.

26. **True or false?** To check whether two ratios are equal, you can convert both to unit ratios (1 : n) and compare. Explain.

27. Priya solves x : 21 = 4 : 7 by writing x = 21 × 4 = 84. What mistake has she made? Find the correct value of x.

28. Explain why 8 : 12 and 2 : 3 represent the same proportion.

29. Sam says that if one ratio has bigger numbers than another, it must represent a bigger relationship. Use 4 : 8 and 3 : 5 to check if Sam is right.

30. A recipe ratio flour : water = 5 : 2. Leo scales it up and says 15 : 8 is still the same proportion. Is Leo correct? Check by scaling 5 : 2 up by the same factor.

## Problem (AO3)

31. A cordial is mixed water : squash = 6 : 1. If Archer uses 3 litres of squash, how much water does he need, keeping the same proportion?

32. A map has a scale ratio of drawn distance to real distance of 1 : 50 000. If a road is drawn 3.2 cm long, find the real distance in km.

33. Paint is mixed red : yellow = 2 : 5. Sloan has 400 ml of red paint. Using the same proportion, how much yellow does she need?

34. A concrete mix uses cement : sand : gravel = 1 : 2 : 3. If 8 kg of cement is used, find the amounts of sand and gravel needed.

35. A photo keeps width : height in the ratio 3 : 2. The original photo is 15 cm wide and 10 cm tall. It is enlarged to a new width of 45 cm. Find the new height.

36. Two similar rectangles have sides in the ratio 5 : 8. If the shorter side of the larger rectangle is 40 cm, find its longer side.

37. A fruit punch mixes juice, lemonade and soda in the ratio 3 : 2 : 5. Mia uses 15 litres of juice. Using the same proportion, find the amounts of lemonade and soda needed.

## Stretch

38. Solve for x: (x + 2) : 6 = 5 : 3.

39. Two equal ratios are written 4 : x = x : 9, where x is positive. Find x.

40. A ladder resting against a wall must keep a height : base ratio of 4 : 3 for safety. If the base is 1.5 m, find the required height.

41. Explain why cross-multiplication (a × d = b × c) works for checking if a : b = c : d. Connect your explanation to writing both ratios as fractions a/b and c/d.

42. Write your own problem where you must find a missing number to make two ratios equal, then solve it.
