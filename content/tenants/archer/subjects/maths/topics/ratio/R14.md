## Getting started

1. A line rises 4 squares while moving 2 squares right. Find rise ÷ run.
2. Complete: gradient = change in y ÷ change in ___.
3. A line passes through (0, 0) and (1, 3). Find its gradient.
4. A line passes through (0, 0) and (2, 4). Find its gradient.
5. A horizontal line has gradient ___.
6. A distance rises by 10 km in 2 hours. Find the rate of change.
7. A cost rises by £6 for 3 items. Find the cost per item.
8. Does a line with positive gradient rise or fall from left to right?
9. A line passes through (0, 0) and (3, 6). Find its gradient.
10. A distance rises by 5 m in 2 s. Find the rate of change in m/s.
11. A line has gradient 4. When x increases by 2, y increases by ___.
12. Does a line with gradient 0 go up or stay flat?

## Fluency (AO1)

13. A straight-line graph passes through (0, 0) and (4, 20). Find its gradient.
14. Find the gradient of the line through (0, 0) and (5, 15).
15. A distance-time graph line (time in hours, distance in km) passes through (2, 60) and (5, 150). Find the gradient (the speed).
16. A cost graph for buying pens (number of pens, cost in £) passes through (0, 0) and (6, 12). Find the gradient (the cost per pen).
17. Find the gradient between the points (1, 3) and (4, 15).
18. A line has gradient 4 and passes through the origin. Find the y-value when x = 7.
19. A conversion graph line passes through (0, 0) and (10, 25). Find its gradient.
20. Find the gradient of the line joining (2, 8) and (6, 20).
21. A tank fills at a constant rate; its graph (minutes, litres) passes through (0, 0) and (3, 45). Find the rate in litres per minute.
22. Find the gradient between (0, 5) and (4, 17).
23. A graph line passes through (2, 10) and (5, 19). Find its gradient.

## Reasoning (AO2)

24. Mia says the gradient of a distance-time graph tells you the speed. Is she correct? Explain.

25. **Always, sometimes, or never?** A steeper line on a distance-time graph means a faster speed.

26. **True or false?** Gradient is calculated as (change in x) ÷ (change in y). Explain.

27. Priya says a horizontal line has a gradient of 0. Is she correct? Explain what this means in a real context, such as a car's distance-time graph.

28. Explain how the gradient of a cost graph (total cost vs number of items) relates to the price per item.

29. Sam calculates the gradient between (1, 4) and (3, 10) as (10 − 4) ÷ (3 − 1) and gets 3. Check Sam's calculation.

30. Leo says a negative gradient on a graph of water level vs time means the water level is falling. Is Leo correct?

## Problem (AO3)

31. A cyclist's distance-time graph (hours, km) passes through (0, 0) and (3, 36). Find her speed in km/h.

32. A currency conversion graph (pounds, euros) passes through (0, 0) and (50, 57.50). Find the gradient and explain what it represents.

33. A hosepipe fills a paddling pool; its graph (minutes, litres) passes through (0, 0) and (8, 120). Find the rate of filling in litres per minute.

34. A taxi fare graph, after the fixed charge (miles, £), passes through (2, 9) and (6, 21). Find the gradient and explain what it represents.

35. A runner's distance-time graph (seconds, metres) passes through (0, 0) and (25, 200). Find her speed in m/s.

36. A phone contract cost graph (minutes used, total cost in £) passes through (0, 15) and (100, 35). Find the gradient and explain what it represents.

37. Archer's savings graph (weeks, £ saved) passes through (0, 20) and (8, 100). Find the gradient and explain what it represents.

## Stretch

38. Two distance-time graph lines are drawn on the same axes: Line A passes through (0, 0) and (4, 100); Line B passes through (0, 0) and (5, 100). Which line represents the faster speed, and by how much?

39. A graph shows temperature (°C) against time (minutes), passing through (0, 20) and (10, 5). Find the gradient and explain what a negative gradient means here.

40. A conversion graph for miles to km passes through (0, 0) and (5, 8). Find the gradient, and use it to convert 35 miles to km.

41. Explain why the gradient of a straight line is the same no matter which two points on the line you use to calculate it. Illustrate using the line through (0, 0), (2, 6) and (5, 15).

42. Design your own real-life graph scenario (cost, distance, or temperature vs time), give two points on the line, and find and interpret the gradient.
