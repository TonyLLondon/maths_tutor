## Fluency (AO1)

1. Find 10% of 80.
2. Find 25% of 60.
3. Find 15% of 40.
4. Write 3/4 as a percentage.
5. Write 0.6 as a percentage.
6. Write 45% as a decimal.
7. Write 20% as a fraction in simplest form.
8. Increase £50 by 10%.
9. Decrease £80 by 25%.
10. Find 12% of 150.
11. What percentage is 18 out of 40?

## Reasoning (AO2)

12. Mia says finding 50% of a number is the same as halving it. Is she correct? Explain.

13. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Increasing a number by 100% doubles it.

14. **True or false?** 10% of a (positive) number is always less than the number itself. Explain.

15. Priya says decreasing £60 by 20% and then increasing the result by 20% gets you back to £60. Is she correct? Check by calculating.

16. Explain how you can find 5% of an amount using 10% of the same amount.

17. Sam scored 35 out of 50 on a test and says that's 60%. Is Sam correct? Find the correct percentage.

18. Leo says a 50% discount means the item now costs half its original price. Is Leo correct?

## Problem (AO3)

19. A London Underground ticket usually costs £6.50. A discount takes 20% off. Find the new price.

20. Archer scored 42 out of 60 in a maths test. What percentage did he get?

21. A shop increases the price of a £25 jacket by 8%. Find the new price.

22. Sloan's savings grow from £120 to £150. Find the percentage increase.

23. A school of 400 pupils has 25% who walk to school. How many pupils walk to school?

24. A television originally costs £450 and is reduced by 30% in a sale. Find the sale price.

25. In a survey of 250 people, 68% said they liked football. How many people is that?

## Stretch

26. A price is reduced by 15% to £102. Find the original price.

27. After a 20% pay rise, someone earns £30,000. Find their original salary.

28. A number is increased by 25% then decreased by 20%. Show that, starting from 100, this returns you to the original value.

29. Explain why decreasing a number by 50% and then increasing the result by 50% does **not** return you to the original number. Use a starting value of 100 to show this.

30. Design your own two-step percentage problem (for example, a price increase followed by a discount), and solve it.
