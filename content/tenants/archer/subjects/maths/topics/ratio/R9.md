## Getting started

1. Write 50% as a fraction in simplest form.
2. Write 50% as a decimal.
3. Find 50% of 10.
4. Find 10% of 20.
5. Write 100% as a decimal.
6. Write 25% as a fraction in simplest form.
7. Which is larger: 20% or 50%?
8. A £10 toy is reduced by 10%. The reduction is how much?
9. Write 75% as a fraction in simplest form.
10. Find 20% of 50.
11. Write 0.25 as a percentage.
12. A £20 item is reduced by 50%. Find the sale price.

## Fluency (AO1)

13. Find 10% of 80.
14. Find 25% of 60.
15. Find 15% of 40.
16. Write 3/4 as a percentage.
17. Write 0.6 as a percentage.
18. Write 45% as a decimal.
19. Write 20% as a fraction in simplest form.
20. Increase £50 by 10%.
21. Decrease £80 by 25%.
22. Find 12% of 150.
23. What percentage is 18 out of 40?

## Reasoning (AO2)

24. Mia says finding 50% of a number is the same as halving it. Is she correct? Explain.

25. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Increasing a number by 100% doubles it.

26. **True or false?** 10% of a (positive) number is always less than the number itself. Explain.

27. Priya says decreasing £60 by 20% and then increasing the result by 20% gets you back to £60. Is she correct? Check by calculating.

28. Explain how you can find 5% of an amount using 10% of the same amount.

29. Sam scored 35 out of 50 on a test and says that's 60%. Is Sam correct? Find the correct percentage.

30. Leo says a 50% discount means the item now costs half its original price. Is Leo correct?

## Problem (AO3)

31. A London Underground ticket usually costs £6.50. A discount takes 20% off. Find the new price.

32. Archer scored 42 out of 60 in a maths test. What percentage did he get?

33. A shop increases the price of a £25 jacket by 8%. Find the new price.

34. Sloan's savings grow from £120 to £150. Find the percentage increase.

35. A school of 400 pupils has 25% who walk to school. How many pupils walk to school?

36. A television originally costs £450 and is reduced by 30% in a sale. Find the sale price.

37. In a survey of 250 people, 68% said they liked football. How many people is that?

## Stretch

38. A price is reduced by 15% to £102. Find the original price.

39. After a 20% pay rise, someone earns £30,000. Find their original salary.

40. A number is increased by 25% then decreased by 20%. Show that, starting from 100, this returns you to the original value.

41. Explain why decreasing a number by 50% and then increasing the result by 50% does **not** return you to the original number. Use a starting value of 100 to show this.

42. Design your own two-step percentage problem (for example, a price increase followed by a discount), and solve it.
