## Getting started

1. A shape is copied at the same size. What is its scale factor?
2. A 3 cm line is enlarged by scale factor 2. How long is the new line?
3. A 10 cm line is reduced by scale factor 1/2. How long is the new line?
4. Does a scale factor of 3 enlarge or reduce a shape?
5. Does a scale factor of 1/4 enlarge or reduce a shape?
6. A map uses 1 cm for 2 km. What real distance does 1 cm represent?
7. A map uses 1 cm for 2 km. What real distance does 3 cm represent?
8. Complete: at scale 1 : 10, 1 cm on a model represents ___ cm in real life.
9. A 4 cm line is enlarged by scale factor 3. How long is the new line?
10. A map uses 1 cm for 5 km. What real distance does 2 cm represent?
11. Does a scale factor of 1/2 enlarge or reduce a shape?
12. At scale 1 : 100, 2 cm on a drawing represents ___ cm in real life.

## Fluency (AO1)

13. Write the scale factor for enlarging a shape using the ratio 1 : 3.
14. A rectangle 4 cm by 6 cm is enlarged by scale factor 2. Find the new dimensions.
15. A model is built at scale 1 : 10 of the real object. The real height is 3.5 m. Find the model's height, in cm.
16. Write the ratio 1 : 20 as a scale factor for reducing a length.
17. A photo 10 cm wide is enlarged to 25 cm wide. What is the scale factor of the enlargement?
18. A map has scale 1 : 200 000. What real distance, in km, does 1 cm represent?
19. On a map with scale 1 : 50 000, how many metres does 1 cm represent?
20. A drawing is made at scale factor 1/25 of the real object. The real length is 300 cm. Find the drawing's length.
21. Two similar rectangles have a scale factor of 4 between them. The smaller rectangle is 3 cm by 5 cm. Find the dimensions of the larger rectangle.
22. Write the scale 1 : 4 as a fraction scale factor for reduction.
23. A scale factor of enlargement is 1.5. An original length is 8 cm. What is the new length?

## Reasoning (AO2)

24. Mia says a scale factor greater than 1 makes a shape **smaller**. Is she correct? Explain.

25. A map scale is 1 : 100 000. Ali says this means 1 cm represents 100 000 cm in real life. Is he correct? Convert 100 000 cm to km.

26. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Multiplying a shape's dimensions by a scale factor between 0 and 1 makes the shape larger.

27. Two maps of the same area use scales 1 : 10 000 and 1 : 25 000. Which map would show more detail? Explain using the scale numbers.

28. Priya says, "A scale factor of 1 means the shape stays exactly the same size." Is Priya correct? Explain.

29. A rectangle is enlarged by scale factor 3, then the image is enlarged again by scale factor 2. What single scale factor would have the same overall effect?

30. **True or false?** A scale of 1 : 1000 means a real object is 1000 times bigger than its drawing. Explain your answer.

## Problem (AO3)

31. Archer builds a scale model of a London bus at scale 1 : 24. The real bus is 12 m long. How long is the model, in cm?

32. An architect's plan of a house uses scale factor 1/50. A wall is drawn as 6 cm long on the plan. Find the real length of the wall, in metres.

33. A model railway uses scale 1 : 76. A model carriage is 25 cm long. Find the length of the real carriage, in metres.

34. A tourist map of central London uses scale 1 : 20 000. Sloan measures 8 cm between the Tower of London and St Paul's Cathedral. Find the real distance, in km.

35. A scale model of the Eiffel Tower uses scale factor 1/300. The model is 110 cm tall. Find the height of the real tower, in metres.

36. A floor plan uses scale factor 1/40. A bedroom wall is 3.2 m long in real life. How long should it be drawn on the plan, in cm?

37. Two scale drawings of the same bridge use scale factors 1/100 and 1/250. On the first drawing the bridge is 24 cm long. Find its length on the second drawing.

## Stretch

38. A model of a dinosaur skeleton is built at scale 1 : 15. The model is 1.2 m tall. Find the height of the real skeleton, in metres.

39. A scale factor of enlargement is written as the ratio 5 : 2 (image : original). Write this as a decimal scale factor and use it to find the new length of an 8 cm original.

40. A cartographer wants a real distance of 45 km to appear as 9 cm on a new map. What scale, in the form 1 : n, should the map use?

41. A model bridge uses a 1/120 scale. The model uses 40 cm of wire for one cable. How much wire, in metres, would the real cable need?

42. Design your own scale-model problem using a scale factor between 1 : 10 and 1 : 1000. State the scale, a model length, and calculate the real length.
