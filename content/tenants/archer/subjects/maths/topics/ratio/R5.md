## Fluency (AO1)

1. A map has scale 1 cm : 2 km. Two places are 5 cm apart on the map. How far apart are they in real life?

2. A plan uses scale 1 cm : 5 m. A room is 8 cm long on the plan. How long is the real room?

3. Real distance is 12 km. On a map with scale 1 cm : 3 km, how many centimetres apart should the places be?

4. A recipe for 4 people uses 200 g flour. Write the scale factor from 4 people to 8 people.

5. A scale drawing of a toy bus is 6 cm long. The real bus is 12 m long. What is the scale of the drawing in the form 1 cm : … m?

6. A map has scale 1 cm : 10 km. Two towns are 4.5 cm apart on the map. What is the real distance between them?

7. A model car has scale 1 : 20. If the model is 8 cm long, how long is the real car in metres?

8. A floor plan uses scale 1 cm : 2 m. A wall on the plan measures 3.5 cm. How long is the real wall?

9. A map has scale 1 cm : 50 km. What distance on the map, in centimetres, represents a real distance of 325 km?

10. A recipe for 3 people uses 150 g of rice. Write the scale factor needed to make enough rice for 12 people.

11. A scale model of a building uses scale 1 cm : 25 m. The real building is 175 m tall. How tall is the model?

## Reasoning (AO2)

12. On a London tourist map the scale is 1 cm : 500 m. Alex measures 3 cm between two Tube stations. Alex says the real distance is 15 km. **Is Alex correct?** Show your working and explain.

13. **True or false?** If you double every length on a scale drawing, the real distances also double. Explain your answer in one or two sentences.

14. A map says 1 : 25 000. What does the “1” represent and what does the “25 000” represent?

15. Priya says that a scale of 1 cm : 1 m means the drawing is “life size.” Is Priya correct? Explain what "life size" would actually mean.

16. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Changing a map's scale from 1 cm : 5 m to 1 cm : 10 m makes the map show **more** detail. Explain your reasoning.

17. A scale drawing of a garden measures 20 cm by 15 cm and uses a scale of 1 cm : 4 m. Sam says the real garden's perimeter is 35 m. **Is Sam correct?** Show your working.

18. Two maps show the same park. Map A has scale 1 cm : 100 m and Map B has scale 1 cm : 50 m. On which map would the park's drawing be bigger? Explain your reasoning using the scale numbers.

## Problem (AO3)

19. On a map of central London, scale 1 cm : 250 m, the distance from the London Eye to Big Ben is 4 cm. How far is the real walk?

20. A cycle route map uses 1 cm : 1 km. The route from Hyde Park Corner to Tower Bridge is 7.5 cm on the map. How many kilometres is the ride?

21. A recipe for 6 scones uses 300 ml milk. You want to make 18 scones for a trip picnic. How much milk do you need?

22. A school minibus follows a road map with scale 1 cm : 20 km. The distance from school to the museum is 6.5 cm on the map. How far is the real journey?

23. Archer is drawing a scale map of the playground for a school project. The real playground is 40 m long, and Archer uses scale 1 cm : 5 m. How long should Archer draw the playground on paper?

24. A model railway uses scale 1 : 76. A real train carriage is 19 m long. How long is the model carriage, in centimetres?

25. Sloan's biscuit recipe makes 10 biscuits using 250 g of butter. Sloan wants to make 35 biscuits. How much butter is needed?

## Stretch

26. A rectangular playground is 45 m by 30 m. Draw a scale plan that fits in a 9 cm by 6 cm rectangle. Write the scale you used as 1 cm : … m.

27. Two maps of the same street show the same real distance as 2 cm on Map A (1 cm : 400 m) and as 5 cm on Map B. What is the scale of Map B in the form 1 cm : … m?

28. A scale model of the Eiffel Tower is built at 1 : 300. If the real tower is 330 m tall, how tall is the model, in centimetres?

29. Two scale drawings of the same field use different scales. On the first drawing (scale 1 cm : 20 m) the field is 12 cm long. On the second drawing the field is 8 cm long. Find the scale of the second drawing.

30. Design a scale plan for your bedroom, choosing a sensible scale so it fits on an A4 page (about 28 cm by 20 cm). State the scale you chose and the real length of one wall using it.
