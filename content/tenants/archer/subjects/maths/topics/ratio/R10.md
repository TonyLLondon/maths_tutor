## Fluency (AO1)

1. y is directly proportional to x. When x = 4, y = 20. Find the constant of proportionality, k, in y = kx.
2. Using y = 5x, find y when x = 7.
3. y is directly proportional to x. When x = 3, y = 12. Find y when x = 8.
4. y is inversely proportional to x. When x = 2, y = 15. Find the constant, k, in y = k/x.
5. Using y = 30/x, find y when x = 5.
6. y is inversely proportional to x. When x = 4, y = 9. Find y when x = 6.
7. a is directly proportional to b. When a = 10, b = 2. Find a when b = 7.
8. p is inversely proportional to q. When p = 8, q = 3. Find p when q = 12.
9. Which equation shows direct proportion: y = 3x or y = 3/x?
10. Write the equation for "y is inversely proportional to x" using a constant, k.
11. Write the equation for "y is directly proportional to x" using a constant, k.

## Reasoning (AO2)

12. Mia says that in direct proportion, when x doubles, y also doubles. Is she correct? Explain.

13. **Always, sometimes, or never?** In inverse proportion, when x doubles, y halves.

14. **True or false?** y = kx + 2 represents direct proportion. Explain.

15. Priya says that if y is inversely proportional to x, then x × y is always the same value. Is she correct? Explain.

16. Explain the difference between direct and inverse proportion, using one example of each.

17. Sam says "the more workers on a job, the less time it takes" is an example of direct proportion. Is Sam correct? What type of proportion is it really?

18. Leo has a table: x = 1, y = 12; x = 2, y = 6; x = 3, y = 4. Is this direct or inverse proportion? Explain how you know.

## Problem (AO3)

19. The cost of apples is directly proportional to the number bought. 5 apples cost £2. Find the cost of 12 apples.

20. The time to fill a pool is inversely proportional to the number of hoses used. 2 hoses take 6 hours. How long would 3 hoses take?

21. The weight of a metal bar is directly proportional to its length. A 4 m bar weighs 20 kg. Find the weight of a 7 m bar.

22. For a fixed distance, speed is inversely proportional to the time taken. At 40 mph a trip takes 3 hours. How long would it take at 60 mph?

23. It takes 6 painters 8 days to paint a building. Assuming inverse proportion, how many days would 4 painters take?

24. The cost of hiring a bus is shared equally, so the cost per person is inversely proportional to the number of people. For 10 people, the cost per person is £15. Find the cost per person for 25 people.

25. The extension of a spring is directly proportional to the force applied. A force of 6 N stretches a spring 9 cm. Find the extension for a force of 10 N.

## Stretch

26. p and q are in inverse proportion. When p = 5, q = 8. Find q when p = 20.

27. A quantity y is directly proportional to x. When x increases by 50%, by what percentage does y increase?

28. A quantity y is inversely proportional to x. When x increases by 100% (doubles), what happens to y?

29. Explain how you could tell, from a table of x and y values, whether the relationship is direct proportion, inverse proportion, or neither.

30. Design your own real-life direct or inverse proportion problem. State which type it is, and solve it.
