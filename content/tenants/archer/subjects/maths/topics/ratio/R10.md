## Getting started

1. If 1 apple costs £2, how much do 2 apples cost?
2. In direct proportion, if one quantity doubles, what happens to the other?
3. Using y = 2x, find y when x = 4.
4. In y = 3x, what is the constant of proportionality?
5. If 2 workers take 6 hours in an inverse relationship, would more workers take more or less time?
6. Using y = 12/x, find y when x = 3.
7. Which equation is direct proportion: y = 5x or y = 5/x?
8. Which equation is inverse proportion: y = 8x or y = 8/x?
9. Using y = 4x, find y when x = 5.
10. Using y = 24/x, find y when x = 6.
11. y is directly proportional to x. When x = 2, y = 10. Find y when x = 5.
12. y is inversely proportional to x. When x = 4, x × y = 20. Find y when x = 5.

## Fluency (AO1)

13. y is directly proportional to x. When x = 4, y = 20. Find the constant of proportionality, k, in y = kx.
14. Using y = 5x, find y when x = 7.
15. y is directly proportional to x. When x = 3, y = 12. Find y when x = 8.
16. y is inversely proportional to x. When x = 2, y = 15. Find the constant, k, in y = k/x.
17. Using y = 30/x, find y when x = 5.
18. y is inversely proportional to x. When x = 4, y = 9. Find y when x = 6.
19. a is directly proportional to b. When a = 10, b = 2. Find a when b = 7.
20. p is inversely proportional to q. When p = 8, q = 3. Find p when q = 12.
21. Which equation shows direct proportion: y = 3x or y = 3/x?
22. Write the equation for "y is inversely proportional to x" using a constant, k.
23. Write the equation for "y is directly proportional to x" using a constant, k.

## Reasoning (AO2)

24. Mia says that in direct proportion, when x doubles, y also doubles. Is she correct? Explain.

25. **Always, sometimes, or never?** In inverse proportion, when x doubles, y halves.

26. **True or false?** y = kx + 2 represents direct proportion. Explain.

27. Priya says that if y is inversely proportional to x, then x × y is always the same value. Is she correct? Explain.

28. Explain the difference between direct and inverse proportion, using one example of each.

29. Sam says "the more workers on a job, the less time it takes" is an example of direct proportion. Is Sam correct? What type of proportion is it really?

30. Leo has a table: x = 1, y = 12; x = 2, y = 6; x = 3, y = 4. Is this direct or inverse proportion? Explain how you know.

## Problem (AO3)

31. The cost of apples is directly proportional to the number bought. 5 apples cost £2. Find the cost of 12 apples.

32. The time to fill a pool is inversely proportional to the number of hoses used. 2 hoses take 6 hours. How long would 3 hoses take?

33. The weight of a metal bar is directly proportional to its length. A 4 m bar weighs 20 kg. Find the weight of a 7 m bar.

34. For a fixed distance, speed is inversely proportional to the time taken. At 40 mph a trip takes 3 hours. How long would it take at 60 mph?

35. It takes 6 painters 8 days to paint a building. Assuming inverse proportion, how many days would 4 painters take?

36. The cost of hiring a bus is shared equally, so the cost per person is inversely proportional to the number of people. For 10 people, the cost per person is £15. Find the cost per person for 25 people.

37. The extension of a spring is directly proportional to the force applied. A force of 6 N stretches a spring 9 cm. Find the extension for a force of 10 N.

## Stretch

38. p and q are in inverse proportion. When p = 5, q = 8. Find q when p = 20.

39. A quantity y is directly proportional to x. When x increases by 50%, by what percentage does y increase?

40. A quantity y is inversely proportional to x. When x increases by 100% (doubles), what happens to y?

41. Explain how you could tell, from a table of x and y values, whether the relationship is direct proportion, inverse proportion, or neither.

42. Design your own real-life direct or inverse proportion problem. State which type it is, and solve it.
