## Fluency (AO1)

1. Write the ratio 3 : 5 as a fraction: "the first amount is ___ of the second amount."
2. There are 8 red counters and 4 blue counters. Write the number of red counters as a fraction of the number of blue counters.
3. A rope is 6 m long and another rope is 15 m long. Write the length of the shorter rope as a fraction of the longer rope.
4. A bag has 10 apples and 4 pears. Write the number of pears as a fraction of the number of apples.
5. A small cake weighs 200 g and a large cake weighs 800 g. Write the weight of the small cake as a fraction of the large cake.
6. Write the fraction 3/4 as a ratio.
7. Write the ratio 5 : 2 as "the first number is ___ times the second number."
8. £45 is compared to £15. Write £45 as a fraction of £15, simplified.
9. A tree is 2/3 the height of a lamppost. Write this comparison as a ratio, tree : lamppost.
10. Sam is 4/5 as tall as his older brother. Write this comparison as a ratio.
11. Write the ratio 9 : 12 in its simplest form, then write it as a fraction: "first amount is ___ of second amount."

## Reasoning (AO2)

12. Mia says that if A is 2/5 of B, then the ratio A : B is 2 : 5. Is she correct? Explain.

13. A model car is 1/20 the length of the real car. Priya says this means the ratio model : real is 1 : 20. Is she correct?

14. **Always, sometimes, or never?** If quantity A is written as a fraction of quantity B, that fraction must be less than 1.

15. Two numbers are in the ratio 3 : 7. Leo says the smaller number is 3/7 of the larger number. Is Leo correct? Explain using the ratio.

16. **True or false?** "A is 3/4 of B" means the same as "B is 4/3 of A." Explain your answer.

17. Archer says that a boy being "half as tall" as his dad is the same as writing the ratio boy : dad = 1 : 2. Is Archer correct?

18. Sloan has £20 and her brother has £50. Write Sloan's money as a fraction of her brother's money, and explain what the fraction tells you.

## Problem (AO3)

19. Archer's paper aeroplane flew 4 m. Sloan's flew 10 m. Write Archer's distance as a fraction of Sloan's distance, in simplest form.

20. In a school of 900 pupils, 360 are in Key Stage 2. Write the number of Key Stage 2 pupils as a fraction of the whole school, in simplest form.

21. A recipe uses 150 g sugar and 450 g flour. Write the amount of sugar as a fraction of the amount of flour, in simplest form.

22. A London bus route is 18 km long. A shorter local route is 6 km long. Write the length of the local route as a fraction of the bus route, in simplest form.

23. A child ticket costs £6 and an adult ticket costs £15. Write the cost of a child ticket as a fraction of an adult ticket, in simplest form.

24. Team A scored 24 points and Team B scored 18 points. Write Team B's score as a fraction of Team A's score, in simplest form.

25. A tank holds 40 litres when full. Right now it has 25 litres. Write the amount in the tank as a fraction of the full amount, in simplest form.

## Stretch

26. A photo is enlarged so the new width is 7/4 of the original width. If the original width was 12 cm, find the new width.

27. Two ribbons are in the ratio 5 : 8. Write the length of the shorter ribbon as a fraction of the longer, then find the shorter ribbon's length if the longer ribbon is 40 cm.

28. A number, N, is 3/8 of another number, M. If N = 27, find M.

29. In a fruit bowl, the number of apples is 2/3 of the number of oranges. There are 18 oranges. How many apples are there, and what is the ratio apples : oranges in simplest form?

30. Design your own comparison of two real-life quantities (money, distance, or weight). Express one as a fraction of the other, and also write it as a ratio in simplest form.
