## Getting started

1. A car travels 10 miles in 1 hour. Find its speed.
2. A runner covers 20 metres in 4 seconds. Find the speed.
3. Complete: speed = distance ÷ ___.
4. At 3 miles per hour for 2 hours, how far do you travel?
5. A 2 kg bag costs £4. Find the price per kg.
6. Complete: density = mass ÷ ___.
7. A 6 kg object fills 2 m³. Find its density.
8. A 12-mile journey at 4 mph takes how many hours?
9. A car travels 30 miles in 2 hours. Find its speed in miles per hour.
10. A 5 kg object has volume 2 m³. Find its density in kg/m³.
11. At 60 mph for 0.5 hours, how far do you travel?
12. Complete: pressure = force ÷ ___.

## Fluency (AO1)

13. A car travels 120 miles in 2 hours. Find its average speed.
14. Find the speed of a runner who covers 100 m in 20 s.
15. A block of metal has mass 40 kg and volume 5 m³. Find its density.
16. A cyclist travels at 15 mph for 4 hours. Find the distance travelled.
17. A journey of 90 miles takes 3 hours. Find the average speed.
18. Find the price per kg if 4 kg of apples costs £6.
19. Convert a speed of 20 m/s to km/h (multiply by 3.6).
20. A car uses 40 litres of fuel to travel 320 miles. Find the fuel consumption in miles per litre.
21. A liquid has mass 250 g and volume 200 cm³. Find its density in g/cm³.
22. Find the time taken to travel 150 miles at 50 mph.
23. Find the cost of 3.5 kg of cheese at £8 per kg.

## Reasoning (AO2)

24. Mia says speed = distance ÷ time always gives units like "miles per hour" or "m/s." Is she correct? Explain what these units tell you.

25. **Always, sometimes, or never?** If you know an object's density and mass, you can find its volume.

26. **True or false?** Converting different pack sizes to "price per kg" (or per litre) is a fair way to compare their value. Explain.

27. Priya travels 60 miles in 1.5 hours and says her speed was 40 mph. Is she correct?

28. Explain why speed, density and price per unit are all called "compound units."

29. Sam has 2 litres of a liquid weighing 2.5 kg and says its density is 1.25 g/cm³. Check Sam's answer (2.5 kg = 2500 g, 2 litres = 2000 cm³).

30. Leo says a car that goes faster always uses more fuel per mile. Is this necessarily true? Explain.

## Problem (AO3)

31. On the M25, a car travels at an average speed of 65 mph for 2.5 hours. Find the distance travelled.

32. A train travels 180 km in 1 hour 30 minutes. Find its average speed in km/h.

33. A gold bar has mass 1.93 kg and volume 100 cm³. Find its density in g/cm³.

34. A shop sells rice in two sizes: 500 g for £1.20, or 2 kg for £4.40. Find the price per kg for each, and say which is better value.

35. Archer runs 400 m in 80 seconds. Find his average speed in m/s, then convert this to km/h.

36. A tank contains 15 litres of a chemical weighing 18 kg. Find its density in kg per litre.

37. A plane flies 2,400 miles in 4 hours. Find its average speed, then find how long a 3,600-mile flight would take at the same speed.

## Stretch

38. A car travels the first 60 miles of a journey at 30 mph and the next 60 miles at 60 mph. Find the average speed for the whole 120-mile journey.

39. Two blocks have the same mass but different volumes: Block A is 8 cm³ and Block B is 20 cm³. Which is denser, and by what factor?

40. A runner's pace is given as 5 minutes per km. Convert this into a speed in km/h.

41. Explain why "price per kg" is a compound unit, and describe how you would use it to find the cheapest of three different pack sizes of the same product.

42. Design your own compound-unit problem (speed, density, or price per unit), and solve it.
