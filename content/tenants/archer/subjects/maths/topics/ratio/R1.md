## Fluency (AO1)

1. Share 12 counters in the ratio 1 : 1. How many does each person get?
2. Share 18 counters in the ratio 2 : 1.
3. Share 20 in the ratio 3 : 2.
4. A total of 24 is shared in the ratio 1 : 3. How much is the smaller share?
5. Write in ratio form: “For every 2 red counters there are 5 blue counters.”

## Reasoning (AO2)

6. Mia says that sharing 30 in the ratio 2 : 3 means the two parts are 12 and 18. Is Mia correct? **Show your working** and explain in one sentence.

7. A bar model shows two bars labelled A and B in the ratio 4 : 1. The bar for A is 16 cm long on your paper. How long should the bar for B be? **Explain** how you know.

8. **Always, sometimes, or never?** When you share a whole number in a ratio, each part is a whole number. Give an example if it is *sometimes* true.

## Problem (AO3)

9. On a school trip to London, coach seats are booked for adults and children in the ratio 1 : 4. There are 6 adult seats. How many child seats are booked? How many seats altogether?

10. At the London Eye, a group buys souvenir badges and keyrings in the ratio 3 : 2. They buy 15 badges. How many keyrings do they buy?

11. Lunch vouchers for the trip are shared between Year 5 and Year 6 pupils in the ratio 5 : 7. Year 5 receive 25 vouchers. How many vouchers does Year 6 receive? What is the total number of vouchers?

## Stretch

12. £84 is shared between three friends in the ratio 2 : 3 : 5. How much does each friend get?

13. Write your own word problem about a London trip that uses the ratio 4 : 3 and a total of 35 items. Include the answer on a separate line.
