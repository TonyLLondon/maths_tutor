## Getting started

1. Two children share 10 counters equally. How many counters does each child get?
2. Complete: 1 : 1 means both parts are ___.
3. There are 2 red beads and 3 blue beads. Write the ratio red : blue.
4. How many parts are there altogether in the ratio 2 : 3?
5. Share 8 sweets in the ratio 1 : 1.
6. In the ratio 1 : 4, which is the larger part: the first or the second?
7. Complete the equivalent ratio: 1 : 2 = 2 : ___.
8. Share 12 stickers in the ratio 1 : 2. How many stickers is one ratio part worth?

## Fluency (AO1)

9. Share 12 counters in the ratio 1 : 1. How many does each person get?
10. Share 18 counters in the ratio 2 : 1.
11. Share 20 in the ratio 3 : 2.
12. A total of 24 is shared in the ratio 1 : 3. How much is the smaller share?
13. Write in ratio form: “For every 2 red counters there are 5 blue counters.”
14. Share 15 sweets in the ratio 2 : 3.
15. Share 40 marbles in the ratio 3 : 5.
16. Write the ratio 8 : 12 in its simplest form.
17. Write the ratio 15 : 25 in its simplest form.
18. Share £50 in the ratio 1 : 4.
19. A necklace uses blue and green beads in the ratio 2 : 3. There are 10 blue beads. How many green beads are there?

## Reasoning (AO2)

20. Mia says that sharing 30 in the ratio 2 : 3 means the two parts are 12 and 18. Is Mia correct? **Show your working** and explain in one sentence.

21. A bar model shows two bars labelled A and B in the ratio 4 : 1. The bar for A is 16 cm long on your paper. How long should the bar for B be? **Explain** how you know.

22. **Always, sometimes, or never?** When you share a whole number in a ratio, each part is a whole number. Give an example if it is *sometimes* true.

23. Ravi shares 21 stickers between himself and his sister in the ratio 3 : 4 and says his sister ends up with fewer stickers than him. Is Ravi correct? Show your working.

24. **True or false?** If a ratio is written 1 : 1, both shares must be equal, no matter what the total is. Explain your answer.

25. Sloan says that to find the value of "one part" of a ratio, you divide the total by the **number of people**, not by the **number of parts**. Is Sloan correct? Give an example where these two are different.

26. Explain why the ratio 3 : 2 is equivalent to the ratio 6 : 4. Use the word "scale" or "multiply" in your answer.

## Problem (AO3)

27. On a school trip to London, coach seats are booked for adults and children in the ratio 1 : 4. There are 6 adult seats. How many child seats are booked? How many seats altogether?

28. At the London Eye, a group buys souvenir badges and keyrings in the ratio 3 : 2. They buy 15 badges. How many keyrings do they buy?

29. Lunch vouchers for the trip are shared between Year 5 and Year 6 pupils in the ratio 5 : 7. Year 5 receive 25 vouchers. How many vouchers does Year 6 receive? What is the total number of vouchers?

30. Archer and Sloan share a bag of 45 conkers in the ratio 4 : 5. How many conkers does each person get?

31. A fruit drink recipe mixes juice and water in the ratio 2 : 7. How much juice and how much water are needed to make 18 litres of drink altogether?

32. A class of 32 pupils is split into a maths group and an art group in the ratio 3 : 5. How many pupils are in each group?

33. At the school fair, raffle tickets and tombola tickets are sold in the ratio 7 : 3. If 70 raffle tickets were sold, how many tombola tickets were sold, and how many tickets were sold in total?

## More practice

34. Share 10 counters equally between two children. How many does each child get?

35. Share 12 sweets in the ratio 1 : 2. How many sweets does the person with the smaller share get?

36. Share 20 stickers in the ratio 3 : 2. How many stickers does the person with the larger share get?

37. Red and blue counters are in the ratio 2 : 3. If there are 10 red counters, how many blue counters are there?

38. Share 36 points in the ratio 1 : 2 : 3. How many points does the person with the middle share get?

39. Archer and Sloan share £45 in the ratio 2 : 3. How much does Sloan get?

## Stretch

40. £84 is shared between three friends in the ratio 2 : 3 : 5. How much does each friend get?

41. Write your own word problem about a London trip that uses the ratio 4 : 3 and a total of 35 items. Include the answer on a separate line.

42. A bag of 72 sweets is shared in the ratio 3 : 4 : 5. How many sweets does the person with the **largest** share get?

43. Are the ratios 2 : 5 and 4 : 10 equivalent? Explain how you can check this by simplifying or scaling.

44. A mixture of flour, sugar, and butter is made in the ratio 6 : 3 : 2. If the total mixture weighs 220 g, how much of each ingredient is used?
