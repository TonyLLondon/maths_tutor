## Fluency (AO1)

1. A class was asked, “What is your favourite fruit?” The answers were: apple, banana, apple, orange, banana, apple, grape, banana, apple. Complete a tally chart for **apple**, **banana**, **orange**, and **grape**.
2. From question 1, which fruit was chosen **most often**? How many chose it?
3. Here is a bar chart title: **Favourite pet** (Cat 8, Dog 5, Fish 3, Rabbit 4). Which pet is the **least** popular? How many more chose cats than fish?
4. The table shows books read last month: **0 books — 2 pupils**, **1 book — 5 pupils**, **2 books — 4 pupils**, **3 books — 1 pupil**. How many pupils took part altogether?
5. A tally shows |||| (four single marks). How many does this represent?
6. A tally shows a full gate of five marks plus two more marks. What number does this tally represent?
7. In a survey, 15 pupils chose swimming, 9 chose football, and 6 chose tennis as their favourite sport. How many pupils were surveyed in total?
8. A pictogram uses one symbol to represent 2 pupils. If a row has 5 symbols, how many pupils does that row represent?
9. A frequency table shows: red 6, blue 9, green 3, yellow 2. What is the total frequency?
10. A bar chart shows rainfall (mm) for a week: Mon 4, Tue 0, Wed 8, Thu 2, Fri 6. On which day did it rain the most? How many more mm fell on that day than on Thursday?
11. A frequency table for pupils' shoe colours shows: black 14, white 5, other 3. How many pupils are **not** wearing black shoes?

## Reasoning (AO2)

12. Sam's bar chart has a vertical axis that goes 0, 10, 20, 30 but the tallest bar only reaches **12**. Sam says the chart is wrong. Do you agree? Explain.

13. **True or false?** If every bar in a bar chart is the same height, then everyone chose a different category. Explain your answer.

14. A pictogram uses a symbol worth 4 pupils, but one row has half a symbol. Sloan says this must mean 2 pupils. Is Sloan correct? Explain.

15. **Always, sometimes, or never?** A pie chart and a bar chart showing the same data will have the same shape. Explain your answer.

16. Archer says a tally chart and a frequency table show exactly the same information, just in a different way. Is Archer correct? Explain.

17. A survey asks pupils how they travel to school, and each pupil can only choose one method. Priya says the total of all the frequencies must equal the number of pupils surveyed. Is Priya correct? Explain why or why not.

18. Two bar charts show the same data, but one has a vertical axis starting at 0 and the other starts at 10. Explain why the second chart might make the differences between bars look bigger than they really are.

## Problem (AO3)

19. On the walk to school, Archer counts types of transport in 20 minutes: **car 12**, **bus 3**, **bike 4**, **walk 1**. Draw a bar chart for this data (label axes and give your chart a title). Which type did Archer see most?

20. Sloan surveys a class of 28 pupils on their favourite school subject: Maths 8, Art 6, PE 10, Science 4. Which subject is most popular? How many more pupils chose PE than Art?

21. A tuck shop records sales over a week: Mon 12, Tue 15, Wed 9, Thu 20, Fri 18. What is the total number of sales for the week? On which day were sales lowest?

22. Sloan surveys 24 friends about pets: 10 have a dog, 6 have a cat, and 5 have a fish. The rest have no pet. How many friends have no pet?

23. A school recorded the number of book club attendees each week for 4 weeks: 15, 18, 12, 21. Describe how attendance changed week to week, and find the total attendance over the 4 weeks.

24. A tally records eye colours in Archer's class: brown |||| |||| ||, blue |||| |, green |||. Convert the tallies to numbers and find the total number of pupils.

25. A tally chart is used to record how pupils travel to school from this list: walk, car, bike, walk, bus, car, walk, bike, walk, car. Find the frequency of each method and the total number of pupils.

## Stretch

26. A pictogram key states that one symbol represents 5 pupils. A category has 17 pupils. Explain how you would show this on the pictogram, including the idea of a part-symbol.

27. Explain the difference between "discrete" data (like number of siblings) and "continuous" data (like height). Give one more example of each.

28. A frequency table has categories A, B, C, and D. The frequencies must add up to 40. If A = 12, B = 9, and C = 11, find D. Then explain which chart type — bar chart or pie chart — you think would present this data most clearly, and why.

29. Write **three** survey questions you could ask your class. For one question, say whether answers would be **numbers** or **categories**, and how you would present the results (tally, table, or bar chart).

30. Design your own investigation: pick a topic to survey your class about, decide whether the data will be discrete or categorical, and say which chart you would use to present the results.
