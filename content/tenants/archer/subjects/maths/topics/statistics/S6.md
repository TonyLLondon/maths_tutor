## Getting started

1. As you grow taller, your shoe size usually gets bigger too. When one goes up, does the other usually go up as well? Answer yes or no.
2. The more it rains, the less time people spend in the park. When rain goes up, does time in the park usually go down? Answer yes or no.
3. On a scatter graph, dots that rise from left to right show ___ correlation. (Write positive or negative.)
4. A straight line drawn through a scatter graph to show the trend is called a line of best ___?
5. One point on a graph that sits far away from the main pattern is called an ___?
6. If the dots on a scatter graph show no clear pattern, is there correlation? Answer yes or no.
7. A line of best fit goes through $(0, 0)$ and $(2, 4)$. When $x$ increases by 1, $y$ increases by ___?
8. A line of best fit goes through $(0, 10)$ and $(10, 20)$. Estimate $y$ when $x = 5$.
9. On a scatter graph, points fall from top-left to bottom-right. Is this positive or negative correlation?
10. A line of best fit passes through (0, 0) and (4, 8). What is its gradient?
11. A line of best fit passes through (0, 5) and (10, 15). Estimate y when x = 5.
12. True or false: strong correlation on a scatter graph always means one variable causes the other.

## Fluency (AO1)

13. A scatter graph shows points rising from bottom-left to top-right. What type of correlation does this show?
14. A scatter graph shows points falling from top-left to bottom-right. What type of correlation does this show?
15. A scatter graph shows points scattered with no clear pattern. What type of correlation (or lack of it) does this show?
16. What is the name of the straight line drawn through a scatter graph to show the trend of the data?
17. A scatter graph plots hours of revision against test score, showing positive correlation. What does this suggest about the relationship between the two variables?
18. A scatter graph plots outdoor temperature against hot chocolate sales, showing negative correlation. What does this suggest?
19. A line of best fit passes through the points $(0, 10)$ and $(10, 30)$. Find the gradient of this line.
20. Using the line of best fit from Q19, estimate the $y$-value when $x = 5$.
21. A scatter graph has one point far away from the main pattern of the data. What is this point called?
22. Should you include an outlier when drawing a line of best fit? Explain briefly.
23. What does it mean to use a line of best fit to estimate a value **within** the range of the given data (interpolation)?
24. What does it mean to use a line of best fit to estimate a value **outside** the range of the given data (extrapolation)?

## Reasoning (AO2)

25. Mia says a scatter graph with points close to a straight line shows strong correlation. Explain what "strong" correlation means, using the idea of how close the points are to the line.

26. **True or false?** Correlation between two variables always means that one variable causes the other to change.

27. Ravi says extrapolation (estimating far outside the range of data) is always just as reliable as interpolation (estimating within the range). Explain why this is not true.

28. **Always, sometimes, or never?** A scatter graph with no correlation can still have a line of best fit drawn on it.

29. Priya finds a scatter graph showing a strong positive correlation between ice cream sales and the number of drownings at beaches, and says "more ice cream sales cause more drownings." Explain why her conclusion is flawed, and suggest what might really be causing both to rise.

30. Explain why an outlier should usually be ignored when drawing a line of best fit, but should not simply be deleted from the data set without a good reason.

31. Sloan says a line of best fit must always pass exactly through at least two of the plotted points. Explain why this is not necessary.

32. Explain the difference between positive, negative, and no correlation, using an example situation for each.

## Problem (AO3)

33. A scatter graph plots the age of a car (years) against its value (£1000s): the line of best fit passes through $(2, 18)$ and $(8, 6)$. Find the gradient of this line, and describe what it represents about how the car's value changes with age.

34. Using the line of best fit from Q33, estimate the value of a 5-year-old car.

35. A scatter graph plots hours studied against exam score, with a line of best fit passing through $(0, 40)$ and $(10, 90)$. Estimate the exam score for a pupil who studies for 6 hours.

36. A scatter graph shows a strong negative correlation between a car's speed and its fuel efficiency (miles per gallon) above a certain speed. Describe, in a sentence, what this relationship suggests for drivers wanting to save fuel.

37. A scientist plots temperature (°C) against the number of ice creams sold, for temperatures between 10°C and 30°C, and finds a line of best fit. Explain why using this line to estimate ice cream sales at 45°C (extrapolation) would be unreliable.

38. Two variables, plant height and number of leaves, are plotted on a scatter graph, showing positive correlation but with one point far from the trend (a very tall plant with very few leaves). Explain how you would treat this point when describing the correlation and drawing a line of best fit.

## Stretch

39. A scatter graph of 10 data points has a line of best fit passing through $(1, 5)$ and $(9, 45)$. Find the equation of the line of best fit in the form $y = mx + c$.

40. Using the equation found in Q39, estimate the value of $y$ when $x = 6$, and state whether this is interpolation or extrapolation.

41. Explain why two variables can show a strong correlation on a scatter graph without one variable actually causing the other to change, giving an example other than ice cream and drownings.

42. Design your own scatter graph scenario with two variables that you would expect to show positive correlation, and two variables you would expect to show negative correlation. Explain your reasoning for each.
