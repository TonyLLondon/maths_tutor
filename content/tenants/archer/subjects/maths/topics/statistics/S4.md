## Fluency (AO1)

1. Find the **mean** of: 4, 6, 8, 2.
2. Find the **mean** of: 5, 5, 5, 5.
3. Five pupils scored these marks on a quiz: 7, 9, 6, 8, 10. What is the mean mark?
4. The mean of 3, 7, and □ is 6. What is □?

## Reasoning (AO2)

5. Two classes have the same **mean** score on a test. Class A’s scores are all close to the mean; Class B’s scores are spread out. Which class would you expect to have a **higher** highest score? Explain in one or two sentences.

6. **Always, sometimes, or never?** The mean of a list of whole numbers is always a whole number. Give an example if it is *sometimes* true.

## Problem (AO3)

7. Archer records how many minutes four friends spent reading yesterday: **20, 25, 15, 30**. What is the mean reading time in minutes? If Archer also read for **40** minutes, what is the new mean for all five children?

## Stretch

8. Four numbers have a mean of 12. Three of the numbers are 10, 14, and 11. What is the fourth number?
