## Fluency (AO1)

1. What does it mean for two shapes to be **congruent**?
2. Name the congruence rule where all three sides of one triangle match all three sides of another.
3. Name the congruence rule where two sides and the angle *between* them match.
4. Name the congruence rule where two angles and a matching side match.
5. Name the congruence rule for right-angled triangles where the right angle, hypotenuse, and one other side match.
6. **True or false?** If two triangles have the same three angles, they must be congruent.
7. Two triangles have sides 3 cm, 4 cm, 5 cm and 3 cm, 4 cm, 5 cm. Which congruence rule proves they are congruent?
8. Two triangles both have a 50° angle sitting exactly between sides of 6 cm and 8 cm. Which rule proves they are congruent?
9. If two triangles are congruent, are their corresponding angles always equal?
10. If two triangles are congruent, are their areas always equal?
11. Triangle A has angles 40°, 60°, 80°. Triangle B also has angles 40°, 60°, 80° but is bigger. Are A and B congruent?
12. Which of these is **not** a valid congruence rule: SSA, SSS, SAS, ASA, RHS?

## Reasoning (AO2)

13. Two right-angled triangles both have a hypotenuse of 10 cm and one other side of 6 cm. Explain which rule proves they are congruent, then find the missing third side (use 10² − 6² and take the square root).

14. Explain the difference between **congruent** shapes and **similar** shapes.

15. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Two triangles with the same perimeter are congruent.

16. Two triangles share a side of 7 cm. Both have a 90° angle at one end of that side and a 40° angle at the other end. Which congruence rule proves they are congruent?

17. Sam says, "SSA (two sides and a non-included angle) proves congruence, just like SAS." Is Sam correct? Explain why SSA is not generally a reliable test.

18. A triangle is reflected to form its mirror image. Explain why the original triangle and its reflection are congruent.

19. Using an example, explain why AAA (three matching angles) does **not** prove two triangles are congruent.

20. Two isosceles triangles both have equal sides of 5 cm and a base angle of 70°, but different base lengths. Are they congruent? Explain.

## Problem (AO3)

21. A carpenter checks two triangular brackets. Both have sides 15 cm, 20 cm, 25 cm. Which rule proves they are identical in shape and size?

22. Two triangular shadows are both right-angled, with hypotenuse 13 m and one other side 5 m. Find the missing third side for both shadows, and name the congruence rule that shows they are congruent.

23. Archer draws two triangles, ABC and DEF, where AB = DE, angle A = angle D, and AC = DF. Which congruence rule applies here, and are the triangles necessarily congruent?

24. A survey team measures a triangular plot of land twice, getting the same two angles (55° and 65°) and the same included side (40 m) both times. Which rule confirms both measurements describe an identical plot?

25. Two triangles have sides 6 cm, 6 cm, 10 cm and 6 cm, 6 cm, 8 cm. Are these two triangles congruent? Explain.

26. Two congruent right-angled triangular window panes are joined along their hypotenuses to form a rectangle. Each pane has legs of 30 cm and 40 cm. The hypotenuse is a well-known Pythagorean triple result — what length is it?

## Stretch

27. RHS (right angle, hypotenuse, side) can be thought of as a special version of SSS. Explain why, using the fact that the third side of a right-angled triangle can always be worked out from the other two.

28. Sketch (or describe) two triangles that have the *same area* but are **not** congruent. Explain why equal area alone cannot prove congruence.

29. Two triangles share all three matching angles (AAA) but have different side lengths. What single word describes this relationship, if not "congruent"?

30. Design your own pair of triangles with given side and/or angle measurements, and state which congruence rule (if any) proves them congruent.
