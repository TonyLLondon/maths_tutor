## Fluency (AO1)

1. An isosceles triangle has two equal angles of 55° each. Find the third angle.
2. A triangle has one angle of 90° and another of 35°. Find the third angle.
3. On a straight line, one angle is 3x and the other is 2x. Find x, then find each angle.
4. A quadrilateral has three angles of 80°, 95°, and 100°. Find the fourth angle.
5. Two angles around a point are 145° and 110°. Find the third angle around that point.
6. An equilateral triangle has three equal sides. What are all three of its angles?
7. An isosceles triangle has a base angle of 72°. Find its apex (top) angle.
8. Two parallel lines are cut by a transversal, creating co-interior angles of 3x and x. Find x, then find each angle.
9. A right-angled triangle has one other angle of 28°. Find the third angle.
10. Vertically opposite angles are equal. If one is (2x + 10)° and the opposite one is 50°, find x.
11. Each interior angle of a square measures how many degrees?
12. In an isosceles triangle, the angles opposite the two equal sides are called __________ angles.

## Reasoning (AO2)

13. A triangle has angles in the ratio 2 : 3 : 4. Find all three angles.

14. Explain why the base angles of an isosceles triangle must always be equal, using the idea of folding the triangle along its line of symmetry.

15. A kite has two equal angles of 100° each, and one further angle of 45°. Find the fourth angle.

16. **Always, sometimes, or never?** In a triangle, the angle opposite the longest side is the largest angle.

17. A triangle has angles x, (x + 20)°, and (x + 40)°. Find x, then find all three angles.

18. Sam says, "The exterior angle of a triangle at one vertex is always 180° minus the interior angle at that same vertex." Is Sam correct? Explain, using angles on a straight line.

19. A parallelogram has one angle of x° and an adjacent angle of (2x + 30)°, which are co-interior angles (they add to 180°). Find x, then find both angles.

20. An isosceles triangle's apex angle is three times the size of one base angle. Find all three angles.

## Problem (AO3)

21. A roof truss is an isosceles triangle with an apex angle of 40°. Find each base angle.

22. A parallelogram-shaped path has one angle of (4x + 10)° and the co-interior angle next to it is (2x + 20)°. Find x, then find both angles.

23. Three paths meet at a point in a park, making angles in the ratio 3 : 4 : 5. Find each angle.

24. A bridge truss triangle has a right angle and one other angle of 34°. Find the third angle, then say whether the triangle is isosceles.

25. A kite has two equal angles of 115° each. The other two angles are in the ratio 3 : 2. Find both of the other angles.

26. A trapezium-shaped field has two co-interior angles that must add to 180° along one non-parallel side. If one of them is 95°, find the other.

## Stretch

27. Explain why, in any triangle, the exterior angle at one vertex always equals the sum of the interior angles at the other two vertices.

28. A regular pentagon has interior angles that add up to 540° and are all equal. Find the size of each interior angle.

29. In triangle ABC, angle A = 2x, angle B = 3x, and angle C = 4x. Find x, find all three interior angles, then find the exterior angle at C and check it equals angle A + angle B.

30. Write your own multi-step angle problem that combines at least two facts (for example, parallel lines with a triangle, or an isosceles triangle with a quadrilateral), and solve it.
