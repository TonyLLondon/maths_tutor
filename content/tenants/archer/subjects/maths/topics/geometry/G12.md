## Getting started

1. Name the 3D shape that looks like a ball.
2. Name the 3D shape with 6 equal square faces.
3. Name the 3D shape with two circular ends and one curved surface.
4. How many faces does a cube have?
5. How many vertices (corners) does a cube have?
6. Which can roll easily: a sphere or a cube?
7. A square-based pyramid has what shape on its base?
8. How many circular faces does a cylinder have?

## Fluency (AO1)

9. Name the flat surfaces of a 3D shape.
10. Name the line where two faces of a 3D shape meet.
11. Name the point where edges meet.
12. How many faces does a cube have?
13. How many edges does a cube have?
14. How many vertices does a cube have?
15. How many faces does a triangular prism have?
16. How many faces does a square-based pyramid have?
17. Name the 3D shape with one circular face, one curved surface, and a point at the top.
18. Name the 3D shape with two circular faces joined by one curved surface.
19. Name the 3D shape with no flat faces at all.
20. **True or false?** A prism has two identical (congruent) end faces and the same cross-section all along its length.


## More fluency

21. How many faces does a cube have?

22. How many edges does a triangular prism have?

## Reasoning (AO2)

23. A cuboid has 6 faces, 12 edges, and 8 vertices. Check Euler's formula, faces + vertices − edges = 2, using these numbers.

24. Explain the difference between a prism and a pyramid.

25. A hexagonal prism has 8 faces, 18 edges, and 12 vertices. Verify Euler's formula for this solid.

26. **Always, sometimes, or never?** For any prism, the number of edges is 3 times the number of sides on its cross-section (test it on a triangular prism, a cuboid, and a hexagonal prism).

27. A square-based pyramid has 5 faces, 8 edges, and 5 vertices. Verify Euler's formula for this solid.

28. Sam says, "A cylinder is a kind of prism, because it has two identical circular ends and the same cross-section all the way through." Is Sam correct?

29. A triangular prism has how many rectangular faces, and how many triangular faces?

30. Explain why a cone is **not** a prism, even though it has one circular face.

## Problem (AO3)

31. A tent is shaped like a triangular prism (2 triangular ends, 3 rectangular sides). State its number of faces, edges, and vertices.

32. A gift box is a cube with edge length 5 cm. State the number of faces, edges, and vertices, then find the total length of all its edges added together.

33. A model house has a cuboid base topped by a triangular-prism roof, joined so that one rectangular face of each solid disappears where they meet. The cuboid alone has 6 faces and the roof alone has 5 faces. Find the total number of visible faces on the combined solid.

34. A regular octahedron has 8 triangular faces, 12 edges, and 6 vertices. Verify Euler's formula for this solid.

35. A pentagonal prism has a 5-sided cross-section. Using the pattern from question 16, predict its number of edges, then state its number of faces and vertices.

36. A square-based pyramid sits on top of a cube of matching base size, joined face to face (one face from each disappears). The cube has 6 faces and the pyramid has 5 faces. Find the total number of faces on the combined solid.

## Stretch

37. A solid has 12 faces and 20 vertices. Use Euler's formula (faces + vertices − edges = 2) to find its number of edges.

38. A prism has an n-sided polygon as its cross-section. Write general formulas, in terms of n, for its number of faces, edges, and vertices.

39. Use your formulas from question 28 to predict the number of faces, edges, and vertices of a prism with a 10-sided (decagon) cross-section, then check your answer using Euler's formula.

40. Design your own 3D solid (a simple one, or one made by combining simpler solids), state its number of faces, edges, and vertices, and check Euler's formula.


## Mixed practice

41. Name a 3D shape with 1 curved surface and 0 edges.

42. A cuboid is 3 cm × 4 cm × 5 cm. How many faces are rectangles?
