## Fluency (AO1)

1. A rectangle is 8 cm long and 5 cm wide. Work out its **perimeter**.
2. The same rectangle (8 cm by 5 cm). Work out its **area**.
3. Write the formula for the area of a rectangle using **length** and **width**.
4. A square has side length 6 m. Find its perimeter and its area.
5. Change 200 cm to metres. Change 3 m² to cm² (remember: 1 m = 100 cm).
6. An L-shape is made from two rectangles: one is 10 cm by 4 cm, the other is 6 cm by 4 cm (they share the 4 cm side). Find the **total area** of the L-shape.
7. Write the formula for the perimeter of a rectangle using **length** and **width**.
8. A square has area 49 cm². What is the length of each side?
9. A rectangle has length 12 cm and area 60 cm². Find its width.
10. Find the perimeter of a regular pentagon with each side 7 cm.
11. A rectangular field is 40 m by 25 m. Find its perimeter in metres.
12. Change 5 m to cm. Change 15 000 cm² to m².

## Reasoning (AO2)

13. Two rectangles each have area 24 cm². Rectangle A is 6 cm by 4 cm. Rectangle B is 12 cm by 2 cm. Do they have the same **perimeter**? Show working for both perimeters.

14. **Always, sometimes, or never?** If you double the length and width of a rectangle, you double its area. Test with the rectangle 3 cm by 2 cm.

15. Sam finds the perimeter of a 7 cm by 3 cm rectangle by calculating $7 \times 3 = 21$ cm. Explain Sam’s mistake and give the correct perimeter.

16. Two shapes have the same perimeter of 20 cm. Shape A is a square. Shape B is a rectangle 8 cm by 2 cm. Do they have the same area? Show your working.

17. Priya says a shape with a bigger perimeter always has a bigger area. Give an example to show she is wrong.

18. A rectangle’s length is doubled but its width stays the same. What happens to its perimeter? What happens to its area? Explain using an example.

19. Leo says that all rectangles with area 36 cm² have the same perimeter. Test this with two different rectangles of area 36 cm².

20. **True or false?** A square is the rectangle with the smallest perimeter for a given area. Test with a 6 cm × 6 cm square compared with a 9 cm × 4 cm rectangle (both area 36 cm²).

## Problem (AO3)

21. A classroom wall is covered with rectangular posters. Each poster is 50 cm wide and 70 cm tall. How much **wall area** do three posters cover altogether? Give your answer in cm².

22. A garden path is an L-shape. The whole shape fits in a 9 m by 6 m rectangle, but a 3 m by 2 m corner is grass (not path). The rest is path. Work out the **area of the path** in m². Sketch the L-shape and label the measurements you use.

23. A school football pitch is 90 m long and 45 m wide. A groundsperson needs to walk around the pitch twice to mark the lines. How far do they walk in total?

24. Carpet costs £12 per m². How much would it cost to carpet a rectangular room that is 4 m by 3.5 m?

25. A picture frame is a rectangle 30 cm by 20 cm. A 2 cm wide wooden border runs around the outside, so the framed picture (border included) is 34 cm by 24 cm. Find the **area of just the wooden border**.

26. A rectangular allotment, 12 m by 8 m, is divided into two equal rectangular plots by a fence down the middle, parallel to the shorter (8 m) side. Find the area of one plot and the length of the new dividing fence.

## Stretch

27. A rectangle has perimeter 30 cm and length 10 cm. Find its width and its area.

28. Design a compound shape made from two rectangles with **total area** 48 cm². Write the dimensions you chose and show how you calculated the area.

29. A rectangle has area 72 cm². Find two different possible pairs of whole-number dimensions, and work out the perimeter for each.

30. A square is cut into 4 identical smaller squares. If the original square has side length 8 cm, find the perimeter of one of the small squares.
