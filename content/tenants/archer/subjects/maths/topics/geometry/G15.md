## Fluency (AO1)

1. What piece of equipment is used to measure the length of a line segment?
2. What piece of equipment is used to measure the size of an angle?
3. A line segment measures 7.4 cm on a ruler. Write this length to the nearest whole centimetre.
4. An angle is measured with a protractor as 65°. Is this acute, right, obtuse, or reflex?
5. An angle measures 120°. Is this acute, right, obtuse, or reflex?
6. An angle measures 200°. Is this acute, right, obtuse, or reflex?
7. An angle measures exactly 90°. What is this type of angle called?
8. Draw (in words, describing your method) how you would use a protractor to measure an angle accurately.
9. Two line segments measure 3.2 cm and 5.7 cm. Find the difference in their lengths.
10. A line segment is measured as 12 cm using a ruler marked in millimetres. Give this length in millimetres.
11. An angle is estimated to be about 45°, but a protractor reading gives 47°. Find the difference between the estimate and the actual measurement.
12. What is a bearing measured from, and in which direction is it measured (clockwise or anticlockwise)?

## Reasoning (AO2)

13. Mia measures a line segment as "about 8", without giving units. Explain why this measurement is incomplete.

14. **True or false?** All angles between 90° and 180° are obtuse.

15. Ravi says he measured an angle as 370°. Explain why this cannot be correct for a single angle measurement using a standard protractor placed once.

16. A student places a ruler starting at the 1 cm mark (not 0 cm) and reads the length as "9 cm" at the other end, without subtracting. Explain the mistake, and find the correct length.

17. **Always, sometimes, or never?** A reflex angle is bigger than a straight-line angle (180°).

18. Priya says all bearings must be written using exactly 3 digits, for example writing 60° as "060°". Explain why this convention exists.

19. Explain the difference between an angle of elevation and an angle measured going clockwise from North (a bearing).

20. Sloan measures the two shorter sides of a right-angled triangle as 3 cm and 4 cm. Explain how she could measure the angle between them to check it is 90°, using a protractor.

## Problem (AO3)

21. A rectangle has a diagonal that measures 13 cm when checked with a ruler, and its two sides measure 5 cm and 12 cm. Does this match a right-angled triangle relationship (check informally, without using Pythagoras' theorem directly)?

22. A ship sails on a bearing of 075°. Describe, using a diagram in words, where North is and how the bearing angle of 075° is measured from it.

23. Archer draws a triangle and measures its three angles as 50°, 65°, and 70° using a protractor. Find the sum of these measured angles, and explain whether this is a sensible measurement for a triangle.

24. A map uses a scale where 1 cm represents 2 km. A route is measured as 8.5 cm on the map. Find the real-life distance.

25. Sloan measures an angle as 130° using a protractor, but she reads the wrong scale and initially writes 50°. Explain the mistake (using the two scales on a protractor) and confirm which reading, 130° or 50°, matches an obtuse angle.

26. A bearing of a lighthouse from a boat is measured as 250°. Find the bearing of the boat from the lighthouse (the back bearing), explaining your method.

## Stretch

27. Explain why measuring an angle correctly requires lining up the centre point of the protractor exactly with the vertex (corner) of the angle, using an example of what goes wrong if it is not aligned.

28. A surveyor measures a line segment as 45.6 m using a scale drawing where 1 cm represents 5 m. Find the length of the line segment on the scale drawing, in cm.

29. Two bearings are measured from the same point: bearing A is 040° and bearing B is 160°. Find the angle between the two bearings.

30. Design your own scale drawing scenario (state the scale, e.g. "1 cm represents 4 m") and a real-life length. Work out the length that should be drawn on the scale diagram.
