## Fluency (AO1)

1. Area of a triangle = 1/2 × base × height. Find the area of a triangle with base 8 cm and height 5 cm.
2. Area of a parallelogram = base × height. Find the area of a parallelogram with base 6 cm and height 4 cm.
3. A triangle has base 10 cm and height 6 cm. Find its area.
4. A parallelogram has base 9 cm and height 5 cm. Find its area.
5. Area of a trapezium = 1/2 × (a + b) × height, where a and b are the parallel sides. A trapezium has parallel sides 4 cm and 8 cm, and height 3 cm. Find its area.
6. How many unit cubes (1 cm cubes) fit into one layer of a cuboid that is 4 cubes long and 3 cubes wide?
7. The same cuboid is 2 cubes high. Find its volume in cm³.
8. Volume of a cuboid = length × width × height. Find the volume of a cuboid 5 cm × 2 cm × 2 cm.
9. Volume of a prism = area of cross-section × length. A triangular prism has a cross-sectional area of 12 cm² and length 10 cm. Find its volume.
10. Volume of a cylinder = π × radius² × height. Find the volume of a cylinder with radius 3 cm and height 10 cm (use π ≈ 3.14, round to the nearest whole number).
11. A cube has edge length 4 cm. Find its volume.
12. A triangle has base 12 cm and area 30 cm². Find its height.

## Reasoning (AO2)

13. Explain why the area formula for a parallelogram (base × height) works, thinking about cutting a triangle off one end and moving it to the other to make a rectangle.

14. A trapezium has parallel sides a and b, and height h. Using the idea of splitting it into a rectangle and a triangle (or two triangles), explain why its area formula is 1/2 × (a + b) × h.

15. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Doubling the base of a triangle doubles its area, if the height stays the same.

16. A parallelogram and a rectangle have the same base and the same height. Compare their areas.

17. Sam says, "A prism's volume is always length × width × height, just like a cuboid." Is Sam correct? Explain using a triangular prism.

18. Two cuboids both have volume 12 cm³. One is 6 × 2 × 1 cm. Give different whole-number dimensions for another cuboid with the same volume.

19. A cylinder and a cuboid have the same height. Explain what determines which one has the larger volume.

20. Leo says, "Doubling both the base and height of a triangle doubles its area." Is Leo correct? Explain using an example.

## Problem (AO3)

21. A triangular garden bed has base 6 m and height 4 m. Find its area.

22. A parallelogram-shaped field has base 25 m and height 12 m. Find its area.

23. A trapezium-shaped plot has parallel sides 15 m and 9 m, and height 8 m. Find its area.

24. A triangular prism tent has a triangular cross-section of area 3.5 m² and is 6 m long. Find its volume.

25. A cylindrical water tank has radius 2 m and height 5 m. Find its volume, using π ≈ 3.14, rounded to 1 decimal place.

26. A toy box shaped like a cuboid is 40 cm long, 30 cm wide, and 25 cm high. Find its volume, then convert your answer to litres (1000 cm³ = 1 litre).

## Stretch

27. A triangle has area 45 cm² and height 9 cm. Find its base.

28. A composite shape is made from a rectangle 8 cm × 5 cm with a triangle of base 8 cm and height 3 cm attached on top. Find the total area.

29. A cylinder has volume 785 cm³ (using π ≈ 3.14) and height 10 cm. Find its radius.

30. Explain, using the idea of stacking identical cross-sections on top of each other, why the volume formula for any right prism is always "area of cross-section × length" — whether the cross-section is a triangle, rectangle, trapezium, or circle (a cylinder).
