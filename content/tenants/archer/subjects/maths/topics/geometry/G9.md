## Getting started

1. Name the round 2D shape with no corners.
2. What is the point in the exact middle of a circle called?
3. True or false? Every point on the edge of a circle is the same distance from its centre.
4. A line goes from the centre to the edge of a circle. Is it a radius or a diameter?
5. A line goes across a circle through its centre. Is it a radius or a diameter?
6. A radius is 3 cm. A diameter is two radii. Find the diameter.
7. A diameter is 10 cm. Find the radius.
8. What word names the outside edge of a circle?

## Fluency (AO1)

9. Name the middle point of a circle.
10. Name the distance from the centre to the edge of a circle.
11. Name the distance straight across a circle, through its centre.
12. Complete: diameter = 2 × __________.
13. Name the distance all the way around a circle.
14. Name a straight line that touches a circle at exactly one point, without crossing it.
15. Name a straight line joining two points on a circle's circumference.
16. Name a part of a circle's circumference between two points.
17. Name the "pizza slice" region enclosed between two radii and an arc.
18. Name the region enclosed between a chord and an arc.
19. **True or false?** A diameter is also a chord.
20. What angle does a tangent always make with the radius drawn to the point where it touches the circle?


## More fluency

21. What is the name of a line segment from the centre to the circle?

22. What is a chord?

## Reasoning (AO2)

23. A circle has radius 6 cm. Find its diameter.

24. Explain why a tangent to a circle is always perpendicular to the radius drawn to the point of contact.

25. A chord divides a circle into two regions, each made from the chord and an arc. What are these two regions called?

26. **Always, sometimes, or never?** A diameter of a circle is the longest possible chord.

27. Two tangents are drawn to a circle from the same point outside the circle. What is always true about the lengths of these two tangents?

28. Sam says, "A radius and a chord are the same thing." Is Sam correct? Explain the difference between them.

29. A sector has an angle of 90° at the centre of the circle. What fraction of the whole circle is this sector?

30. Explain the difference between a **segment** and a **sector** of a circle.

## Problem (AO3)

31. A circular table has diameter 1.4 m. Find its radius.

32. A cyclist rides once around a perfectly circular track. Name the circle feature that matches the path traced by their front tyre.

33. A satellite dish has two support struts, both tangent to the dish's circular rim, meeting at a point outside the dish. One strut is 1.6 m long. Using the equal tangents property, find the length of the other strut.

34. A pizza is cut into 6 equal sector slices from the centre. Find the angle at the centre of each slice.

35. A clock's minute hand is a radius of length 8 cm. Find the length of the diameter drawn through the centre and the tip of the hand's opposite point.

36. A running track has a semicircular end. Name the circle feature that describes the straight line running across the flat side of that semicircle, through the centre.

## Stretch

37. Explain, using the tangent–radius right angle, why the shortest distance from the centre of a circle to a tangent line is always exactly equal to the radius.

38. Two circles have radii 5 cm and 3 cm and touch each other externally at a single point (they don't overlap). Find the distance between their two centres.

39. A circle has radius 10 cm. A chord is drawn 6 cm from the centre. Using the right-angled triangle formed by the radius, half the chord, and the 6 cm distance, find the full length of the chord.

40. Design your own circle diagram using at least three circle features (centre, radius, diameter, chord, tangent, arc, sector, segment) and describe how they relate to each other.


## Mixed practice

41. A radius is 5 cm. What is the diameter?

42. Name the line that touches a circle at exactly one point.
