## Getting started

1. A semicircle is what fraction of a full circle?
2. A quarter-circle is what fraction of a full circle?
3. How many quarter-circles make one full circle?
4. How many semicircles make one full circle?
5. The curved part of a circle’s edge between two points is called an arc. Complete the word: a__.
6. A slice of a circle is called a sector. Which everyday object looks like a sector: a pizza slice or a football?
7. A 90° turn is what fraction of a full 360° turn?
8. A 180° turn is what fraction of a full 360° turn?

## Reasoning (AO2)

23. Mia says a sector with angle 90° is always a quarter of the circle's area, no matter the radius. Explain why she is correct.

24. **True or false?** The arc length of a sector depends only on the angle, not on the radius.

25. Ravi finds the area of a 180° sector by using the full circle formula $\pi r^2$ without adjusting for the angle. Explain his mistake.

26. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Doubling the radius of a sector (keeping the angle the same) doubles the arc length.

27. Priya says doubling the radius of a sector doubles its area. Explain why this is incorrect, and describe what actually happens.

28. A sector has angle 270°. Sloan says this means the sector is smaller than half the circle. Explain why she is wrong.

29. Explain why the formula for arc length uses $\frac{\theta}{360}$ multiplied by the full circumference, using the idea of a fraction of a full turn.

30. Leo says the perimeter of a sector is just the arc length. Explain what he has forgotten to include, and describe the full perimeter.

## Problem (AO3)

31. A pizza slice is a sector with angle 45° cut from a pizza of radius 15 cm. Find the area of the pizza slice (π ≈ 3.14), to 1 decimal place.

32. A garden sprinkler sprays water over a sector of angle 120° with radius 5 m. Find the area of grass watered (π ≈ 3.14).

33. A door swings open through an angle of 90°, sweeping out a sector shape with radius (the door's width) 0.8 m. Find the arc length swept by the edge of the door (π ≈ 3.14), to 2 decimal places.

34. A windscreen wiper sweeps through an angle of 150° with a length of 40 cm. Find the area it cleans (π ≈ 3.14), to the nearest cm².

35. A sector has radius 10 cm and arc length 15.7 cm (π ≈ 3.14). Find the angle of the sector, to the nearest degree.

36. Find the perimeter of a sector with radius 6 cm and angle 60° (π ≈ 3.14), to 1 decimal place (remember to include the two straight radii).

## Stretch

37. A sector has area 25.12 cm² and radius 8 cm (π ≈ 3.14). Find the angle of the sector, to the nearest degree.

38. Two sectors have the same angle of 90°. One has radius 4 cm, the other has radius 8 cm. Find the ratio of their areas, and explain how this connects to the idea of area scale factors.

39. A cake is cut into 8 equal sectors from a circular cake of radius 12 cm. Find the arc length of one slice's curved edge (π ≈ 3.14), to 1 decimal place.

40. A sector has a perimeter of 30 cm and radius 8 cm. Find the arc length, then find the angle of the sector, to the nearest degree (π ≈ 3.14).


## Mixed practice

41. Find the area of a sector radius 10 cm, angle 72° (π ≈ 3.14).

42. A sector has radius 9 cm and arc length 6.28 cm. Find the angle (π ≈ 3.14).
