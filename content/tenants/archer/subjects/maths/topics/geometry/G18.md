## Fluency (AO1)

1. A sector has angle 90° in a circle of radius 4 cm. What fraction of the full circle is this sector?
2. A sector has angle 180° in a circle of radius 6 cm. What fraction of the full circle is this?
3. Find the arc length of a sector with angle 90° in a circle of radius 4 cm (π ≈ 3.14).
4. Find the area of the sector in Q3.
5. A sector has angle 60° in a circle of radius 9 cm. What fraction of the full circle is this?
6. Find the arc length of the sector in Q5 (π ≈ 3.14).
7. Find the area of the sector in Q5 (π ≈ 3.14).
8. A sector has angle 45° in a circle of radius 8 cm. What fraction of the full circle is this?
9. Find the area of the sector in Q8 (π ≈ 3.14).
10. A sector has angle 120° in a circle of radius 6 cm. Find its arc length (π ≈ 3.14).
11. Find the area of the sector in Q10 (π ≈ 3.14).
12. A sector has angle 30° in a circle of radius 12 cm. Find its arc length (π ≈ 3.14).

## Reasoning (AO2)

13. Mia says a sector with angle 90° is always a quarter of the circle's area, no matter the radius. Explain why she is correct.

14. **True or false?** The arc length of a sector depends only on the angle, not on the radius.

15. Ravi finds the area of a 180° sector by using the full circle formula $\pi r^2$ without adjusting for the angle. Explain his mistake.

16. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Doubling the radius of a sector (keeping the angle the same) doubles the arc length.

17. Priya says doubling the radius of a sector doubles its area. Explain why this is incorrect, and describe what actually happens.

18. A sector has angle 270°. Sloan says this means the sector is smaller than half the circle. Explain why she is wrong.

19. Explain why the formula for arc length uses $\frac{\theta}{360}$ multiplied by the full circumference, using the idea of a fraction of a full turn.

20. Leo says the perimeter of a sector is just the arc length. Explain what he has forgotten to include, and describe the full perimeter.

## Problem (AO3)

21. A pizza slice is a sector with angle 45° cut from a pizza of radius 15 cm. Find the area of the pizza slice (π ≈ 3.14), to 1 decimal place.

22. A garden sprinkler sprays water over a sector of angle 120° with radius 5 m. Find the area of grass watered (π ≈ 3.14).

23. A door swings open through an angle of 90°, sweeping out a sector shape with radius (the door's width) 0.8 m. Find the arc length swept by the edge of the door (π ≈ 3.14), to 2 decimal places.

24. A windscreen wiper sweeps through an angle of 150° with a length of 40 cm. Find the area it cleans (π ≈ 3.14), to the nearest cm².

25. A sector has radius 10 cm and arc length 15.7 cm (π ≈ 3.14). Find the angle of the sector, to the nearest degree.

26. Find the perimeter of a sector with radius 6 cm and angle 60° (π ≈ 3.14), to 1 decimal place (remember to include the two straight radii).

## Stretch

27. A sector has area 25.12 cm² and radius 8 cm (π ≈ 3.14). Find the angle of the sector, to the nearest degree.

28. Two sectors have the same angle of 90°. One has radius 4 cm, the other has radius 8 cm. Find the ratio of their areas, and explain how this connects to the idea of area scale factors.

29. A cake is cut into 8 equal sectors from a circular cake of radius 12 cm. Find the arc length of one slice's curved edge (π ≈ 3.14), to 1 decimal place.

30. A sector has a perimeter of 30 cm and radius 8 cm. Find the arc length, then find the angle of the sector, to the nearest degree (π ≈ 3.14).
