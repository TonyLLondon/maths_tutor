## Fluency (AO1)

1. Write the column vector that describes a movement of 3 right and 2 up.
2. Write the column vector that describes a movement of 4 left and 1 up.
3. Write the column vector that describes a movement of 5 right and 3 down.
4. Point A is at $(2, 3)$. It is translated by the vector $\binom{4}{1}$. Find the coordinates of the image point.
5. Point B is at $(0, 0)$. It is translated by the vector $\binom{-3}{5}$. Find the coordinates of the image point.
6. Point C is at $(6, -2)$. It is translated by the vector $\binom{-2}{-4}$. Find the coordinates of the image point.
7. Point D is at $(-1, -1)$. It is translated by the vector $\binom{5}{2}$. Find the coordinates of the image point.
8. Point A is at $(1, 2)$ and its image after translation is $(5, 6)$. Find the column vector of the translation.
9. Point B is at $(3, 7)$ and its image after translation is $(3, 2)$. Find the column vector of the translation.
10. Point C is at $(-4, 2)$ and its image after translation is $(0, 0)$. Find the column vector of the translation.
11. What single word describes a transformation that slides a shape without turning or flipping it?
12. If a shape is translated by $\binom{0}{0}$, what happens to the shape?

## Reasoning (AO2)

13. Mia says that translating a shape by $\binom{2}{3}$ makes it move 2 up and 3 right. Explain her mistake.

14. **True or false?** Translation always produces an image that is congruent to the original shape.

15. Ravi translates point $(4, 5)$ by $\binom{-6}{2}$ and gets $(2, 7)$. Check his working and correct it if needed.

16. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Translating a shape changes its size or orientation.

17. Priya says the vector $\binom{3}{-3}$ describes the same movement as $\binom{-3}{3}$. Explain why she is wrong.

18. Explain how you would find the column vector describing the translation from point $A(2, 1)$ to point $B(7, 4)$.

19. Sloan translates a shape by $\binom{5}{0}$. Describe what happens to the shape's position, and explain why the $y$-coordinates of every point stay the same.

20. Leo says that translating a shape twice by the same vector $\binom{2}{1}$ is the same as translating it once by $\binom{4}{2}$. Is he correct? Explain.

## Problem (AO3)

21. Triangle A has vertices $(1, 1)$, $(3, 1)$, and $(1, 4)$. It is translated by the vector $\binom{2}{-3}$. Find the coordinates of the translated triangle's vertices.

22. A shape is translated by $\binom{4}{2}$ and then by $\binom{-1}{3}$. Find the single column vector that describes the overall (combined) translation.

23. A point starts at $(5, 5)$, is translated by $\binom{-2}{4}$, and then translated again by $\binom{3}{-6}$. Find the final coordinates of the point.

24. A robot on a grid starts at $(0, 0)$. It follows three translations in order: $\binom{3}{2}$, $\binom{-1}{4}$, $\binom{2}{-5}$. Find the robot's final position.

25. A quadrilateral has vertices $(0, 0)$, $(2, 0)$, $(2, 3)$, and $(0, 3)$. Find the coordinates after translation by $\binom{-3}{-1}$, then describe whether the shape's size and angles have changed.

26. The vector from point $P$ to point $Q$ is $\binom{6}{-2}$. If $P$ is at $(-1, 5)$, find the coordinates of $Q$.

## Stretch

27. Point $A(3, -2)$ is translated to point $B(-1, 6)$. Find the column vector of this translation, then find the vector that would translate $B$ back to $A$.

28. A shape is translated by vector $\binom{a}{b}$ so that the point $(2, -3)$ maps to $(6, 1)$. Find the values of $a$ and $b$.

29. A shape undergoes two translations, $\binom{x}{2}$ then $\binom{3}{y}$, and the overall effect is the same as a single translation of $\binom{7}{-1}$. Find the values of $x$ and $y$.

30. Design your own translation puzzle: choose a starting point and a column vector, and find the image point. Then find the vector that would translate the image back to the starting point.
