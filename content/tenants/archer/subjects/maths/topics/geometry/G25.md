## Getting started

1. An arrow shows a move 3 squares right. How many squares is the horizontal move?
2. An arrow shows a move 2 squares up. How many squares is the vertical move?
3. Move 1 right, then 2 more right. How far right have you moved altogether?
4. Move 4 up, then 1 down. How far up are you from the start?
5. What is 2 + 3?
6. What is 5 − 2?
7. A move has no horizontal change. What number describes its horizontal part?
8. Combine a move of 1 right with another move of 1 right. What is the total horizontal move?

## Fluency (AO1)

9. $\mathbf{a} = \binom{2}{3}$ and $\mathbf{b} = \binom{1}{4}$. Find $\mathbf{a} + \mathbf{b}$.
10. Using $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{b}$ from Q9, find $\mathbf{a} - \mathbf{b}$.
11. $\mathbf{a} = \binom{4}{-1}$. Find $2\mathbf{a}$.
12. $\mathbf{b} = \binom{-3}{6}$. Find $\frac{1}{3}\mathbf{b}$.
13. $\mathbf{a} = \binom{5}{2}$ and $\mathbf{b} = \binom{-2}{3}$. Find $\mathbf{a} + \mathbf{b}$.
14. Using $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{b}$ from Q13, find $2\mathbf{a} - \mathbf{b}$.
15. $\mathbf{c} = \binom{6}{-4}$. Find $-\mathbf{c}$.
16. $\mathbf{a} = \binom{1}{1}$ and $\mathbf{b} = \binom{3}{-2}$. Find $3\mathbf{a} + \mathbf{b}$.
17. $\mathbf{a} = \binom{2}{5}$. Find $-3\mathbf{a}$.
18. $\mathbf{a} = \binom{4}{2}$ and $\mathbf{b} = \binom{4}{2}$. Find $\mathbf{a} - \mathbf{b}$, and explain what this result means.
19. $\mathbf{a} = \binom{3}{0}$ and $\mathbf{b} = \binom{0}{5}$. Find $\mathbf{a} + \mathbf{b}$.
20. If $\mathbf{p} = 2\mathbf{q}$, and $\mathbf{q} = \binom{3}{1}$, find $\mathbf{p}$.


## More fluency

21. Add vectors $\begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix}$ and $\begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix}$.

22. Find $\begin{pmatrix} 5 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix} - \begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ 6 \end{pmatrix}$.

## Reasoning (AO2)

23. Mia says $\binom{2}{3} + \binom{1}{4} = \binom{3}{7}$ by adding the top numbers and then adding the bottom numbers separately. Explain why her method is correct.

24. **True or false?** $2\mathbf{a}$ always points in exactly the same direction as $\mathbf{a}$, just twice as long.

25. Ravi says $-\mathbf{a}$ has the same length as $\mathbf{a}$ but points in the opposite direction. Is he correct? Explain using an example.

26. **Always, sometimes, or never?** $\mathbf{a} + (-\mathbf{a}) = \binom{0}{0}$.

27. Priya says that if two vectors are scalar multiples of each other (like $\mathbf{a}$ and $2\mathbf{a}$), they must be parallel. Explain why this is true.

28. Explain the difference between $\mathbf{a} - \mathbf{b}$ and $\mathbf{b} - \mathbf{a}$, using an example with $\mathbf{a} = \binom{4}{1}$ and $\mathbf{b} = \binom{1}{3}$.

29. Sloan says $3\mathbf{a}$ means adding 3 to both parts of the vector $\mathbf{a}$. Explain her mistake, using $\mathbf{a} = \binom{2}{5}$.

30. In a diagram, $\overrightarrow{OA} = \mathbf{a}$ and $\overrightarrow{OB} = \mathbf{b}$. Explain how to find $\overrightarrow{AB}$ in terms of $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{b}$.

## Problem (AO3)

31. $\overrightarrow{OA} = \binom{3}{2}$ and $\overrightarrow{OB} = \binom{7}{5}$. Find $\overrightarrow{AB}$ (remember $\overrightarrow{AB} = \overrightarrow{OB} - \overrightarrow{OA}$).

32. In triangle $OAB$, $\overrightarrow{OA} = \mathbf{a}$ and $\overrightarrow{OB} = \mathbf{b}$. $M$ is the midpoint of $AB$. Show that $\overrightarrow{OM} = \mathbf{a} + \frac{1}{2}(\mathbf{b} - \mathbf{a})$, and simplify this expression.

33. $OABC$ is a parallelogram with $\overrightarrow{OA} = \mathbf{a}$ and $\overrightarrow{OC} = \mathbf{c}$. Write $\overrightarrow{OB}$ in terms of $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{c}$, explaining your reasoning.

34. $\mathbf{a} = \binom{6}{3}$ and $\mathbf{b} = \binom{2}{1}$. Show that $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{b}$ are parallel, by expressing $\mathbf{a}$ as a scalar multiple of $\mathbf{b}$.

35. A point $P$ divides the line from $A(2, 1)$ to $B(8, 7)$ so that $AP:PB = 1:2$. Using vectors, find the position vector of $P$ (find $\overrightarrow{OA} + \frac{1}{3}\overrightarrow{AB}$).

36. $\overrightarrow{AB} = \binom{4}{-2}$ and $\overrightarrow{BC} = \binom{-1}{5}$. Find $\overrightarrow{AC}$, and explain why $\overrightarrow{AC} = \overrightarrow{AB} + \overrightarrow{BC}$.

## Stretch

37. In triangle $OAB$, $\overrightarrow{OA} = \mathbf{a}$ and $\overrightarrow{OB} = \mathbf{b}$. $C$ is the point on $AB$ such that $AC:CB = 2:1$. Find $\overrightarrow{OC}$ in terms of $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{b}$.

38. $\mathbf{a} = \binom{2}{k}$ and $\mathbf{b} = \binom{6}{9}$. Given that $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{b}$ are parallel, find the value of $k$.

39. $OABC$ is a parallelogram with $\overrightarrow{OA} = \mathbf{a}$ and $\overrightarrow{OC} = \mathbf{c}$. $M$ is the midpoint of $AC$ (the diagonal). Show, using vectors, that $\overrightarrow{OM} = \frac{1}{2}(\mathbf{a} + \mathbf{c})$, and explain why this means the diagonals of a parallelogram bisect each other (since $M$ is also the midpoint of $OB$).

40. Design your own vector diagram problem involving a triangle or parallelogram labelled with vectors $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{b}$, and find an unknown vector (such as a midpoint or a side) in terms of $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{b}$.


## Mixed practice

41. If $\mathbf{a} = \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}$ and $\mathbf{b} = \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix}$, find $\mathbf{a} + \mathbf{b}$.

42. Parallelogram OABC has $\overrightarrow{OA} = \mathbf{a}$ and $\overrightarrow{OC} = \mathbf{c}$. Write $\overrightarrow{OB}$ in terms of $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{c}$.
