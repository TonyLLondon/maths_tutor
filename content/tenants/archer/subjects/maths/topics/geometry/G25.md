## Fluency (AO1)

1. $\mathbf{a} = \binom{2}{3}$ and $\mathbf{b} = \binom{1}{4}$. Find $\mathbf{a} + \mathbf{b}$.
2. Using $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{b}$ from Q1, find $\mathbf{a} - \mathbf{b}$.
3. $\mathbf{a} = \binom{4}{-1}$. Find $2\mathbf{a}$.
4. $\mathbf{b} = \binom{-3}{6}$. Find $\frac{1}{3}\mathbf{b}$.
5. $\mathbf{a} = \binom{5}{2}$ and $\mathbf{b} = \binom{-2}{3}$. Find $\mathbf{a} + \mathbf{b}$.
6. Using $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{b}$ from Q5, find $2\mathbf{a} - \mathbf{b}$.
7. $\mathbf{c} = \binom{6}{-4}$. Find $-\mathbf{c}$.
8. $\mathbf{a} = \binom{1}{1}$ and $\mathbf{b} = \binom{3}{-2}$. Find $3\mathbf{a} + \mathbf{b}$.
9. $\mathbf{a} = \binom{2}{5}$. Find $-3\mathbf{a}$.
10. $\mathbf{a} = \binom{4}{2}$ and $\mathbf{b} = \binom{4}{2}$. Find $\mathbf{a} - \mathbf{b}$, and explain what this result means.
11. $\mathbf{a} = \binom{3}{0}$ and $\mathbf{b} = \binom{0}{5}$. Find $\mathbf{a} + \mathbf{b}$.
12. If $\mathbf{p} = 2\mathbf{q}$, and $\mathbf{q} = \binom{3}{1}$, find $\mathbf{p}$.

## Reasoning (AO2)

13. Mia says $\binom{2}{3} + \binom{1}{4} = \binom{3}{7}$ by adding the top numbers and then adding the bottom numbers separately. Explain why her method is correct.

14. **True or false?** $2\mathbf{a}$ always points in exactly the same direction as $\mathbf{a}$, just twice as long.

15. Ravi says $-\mathbf{a}$ has the same length as $\mathbf{a}$ but points in the opposite direction. Is he correct? Explain using an example.

16. **Always, sometimes, or never?** $\mathbf{a} + (-\mathbf{a}) = \binom{0}{0}$.

17. Priya says that if two vectors are scalar multiples of each other (like $\mathbf{a}$ and $2\mathbf{a}$), they must be parallel. Explain why this is true.

18. Explain the difference between $\mathbf{a} - \mathbf{b}$ and $\mathbf{b} - \mathbf{a}$, using an example with $\mathbf{a} = \binom{4}{1}$ and $\mathbf{b} = \binom{1}{3}$.

19. Sloan says $3\mathbf{a}$ means adding 3 to both parts of the vector $\mathbf{a}$. Explain her mistake, using $\mathbf{a} = \binom{2}{5}$.

20. In a diagram, $\overrightarrow{OA} = \mathbf{a}$ and $\overrightarrow{OB} = \mathbf{b}$. Explain how to find $\overrightarrow{AB}$ in terms of $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{b}$.

## Problem (AO3)

21. $\overrightarrow{OA} = \binom{3}{2}$ and $\overrightarrow{OB} = \binom{7}{5}$. Find $\overrightarrow{AB}$ (remember $\overrightarrow{AB} = \overrightarrow{OB} - \overrightarrow{OA}$).

22. In triangle $OAB$, $\overrightarrow{OA} = \mathbf{a}$ and $\overrightarrow{OB} = \mathbf{b}$. $M$ is the midpoint of $AB$. Show that $\overrightarrow{OM} = \mathbf{a} + \frac{1}{2}(\mathbf{b} - \mathbf{a})$, and simplify this expression.

23. $OABC$ is a parallelogram with $\overrightarrow{OA} = \mathbf{a}$ and $\overrightarrow{OC} = \mathbf{c}$. Write $\overrightarrow{OB}$ in terms of $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{c}$, explaining your reasoning.

24. $\mathbf{a} = \binom{6}{3}$ and $\mathbf{b} = \binom{2}{1}$. Show that $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{b}$ are parallel, by expressing $\mathbf{a}$ as a scalar multiple of $\mathbf{b}$.

25. A point $P$ divides the line from $A(2, 1)$ to $B(8, 7)$ so that $AP:PB = 1:2$. Using vectors, find the position vector of $P$ (find $\overrightarrow{OA} + \frac{1}{3}\overrightarrow{AB}$).

26. $\overrightarrow{AB} = \binom{4}{-2}$ and $\overrightarrow{BC} = \binom{-1}{5}$. Find $\overrightarrow{AC}$, and explain why $\overrightarrow{AC} = \overrightarrow{AB} + \overrightarrow{BC}$.

## Stretch

27. In triangle $OAB$, $\overrightarrow{OA} = \mathbf{a}$ and $\overrightarrow{OB} = \mathbf{b}$. $C$ is the point on $AB$ such that $AC:CB = 2:1$. Find $\overrightarrow{OC}$ in terms of $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{b}$.

28. $\mathbf{a} = \binom{2}{k}$ and $\mathbf{b} = \binom{6}{9}$. Given that $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{b}$ are parallel, find the value of $k$.

29. $OABC$ is a parallelogram with $\overrightarrow{OA} = \mathbf{a}$ and $\overrightarrow{OC} = \mathbf{c}$. $M$ is the midpoint of $AC$ (the diagonal). Show, using vectors, that $\overrightarrow{OM} = \frac{1}{2}(\mathbf{a} + \mathbf{c})$, and explain why this means the diagonals of a parallelogram bisect each other (since $M$ is also the midpoint of $OB$).

30. Design your own vector diagram problem involving a triangle or parallelogram labelled with vectors $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{b}$, and find an unknown vector (such as a midpoint or a side) in terms of $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{b}$.
