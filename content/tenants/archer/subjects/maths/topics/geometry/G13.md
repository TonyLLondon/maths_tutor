## Getting started

1. Looking straight down at an object gives which view: top or front?
2. Looking straight at the front of an object gives which view?
3. A cube seen straight from the front looks like which 2D shape?
4. A cylinder standing upright looks like which 2D shape from above?
5. A ball looks like which 2D shape from any direction?
6. A stack is 3 cubes tall. How many cubes high is its side view?
7. Two cubes sit side by side. How many squares appear in the top view?
8. True or false? The top and front views of an object can look different.

## Fluency (AO1)

9. What is the name for the view of a 3D shape looking straight down from above?
10. What is the name for the view of a 3D shape looking straight at it from the front?
11. What is the name for the view of a 3D shape looking straight at it from the side?
12. A cube is viewed from the front. What 2D shape is its front elevation?
13. A cylinder standing upright is viewed from above (the plan). What 2D shape is seen?
14. The same upright cylinder is viewed from the front (the front elevation). What 2D shape is seen?
15. A cuboid measuring 4 cm by 3 cm by 2 cm stands with its 4 cm by 3 cm face on the table. Describe the shape of the plan view.
16. For the same cuboid (4 cm by 3 cm by 2 cm), describe the shape and dimensions of the front elevation, if the front face is 4 cm wide and 2 cm tall.
17. A square-based pyramid is viewed from directly above. What 2D shape is the plan?
18. A triangular prism lies on one of its rectangular faces. What 2D shape would you expect to see as the plan (looking down)?
19. How many different views (plan, front elevation, side elevation) are usually drawn to fully describe a 3D shape?
20. A sphere is viewed from any direction (plan, front, or side). What 2D shape is always seen?


## More fluency

21. What view is seen when you look straight down on a solid?

22. What view is seen when you look from the front?

## Reasoning (AO2)

23. Mia says that the plan, front elevation, and side elevation of a cube all look exactly the same. Explain why she is correct.

24. **True or false?** The front elevation and side elevation of a cuboid are always identical.

25. A solid is made of 3 cubes in a row on the table. Explain what the plan view would look like, and what the front elevation would look like if viewed along the row.

26. Ravi is given the plan, front elevation, and side elevation of a shape, all of which are rectangles of different sizes. What 3D shape is this most likely to be? Explain your reasoning.

27. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Two different 3D shapes can have the same plan view.

28. Explain why a single 2D view (just the plan, for example) is not always enough to identify a 3D shape uniquely, using an example of two different solids with the same plan.

29. A cone standing point-up is viewed from above (plan) and from the front (elevation). Describe each view and explain why they are different shapes.

30. Priya says that the front elevation of a triangular prism lying on its rectangular face is always a triangle. Explain when this is true and when it might not be.

## Problem (AO3)

31. A solid is built from 4 identical cubes stacked in a single vertical tower. Describe the plan view, the front elevation, and the side elevation.

32. A solid is made from two cuboids: a large one at the bottom (6 cm by 4 cm by 2 cm) and a smaller one on top (2 cm by 2 cm by 3 cm), centred on the large cuboid's top face. Describe what the front elevation would look like, including approximate measurements.

33. A staircase-shaped solid is built from 3 steps, each step made of one cube, with each cube 1 cm higher and 1 cm further back than the last. Describe the side elevation of this staircase.

34. A cylinder of radius 3 cm and height 8 cm stands upright. Sketch (in words) the plan and front elevation, including their key measurements.

35. A model house is made from a cuboid with a triangular prism roof on top. Describe the front elevation of the whole model, including the shapes you would see.

36. Given a plan view that is a circle, a front elevation that is a rectangle, and a side elevation that is a rectangle of the same size as the front, name the 3D solid and explain your reasoning.

## Stretch

37. A solid has a plan view of a rectangle, a front elevation of a triangle, and a side elevation of a triangle. Name a 3D solid that matches all three views, and explain your reasoning.

38. Explain how you could use the plan, front elevation, and side elevation together to work out the volume of a simple cuboid-based solid, without seeing the 3D shape itself.

39. Two different solids — a cube and a square-based pyramid with the same base — both have a square plan view. Explain how the front elevations of these two solids differ, and why.

40. Design your own solid made from combined cuboids (describe it in words, including measurements). Describe its plan, front elevation, and side elevation in detail.


## Mixed practice

41. Sketch the plan of an L-shaped block made from two cubes.

42. A solid has a circular plan and a rectangular front elevation. Name a possible solid.
