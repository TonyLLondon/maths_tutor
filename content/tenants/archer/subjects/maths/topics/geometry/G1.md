## Fluency (AO1)

1. Write the missing word: Two lines that never meet are __________ lines.
2. Write the missing word: Two lines that meet at a right angle are __________ lines.
3. How many sides does a triangle have? How many vertices (corners)?
4. Circle the quadrilaterals: square, triangle, rectangle, pentagon, rhombus.
5. A square has four equal sides and four equal angles. What is the size of each angle in a square?
6. Name this triangle: all three sides are different lengths. (scalene / isosceles / equilateral)
7. Name this triangle: two sides are equal in length. (scalene / isosceles / equilateral)
8. True or false? Every rectangle is a square.
9. Name this triangle: all three sides and all three angles are equal. (scalene / isosceles / equilateral)
10. How many sides does a pentagon have? How many vertices?
11. How many sides does a hexagon have?
12. Write the missing word: A four-sided shape is called a __________.

## Reasoning (AO2)

13. **Always, sometimes, or never?** A shape with four right angles is a rectangle. Give an example if it is *sometimes* true.

14. On grid paper, draw one pair of parallel lines and mark them with arrows. Draw one line perpendicular to one of them. Label **parallel** and **perpendicular**.

15. Maya says, “If a quadrilateral has two pairs of equal sides, it must be a rectangle.” Is Maya correct? Explain in one or two sentences.

16. Sam says a square is a special type of rectangle. Is Sam correct? Explain your answer.

17. **Always, sometimes, or never?** A triangle has at least two equal sides.

18. Explain the difference between a rhombus and a square, using their angles.

19. **True or false?** A regular polygon has all sides equal, but its angles can be different sizes. Explain your answer.

20. Leo draws a four-sided shape where opposite sides are parallel, but not all four sides are the same length. What is the best name for Leo’s shape?

## Problem (AO3)

21. Archer walks along a path that turns a corner. The corner is a right angle. Sketch the corner and mark the angle with a small square. Write one sentence describing what “perpendicular” means, using your sketch.

22. A floor tile is a regular hexagon (six equal sides). How many sides and how many vertices does it have? How is it different from a square?

23. A stop sign is shaped like a regular octagon. How many sides and how many vertices does it have?

24. A kite-shaped flag has two pairs of adjacent equal sides (not opposite sides). Sketch a kite shape and mark the two pairs of equal sides.

25. A rectangular playground marking is 6 m by 4 m, with one diagonal line drawn across it, splitting it into two triangles. What type of triangle is made on each side? Are the two triangles the same size and shape?

26. Archer sees a road sign shaped like an equilateral triangle. Write two facts you know about its sides and its angles.

## Stretch

27. Draw a quadrilateral that is **not** a rectangle but **has** exactly one pair of parallel sides. Write its name if you know it.

28. Write your own “Always, sometimes, or never?” question about triangles or quadrilaterals, then answer it.

29. Draw a hexagon that is **not** regular (its sides are not all equal). Explain how you can still tell it is a hexagon.

30. Sort these shapes into two groups — “always has a right angle” and “does not always have a right angle”: square, rectangle, rhombus, kite. Explain your sorting for each shape.
