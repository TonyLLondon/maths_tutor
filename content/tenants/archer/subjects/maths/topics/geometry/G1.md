## Fluency (AO1)

1. Write the missing word: Two lines that never meet are __________ lines.
2. Write the missing word: Two lines that meet at a right angle are __________ lines.
3. How many sides does a triangle have? How many vertices (corners)?
4. Circle the quadrilaterals: square, triangle, rectangle, pentagon, rhombus.
5. A square has four equal sides and four equal angles. What is the size of each angle in a square?
6. Name this triangle: all three sides are different lengths. (scalene / isosceles / equilateral)
7. Name this triangle: two sides are equal in length. (scalene / isosceles / equilateral)
8. True or false? Every rectangle is a square.

## Reasoning (AO2)

9. **Always, sometimes, or never?** A shape with four right angles is a rectangle. Give an example if it is *sometimes* true.

10. On grid paper, draw one pair of parallel lines and mark them with arrows. Draw one line perpendicular to one of them. Label **parallel** and **perpendicular**.

11. Maya says, “If a quadrilateral has two pairs of equal sides, it must be a rectangle.” Is Maya correct? Explain in one or two sentences.

## Problem (AO3)

12. Archer walks along a path that turns a corner. The corner is a right angle. Sketch the corner and mark the angle with a small square. Write one sentence describing what “perpendicular” means using your sketch.

13. A floor tile is a regular hexagon (six equal sides). How many sides and how many vertices does it have? How is it different from a square?

## Stretch

14. Draw a quadrilateral that is **not** a rectangle but **has** exactly one pair of parallel sides. Write its name if you know it.

15. Write your own “Always, sometimes, or never?” question about triangles or quadrilaterals, then answer it.
