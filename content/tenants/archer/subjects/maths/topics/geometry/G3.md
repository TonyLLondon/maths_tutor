## Fluency (AO1)

1. Angles on a straight line add up to __________°.
2. Angles around a point add up to __________°.
3. Vertically opposite angles (made where two lines cross) are always __________ (equal/different).
4. Two angles on a straight line are 70° and x. Find x.
5. Three angles around a point are 100°, 150°, and y. Find y.
6. The angles inside a triangle add up to __________°.
7. A triangle has angles 50° and 60°. Find the third angle.
8. The angles inside a quadrilateral add up to __________°.
9. When a line crosses two parallel lines, matching angles in the same corner position are called __________ angles, and they are equal.
10. Alternate angles (the "Z" pattern) between two parallel lines are always __________.
11. Co-interior angles (the "C" pattern) between two parallel lines add up to __________°.
12. A straight line is split into three angles: 35°, 85°, and z. Find z.

## Reasoning (AO2)

13. Two parallel lines are cut by a third line (a transversal). One angle is 65°. Find its corresponding angle, and explain how you know.

14. Explain, describing a diagram with two parallel lines and a transversal, why alternate angles are equal.

15. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Vertically opposite angles are equal.

16. A quadrilateral has angles 90°, 90°, 100°, and w. Find w and explain your method.

17. Sam says, "Co-interior angles are always equal." Is Sam correct? Explain, using an example.

18. The exterior angle of a triangle equals the sum of the two interior angles at the other two vertices. A triangle has interior angles 50° and 70°. Find the exterior angle at the third vertex.

19. Three angles meet at a point. Two of them are in the ratio 2 : 3, and the third angle is 100°. Find the two angles in the ratio.

20. Leo says a quadrilateral can never have three right angles and one obtuse angle. Is Leo correct? Explain your answer.

## Problem (AO3)

21. A ladder leans against a vertical wall, making a 65° angle with the ground. Assuming the wall and ground meet at a right angle, what angle does the ladder make with the wall?

22. Two roads are parallel, and a crossing road creates one angle of 118° where it meets the first road. Give the sizes of the two *different* angles found at that crossing (there are only two distinct sizes among the four angles).

23. A roof truss is an isosceles triangle with a base angle of 72° on both sides. Find the angle at the top.

24. A gate hinge crosses two bars, forming vertically opposite angles of (3x + 10)° and (2x + 40)°. Find x, then find the angle.

25. A kite-shaped flag has two equal angles of 100° each. Its other two angles are in the ratio 3 : 1. Find both of the other angles.

26. A bridge support triangle has an exterior angle of 130° at one vertex. The two interior angles at the other vertices are equal to each other. Find each of those two angles.

## Stretch

27. Explain how you could prove the angles in a triangle add up to 180°, by imagining a line drawn through one vertex, parallel to the opposite side, and using alternate angles.

28. A pentagon can be split into 3 triangles by drawing diagonals from one vertex. Use this to explain why the angles inside a pentagon add up to 540°.

29. Two parallel lines are cut by two transversals, forming a triangle between them. The two base angles of the triangle (found using alternate angles with the parallel lines) turn out to be 55° and 65°. Find the third angle of the triangle.

30. Write your own angle puzzle that uses parallel lines *and* a triangle, combining at least two angle facts. Solve it.
