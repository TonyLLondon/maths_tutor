## Getting started

1. A circle has a radius of 2 cm. Its diameter is twice as long. Find the diameter.
2. A circle has a diameter of 8 cm. Find its radius.
3. Which is the distance around a circle: circumference or area?
4. Which measures the space inside a circle: circumference or area?
5. A circle has radius 5 cm. Find its diameter.
6. True or false? A larger circle has a longer circumference.
7. Which unit is suitable for a circle’s area: cm or cm²?
8. A semicircle is what fraction of a whole circle?

## Fluency (AO1)

9. Circumference = π × diameter. Find the circumference of a circle with diameter 10 cm (use π ≈ 3.14).
10. Circumference = 2 × π × radius. Find the circumference of a circle with radius 5 cm (π ≈ 3.14).
11. Area of a circle = π × radius². Find the area of a circle with radius 4 cm (π ≈ 3.14).
12. A circle has diameter 14 cm. Find its radius.
13. A circle has radius 6 cm. Find its diameter.
14. Find the circumference of a circle with radius 10 cm (π ≈ 3.14).
15. Find the area of a circle with radius 3 cm (π ≈ 3.14).
16. Find the area of a circle with diameter 8 cm (π ≈ 3.14).
17. A circle has circumference 31.4 cm (π ≈ 3.14). Find its diameter.
18. A wheel has diameter 50 cm. How far does it travel in one full turn (π ≈ 3.14)?
19. Round π to 2 decimal places.
20. Which formula finds the **area** of a circle: πd, or πr²?


## More fluency

21. Write the formula for circumference of a circle with radius $r$.

22. Write the formula for area of a circle with radius $r$.

## Reasoning (AO2)

23. Explain why circumference = π × diameter, using the idea that π is the ratio of circumference to diameter for every circle.

24. A circle's radius is doubled. What happens to its circumference? Explain using the formula.

25. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Doubling a circle's radius doubles its area.

26. A circle's radius is doubled. What happens to its area? Explain using the formula.

27. Sam says, "If I know a circle's circumference, I can't find its radius." Is Sam correct? Explain how you could find it.

28. Two circles have radii in the ratio 1 : 2. What is the ratio of their circumferences? What is the ratio of their areas?

29. A circle has area 78.5 cm² (π ≈ 3.14). Find its radius.

30. Leo says, "A semicircle's perimeter is just half of the full circle's circumference." Is Leo correct? Explain your answer.

## Problem (AO3)

31. A circular table has diameter 1.2 m. Find its circumference (π ≈ 3.14), to 2 decimal places.

32. A circular garden has radius 7 m. Find its area (π ≈ 3.14).

33. A bicycle wheel has radius 30 cm. Find the distance travelled in 10 full turns (π ≈ 3.14), in metres.

34. A pizza has diameter 30 cm. Find its area (π ≈ 3.14), rounded to the nearest whole number.

35. A circular running track has a circumference of 400 m. Find its radius (π ≈ 3.14), rounded to 1 decimal place.

36. A clock face has radius 10 cm. The minute hand sweeps the whole circle in 60 minutes. Find the area swept in 15 minutes (a quarter of the circle), using π ≈ 3.14.

## Stretch

37. Explain why the ratio of a circle's circumference to its diameter is always exactly the same number (π), no matter the size of the circle.

38. A smaller circle has radius 5 cm. A larger circle's area is 200 cm² greater than the smaller circle's area. Find the larger circle's radius, using π ≈ 3.14, rounded to 1 decimal place.

39. A square has the same area as a circle of radius 5 cm. Find the side length of the square, using π ≈ 3.14, rounded to 1 decimal place.

40. Write your own circumference or area of a circle problem, and solve it, showing your working and using π ≈ 3.14.


## Mixed practice

41. Find the circumference of a circle radius 7 cm (π ≈ 3.14).

42. Find the area of a circle diameter 10 cm (π ≈ 3.14).
