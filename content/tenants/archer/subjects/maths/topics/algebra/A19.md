## Fluency (AO1)

1. Solve: $x + y = 9$, $x - y = 3$.
2. Solve: $x + y = 15$, $x - y = 5$.
3. Solve: $2x + y = 13$, $x + y = 8$.
4. Solve: $3x + y = 17$, $x + y = 7$.
5. Solve: $x + 3y = 14$, $x + y = 6$.
6. Solve: $2x + y = 11$, $2x - y = 5$.
7. Solve: $x = y + 4$, $x + y = 10$.
8. Solve: $y = 2x$, $x + y = 15$.
9. Solve: $x + y = 8$, $y = x - 2$.
10. Solve: $y = x + 1$, $2x + y = 13$.
11. Solve: $x + y = 20$, $x = 3y$.
12. Solve: $4x + y = 19$, $y = x + 4$.

## Reasoning (AO2)

13. Sam adds the equations $x + y = 10$ and $x - y = 2$ and writes $2x = 8$, so $x = 4$. Explain Sam's mistake and find the correct value of $y$.

14. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Adding two simultaneous equations together eliminates one of the variables.

15. Priya solves $x + 2y = 12$, $x + y = 7$ by subtracting the equations. Show her working and find $x$ and $y$.

16. Two pairs of equations: Pair A is $x + y = 6$, $x - y = 2$. Pair B is $x + y = 6$, $2x + 2y = 12$. Explain why Pair B doesn't have one unique solution, but Pair A does.

17. **True or false?** The point where two lines cross on a graph is the solution to their simultaneous equations. Give a reason for your answer.

18. Leo solves $x + y = 11$, $x - y = 1$ by subtracting to get $2y = 10$, so $y = 5$. Check his working and find $x$.

19. Compare solving $x + y = 10$, $x - y = 4$ by (a) **adding** the equations and (b) **subtracting** them. What does each method eliminate?

20. Mo says simultaneous equations always have exactly one solution. Give an example of two simultaneous equations with **no** solution, and explain why.

## Problem (AO3)

21. Two numbers have a sum of 15 and a difference of 5. Form and solve simultaneous equations to find the numbers.

22. Archer buys 2 pencils and 1 ruler for £3.50. Sloan buys 1 pencil and 1 ruler for £2.50. Form simultaneous equations and find the cost of a pencil and the cost of a ruler.

23. A cinema sells adult tickets for £$x$ and child tickets for £$y$. Three adults and two children cost £39. Two adults and two children cost £30. Form simultaneous equations and find $x$ and $y$.

24. The sum of two numbers is 20, and one number is three times the other. Form and solve simultaneous equations to find the numbers.

25. Two adult fun-run tickets and one family ticket cost £25. One adult ticket and one family ticket cost £18. Form simultaneous equations and find the cost of each ticket type.

26. A number $x$ plus twice a number $y$ equals 16. The number $x$ minus $y$ equals 1. Form and solve simultaneous equations to find $x$ and $y$.

## Stretch

27. Solve using elimination, multiplying one equation first: $3x + 2y = 16$, $x + y = 6$.

28. Solve: $2x + 3y = 16$, $3x - y = 2$.

29. Two rectangles have side lengths $x$ and $y$ that satisfy $2x + 2y = 26$ and $x + 3y = 27$. Solve to find $x$ and $y$.

30. Design your own real-world problem that leads to two simultaneous equations with whole-number solutions. Write the equations and solve them.
