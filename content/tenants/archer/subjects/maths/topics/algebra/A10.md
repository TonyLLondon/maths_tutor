## Fluency (AO1)

1. Find the gradient of the line joining $(0, 0)$ and $(1, 3)$.
2. Find the gradient of the line joining $(0, 2)$ and $(1, 5)$.
3. Find the gradient of the line joining $(0, 0)$ and $(2, 8)$.
4. Find the gradient of the line joining $(1, 2)$ and $(3, 8)$.
5. For $y = 3x + 2$, what is the gradient?
6. For $y = 3x + 2$, what is the $y$-intercept?
7. For $y = 5 - 2x$, what is the gradient?
8. For $y = 5 - 2x$, what is the $y$-intercept?
9. Write the equation of a line with gradient 4 and $y$-intercept 1.
10. Write the equation of a line with gradient $-2$ and $y$-intercept 6.
11. A line has gradient 2 and passes through $(0, 5)$. Find $y$ when $x = 3$.
12. A line has gradient $-1$ and passes through $(0, 4)$. Find $y$ when $x = 3$.

## Reasoning (AO2)

13. Priya finds the gradient of the line through $(0, 1)$ and $(2, 7)$ by calculating $\dfrac{7-1}{2-0}$. Show her calculation and confirm her answer.

14. **Always, sometimes, or never?** A line with a positive gradient slopes upward from left to right.

15. Sam says the gradient of $y = 4 - 3x$ is 4, because 4 is the first number in the equation. Explain Sam's mistake and give the correct gradient.

16. Two lines, $y = 2x + 3$ and $y = 2x - 1$, have the same gradient. Explain what this tells you about how the two lines are positioned relative to each other.

17. **True or false?** A steeper line always has a bigger gradient number, ignoring whether it is positive or negative.

18. Leo calculates the gradient between $(1, 5)$ and $(4, 5)$ and gets an error message on his calculator because he divided by zero. Explain what type of line this must be, and state its gradient.

19. Compare the lines $y = 5x + 1$ and $y = x + 1$. Which is steeper? Explain using the gradients.

20. Mo says a line with equation $y = -4x + 2$ has a gradient of 4. Explain Mo's mistake and give the correct gradient.

## Problem (AO3)

21. A taxi fare line has gradient 2 (£ per mile) and $y$-intercept 3 (£ fixed fee). Write the equation of the line, then find the fare for 6 miles.

22. A savings account graph has gradient 5 (£ per month) and starts at £20. Write the equation, then find the balance after 10 months.

23. A cycling distance-time graph has gradient 15 (km per hour). Write the equation for distance $d$ after $t$ hours, assuming it starts at $d = 0$.

24. A gas bill graph has gradient 0.1 (£ per unit used) and a standing charge of £15. Write the equation for the cost $C$ in terms of units used, $u$, then find the cost for 200 units.

25. Two phone plans have equations $y = 10 + 0.05x$ and $y = 5 + 0.1x$, where $x$ is the number of texts. Compare the gradients and explain which plan gets more expensive faster as texts increase.

26. A hire-car company charges according to $y = 40 + 25d$, where $d$ is the number of days. State the gradient and $y$-intercept, and explain what each means for the customer.

## Stretch

27. Find the gradient of the line joining $(-2, 1)$ and $(3, 11)$.

28. A line passes through $(0, 4)$ and has the same gradient as $y = 3x - 5$. Write the equation of this new line.

29. Two lines cross at $(2, 7)$. One has gradient 3, the other has gradient $-1$. Find the equation of each line.

30. A line has gradient $m$ and passes through $(0, c)$ and also through $(4, 20)$, where $c = 4$. Find the gradient $m$, then write the full equation of the line.
