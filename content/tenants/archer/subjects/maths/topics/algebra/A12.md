## Fluency (AO1)

1. What shape is the graph of $y = 2x + 1$?
2. What shape is the graph of $y = x^2 - 3$?
3. What shape is the graph of $y = x^3$?
4. What shape is the graph of $y = \dfrac{4}{x}$?
5. Complete the table for $y = x^3$.

   | $x$ | -2 | -1 | 0 | 1 | 2 |
   |---|---|---|---|---|---|
   | $y$ |   |   |   |   |   |

6. Complete the table for $y = \dfrac{6}{x}$.

   | $x$ | 1 | 2 | 3 | 6 |
   |---|---|---|---|---|
   | $y$ |   |   |   |   |

7. Which family of graph is always a straight line: linear, quadratic, cubic, or reciprocal?
8. Which family of graph is a U-shape: linear, quadratic, cubic, or reciprocal?
9. Can $y = \dfrac{k}{x}$ (for $k \ne 0$) ever equal 0? Explain.
10. Does the graph of $y = x^3$ pass through negative $y$-values for negative $x$?
11. Match each equation to its family: $y = 5x$, $y = x^2$, $y = x^3 + 1$, $y = \dfrac{2}{x}$.
12. How many times does the graph of $y = x^2$ change direction (turning points)? How many times does $y = x^3$ change direction?

## Reasoning (AO2)

13. Priya says $y = x^2$ and $y = x^3$ must look the same because both have "$x$ to a power". Explain why their graphs actually look different.

14. **Always, sometimes, or never?** A cubic graph like $y = x^3$ passes through the origin, $(0, 0)$.

15. Sam says the graph of $y = \dfrac{4}{x}$ can be plotted at $x = 0$. Explain why Sam is wrong.

16. Two graphs, $y = x^2$ and $y = -x^2$, are both quadratics. Describe how their shapes compare.

17. **True or false?** As $x$ gets very large in $y = x^3$, $y$ grows much faster than it does for $y = x^2$.

18. Leo says $y = 2x + 3$ and $y = x^2 + 3$ both cross the $y$-axis at the same point. Check this by finding the $y$-intercept of each, and explain what you find.

19. Compare the graph of $y = 3x$ (linear) with $y = \dfrac{3}{x}$ (reciprocal). How are their equations similar, and how are their graphs completely different?

20. Mo says every graph with an $x^2$ term must be a quadratic. Is $y = x^3 + x^2$ a quadratic? Explain, using the highest power of $x$ to decide the family.

## Problem (AO3)

21. A scientist plots data and notices the points curve upward slowly at first, then very steeply, matching a cubic pattern. Which equation type would best model this: linear, quadratic, or cubic?

22. A shop finds that as the price of an item, $x$, increases, the number sold, $y$, decreases according to $y = \dfrac{200}{x}$. What is $y$ when $x = 10$? What happens to $y$ as $x$ gets very large?

23. Explain why a reciprocal graph like $y = \dfrac{10}{x}$ can never touch the $x$-axis or the $y$-axis.

24. A ball's height over time follows a quadratic pattern (rises, then falls). Explain why a linear equation would not correctly model the ball's full flight.

25. A company's costs follow $y = x^3$ for very large orders, $x$. Explain, using the shape of the graph, why costs increase much faster for large $x$ than for small $x$.

26. Given the equations $y = 4x - 1$, $y = x^2 + 2$, and $y = x^3 - 5$, identify which one has a graph that is always increasing without ever turning back on itself in the same way a straight line does.

## Stretch

27. Explain, using values of $x = -2, -1, 0, 1, 2$, why $y = x^2$ produces only non-negative outputs but $y = x^3$ produces both negative and positive outputs.

28. Sketch (describe in words) how $y = \dfrac{1}{x}$ behaves as $x$ gets close to 0 from the positive side, and as $x$ gets very large.

29. A student says all cubic graphs cross the $x$-axis exactly once. Test this idea by considering $y = x^3 - x$, finding where it equals 0 for $x = -1, 0, 1$.

30. Explain how you could tell, just from an equation, whether its graph belongs to the linear, quadratic, cubic, or reciprocal family, without plotting any points.
