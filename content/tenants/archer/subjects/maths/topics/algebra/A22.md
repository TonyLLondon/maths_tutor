## Fluency (AO1)

1. Solve $x + 4 > 9$.
2. Solve $x - 3 < 5$.
3. Solve $2x > 14$.
4. Solve $\dfrac{x}{3} \le 4$.
5. Solve $3x - 2 < 10$.
6. Solve $2x + 5 \ge 13$.
7. Solve $4x - 1 > 11$.
8. Solve $x + 6 \le 2$.
9. Solve $5x < 20$.
10. Solve $\dfrac{x}{2} + 3 \ge 7$.
11. List all the integers that satisfy $-2 < x \le 3$.
12. Solve $3x + 1 \ge 2x + 6$.

## Reasoning (AO2)

13. Sam solves $-2x > 8$ and writes $x > -4$. What did Sam forget to do? Find the correct solution.

14. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Multiplying both sides of an inequality by a negative number reverses the inequality sign.

15. Priya says the solution to $x + 3 < 10$ is the same set of numbers as the solution to $x < 7$. Is she correct? Explain.

16. Two inequalities, $2x > 10$ and $x > 5$, look different. Explain why they have exactly the same solution.

17. **True or false?** The inequality $x \ge 4$ includes the number 4 itself. Give a reason for your answer.

18. Leo solves $-3x < 12$ and writes $x < -4$. Explain Leo's mistake, then solve the inequality correctly.

19. Compare the solutions of $x > 3$ and $x \ge 3$. What is the key difference between them?

20. Mo says every inequality has infinitely many solutions. Is Mo correct? Explain using an example.

## Problem (AO3)

21. Archer has £20 and wants to buy pens costing £3 each. Write an inequality for the number of pens, $p$, he can afford, and solve it.

22. A lift has a weight limit of 500 kg. Four people, each of mass $m$ kg, must not exceed this limit together. Write and solve an inequality for $m$.

23. Sloan needs more than 24 points to win a prize. She already has 15 points and earns 3 points per game. Write and solve an inequality for the number of games, $g$, she needs to play.

24. A rectangle has width $x$ cm and length $(x + 4)$ cm. Its perimeter must be less than 40 cm. Write and solve an inequality for $x$.

25. To ride a rollercoaster you must be at least 120 cm tall. Write this as an inequality using $h$ for height, and state whether a rider exactly 120 cm tall is allowed.

26. A car park charges £2 plus £1.50 per hour. Archer has £11 to spend. Write and solve an inequality for the number of hours, $t$, he can park.

## Stretch

27. Solve $2x - 3 \le 5x + 6$, giving your answer in the form $x \ge \ldots$.

28. Solve the inequality $-4 < 2x + 2 \le 10$ and list its integer solutions.

29. Find all integer values of $x$ that satisfy both $x + 2 > 3$ and $2x < 16$.

30. Design your own real-world problem that leads to a linear inequality with solution $x \le 5$. Write the inequality and show your working.
