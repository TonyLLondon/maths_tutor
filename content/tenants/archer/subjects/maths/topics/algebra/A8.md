## Getting started

1. A point is at $(1, 2)$. What is its $x$-coordinate?
2. A point is at $(1, 2)$. What is its $y$-coordinate?
3. Which point has $x = 2$ and $y = 3$?
4. Start at $(0, 0)$ and move 1 right. Which point do you reach?
5. Start at $(0, 0)$ and move 2 up. Which point do you reach?
6. For $y = x$, find $y$ when $x = 1$.
7. For $y = x + 1$, find $y$ when $x = 1$.
8. For $y = 2x$, find $y$ when $x = 2$.

## Fluency (AO1)

9. Write the coordinates of a point that is 3 units right and 2 units up from the origin.
10. Write the coordinates of a point that is 4 units left and 1 unit down from the origin.
11. Complete the table for $y = x + 1$.

   | $x$ | 0 | 1 | 2 | 3 |
   |---|---|---|---|---|
   | $y$ |   |   |   |   |

12. Complete the table for $y = 2x$.

   | $x$ | 0 | 1 | 2 | 3 |
   |---|---|---|---|---|
   | $y$ |   |   |   |   |

13. The points $(0, 1)$, $(1, 2)$, $(2, 3)$ lie on a straight line. What is $y$ when $x = 4$?
14. The points $(0, 0)$, $(1, 2)$, $(2, 4)$ lie on a straight line. What is $y$ when $x = 5$?
15. Which point is on the $x$-axis: $(0, 4)$, $(3, 0)$, or $(2, 2)$?
16. Which point is on the $y$-axis: $(0, 5)$, $(5, 0)$, or $(5, 5)$?
17. Is the point $(2, -3)$ above or below the $x$-axis?
18. For $y = x + 1$, find $y$ when $x = 10$.
19. For $y = 3x$, find $y$ when $x = 4$.
20. A straight line passes through $(0, 2)$ and $(1, 5)$. How much does $y$ increase when $x$ increases by 1?

## Reasoning (AO2)

21. A line goes through $(0, 0)$ and $(1, 3)$. Does it also go through $(2, 6)$? Explain how you know without plotting every point.

22. **True or false?** Every point with $y = x + 1$ has $y$ one more than $x$. Give one example to support your answer.

23. Priya says the point $(3, 3)$ is on the line $y = x$. Is she correct? Explain.

24. Two lines both pass through $(0, 0)$. Line A also passes through $(1, 2)$; Line B also passes through $(1, 4)$. Which line is steeper? Explain how you know.

25. **Always, sometimes, or never?** A straight line that passes through the origin has an equation of the form $y = mx$ (no number added on).

26. Sam completes a table for $y = x + 3$ but writes $y = 2$ when $x = 0$. Explain Sam’s mistake and give the correct value.

27. A line has equation $y = x - 2$. Does it pass through $(5, 3)$? Show your working.

28. Compare the lines $y = x + 1$ and $y = x + 5$. Explain how their graphs would look, drawn on the same axes.

## Problem (AO3)

29. A London taxi charges a fixed £3 hire fee plus £2 per mile. Complete the table and describe what the graph of cost against miles would look like.

    | Miles | 0 | 1 | 2 | 3 |
    |---|---|---|---|---|
    | Cost (£) |   |   |   |   |

30. A bike hire scheme charges £1 to unlock plus 50p for every 10 minutes. Write a table of cost for 0, 10, 20, and 30 minutes, and describe what the graph would look like.

31. A phone plan costs £5 a month plus 10p per text. Write the monthly cost as an equation using $t$ for the number of texts. How much would 20 texts cost in a month?

32. A tank starts with 5 litres of water and is filled at 2 litres per minute. Write an equation for the amount of water $w$ after $m$ minutes. How much water is there after 6 minutes?

33. Market Stall A charges (in £) the number of items plus £4. Market Stall B charges £2 per item. For how many items do both stalls cost the same? What is that cost?

34. A cyclist’s distance from home is given by $d = 3t$ (km, where $t$ is hours). After how many hours is the cyclist 15 km from home?

## More practice

35. A point is 2 units right and 3 units up from the origin. Write its coordinates.

36. Complete the table for **y = x + 2** when x = 0, 1, 2.

37. For the rule **y = 2x**, find y when x = 4.

38. Which axis does the point (0, 5) lie on?

39. Start at (1, 2) and move 3 units right. What is the new x-coordinate?

40. A straight line passes through (0, 0) and (2, 4). Using the same rule, what is y when x = 3?

## Stretch

41. Write coordinates for three points on a straight line that is neither horizontal nor vertical. Describe in words how $y$ changes when $x$ increases by 1.

42. A line has equation $y = 2x + 1$. Another line has equation $y = 2x + 5$. Will these two lines ever cross? Explain your reasoning.

43. Find the equation of a straight line that passes through $(0, 3)$ and $(2, 7)$. Show how you worked out the gradient and the number added on.

44. A graph shows the cost (£) of pizzas against the number bought. It passes through $(0, 5)$ and $(2, 17)$. Assuming a straight line, find the equation of the line and use it to predict the cost of 5 pizzas.
