## Getting started

1. A walk starts at 0 minutes. How much time has passed after 2 minutes?
2. A toy car travels 1 metre, then 1 more metre. How far has it travelled?
3. A distance graph starts at 0. Where is the object at the start?
4. A flat line on a distance graph means the distance does not change. Is the object moving?
5. A line rises from 0 metres to 3 metres. How much did the distance increase?
6. At 1 minute a graph shows 2 metres. What distance does it show?
7. At 3 minutes a graph shows 5 metres. What time is this point at?
8. A graph goes up as time passes. Is the distance increasing or decreasing?

## Fluency (AO1)

9. On a distance-time graph, what does a flat (horizontal) section mean about the journey?
10. On a distance-time graph, what does a steep upward line mean, compared with a gentle upward line?
11. Archer walks at a steady 4 km/h for 2 hours. How far has he travelled?
12. A car travels at a steady speed and covers 60 km in 2 hours. Find the speed in km/h.
13. On a container-filling graph (depth against time), what does a flat section mean is happening?
14. A narrow container is filled at a steady rate. Is the depth-time graph steeper or shallower than for a wide container filled at the same rate?
15. Sloan's bath fills steadily for 5 minutes, reaching a depth of 20 cm. Find the rate of filling in cm per minute.
16. A cyclist speeds up gradually. Does the distance-time graph curve, or stay a straight line?
17. On a distance-time graph, what does a line sloping downward mean?
18. A journey has three stages: walking, resting, then walking again. How many sections would the distance-time graph have?
19. Read a speed from a distance-time graph: the line rises 30 km over 2 hours. Find the speed.
20. A water tank is filled at 3 litres per minute. Find the volume after 4 minutes, assuming it starts empty.


## More fluency

21. A distance–time graph is flat (horizontal). What does that mean about speed?

22. On a distance–time graph, a steeper line means what?

## Reasoning (AO2)

23. Priya says a flat section on a distance-time graph means the person is walking very slowly. Explain why this is incorrect, and say what a flat section actually shows.

24. **Always, sometimes, or never?** A steeper line on a distance-time graph means a faster speed.

25. Sam looks at a depth-time graph for a bottle-shaped container (narrow middle, wide top and bottom) and says the graph must be a single straight line. Explain why Sam is wrong.

26. Two distance-time graphs both start at (0, 0). One reaches (2, 10), the other reaches (2, 20). Which represents the faster journey? Explain how you know.

27. **True or false?** A distance-time graph can never show a negative gradient if distance is measured from the starting point.

28. Leo looks at a container-filling graph with a very steep section and says "the container must be very wide there." Explain why Leo has it backwards, and describe what a steep section actually means.

29. Compare a distance-time graph for someone standing still with one for someone walking at a constant speed. How do the two graphs look different?

30. Mo says the graph for filling a cone-shaped container (point down, wide at top) would be a straight line, since it's a single smooth container. Explain why the graph would actually curve.

## Problem (AO3)

31. Archer cycles from home to the park (steady speed), stops for 10 minutes, then cycles home at a faster steady speed. Describe the three sections of his distance-time graph.

32. A bath is run with the plug in (filling), then the plug is pulled and the water drains out. Describe how the depth-time graph would look across both stages.

33. A train travels at 80 km/h for 1.5 hours, then slows and stops for a 20-minute break, then continues at 60 km/h for 1 hour. Find the total distance travelled.

34. A U-shaped vase is filled at a steady rate of water flow. Explain why the depth-time graph would start steep, become shallower in the wide middle, then get steeper again near the narrow top.

35. Sloan's heart rate graph rises quickly during a sprint, stays high and flat during steady running, then drops during rest. Sketch (describe in words) the three sections and what each means physically.

36. A delivery van's distance-time graph shows a straight line from (0, 0) to (3, 90), where distance is in km and time is in hours. Find the van's average speed, and explain what a curved section would have meant instead.

## Stretch

37. A cyclist's distance-time graph has gradient 20 km/h for the first hour, then gradient 10 km/h for the second hour. Find the total distance travelled after 2 hours.

38. Two containers are filled at the same rate: a cylinder and a cone (point up, wide base). Explain which container's depth-time graph would be a straight line, and why the other's would curve.

39. A runner's speed-time graph (not distance-time) shows speed increasing steadily from 0 to 8 m/s over 4 seconds, then staying constant. Describe what the corresponding distance-time graph would look like during these two stages.

40. Explain, using the idea of gradient, why a distance-time graph and a depth-time graph both use the *steepness* of the line to represent a rate — but describe what that rate means in each context.


## Mixed practice

41. Archer walks 2 km in 30 minutes. What is his average speed in km/h?

42. A tank drains at a constant rate. Would the volume–time graph slope up or down?
