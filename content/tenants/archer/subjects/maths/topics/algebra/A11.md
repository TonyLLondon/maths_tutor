## Getting started

1. Find $1^2$.
2. Find $2^2$.
3. Find $3^2$.
4. Find $4^2$.
5. For $y = x^2$, find $y$ when $x = 2$.
6. For $y = x^2$, find $y$ when $x = 3$.
7. For $y = x^2 + 1$, find $y$ when $x = 2$.
8. Which is greater: $2^2$ or $3^2$?

## Fluency (AO1)

9. Complete the table for $y = x^2$.

   | $x$ | -2 | -1 | 0 | 1 | 2 |
   |---|---|---|---|---|---|
   | $y$ |   |   |   |   |   |

10. Complete the table for $y = x^2 + 1$.

   | $x$ | -2 | -1 | 0 | 1 | 2 |
   |---|---|---|---|---|---|
   | $y$ |   |   |   |   |   |

11. What shape is the graph of $y = x^2$ called?
12. What is the smallest value that $y = x^2$ can ever take?
13. At what value of $x$ does $y = x^2$ reach its lowest point?
14. Find $y$ when $x = 3$ for $y = x^2 - 4$.
15. Find $y$ when $x = -3$ for $y = x^2 - 4$.
16. For $y = x^2$, is the graph the same shape on the left of the $y$-axis as on the right? What is this property called?
17. Find the two values of $x$ where $y = x^2 - 9$ crosses the $x$-axis (where $y = 0$).
18. Find the two values of $x$ where $y = x^2 - 4$ crosses the $x$-axis.
19. For the graph $y = x^2 + 2$, what is the $y$-intercept?
20. Does the graph of $y = x^2 + 5$ ever cross the $x$-axis? Explain your answer.


## More fluency

21. Does $y = x^2$ go through the point $(3, 9)$?

22. When $x = 0$, what is $y$ on $y = x^2 + 1$?

## Reasoning (AO2)

23. Priya says the graph of $y = x^2$ is a straight line because the equation has an $x$ in it. Explain why Priya is wrong.

24. **Always, sometimes, or never?** The graph of $y = x^2 + c$ (where $c$ is a positive number) crosses the $x$-axis.

25. Sam says $y = x^2$ has only one point where $y = 4$, because $2^2 = 4$. Explain why Sam is missing a solution, and find both values.

26. Two graphs, $y = x^2$ and $y = x^2 + 3$, have the same U-shape. Explain how the second graph's position compares to the first.

27. **True or false?** The lowest point (turning point) of $y = x^2 - 5$ is at $(0, -5)$.

28. Leo says the graph of $y = x^2$ has a turning point at $(0, 1)$ because he substituted $x = 1$. Explain Leo's mistake and find the correct turning point.

29. Compare the graphs of $y = x^2$ and $y = x^2 - 4$. How many times does each cross the $x$-axis, and why is there a difference?

30. Mo says the graph of $y = -x^2$ would look exactly like $y = x^2$. Test both at $x = 2$ and explain whether Mo is correct.

## Problem (AO3)

31. A ball's height in metres after being thrown is modelled by $h = 20 - x^2$, where $x$ is horizontal distance in metres. Find the height when $x = 3$.

32. Using the model $h = 20 - x^2$ from question 21, find the horizontal distance when the ball's height is 0 (hits the ground). Give your answer to 1 decimal place if needed.

33. A company's profit in thousands of pounds is modelled by $P = x^2 - 6x$, where $x$ is the number of products sold (in hundreds). Find the profit when $x = 2$.

34. Using the model from question 23, find the value(s) of $x$ that make the profit zero.

35. A diver's height above the water is modelled by $y = 4 - x^2$, where $x$ is time in seconds after leaving the board. Find the height at $x = 1$ second, and explain what a negative value of $y$ would mean physically.

36. A square garden bed has area $A = s^2$, where $s$ is the side length in metres. Sketch (describe in words) how the graph of $A$ against $s$ would look for positive values of $s$, and explain why negative values of $s$ don't make sense here.

## Stretch

37. Find the coordinates of the turning point of $y = x^2 - 6x + 9$ by first checking $y$ at $x = 3$.

38. Show that $y = x^2 + 2x + 1$ touches the $x$-axis at exactly one point by finding $y$ at $x = -1$.

39. Compare $y = 2x^2$ with $y = x^2$ at $x = 3$. Which graph is steeper (grows faster), and by how much at this point?

40. A quadratic graph $y = x^2 - k$ crosses the $x$-axis at $x = 4$ and $x = -4$. Find the value of $k$.


## Mixed practice

41. Sketch $y = x^2$ showing the turning point at $(0, 0)$.

42. The graph $y = x^2 + 2$ is the graph $y = x^2$ shifted how many units up?
