## Fluency (AO1)

1. Write an expression for "5 more than a number $x$".
2. Write an expression for "3 less than a number $y$".
3. Write an expression for "double a number $n$, then add 4".
4. Write an equation for "a number $x$ plus 7 equals 15".
5. Write an equation for "three times a number $y$ is 21".
6. Archer has $x$ sweets. Sloan has 4 more than Archer. Write an expression for the number of sweets Sloan has.
7. A ticket costs £$t$. Write an expression for the cost of 5 tickets.
8. A journey costs £3 plus £2 per mile, $m$. Write an expression for the total cost.
9. Write an equation for "a number $x$, when doubled and increased by 3, gives 17".
10. Write an expression for the perimeter of a square with side length $s$.
11. A rectangle has width $w$ and a length that is 4 cm longer than the width. Write an expression for the length.
12. Write an equation for "half of a number $n$, plus 6, is 10".

## Reasoning (AO2)

13. Sam translates "7 less than a number" as $7 - x$. Explain Sam's mistake and give the correct expression.

14. **Always, sometimes, or never?** "Twice a number plus 3" gives the same result as "3 plus twice a number".

15. Priya writes the equation for "a number $x$ tripled is 6 more than $x$" as $3x = x + 6$. Check her equation is correctly formed, then solve it.

16. Two students write different equations for the same problem, "a number plus 5 is 12". Student A writes $x + 5 = 12$. Student B writes $5 + x = 12$. Explain why both are correct.

17. **True or false?** The equation for "the sum of a number and its double is 18" is $x + 2x = 18$.

18. Leo writes "3 more than double a number $x$" as $2x + 3$, then writes the equation for "this equals 11" as $2x + 3 = 11$ and solves to get $x = 4$. Check his working.

19. Compare the expressions "$n + 4$" and "$4n$" for $n = 3$. Explain when each phrase — "4 more than $n$" or "4 times $n$" — would be used.

20. Mo is asked to write an equation for "a rectangle's perimeter is 20 cm, and the length is twice the width $w$". He writes $2w + 2(2w) = 20$. Is his equation correct? Explain, then solve for $w$.

## Problem (AO3)

21. Archer is $x$ years old. In 5 years he will be twice his current age minus 3. Write and solve the equation.

22. A taxi charges a £2.50 fixed fee plus £1.50 per mile, $m$. The total fare is £11.50. Write and solve an equation to find the number of miles.

23. Sloan buys 3 identical notebooks and gets £1 off with a voucher, paying £8 in total. Write and solve an equation for the cost of one notebook, $n$.

24. The width of a rectangle is $w$ cm, and the length is 5 cm more than the width. The perimeter is 34 cm. Write and solve an equation for $w$.

25. Three consecutive whole numbers add up to 54. Write and solve an equation to find the three numbers.

26. A school trip costs £5 per student plus a £40 coach hire fee. The total cost is £115. Write and solve an equation to find the number of students, $s$.

## Stretch

27. A number $x$ satisfies: "double the number, subtract 5, equals half the number plus 10". Write and solve the equation.

28. Two sisters' ages add to 26. In 4 years, the older sister will be twice as old as the younger sister is now. Write and solve equations to find their current ages.

29. A rectangle's length is 3 cm more than twice its width, $w$. Its area is 65 cm². Write an equation and solve it to find $w$.

30. Design your own word problem that leads to an equation with a fraction in it, like "a third of a number plus 4 is 9". Write the equation and solve it.
