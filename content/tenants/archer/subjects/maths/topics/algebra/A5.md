## Getting started

1. A bag has 2 red counters and $b$ blue counters. Write the total.
2. There are $n$ birds, then 1 more arrives. Write the new total.
3. A box holds 2 rows of $x$ toys. Write the total number of toys.
4. Use $C = a + b$. Find $C$ when $a = 2$ and $b = 3$.
5. Use $P = 2 + w$. Find $P$ when $w = 4$.
6. Use $T = 2n$. Find $T$ when $n = 3$.
7. Make $a$ the missing value in $a + 1 = 5$.
8. Make $b$ the missing value in $2b = 8$.

## Fluency (AO1)

9. The formula for perimeter of a square is $P = 4s$. Find $P$ when $s = 6$.
10. The formula for area of a rectangle is $A = lw$. Find $A$ when $l = 5$ and $w = 3$.
11. Rearrange $P = 4s$ to make $s$ the subject.
12. Rearrange $y = x + 3$ to make $x$ the subject.
13. Rearrange $y = x - 5$ to make $x$ the subject.
14. Rearrange $y = 2x$ to make $x$ the subject.
15. Rearrange $A = lw$ to make $l$ the subject.
16. The formula for a rectangle's perimeter is $P = 2l + 2w$. Find $P$ when $l = 7$ and $w = 4$.
17. Rearrange $y = 3x + 1$ to make $x$ the subject.
18. Rearrange $y = 5x - 2$ to make $x$ the subject.
19. The formula $C = 5 + 2n$ gives the cost, $C$, of $n$ items. Find $C$ when $n = 8$.
20. Rearrange $v = u + t$ to make $u$ the subject.


## More fluency

21. In $A = lw$, which letter is the subject?

22. In $v = u + at$, which letter is the subject?

## Reasoning (AO2)

23. Sam rearranges $P = 4s$ and writes $s = P - 4$. Explain Sam's mistake and give the correct rearrangement.

24. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Rearranging a formula changes which letter you can calculate directly, but not the relationship between the letters.

25. Priya rearranges $y = x + 3$ to get $x = y + 3$. Explain what Priya did wrong.

26. Two students rearrange $A = lw$ for $w$. One writes $w = \dfrac{A}{l}$, the other writes $w = A - l$. Which is correct? Explain how you know, using $A = 20$ and $l = 4$.

27. **True or false?** If $y = 2x + 5$, then subtracting 5 from both sides and dividing by 2 gives $x$ in terms of $y$.

28. Leo rearranges $y = 3x - 1$ and writes $x = \dfrac{y-1}{3}$, but Sam says it should be $x = \dfrac{y+1}{3}$. Who is correct? Show your working.

29. Compare substituting a value into a formula with rearranging a formula. Explain when you would use each approach.

30. Mo rearranges $C = 5 + 2n$ for $n$ and gets $n = \dfrac{C+5}{2}$. Explain Mo's mistake and find the correct rearrangement.

## Problem (AO3)

31. The formula for converting kilometres, $k$, to miles, $m$, is $m = 0.6k$. Rearrange to make $k$ the subject, then find $k$ when $m = 30$.

32. A taxi's cost is $C = 3 + 2m$ for $m$ miles. Rearrange to make $m$ the subject, then find $m$ when $C = 17$.

33. The formula for the perimeter of a rectangle is $P = 2l + 2w$. Rearrange to make $l$ the subject, then find $l$ when $P = 24$ and $w = 5$.

34. A recipe formula gives the cooking time in minutes as $T = 20 + 30w$, where $w$ is the weight in kg. Rearrange to make $w$ the subject, then find $w$ when $T = 110$.

35. The formula for converting Celsius, $c$, to a rough Fahrenheit estimate is $f = 2c + 30$. Rearrange to make $c$ the subject, then find $c$ when $f = 70$.

36. A phone plan costs $C = 10 + 0.05t$ pounds for $t$ texts. Rearrange to make $t$ the subject, then find $t$ when $C = 15$.

## Stretch

37. Rearrange $y = \dfrac{x}{2} + 4$ to make $x$ the subject.

38. Rearrange $A = \dfrac{1}{2}bh$ to make $h$ the subject.

39. Rearrange $2y = 3x - 6$ to make $x$ the subject.

40. The formula for the volume of a cuboid is $V = lwh$. Rearrange to make $h$ the subject, then find $h$ when $V = 60$, $l = 5$, and $w = 4$.


## Mixed practice

41. Make $x$ the subject of $x + 5 = 12$.

42. The perimeter of a rectangle is $P = 2l + 2w$. Make $w$ the subject.
