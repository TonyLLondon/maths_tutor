## Fluency (AO1)

1. Work out 18 − 3 × 4.
2. Work out 24 ÷ (2 + 2).
3. Work out 5 + 2² × 3.
4. Insert brackets to make a true statement: 3 + 4 × 5 = 35.
5. Work out 6 + 3².
6. Work out (6 + 3)².
7. Work out 20 − 2 × (3 + 1).
8. Work out 12 ÷ 3 + 2 × 5.
9. Work out 3³ − 10.
10. Work out (10 − 4) ÷ 3 + 1.

## More fluency

11. Work out 7 + 6 ÷ 2 − 1.
12. Work out 2 × (5 + 3²).
13. Insert brackets to make this correct: 2 + 3 × 4 − 1 = 19.
14. Work out 100 ÷ (5² − 5).

## Reasoning (AO2)

15. Priya says 10 − 6 ÷ 2 is the same as (10 − 6) ÷ 2. Is she correct? Show the value of each expression.

16. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Brackets change the answer of a calculation. Give one example where they do and one where they do not (if possible).

17. Archer says squaring a number always makes it bigger. Test his idea with a number between 0 and 1. Is he correct?

18. **Always, sometimes, or never?** For a calculation like *a* + *b* × *c*, working left to right (add, then multiply) gives the same answer as multiplying first. Give an example to support your answer.

19. Sloan calculates 4 + 5 × 2 and gets 18 by working left to right. Explain her mistake and give the correct answer.

## Problem (AO3)

20. A Tube day pass costs £8.50. Archer buys 3 passes and pays with a £30 note. He uses the rule "multiply first, then subtract": cost = 3 × 8.50, then change = 30 − cost. Work out his change.

21. A recipe needs 2 trays of 6 buns, then 3 more trays of 4 buns. Write one calculation using brackets to find the total number of buns, then work it out.

22. A garden is a square with side length (3 + 4) m. Work out its area.

23. A phone plan costs £15 a month plus £2 for every GB used over 5 GB. Sloan uses 8 GB in a month. Write a calculation for the total cost and work it out.

24. Work out 50 − (2 + 3)² ÷ 5.

## Stretch

25. Work out 100 − 4 × (3 + 2)².

26. Insert brackets to make this true: 6 + 4 × 2 − 3 = 17.

27. Work out 3 + 2 × (4² − 3 × 5).

28. Work out (2 + 3)² − (4 − 1)³.

## Mixed practice

29. Work out 15 − 3 × (6 − 4)² ÷ 2.

30. Archer says (2 + 3)² is the same as 2² + 3². Is he correct? Show both values.
