## Getting started

1. Write 3 × 3 as a square number.
2. Work out 2².
3. Work out 5².
4. What number multiplied by itself makes 9?
5. Work out 2 × 2 × 2.
6. Which is a square number: 9 or 10?
7. Complete the pattern of square numbers: 1, 4, 9, ___.
8. What is √16?
9. Work out 0².
10. Which is a square number: 7 or 8?
11. Work out √4.
12. Write 2 × 2 × 2 as “2 to the power ___”.

## Reasoning (AO2)

27. Archer says squaring a number always makes it bigger. Is he correct? Explain, using an example with a fraction.

28. **Always, sometimes, or never?** The square of a number is positive.

29. Sloan says $\sqrt{64} = 8$ and also $-8$, since both square to 64. Is she correct? Explain when we use the negative root.

30. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Cubing a negative number gives a negative result.

31. Compare $2^3$ and $3^2$. Which is bigger? Explain what a "power" means for each.

## Problem (AO3)

32. A square patio has an area of 121 m². What is the length of one side?

33. A cube-shaped storage box has a volume of 8 m³. What is the length of one edge?

34. Archer says $10^2$ is double $10^3$. Explain why he is wrong, then work out both values.

35. A square garden bed has sides of 7 m. What area of turf is needed?

36. A cubic gift box has edges of 4 cm. What is its volume?

## Stretch

37. Work out $\sqrt{225}$.

38. Work out $\sqrt[3]{1000}$.

39. Between which two consecutive whole numbers does $\sqrt{70}$ lie? Explain how you know.

40. Work out $2^3 \times 2^2$ without a calculator, then check by finding $2^5$. What do you notice?

## Mixed practice

41. Circle the numbers below that are perfect squares: 16, 20, 25, 32, 36.

42. Sloan says every cube number is also a square number. Test her idea using the cube numbers 1, 8, 27, 64.
