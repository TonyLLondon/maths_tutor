## Reasoning (AO2)

27. Archer says a length given as 8 cm to the nearest cm could only be exactly 8 cm. Is he correct? Explain what "correct to the nearest cm" really means.

28. **Always, sometimes, or never?** The lower bound of a rounded number is included in the error interval, but the upper bound is not.

29. Sloan measures a table as 150 cm to the nearest 10 cm. She says the table could be anywhere from 140 to 160 cm. Explain her mistake and give the correct error interval.

30. **Always, sometimes, or never?** For a number rounded to the nearest whole number, the error interval has a width of 1.

31. Compare the error interval for 30 rounded to the nearest 10 with the error interval for 30 rounded to the nearest 1. Which is wider, and why?

## Problem (AO3)

32. A field is measured as 200 m long, correct to the nearest 10 m. Write the error interval for the true length.

33. A bag of flour has a mass of 1.5 kg, correct to 1 decimal place. What is the smallest possible actual mass?

34. A journey is timed at 45 minutes, to the nearest 5 minutes. Write the error interval for the true time.

35. Archer's height is recorded as 140 cm, to the nearest cm. Sloan's height is recorded as 139 cm, to the nearest cm. Could Sloan actually be taller than Archer? Explain using error intervals.

36. A rectangle has a length of 12 cm, correct to the nearest cm. What is the greatest possible length?

## Stretch

37. A number, $n$, rounds to 60 when rounded to the nearest 10. Write the error interval for $n$ using inequality symbols.

38. A number, $x$, rounds to 4.5 when rounded to 1 decimal place. Write the error interval for $x$ using inequality symbols.

39. A crowd is reported as 5000, correct to the nearest 1000. Write the error interval, and say whether the actual crowd could have been 4400.

40. A measurement of 8.0 cm is given correct to 1 decimal place. Explain why the upper bound is 8.05 cm and not 8.1 cm.

## Mixed practice

41. A number rounds to 25 to the nearest 5. Write the error interval.

42. Sloan says the error interval for a number rounded to the nearest 100 is always wider than one rounded to the nearest 10. Is she correct? Explain.
