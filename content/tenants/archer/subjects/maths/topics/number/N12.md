## Getting started

1. Find half of 10.
2. Find one quarter of 12.
3. Find 50% of 8.
4. Find 10% of 20.
5. Find three quarters of 8.
6. A £10 toy is half price. What is the sale price?
7. Which percentage means one half?
8. Find 25% of 20.

## Reasoning (AO2)

23. A shop label says "10% off £40". Layla says the sale price is £4. What mistake has Layla made?

24. **Always, sometimes, or never?** To find 25% of a number you can divide by 4. Explain with an example.

25. Archer says finding **1/2** of a number always gives a smaller answer than finding **3/4** of the same number, when the number is positive. Is he correct? Explain.

26. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Finding 10% of a number is the same as dividing by 10. Give an example.

27. Sloan says 50% of 30 is the same as 30% of 50. Is she correct? Show both calculations.

## Problem (AO3)

28. A Camden market stall sells juice for £2.50 a cup. Archer buys 4 cups and pays with a £20 note. He is told he gets 10% off the total. How much change should he receive?

29. A jacket costs £64. In a sale it is reduced by **1/4**. What is the sale price?

30. Sloan scores 45 out of 60 in a maths test. What percentage did she get?

31. A school trip costs £36 per pupil. The school pays **1/3** of this cost for every pupil. How much does each family need to pay?

32. A phone normally costs £180. It is reduced by 15% in a sale. What is the sale price?

## More practice

33. Find 50% of 80.

34. Find 10% of 60.

35. Find 25% of 200.

36. Find 20% of £35.

37. A game costs £20 and is reduced by 10% in a sale. What is the sale price?

38. Sloan scores 16 out of 20 on a spelling test. What percentage score is that?

## Stretch

39. Work out 20% of £45 without a calculator. Then work out **2/5** of £45. What do you notice?

40. A price increases by 20% to become £96. What was the original price?

41. Work out 12.5% of £64, using the fact that 12.5% = **1/8**.

42. A number decreases by 30% and then increases by 30%. Is the result the same as the original number? Use £100 as an example to check.

## Mixed practice

43. Work out **3/4** of £120, then find 10% of the same amount. Which is bigger, and by how much?

44. Archer says increasing a number by 100% is the same as doubling it. Is he correct? Explain with an example.
