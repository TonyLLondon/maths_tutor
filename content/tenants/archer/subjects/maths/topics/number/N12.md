## Fluency (AO1)

1. Work out **1/2** of 84.
2. Work out **3/4** of 20.
3. Work out 10% of £60.
4. Work out 50% of 146.
5. Work out **1/4** of 60.
6. Work out 25% of £80.
7. Work out **2/3** of 27.
8. Work out 20% of 150.
9. Work out **3/5** of 40.
10. Work out 5% of £200.

## More fluency

11. Work out **1/3** of 90.
12. Work out 15% of £40.
13. Work out **3/8** of 64.
14. Work out 75% of 96.

## Reasoning (AO2)

15. A shop label says "10% off £40". Layla says the sale price is £4. What mistake has Layla made?

16. **Always, sometimes, or never?** To find 25% of a number you can divide by 4. Explain with an example.

17. Archer says finding **1/2** of a number always gives a smaller answer than finding **3/4** of the same number, when the number is positive. Is he correct? Explain.

18. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Finding 10% of a number is the same as dividing by 10. Give an example.

19. Sloan says 50% of 30 is the same as 30% of 50. Is she correct? Show both calculations.

## Problem (AO3)

20. A Camden market stall sells juice for £2.50 a cup. Archer buys 4 cups and pays with a £20 note. He is told he gets 10% off the total. How much change should he receive?

21. A jacket costs £64. In a sale it is reduced by **1/4**. What is the sale price?

22. Sloan scores 45 out of 60 in a maths test. What percentage did she get?

23. A school trip costs £36 per pupil. The school pays **1/3** of this cost for every pupil. How much does each family need to pay?

24. A phone normally costs £180. It is reduced by 15% in a sale. What is the sale price?

## Stretch

25. Work out 20% of £45 without a calculator. Then work out **2/5** of £45. What do you notice?

26. A price increases by 20% to become £96. What was the original price?

27. Work out 12.5% of £64, using the fact that 12.5% = **1/8**.

28. A number decreases by 30% and then increases by 30%. Is the result the same as the original number? Use £100 as an example to check.

## Mixed practice

29. Work out **3/4** of £120, then find 10% of the same amount. Which is bigger, and by how much?

30. Archer says increasing a number by 100% is the same as doubling it. Is he correct? Explain with an example.
