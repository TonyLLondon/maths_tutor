## Fluency (AO1)

1. List all the factors of 36.
2. List the first six multiples of 8.
3. Circle the prime numbers: 8, 13, 19, 21, 29.
4. Work out the highest common factor (HCF) of 20 and 30.
5. List all the factors of 24.
6. Work out the lowest common multiple (LCM) of 4 and 6.
7. Circle the prime numbers: 2, 9, 11, 15, 17.
8. Work out the HCF of 18 and 27.
9. List the first five multiples of 12.
10. Is 51 a prime number? Explain your answer.

## More fluency

11. Work out the LCM of 5 and 8.
12. List all the factors of 50.
13. Write 45 as a product of its prime factors.
14. Work out the HCF of 24 and 36.

## Reasoning (AO2)

15. Jess says 1 is a prime number because it only has one factor. Is Jess correct? Explain.

16. **Always, sometimes, or never?** The lowest common multiple (LCM) of two numbers is one of the numbers. Give an example for each answer you choose.

17. Sloan says every even number greater than 2 cannot be prime. Is she correct? Explain why, using the factors of an even number.

18. **Always, sometimes, or never?** The highest common factor (HCF) of two numbers is smaller than both numbers. Give an example.

19. Archer thinks that if a number is a multiple of 6, it must also be a multiple of 3. Is he correct? Explain using the relationship between 6 and 3.

## Problem (AO3)

20. A youth club on the Isle of Dogs is packing identical welcome packs for a trip. They have 48 water bottles and 72 snack bars. Each pack must have the same number of bottles and bars, with none left over. What is the greatest number of packs they can make? How many bottles and bars in each pack?

21. Two buses leave a station at the same time. Bus A leaves every 15 minutes and Bus B leaves every 20 minutes. After how many minutes will they next leave the station at the same time?

22. Archer wants to arrange 60 chairs into equal rows, with more than 5 chairs in each row but fewer than 15. List the possible number of chairs per row.

23. A baker has 90 cupcakes and 60 cookies. She wants to put them into boxes with the same number of cupcakes and the same number of cookies in every box, using all the items with none left over. What is the greatest number of boxes she can make?

24. Write 84 as a product of its prime factors.

## Stretch

25. Write 60 as a product of its prime factors.

26. Use prime factorisation to work out the HCF and LCM of 24 and 36.

27. A number less than 100 has exactly three factors. What could the number be? Explain how you found it.

28. Two numbers have HCF 6 and LCM 72. If one of the numbers is 18, what is the other number?

## Mixed practice

29. Circle the numbers below that are multiples of both 4 and 6: 8, 12, 18, 24, 30.

30. Sloan says a number can be both a factor and a multiple of another number only if the two numbers are equal. Is she correct? Give an example.
