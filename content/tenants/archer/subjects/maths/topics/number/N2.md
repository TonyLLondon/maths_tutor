## Getting started

1. Work out 6 + 3.
2. Work out 10 − 4.
3. What is the value of the digit 3 in 34?
4. Work out 5 + 5 + 5.
5. Share 12 counters equally between 2 children. How many does each child get?
6. Work out 4 × 2.
7. What number is 10 more than 27?
8. Work out 20 + 8.
9. Work out 8 + 1.
10. Work out 10 − 5.
11. What is double 6?
12. Work out 20 ÷ 2.

## Reasoning (AO2)

27. Explain why multiplying a number by 10 moves every digit one place to the left in the place-value grid.

28. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Subtracting a negative number gives a bigger answer than the number you started with. Give an example.

29. Archer says 4.5 × 10 = 4.50. Sloan says the answer is 45. Who is correct? Explain using place value.

30. A pupil calculates 3.2 × 100 and writes the answer as 3.200. Explain the mistake and give the correct answer.

31. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Dividing a positive number by 10 makes it ten times smaller.

## Problem (AO3)

32. Archer's bank account has £45. He spends £60 on a bike helmet using his overdraft. What is his new balance?

33. A school trip costs £2376, shared equally between 18 pupils. How much does each pupil pay?

34. A recipe for 4 people needs 250 g of flour. Sloan wants to make the recipe for 24 people. How much flour does she need?

35. The temperature in London is 3 °C. In Moscow it is −18 °C. What is the difference between the two temperatures?

36. A shop orders 48 boxes of pencils, each box containing 144 pencils. How many pencils are there in total?

## Stretch

37. Work out 3072 ÷ 24. Show your method.

38. A submarine is at −250 m (below sea level). It rises 180 m, then dives a further 95 m. What is its final depth?

39. A 4-digit number has 6 in the thousands place, 0 in the hundreds place, 4 in the tens place, and 9 in the units place. Write the number, then multiply it by 100.

40. Work out 5.6 × 34, showing your working.

## Mixed practice

41. A factory makes 2880 biscuits, packed equally into 24 boxes. If 3 boxes are damaged and cannot be sold, how many biscuits are still safe to sell?

42. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Multiplying two negative numbers gives a positive answer.
