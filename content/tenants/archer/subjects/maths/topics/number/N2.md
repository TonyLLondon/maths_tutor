## Reasoning (AO2)

15. Explain why multiplying a number by 10 moves every digit one place to the left in the place-value grid.

16. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Subtracting a negative number gives a bigger answer than the number you started with. Give an example.

17. Archer says 4.5 × 10 = 4.50. Sloan says the answer is 45. Who is correct? Explain using place value.

18. A pupil calculates 3.2 × 100 and writes the answer as 3.200. Explain the mistake and give the correct answer.

19. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Dividing a positive number by 10 makes it ten times smaller.

## Problem (AO3)

20. Archer's bank account has £45. He spends £60 on a bike helmet using his overdraft. What is his new balance?

21. A school trip costs £2376, shared equally between 18 pupils. How much does each pupil pay?

22. A recipe for 4 people needs 250 g of flour. Sloan wants to make the recipe for 24 people. How much flour does she need?

23. The temperature in London is 3 °C. In Moscow it is −18 °C. What is the difference between the two temperatures?

24. A shop orders 48 boxes of pencils, each box containing 144 pencils. How many pencils are there in total?

## Stretch

25. Work out 3072 ÷ 24. Show your method.

26. A submarine is at −250 m (below sea level). It rises 180 m, then dives a further 95 m. What is its final depth?

27. A 4-digit number has 6 in the thousands place, 0 in the hundreds place, 4 in the tens place, and 9 in the units place. Write the number, then multiply it by 100.

28. Work out 5.6 × 34, showing your working.

## Mixed practice

29. A factory makes 2880 biscuits, packed equally into 24 boxes. If 3 boxes are damaged and cannot be sold, how many biscuits are still safe to sell?

30. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Multiplying two negative numbers gives a positive answer.
