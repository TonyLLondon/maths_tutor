## Fluency (AO1)

1. Write 4000 in standard form.
2. Write 350,000 in standard form.
3. Write 0.006 in standard form.
4. Write 72,000 in standard form.
5. Write $3 \times 10^4$ as an ordinary number.
6. Write $5 \times 10^{-3}$ as an ordinary number.
7. Write 0.00081 in standard form.
8. Write 91,000,000 in standard form.
9. Write $6.2 \times 10^5$ as an ordinary number.
10. Write $7 \times 10^{-2}$ as an ordinary number.

## More fluency

11. Write 0.000045 in standard form.
12. Write $2.3 \times 10^6$ as an ordinary number.
13. Write 8,300,000 in standard form.
14. Write $9 \times 10^{-4}$ as an ordinary number.

## Reasoning (AO2)

15. Archer writes 45,000 as $45 \times 10^3$. Explain why this is not correctly written in standard form, and give the correct version.

16. **Always, sometimes, or never?** In standard form, the first number is between 1 and 10.

17. Sloan says a negative power of 10 in standard form means the original number is negative. Is she correct? Explain what a negative power actually shows.

18. **Always, sometimes, or never?** A number written in standard form with a positive power of 10 (10¹ or more) is greater than or equal to 1.

19. Compare $3 \times 10^4$ and $3 \times 10^{-4}$. Which is bigger, and by roughly how many times?

## Problem (AO3)

20. The population of a city is about 2,500,000. Write this in standard form.

21. A red blood cell is about 0.000008 m wide. Write this in standard form.

22. The distance from the Earth to the Moon is about $3.84 \times 10^5$ km. Write this as an ordinary number.

23. A company made a profit of $1.2 \times 10^6$ pounds. Write this as an ordinary number.

24. A virus is about $9 \times 10^{-8}$ m across. Write this as an ordinary number.

## Stretch

25. Work out $(2 \times 10^3) \times (3 \times 10^2)$, giving your answer in standard form.

26. Work out $(6 \times 10^5) \div (2 \times 10^2)$, giving your answer in standard form.

27. Order these numbers from smallest to largest: $4 \times 10^3$, $3.9 \times 10^4$, $5 \times 10^2$, $1 \times 10^5$.

28. Work out $(4 \times 10^{-2}) \times (2 \times 10^3)$, giving your answer in standard form.

## Mixed practice

29. Write 6,050,000 in standard form.

30. Sloan says $8 \times 10^2$ is bigger than $2 \times 10^5$ because 8 is bigger than 2. Explain what is wrong with her thinking and state which is actually bigger.
