## Getting started

1. What is 10 × 10?
2. Write 100 as a power of 10.
3. What is 10³?
4. How many zeros are in 10,000?
5. Work out 3 × 100.
6. Which is larger: 5 × 10 or 5 × 100?
7. Write 700 as 7 multiplied by a power of 10.
8. What is 4 × 1000?
9. Write 1000 as a power of 10.
10. Work out 6 × 10.
11. How many zeros are in 100?
12. Work out 2 × 1000.

## Reasoning (AO2)

27. Archer writes 45,000 as $45 \times 10^3$. Explain why this is not correctly written in standard form, and give the correct version.

28. **Always, sometimes, or never?** In standard form, the first number is between 1 and 10.

29. Sloan says a negative power of 10 in standard form means the original number is negative. Is she correct? Explain what a negative power actually shows.

30. **Always, sometimes, or never?** A number written in standard form with a positive power of 10 (10¹ or more) is greater than or equal to 1.

31. Compare $3 \times 10^4$ and $3 \times 10^{-4}$. Which is bigger, and by roughly how many times?

## Problem (AO3)

32. The population of a city is about 2,500,000. Write this in standard form.

33. A red blood cell is about 0.000008 m wide. Write this in standard form.

34. The distance from the Earth to the Moon is about $3.84 \times 10^5$ km. Write this as an ordinary number.

35. A company made a profit of $1.2 \times 10^6$ pounds. Write this as an ordinary number.

36. A virus is about $9 \times 10^{-8}$ m across. Write this as an ordinary number.

## Stretch

37. Work out $(2 \times 10^3) \times (3 \times 10^2)$, giving your answer in standard form.

38. Work out $(6 \times 10^5) \div (2 \times 10^2)$, giving your answer in standard form.

39. Order these numbers from smallest to largest: $4 \times 10^3$, $3.9 \times 10^4$, $5 \times 10^2$, $1 \times 10^5$.

40. Work out $(4 \times 10^{-2}) \times (2 \times 10^3)$, giving your answer in standard form.

## Mixed practice

41. Write 6,050,000 in standard form.

42. Sloan says $8 \times 10^2$ is bigger than $2 \times 10^5$ because 8 is bigger than 2. Explain what is wrong with her thinking and state which is actually bigger.
