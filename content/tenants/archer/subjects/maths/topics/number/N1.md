## Fluency (AO1)

1. Write these integers in order from smallest to largest: −7, 3, −2, 0, 5.
2. Write these decimals in order from smallest to largest: 0.4, 0.04, 0.44, 0.404.
3. Which is larger: **2/3** or **5/8**? Show how you know.
4. Fill in the correct symbol (< , > , or =): −8 ___ −3.
5. Fill in the correct symbol (< , > , or =): 0.7 ___ 0.70.
6. Order these fractions from largest to smallest: **1/2**, **1/3**, **1/4**, **1/6**.
7. Write these integers in order from largest to smallest: −10, 15, −1, 8, −6.
8. Fill in the correct symbol (< , > , or =): −2.5 ___ −2.
9. Write these numbers in order from smallest to largest: 3.2, 3.02, 3.22, 3.002.
10. Which is smaller: **3/5** or **7/10**? Show how you know.

## More fluency

11. Order these temperatures from coldest to warmest: −6 °C, 2 °C, −1 °C, 0 °C, 4 °C.
12. Write these fractions in order from smallest to largest: **3/4**, **1/4**, **1/2**.
13. Fill in the correct symbol (< , > , or =): **5/8** ___ 0.6.
14. Write these decimals in order from largest to smallest: 1.1, 1.01, 1.11, 1.001.

## Reasoning (AO2)

15. On a number line, −3 is to the left of 1. Where would −3.5 go compared with −3? Explain in one sentence.

16. **Always, sometimes, or never?** A negative number is less than any positive number. Give a counterexample if you think it is *never* true.

17. Sloan says that since 0.5 has fewer digits than 0.45, it must be smaller. Is Sloan correct? Explain using place value.

18. Priya thinks −10 is bigger than −2 because 10 is bigger than 2. Explain why she is wrong.

19. **Always, sometimes, or never?** A fraction with a larger denominator is a smaller fraction, when the numerators are the same. Give an example.

## Problem (AO3)

20. At 8 a.m. the temperature at Greenwich Park was −2 °C. By midday it had risen by 5 °C. At 6 p.m. it had fallen by 7 °C from midday. What was the temperature at 6 p.m.?

21. Archer, Sloan, and their dad share a bag of sweets. Archer eats **1/4** of the bag, Sloan eats **3/10** of the bag, and their dad eats the rest. Who ate more, Archer or Sloan?

22. Four rivers have these depths at a gauge, where negative means below the marker: −0.4 m, 0.6 m, −1.2 m, 0.3 m. Order these from deepest below the marker to highest above it.

23. A shop has three offers on the same football: £12.50, £12.05, £12.55. Which offer is cheapest?

24. Order these four numbers from smallest to largest: **−1/2**, 0.3, −0.8, **3/4**.

## Stretch

25. Write these in order from smallest to largest: **1/2**, 0.45, 40%, **2/5**.

26. Put these in order from smallest to largest: −2², (−2)², −(2²), 2².

27. Order these from smallest to largest: √16, 3², 2³, √9.

28. A number line shows marks at every 0.1 from −1 to 1. Between which two consecutive marks would you place −0.37? Explain how you know.

## Mixed practice

29. Order these five numbers from smallest to largest: −3, 2.5, −3.5, 0, 2.

30. Sloan says any fraction with numerator 1 is smaller than any fraction with a numerator greater than 1. Is Sloan correct? Give an example to support your answer.
