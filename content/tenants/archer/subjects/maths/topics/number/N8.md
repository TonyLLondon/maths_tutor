## Getting started

1. What fraction of 2 equal pieces is one piece?
2. Work out 1/2 + 1/2.
3. What is 1/4 of 8?
4. Which is larger: 1/2 or 1/4?
5. A pizza is cut into 4 equal slices. You eat 1 slice. What fraction is left?
6. A circle has a radius of 3 cm. What is its diameter?
7. What special symbol is used in exact circle calculations?
8. Work out 2/4 + 1/4.
9. Work out **1/3** + **1/3**.
10. One equal slice is taken from a cake cut into 4 equal pieces. What fraction was taken?
11. Work out **1/2** of 10.
12. A circle has radius 2 cm. What is its diameter?

## Fluency (AO1)

13. Work out $\dfrac{1}{4} + \dfrac{1}{8}$ as a fraction in its simplest form.
14. Work out $\dfrac{2}{3} - \dfrac{1}{6}$ as a fraction.
15. Work out $\dfrac{1}{2} \times \dfrac{2}{5}$.
16. Work out $\dfrac{3}{4} \div \dfrac{1}{2}$.
17. A circle has radius 4 cm. Give its circumference **exactly**, in terms of $\pi$.
18. A circle has radius 5 cm. Give its area **exactly**, in terms of $\pi$.
19. Work out $\dfrac{2}{5} + \dfrac{3}{10}$ as a fraction.
20. Work out $\dfrac{5}{6} - \dfrac{1}{3}$ as a fraction.
21. A circle has diameter 10 cm. Give its circumference exactly, in terms of $\pi$.
22. Work out $\dfrac{3}{5} \times \dfrac{2}{3}$.

## More fluency

23. Work out $1\dfrac{1}{4} + \dfrac{3}{4}$, giving your answer as a whole number or simplest fraction.
24. A circle has radius 6 cm. Give its area exactly, in terms of $\pi$.
25. Work out $\dfrac{7}{8} - \dfrac{1}{4}$ as a fraction.
26. Work out $\dfrac{2}{3} \div \dfrac{4}{9}$.

## Reasoning (AO2)

27. Archer works out the circumference of a circle with radius 3 cm using a calculator and gets 18.849... . Sloan writes it exactly as $6\pi$ cm. Explain why Sloan's answer is more exact.

28. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Adding two fractions with different denominators means you must find a common denominator first.

29. Sloan says $\dfrac{1}{2} + \dfrac{1}{3} = \dfrac{2}{5}$ because she added the numerators and the denominators. Explain her mistake and find the correct answer.

30. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Leaving an answer in terms of $\pi$ (like $4\pi$) is more exact than rounding to a decimal.

31. Compare $\dfrac{3}{4} \times \dfrac{2}{3}$ worked out exactly with fractions to the same calculation rounded to a decimal at each step. Which method keeps the answer exact?

## Problem (AO3)

32. A circular pond has radius 7 m. Give the exact area of the pond in terms of $\pi$.

33. Archer eats $\dfrac{1}{3}$ of a pizza and Sloan eats $\dfrac{1}{4}$ of the same pizza. What fraction of the pizza is left?

34. A circular table has diameter 2 m. Give the exact circumference of the table edge, in terms of $\pi$.

35. A recipe needs $\dfrac{2}{3}$ cup of flour for one batch. How much flour is needed for $1\dfrac{1}{2}$ batches?

36. Two circular flowerbeds have radii 3 m and 5 m. Find the exact total area of both flowerbeds, in terms of $\pi$.

## Stretch

37. Work out $2\dfrac{1}{3} \times 1\dfrac{1}{2}$, giving your answer as a mixed number in its simplest form.

38. A circle has area $25\pi$ cm². Find its radius.

39. A circle has circumference $10\pi$ cm. Find its diameter.

40. Work out $\dfrac{3}{4} + \dfrac{2}{3} - \dfrac{1}{6}$ as a single fraction in its simplest form.

## Mixed practice

41. Work out $\dfrac{5}{8} \div \dfrac{5}{6}$ as a fraction in its simplest form.

42. Sloan says the exact area of a circle is always "nicer" to work with than a decimal answer, because it has no rounding error. Is she correct? Explain.
