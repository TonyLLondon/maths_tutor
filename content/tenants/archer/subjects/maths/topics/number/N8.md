## Fluency (AO1)

1. Work out $\dfrac{1}{4} + \dfrac{1}{8}$ as a fraction in its simplest form.
2. Work out $\dfrac{2}{3} - \dfrac{1}{6}$ as a fraction.
3. Work out $\dfrac{1}{2} \times \dfrac{2}{5}$.
4. Work out $\dfrac{3}{4} \div \dfrac{1}{2}$.
5. A circle has radius 4 cm. Give its circumference **exactly**, in terms of $\pi$.
6. A circle has radius 5 cm. Give its area **exactly**, in terms of $\pi$.
7. Work out $\dfrac{2}{5} + \dfrac{3}{10}$ as a fraction.
8. Work out $\dfrac{5}{6} - \dfrac{1}{3}$ as a fraction.
9. A circle has diameter 10 cm. Give its circumference exactly, in terms of $\pi$.
10. Work out $\dfrac{3}{5} \times \dfrac{2}{3}$.

## More fluency

11. Work out $1\dfrac{1}{4} + \dfrac{3}{4}$, giving your answer as a whole number or simplest fraction.
12. A circle has radius 6 cm. Give its area exactly, in terms of $\pi$.
13. Work out $\dfrac{7}{8} - \dfrac{1}{4}$ as a fraction.
14. Work out $\dfrac{2}{3} \div \dfrac{4}{9}$.

## Reasoning (AO2)

15. Archer works out the circumference of a circle with radius 3 cm using a calculator and gets 18.849... . Sloan writes it exactly as $6\pi$ cm. Explain why Sloan's answer is more exact.

16. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Adding two fractions with different denominators means you must find a common denominator first.

17. Sloan says $\dfrac{1}{2} + \dfrac{1}{3} = \dfrac{2}{5}$ because she added the numerators and the denominators. Explain her mistake and find the correct answer.

18. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Leaving an answer in terms of $\pi$ (like $4\pi$) is more exact than rounding to a decimal.

19. Compare $\dfrac{3}{4} \times \dfrac{2}{3}$ worked out exactly with fractions to the same calculation rounded to a decimal at each step. Which method keeps the answer exact?

## Problem (AO3)

20. A circular pond has radius 7 m. Give the exact area of the pond in terms of $\pi$.

21. Archer eats $\dfrac{1}{3}$ of a pizza and Sloan eats $\dfrac{1}{4}$ of the same pizza. What fraction of the pizza is left?

22. A circular table has diameter 2 m. Give the exact circumference of the table edge, in terms of $\pi$.

23. A recipe needs $\dfrac{2}{3}$ cup of flour for one batch. How much flour is needed for $1\dfrac{1}{2}$ batches?

24. Two circular flowerbeds have radii 3 m and 5 m. Find the exact total area of both flowerbeds, in terms of $\pi$.

## Stretch

25. Work out $2\dfrac{1}{3} \times 1\dfrac{1}{2}$, giving your answer as a mixed number in its simplest form.

26. A circle has area $25\pi$ cm². Find its radius.

27. A circle has circumference $10\pi$ cm. Find its diameter.

28. Work out $\dfrac{3}{4} + \dfrac{2}{3} - \dfrac{1}{6}$ as a single fraction in its simplest form.

## Mixed practice

29. Work out $\dfrac{5}{8} \div \dfrac{5}{6}$ as a fraction in its simplest form.

30. Sloan says the exact area of a circle is always "nicer" to work with than a decimal answer, because it has no rounding error. Is she correct? Explain.
