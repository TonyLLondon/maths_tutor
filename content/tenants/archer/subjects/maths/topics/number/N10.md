## Fluency (AO1)

1. Write **7/10** as a decimal.
2. Write 0.25 as a fraction in its simplest form.
3. Write **3/5** as a decimal.
4. Shade one grid of 100 squares to show 0.35. What fraction is that in simplest form?
5. Write 0.8 as a fraction in its simplest form.
6. Write **9/20** as a decimal.
7. Write **1/8** as a decimal.
8. Write 0.12 as a fraction in its simplest form.
9. Write **11/25** as a decimal.
10. Write 0.06 as a fraction in its simplest form.

## More fluency

11. Write **7/8** as a decimal.
12. Write 0.375 as a fraction in its simplest form.
13. Write **3/4** as a percentage.
14. Write 0.6 as a percentage.

## Reasoning (AO2)

15. Mo says 0.8 and **4/5** are the same amount. Is Mo correct? Show your working.

16. **Always, sometimes, or never?** A decimal with one decimal place is the same as a fraction with denominator 10. Give an example if it is *sometimes* true.

17. Archer thinks **1/3** as a decimal is exactly 0.3. Is he correct? Explain.

18. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Multiplying a decimal by 10 moves every digit one place to the left. Give an example.

19. Sloan says **2/5** is bigger than 0.35 because 2 and 5 add up to 7, which is more than 0.35. Explain what is wrong with her reasoning, and find the correct comparison.

## Problem (AO3)

20. Archer walks **3/4** km to school and **1/2** km to the library after school. How far does he walk in total? Give your answer as a decimal number of kilometres.

21. A recipe needs 0.6 kg of flour. Sloan has a bag containing **3/5** kg. Does she have enough? Explain.

22. A shop sells ribbon at 75p per metre. Archer buys **4/5** of a metre and Sloan buys 0.7 of a metre. Who buys more ribbon, and by how much (as a decimal)?

23. A jug holds 1.5 litres. Archer pours out **3/4** of a litre. How many litres are left? Give your answer as a decimal.

24. A pizza is cut into 8 equal slices. Archer eats 0.375 of the pizza. How many slices did he eat?

## Stretch

25. Place these on a blank 0 to 1 line: 0.2, **1/4**, 0.75, **3/10**. List them in order from smallest to largest.

26. Write **5/6** as a decimal, rounded to 2 decimal places.

27. Which is bigger: **7/9** or 0.78? Show how you know using division.

28. A decimal written as 0.3333... has a recurring digit. Write it as a fraction in its simplest form. Explain your method.

## Mixed practice

29. Write these in order from smallest to largest: 0.4, **2/5**, **9/20**, 0.5.

30. Sloan says 0.09 is bigger than 0.1 because "09" looks like a bigger number than "1". Explain what is wrong with her thinking and state which decimal is actually bigger.
