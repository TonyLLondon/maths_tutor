## Getting started

1. What is √4?
2. What is √9?
3. Work out 10².
4. Work out 2³.
5. In 4², what is the small raised number?
6. Write 5 × 5 using an index.
7. Which is larger: 3² or 2²?
8. Fill the box: √□ = 5.
9. Work out 4².
10. Write 3 × 3 using an index: 3 to the power ___.
11. Work out √36.
12. Work out 10¹.

## Fluency (AO1)

15. Work out $4^0$.

17. Simplify $x^3 \times x^5$.
18. Simplify $y^7 \div y^3$.
19. Work out $10^0$.

## More fluency

## Reasoning (AO2)

27. Archer says $2^0 = 0$. Is he correct? Explain what any non-zero number to the power 0 equals.

28. **Always, sometimes, or never?** $a^m \times a^n = a^{m+n}$.

29. Sloan says $2^{-2}$ means "negative 4". Is she correct? Explain what a negative index really means.

30. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Dividing powers with the same base means you subtract the indices.

31. Compare $3^2 \times 3^3$ and $3^5$. Are they equal? Explain using the laws of indices.

## Problem (AO3)

32. A number is multiplied by itself, then the result is multiplied by the same number three more times. Write this as a power and work out $2^4$.

33. Archer says $x^3 \times x^3$ is the same as $x^9$. Use the laws of indices to show he is wrong, and give the correct answer.

34. Simplify $\dfrac{a^5 \times a^2}{a^3}$.

35. Between which two consecutive whole numbers does $\sqrt{40}$ lie? Explain how you know.

36. Between which two consecutive whole numbers does $\sqrt{90}$ lie?

## Stretch

37. Simplify $\dfrac{2^7}{2^4} \times 2^2$, giving your answer as a number.

38. Work out the value of $4^{-2}$ as a fraction.

39. Simplify $(x^3)^0$.

40. Show that $\sqrt{36} \times \sqrt{4} = \sqrt{144}$. Is $\sqrt{a} \times \sqrt{b} = \sqrt{a \times b}$ always true? Test with two more numbers.

## Mixed practice

41. Simplify $m^4 \times m^{-1}$.

42. Sloan says $2^{-1} < 2^0 < 2^1$. Is she correct? Work out each value to check.
