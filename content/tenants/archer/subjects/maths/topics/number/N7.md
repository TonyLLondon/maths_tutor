## Fluency (AO1)

1. Simplify $3^2 \times 3^4$, writing your answer as a single power.
2. Simplify $5^6 \div 5^2$.
3. Work out $4^0$.
4. Work out $2^{-1}$ as a fraction.
5. Simplify $x^3 \times x^5$.
6. Simplify $y^7 \div y^3$.
7. Work out $10^0$.
8. Work out $3^{-2}$ as a fraction.
9. Simplify $(a^2)^3$.
10. Simplify $2^5 \times 2^{-2}$, giving your answer as a single power.

## More fluency

11. Simplify $(b^4)^2$.
12. Work out $5^{-1}$ as a fraction.
13. Simplify $\dfrac{x^6}{x^2}$.
14. Work out $2^{-3}$ as a fraction.

## Reasoning (AO2)

15. Archer says $2^0 = 0$. Is he correct? Explain what any non-zero number to the power 0 equals.

16. **Always, sometimes, or never?** $a^m \times a^n = a^{m+n}$.

17. Sloan says $2^{-2}$ means "negative 4". Is she correct? Explain what a negative index really means.

18. **Always, sometimes, or never?** Dividing powers with the same base means you subtract the indices.

19. Compare $3^2 \times 3^3$ and $3^5$. Are they equal? Explain using the laws of indices.

## Problem (AO3)

20. A number is multiplied by itself, then the result is multiplied by the same number three more times. Write this as a power and work out $2^4$.

21. Archer says $x^3 \times x^3$ is the same as $x^9$. Use the laws of indices to show he is wrong, and give the correct answer.

22. Simplify $\dfrac{a^5 \times a^2}{a^3}$.

23. Between which two consecutive whole numbers does $\sqrt{40}$ lie? Explain how you know.

24. Between which two consecutive whole numbers does $\sqrt{90}$ lie?

## Stretch

25. Simplify $\dfrac{2^7}{2^4} \times 2^2$, giving your answer as a number.

26. Work out the value of $4^{-2}$ as a fraction.

27. Simplify $(x^3)^0$.

28. Show that $\sqrt{36} \times \sqrt{4} = \sqrt{144}$. Is $\sqrt{a} \times \sqrt{b} = \sqrt{a \times b}$ always true? Test with two more numbers.

## Mixed practice

29. Simplify $m^4 \times m^{-1}$.

30. Sloan says $2^{-1} < 2^0 < 2^1$. Is she correct? Work out each value to check.
