## Fluency (AO1)

1. How many unit cubes (1 cm cubes) fit in one layer of a cuboid that is 4 cubes long and 3 cubes wide?
2. The same cuboid is 2 cubes high. How many unit cubes fill it completely? This number is the **volume** in cm³.
3. A cube has edge length 3 cm. How many 1 cm³ cubes fit inside it? Write the volume.
4. Complete: Volume of a cuboid = __________ × __________ × __________ (use words or letters).
5. A box is 5 cm by 2 cm by 2 cm. Work out its volume in cm³.
6. Which holds more: a cuboid 4 × 3 × 2 cm or a cube of edge 3 cm? Write both volumes.

## Reasoning (AO2)

7. Two cuboids have the same volume of 12 cm³. One is 6 × 2 × 1 cm. Give **different** whole-number dimensions for another cuboid with volume 12 cm³.

8. **Always, sometimes, or never?** If you double the height of a cuboid and keep the base the same, you double the volume. Use the cuboid 4 × 3 × 2 cm to test.

9. Lin stacks 1 cm cubes into a tower 5 cubes tall with a 2 × 2 square base. Jess builds a flat layer 4 × 5 × 1 cm. Who used more cubes? Show how you know.

## Problem (AO3)

10. A small storage box in Archer’s room is 10 cm long, 8 cm wide, and 6 cm high. Work out how many 1 cm³ cubes would fill it. Give the volume in cm³.

11. A toy crate is made from unit cubes: 6 long, 4 wide, and 3 high. How many unit cubes were used? If one layer is removed from the top, what is the new volume?

## Stretch

12. A cuboid has volume 60 cm³. Its length is 5 cm and its width is 4 cm. Find its height.

13. Explain in two or three sentences why **area** (cm²) and **volume** (cm³) measure different things. Use an example with a box.
