## Fluency (AO1)

1. A rectangle is 8 cm long and 5 cm wide. Work out its **perimeter**.
2. The same rectangle (8 cm by 5 cm). Work out its **area**.
3. Write the formula for the area of a rectangle using **length** and **width**.
4. A square has side length 6 m. Find its perimeter and its area.
5. Change 200 cm to metres. Change 3 m² to cm² (remember: 1 m = 100 cm).
6. An L-shape is made from two rectangles: one is 10 cm by 4 cm, the other is 6 cm by 4 cm (they share the 4 cm side). Find the **total area** of the L-shape.

## Reasoning (AO2)

7. Two rectangles each have area 24 cm². Rectangle A is 6 cm by 4 cm. Rectangle B is 12 cm by 2 cm. Do they have the same **perimeter**? Show working for both perimeters.

8. **Always, sometimes, or never?** If you double the length and width of a rectangle, you double its area. Test with the rectangle 3 cm by 2 cm.

9. Sam finds the perimeter of a 7 cm by 3 cm rectangle by calculating $7 \times 3 = 21$ cm. Explain Sam’s mistake and give the correct perimeter.

## Problem (AO3)

10. A classroom wall is covered with rectangular posters. Each poster is 50 cm wide and 70 cm tall. How much **wall area** do three posters cover altogether? Give your answer in cm².

11. A garden path is an L-shape. The whole shape fits in a 9 m by 6 m rectangle, but a 3 m by 2 m corner is grass (not path). The rest is path. Work out the **area of the path** in m². Sketch the L-shape and label the measurements you use.

## Stretch

12. A rectangle has perimeter 30 cm and length 10 cm. Find its width and its area.

13. Design a compound shape made from two rectangles with **total area** 48 cm². Write the dimensions you chose and show how you calculated the area.
