## Fluency (AO1)

1. A map has scale 1 cm : 2 km. Two places are 5 cm apart on the map. How far apart are they in real life?

2. A plan uses scale 1 cm : 5 m. A room is 8 cm long on the plan. How long is the real room?

3. Real distance is 12 km. On a map with scale 1 cm : 3 km, how many centimetres apart should the places be?

4. A recipe for 4 people uses 200 g flour. Write the scale factor from 4 people to 8 people.

5. A scale drawing of a toy bus is 6 cm long. The real bus is 12 m long. What is the scale of the drawing in the form 1 cm : … m?

## Reasoning (AO2)

6. On a London tourist map the scale is 1 cm : 500 m. Alex measures 3 cm between two Tube stations. Alex says the real distance is 15 km. **Is Alex correct?** Show your working and explain.

7. **True or false?** If you double every length on a scale drawing, the real distances also double. Explain your answer in one or two sentences.

8. A map says 1 : 25 000. What does the “1” represent and what does the “25 000” represent?

## Problem (AO3)

9. On a map of central London, scale 1 cm : 250 m, the distance from the London Eye to Big Ben is 4 cm. How far is the real walk?

10. A cycle route map uses 1 cm : 1 km. The route from Hyde Park Corner to Tower Bridge is 7.5 cm on the map. How many kilometres is the ride?

11. A recipe for 6 scones uses 300 ml milk. You want to make 18 scones for a trip picnic. How much milk do you need?

## Stretch

12. A rectangular playground is 45 m by 30 m. Draw a scale plan that fits in a 9 cm by 6 cm rectangle. Write the scale you used as 1 cm : … m.

13. Two maps of the same street show the same real distance as 2 cm on Map A (1 cm : 400 m) and as 5 cm on Map B. What is the scale of Map B in the form 1 cm : … m?
